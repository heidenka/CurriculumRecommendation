Academic Information

Content This unit models and promotes the active teaching and learning of mathematics in middle and upper primary school. Students explore current evidence-based practices for promoting understanding, fluency, problem solving and reasoning in teaching and learning, set within the content strands of number and algebra, measurement and geometry, and statistics and probability. Students continue to develop and share a portfolio of teaching and learning strategies that actively engage students in working mathematically, and that develop the general capabilities and address the cross-curriculum priorities of the Australian Curriculum. Students apply their knowledge and skills to prepare a unit of work/program for teaching a mathematics topic or theme in the middle and upper primary years.

Unit Information

Mathematics in Middle and Upper Primary provides an introduction to the knowledge, skills, and competencies required by teachers of primary school mathematics (Years 3 to 6). It is focused on equipping pre-service teachers to help children achieve learning outcomes associated with the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics. The unit is delivered as a series of interactive workshops, each focused on a specific pedagogical aspect of mathematics teaching that is well grounded in theories of teaching and learning. Through participation in these workshops, it is expected that preservice teachers will learn to recognise, appreciate and model effective teaching strategies and construct their own philosophical and practical understanding of the teaching and learning process. As well as addressing pedagogical issues, each workshop is designed to help pre-service teachers to become familiar with the mathematical content encountered during middle and upper primary school, and with a variety of teaching resources suitable to teach this content.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assignment 1 Professional Practice (40%)
This assessment comprises three parts:
Collaborative workshop presentation; portfolio items for teaching and learning; and professional practice (including workshop and online participation)
Due dates
Your group and the date for your workshop presentation will be negotiated in the first workshop.
Portfolio items will be due on Wednesday 28 October.
The assessment of professional practice is ongoing throughout the semester.
Part a: Collaborative workshop presentation (20%)
The date for your workshop presentation will be negotiated in the first workshop, on Monday 20 July. Presentations begin in the second workshop.
Your workshop presentation will provide you with the opportunity to work with one or two colleagues to explore a range of activities related to a “Manipulative Focus”, and to make a 15 minute presentation to the workshop group about this manipulative. It is an opportunity to practise some teaching skills and to try something innovative. Please plan the presentation so that the total time is not longer than 15 minutes. 
Before the workshop, you will need to provide a plan for the presentation. This plan should include: 
the expected outcomes of the presentation;
a brief overview of the history, development and use of the manipulative; 
a learning and teaching activity illustrating an application of the manipulative;
a brief rationale for the chosen activity, including links to the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics;
other related notes, ideas, and suggestions for using the manipulative; and 
references.
Assessment criteria:
the collaborative plan containing the elements above; 
links made to the ACM, and to the wider literature;
understanding of how the manipulative may be used in learning and teaching; 
application of the manipulative in an engaging activity; 
inclusion of resources and suggestions for applying the manipulative in a range of ways; 
the clarity, quality and creativity of the presentation. 
After your presentation, please upload your notes to the appropriate discussion forum on the unit LMS website.
Part b: Professional practice (10%)
Please contribute weekly to the discussion forum for the unit. Your contributions should include at least one learning and teaching resource for each of numeracy, number, algebra, measurement, geometry, statistics, probability, and problem solving, and should include critical appraisal and feedback on at least one contribution made by one of your colleagues. 
Assessment criteria
contributions regularly made to the discussion forum; 
constructive response to a contribution made by one of your colleagues. 
Part c: Portfolio items for teaching and learning (10%)
In this part of the assignment you are asked to collate four (4) of your favourite resources, selected from all of the contributions made to the forums, and comprising one resource from each of numeracy; number and algebra; measurement and geometry; and statistics and probability.
For each of these four items, write a paragraph describing why it is a suitable learning and teaching resource or activity. Indicate if appropriate, how the resource could be improved.
Assessment criteria
collation of four learning and teaching resources from the discussion forum; 
critical appraisal of each of the four resources.
It is expected that these portfolio items would be suitable to include in your teaching portfolio.
Assignment 2 Lesson Sequence (30%)
Due date: Wednesday 12 August.
For this assignment task, you are required to plan a series of four consecutive 45 minute Mathematics lessons that could be implemented in your Semester 2 Professional Practicum Experience. If you are not sure of the class you will be teaching, then plan for a class of 24 mixed ability students in Year 5. 
You are to focus on one part of the content strands of the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics, and to develop learning activities that embed the four proficiencies (understanding, fluency, problem solving, and reasoning) described in the curriculum documents.
Your assignment should:
use the elements of a lesson plan as developed in our workshops; 
begin with a brief statement of your developing approach to learning and teaching in mathematics; 
include a brief overview or outline describing the focus and intentions of the lesson sequence; 
include the relevant lesson outcomes addressing content descriptions (with AC-M code) from Australian Curriculum – Mathematics; 
identify where and how the proficiencies are being addressed; 
identify where and how the General Capabilities of the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics are being addressed; 
identify areas for differentiation, including learning support for low achieving students, and ideas for extension and enrichment; 
include a detailed series of lesson steps (including time allocation) indicating the sequence of activities (use a suitable lesson structure, such as Bybee’s 5Es with timing 1:3:3:1, or similar); 
identify the manipulative resources you will use to develop conceptual understanding; 
include all worksheets, handouts, websites, e-resources, etc., that will be used during the four lessons. 
A draft of this assignment is required for the workshop in Week 3, during which formative peer evaluation and feedback will occur, and generic formative feedback will be provided.
Please refer to the rubric for further marking guidelines. At the beginning of your assignment response, please include your student chosen assessment criterion with standards of achievement.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
