Unit Details

PLNG5411 - Urban and Regional Analysis - SEM-2-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

This unit develops a range of quantitative and qualitative techniques for urban and regional analysis. This includes advanced methods for understanding economic systems and how they interface with the environment, social structure, demography and land use. Specific training is provided in modelling, social and economic indicators, population forecasting, and advanced policy analysis. There will be a field trip to Western Australian regional towns to give students a greater sense of the challenges facing and opportunities of towns outside of major metropolitan areas. This includes specific environmental issues. A Microsoft Excel class will be held prior to the commencement of the course to teach basic skills to those students not familiar with the software, as this will be used throughout the course.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is designed as an intensive learning experience.  Therefore, the workload is quite substantial over the course of the month or so that the unit is offered. The sessions are structured to be highly engaging. There will be a combination of class discussions around assigned readings as well as quantitative and qualitative analytical activities using Excel, policy documents and resources found through desk top research. It is critical that you:
•	Attend all scheduled sessions
•	Complete all assigned readings
•	Ensure you are able to make time outside of the scheduled class times for additional work

Unit Structure

This unit is an intensive unit with classes structured across 2 x 2 week blocks. Between these blocks of 2 weeks, you will have a weeks break so you can catch up on anything you have not had time to do. There will be a field trip at the end of the second week in the first 2 week block. This will be 8:30 to 5pm on a Saturday (see class schedule). You will be required to come to 3 x 3 hour classes each week. Two of these classes will be recorded with lectures and analytical work, and the other will not as it is primarily set up in an interactive class discussion format.
Please check your unit allocations on https://www.cas.uwa.edu.au/student

Participation

There are no particular participation requirements. As much as possible, lectures will be recorded, templates for analysis will be supplied so students can complete in their own time. The lecturer is also available via email, and on Wednesday 12-1pm for consultation. Special meetings with the lecturer can also be arranged. 
However, please note this is an intensive Master's unit - so much of the learning occurs during the discussions with other students, particularly during lessons discussing readings. As such, you are encouraged to come as much as possible as it will be difficult to gain all the learnings remotely.

Referencing Requirements

You are not expected to reference the reading logs you complete each week, as it is assumed that you are referring to the reading you are given. However, all other work (presentations, final report) must be properly referenced using APA citation style. This can be found at: http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa  
Please note: Failing to comply with proper referencing of work from elsewhere is seen as plagiarism or copying. All forms of cheating, plagiarism and copying are condemned by the University as unacceptable behaviour. The Faculty’s policy is to ensure that no student profits from such behaviour. Generally a failure will be recorded for the subject in which the cheating has occurred. Serious cases shall be referred to the University’s Board of Discipline. All students should note that cases of copying are automatically reported to the Dean and documentary evidence along with associated correspondence is placed on the student’s permanent record.

Texts

There are no texts for this unit. However, the unit does involve a close reading of around 16 scholarly papers over the course of four weeks. Completing these readings forms a core part of the assessment. Readings will be provided by email a week in advance of needing to be completed. They will also be available on LMS around one month prior to the beginning of the course.If you would like to do additional reading, the following journals are also a valuable and up to date resource for this course:Area; Annals of the American Association of Geographers; Australian Geographer; Australasian Journal of Regional Studies; Australian and New Zealand Journal of Regional Studies; Economic Geography; Growth and Change; International Review of Regional Science; Journal of Economic Geography; Journal of Regional Science; Geographical Research; Papers in Regional Science; Regional Studies; Spatial Economic Analysis; Urban Studies.

Other

Please ensure you bring your laptop computer to each class, as you will be using this to perform analysis of regional towns as well as additional internet research and the downloading of data sets. You will need Microsoft Excel or equivalent (e.g. Apple Numbers) to perform simple analytical tasks. Whilst you will be given tuition in this software, you may need to do additional background learning by yourself if you have never used it before. Readings will be supplied approximately one month before the beginning of the unit. It will be assumed that you have read the readings for each week before coming to class. It is difficult to fully engage in understanding the course content if you do not do this. 
Other points to note.
1) Late submission of Assignments:
A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
2) Penalty for exceeding word limit:
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
3) Academic conduct:
Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.
Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
