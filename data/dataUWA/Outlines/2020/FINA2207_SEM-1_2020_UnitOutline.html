Academic Information

Content This unit emphasises how parties external to the company employ publicly available information, company reports and financial market information to evaluate the current, and predict the future, financial condition of the company. The analytical techniques of financial statement analysis (e.g. fundamental analysis, cash flow analysis and pro forma analysis) and the issue of the reliability and quality of publicly available information are discussed and explored. Analytical tools are applied in different decision contexts such as equity valuation, mergers and acquisitions, lending decisions and the evaluation of managerial performance.

Unit Information

Welcome to FINA2207: Business Analysis and Valuation. I am Dr. Mahmoud Agha, the lecturer and the unit coordinator. This unit covers two parts: the analysis of financial information and valuation models. The objective of this unit is to equip you with the knowledge and skills you need to process the financial data and value a business for the purpose of making sound investment decisions.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The delivery of the unit involves a series of two-hour weekly lectures and one-hour workshops. During the lectures, a broad overview of the topic will be provided and important issues will be highlighted. Numerical examples will be also discussed to enforce your understanding. You are expected to read the assigned chapters and articles to bring yourself up to speed on the issues. Subsequently, you will be given the opportunity to discuss these issues in the workshops. In addition, you can approach me or your tutor for help with unit content or queries about the assessments. I am also available in office almost every day; please contact me by e-mail to set an appointment if you need any help with regard to the materials covered.
To maximise your learning in this unit, it is expected that you attend all lectures and workshops. Group assignment is a major assessment item for this unit; therefore, it is very important that you attend the workshops to get familiar with your group mates and keep in touch with them outside the class to do the group assignment. All members of a group are expected to contribute to the assessment, and if you are absent you will be letting your team down.
The emphasis of the unit is on developing skills and having a grounded understanding of business valuation. You will have an opportunity to value a business in your team project. You will use real data and understand the problems facing the business and use the skills acquired in the unit. The team project is comprehensive in that it involves a topic that enables you to use your skills in working in a team. The team you work with will be the same team as in your workshop assessments. The tutors will allocate you into teams in your first workshop.

Unit Structure

The unit is presented over a series of two-hour weekly lectures and one-hour workshops. The main lecture will be offered every Wednesday from 2pm-4pm in BUSN: [G91] Wesfarmers Lecture Theatre. An overflow lecture will be offered on Tuesday 4pm -6pm in ARTS: [G59] for the first three weeks only.  The two-hour lecture consists of a discussion of the topic and numerical examples. The one hour workshops are where concepts from the previous week are reinforced by solving a set of questions that cover the concepts discussed in the lecture. You are expected to read the assigned materials before class to familiarise yourself with the broad concepts introduced in the lecture. This will maximise your understanding of the material presented and give you an opportunity to revisit concepts you may have forgotten but are expected to know beforehand. Please refer to the Class Allocation System CAS page for the lecture and tutorial timetable.

Referencing Requirements

It is important that the referencing of any sources used in your written work is done properly, if only to substantiate the points you are making in your assignment or project. The Harvard style is the preferred used unless otherwise stated by the unit coordinator. There are some notes for guidance which have been prepared by the library staff: ‘Citing your Sources Harvard Style’ . The EndNote software package is a really good system for building up a database of references. Not everyone will want to invest the time in using this system but you should consider it if you intend to build up resource materials or plan to undertake extensive research in a particular area. The library staff have also developed a tutoring package: ‘A Quick Guide to Using EndNote’ which provides the basics for using EndNote with an essay http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/endnote

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
