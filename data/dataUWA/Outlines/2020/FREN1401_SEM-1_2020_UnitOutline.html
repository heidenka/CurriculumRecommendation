Unit Details

FREN1401 - French Studies 1 - SEM-1-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

This unit offers an introduction to French grammar and vocabulary to allow students to communicate both orally and in writing in simple situations. It develops cultural and linguistic awareness and focuses on contemporary spoken and written language used in France and other French-speaking countries. Students achieve language proficiency equivalent to Level A1 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages.
This unit leads to FREN1402. Together, FREN1401 and FREN1402 provide access to Level 2 and Level 3 French units, which not only increase language competency but offer the opportunity to further engage with French history and culture, including literature, film and contemporary society, and to take advantage of a range of exchange opportunities.
Learning outcomes 
Students are able to (1) develop elementary skills in reading, writing, listening and speaking in the French language; (2) be familiar with sociolinguistic conventions in French; (3) have an elementary knowledge of culture and society in French-speaking countries.; (4) develop independent learning skills and interpersonal skills; and (5) have an increased awareness of language and language use in both French and English.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Successful language learning can only be achieved by regular practice of the language in all its forms. For this reason, it is highly recommended that students complete the online activities (Connect) for each class uploaded to LMS.  It will help improve the command of the French language.

Unit Structure

4 hrs per week for 12 weeks: 1x lecture, 3x language classes. Lectures are recorded but students must attend all language classes.
For a detailed programme of activities, please see the unit outline on LMS.

Assessment overview
Typically this unit is assessed in the following ways: 
(1) 10 weekly in-class and online tests from week 3 to week 12 (40%)
(2) a role-play in week 12 (10%)
(3) an end-of-semester examination during examination period (50%)
Details of assessments
•	There will be 4 kinds of weekly tests:
- 2 in-class dictations where your teacher will read a dialogue to you in French and you will have to fill in the blanks in the transcript according to what s/he says IN FRENCH (self-corrected in class) 
- 5 online grammar tests which you will access through LMS (corrections and marks available immediately) 
- 1 aural comprehension where you will listen to a dialogue in French and answer True/False questions (corrected by the teacher and handed back the following week)
- 2 in-class written expression tests where you will need to write compositions of 25 words (corrected by the teacher and handed back the following week)
It is important that you get feedback from each test in order to be aware of your mistakes, to troubleshoot problem areas early and to get a better mark on your next test! When immediate feedback is not available the lecturer will hand back the tests on the Monday of the following week. If you do not get your test back, please contact the unit coordinator. 
- Role play: in your last French class (Language 3, week 12) students will perform in pairs a 2 minute role play. A list of topics can be found at the end of this outline and on LMS. Students should not read from a script. Students will be assessed on grammatical accuracy and ease at speaking French. 
- A 2-hour written exam held during the formal exam period. There are 3 sections: 20 sentences where you need to translate the brackets into French (40 marks); 6 x 25-word compositions from a choice of 7 (30 marks) and a reading comprehension where you will read a text in French and answer questions in English (30 marks). One French-English, English-French dictionary (paper only) is allowed in the exam, but there must be no markings in it. Spot checks will be conducted in the examination venue.
Late submission of Assignments
 
A penalty of 5 per cent of the possible mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays), after which the assigned work is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

FREN1401 Role play Topics
•	2-3 minutes, needs to be memorised not read 
•	marked on fluency/pronunciation (50%) & accuracy of language (50%)
1.	You are in a French restaurant. You would like to order typical French food. You ask the waitperson.
2.	You need a nice outfit for your friend’s wedding. You are shopping and ask the shop assistant for advice.
3.	You would like to go out tonight but your friend/partner prefers to stay home and watch TV. You try to convince her/him.
4.	You are planning a trip to France with your friends. Tell each other which cities/tourist attractions/museums… you would like to visit, where you would like to stay… Try to come up with an itinerary for 1 week.
5.	You are organising a surprise Birthday party for your best friend. Discuss what you are going to do with your friends. 
6.	You meet a French person in Perth who seems lost. You tell her/him what there is to do and see around Perth, and the best places to go out at night.
7.	You have invited your French friends over for a typical Australian dinner. You go food shopping.
8.	With your best friend, you reflect on your studies at UWA. List your likes/dislikes/preferences… 
9.	You met someone you really like last night. You describe this person to your best friend.
10.	You meet someone you like in a pub and talk about your interests, family, study…

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
