Academic Information

Content This unit is delivered as a series of interactive workshops with an emphasis on developing science content knowledge, science pedagogy and improving students' attitudes towards science in early childhood settings and primary classrooms. Emphasis is placed on inquiry learning, utilising a 'hands-on, minds-on' approach to engage children. The role of evidence in science is discussed. The important place of prior knowledge in children's science learning is explored with an emphasis on the relationship between everyday concepts and scientific concepts. Students become familiar with the three curriculum strands: science understanding (biological sciences, chemical sciences, earth and space sciences, physical sciences); science inquiry skills; and science as a human endeavour of the Australian curriculum: science. Students also become familiar with the place of science within the Early Years Learning Framework. Students develop understanding of integrating mathematics with science. Students begin to develop a resource file and professional library as a means of developing their own science content knowledge.

Unit Information

As with all Masters level units, there is a considerable amount of reading. Parts of this unit involve a flipped mode - where work MUST be completed before coming to class. You are asked to be fully prepared for each workshop by ensuring that you have completed all required work or readings. You are also expected to be an active participant in each workshop, whether this be performing hands-on activities, group discussion or class discussion.  
The aim of this unit is to enable pre-service teachers to develop their knowledge and skills for the effective teaching of Science, with an emphasis on the use of an integrated curriculum. Curriculum integration allows teachers to transcend subject and disciplinary areas, developing integrative activities that are not restricted with subject or discipline. In curriculum integration, schedules revolve around projects and activities rather than subject areas. Research has shown that integration increases student's motivation, attitudes and allows them to see relationships between concepts (or learning areas) in more meaningful ways. Thus, students develop greater understanding of how the world operates, rather than treating learning areas as separate and unrelated entities.
Within this unit you will analyse key features of the Science Learning Area of the Australian Curriculum and related documents; appraise a range of teaching, learning and assessment strategies suitable for Science at the early childhood and primary school level; plan and sequence teaching and learning experiences appropriate for early childhood and primary students that achieve the outcomes from the Science Learning Area; critique both disciplinary and integrated approaches to teaching and learning in drawing on Science, STEM and sustainability; and begin to build a resource file and professional library as a means of developing your understanding of integrated planning and teaching.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Various learning and teaching strategies are incorporated into EDUC5508 Integrated Studies: Science. 
- Participation in a wide range of hands-on science activities covering different conceptual areas is fundamental to this unit to highlight the needs for concrete experiences
- Various mechanisms for engagement are demonstrated such as the use of popular culture, dramatisation, story books, puppets and small whiteboards
- Various techniques for determining children's prior knowledge are utilised including card sorts, concept cartoons, compare and contrast, and drawings
- Technology is utilised through the use if iPad apps, phone magnifiers, and YouTube clips
- Questioning in its various forms is demonstrated, in particular the importance of allowing students to find ways to answer their own questions
- The natural environment is used as a context and a resource for teaching and learning science
- Individual, small group and whole class activities are used as appropriate

ACTIVE INVOLVEMENT AND PARTICIPATION IN THE UNIT 
In this Masters level unit we recognise that you bring a wealth of knowledge, experience and expertise and it is important to build on this rich resource in order to collaboratively develop and refine knowledge, understandings and skills around science education. Group activities and discussion are important components of the science workshops, and we are confident that all members will feel able to contribute.
Engaging in reading the literature will also be important in developing and refining knowledge and skills. Specific readings have been set for most weeks. It is important that you are familiar with the readings so that you can take part in group discussions and workshop activities. We encourage you to find alternative readings, particularly from recent publications, so that you can access the most current literature on research and practice of the teaching, learning and assessment in early childhood and primary schooling.
Attendance at all classes is strongly encouraged. If you are unable to make class, you should let the unit coordinator know.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assignment 1 - Integrated Science program
You are required to develop an 8 week science teaching program that provides strong links with literacy and/or numeracy. This program must also include literacy and/or sequential (in content) numeracy lessons that integrate with the science program. The program you develop must be professionally presented and self-contained, containing appendices with all resources/worksheets and assessment items. Please see LMS for a full description of this assignment, which is summarised below.
There are four parts to this assignment: 
Part A: feedback on your 5E model
Part B: program summary and APST reflection 
Part C: overview and program developed using provided A3 templates 
Part D: appendices for resources and assessment
Develop a series of at least seven (EC) or eight (P) sequential science lessons based on the 5E Model (Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate and Evaluate) using backward planning. For P students this program must also include one literacy lesson and two sequential (in content) numeracy lessons that integrate with the science program. For EC students, this program must include four sequential (in content) numeracy lessons that integrate with the science program. 
Include the following components for each lesson:
•	Selected learning outcomes (from the WA Curriculum for Science, English, and Mathematics (Code only) or equivalent from the WA Kindergarten Curriculum Guideline)
•	Teaching and learning activities, pedagogy and group structure (whole class, small groups or individual). Demonstrate how you will incorporate a range of ICT in your teaching. 
•	Resources 
•	Assessment
Assignment 2 - Case study
Making decisions about what and how to teach in a sequence of lessons on a particular science topic includes generating information from various places including the curriculum and resource documents. It is critical for teachers of science to consider the children that they will be teaching, in particular their conceptual understandings about the topic that is going to be the focus of the teaching and learning sequence. Research has suggested that young students have many beginning ideas and alternative conceptions about science concepts. Thus, when teachers are planning a program they must take these into consideration. Please see LMS for a full description of this assignment, which is summarised below.
Select one of the case studies presented on LMS under Assignment 1/Case studies which present children's views on particular science topics.
Provide a referenced rationale as to why it is necessary to obtain children’s prior knowledge in science. This should introduce and discuss alternatives conceptions in learning.
Research and report on the science behind the chosen science topic to at least a depth that would be required to teach the concept to a class of bright Year 7 students. This is to ensure that you have gained a sufficient understanding of the content.
Review the literature to find out children’s alternative conceptions about your science topic, and report these findings.
Summarise the alternative conceptions and scientific concepts that the case study child held and compare with the published literature.
Select the most significant alternative conception found with the child. Describe two activities that could be used to challenge this alternative conception. What theory informs these activities in challenging children’s alternative conceptions?

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
15. Students must pass all components of a Master of teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if -
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit.
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the results and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a 'fail' by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the asessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator. 
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
