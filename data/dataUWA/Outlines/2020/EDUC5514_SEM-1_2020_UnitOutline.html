Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit will include a variety of teaching and learning strategies which promote student learning and engagement. Throughout the unit, students will be encouraged to become reflective practitioners through the use of collaborative technologies, small group discussion, small group presentations, analysis of case studies, analysis of student work samples, and discussion of relevant literature and current educational issues.

Assessments
*Please see LMS for more details about the assessments
Assessment 1: Literature review
The purpose of this assessment is to provide students with the opportunity to write a short literature review for a chosen specific area of focus within inclusive education.  The literature review should include a summary of the relevant literature as well as elements of critique, identification of gaps and implications for future research and practice. The review must include: (a) a brief history of inclusive education, (b) identification of relevant legislation in Australia and relevance to teacher professional responsibilities, (c) relevant international treaties and agreements, (d) identified strategies to support inclusive student participation and (e) a specific focus within inclusive education chosen by the student.  
*A minimum of eight references must be included, including at least five peer reviewed journal articles.
*3000 word count    
Assessment 2: Planning document and eportfolio entry
The purpose of this assessment is to provide students with the opportunity to demonstrate their knowledge and understanding of differentiation and inclusive practices in a planning document which includes plans for a sequence of three lesson in their major or minor curriculum area. Differentiation and inclusive practices must be clearly identified as well as links between the information in the class profile, the activities/tasks and the assessment task.  There are two parts to this assessment: (1) developing three sequential lesson plans which include differentiation to cater for the diversity in the class profile, and (2) developing a summative assessment task and rubric.
Eportfolio entry (Pass/Fail).  It is a requirement of this unit that students provide evidence for APST 1.5 in their eportfolio. The eportfolio entry will include selected parts from the planning document assignment as well as brief annotation and reflection. 
Assessment 3: Test
Students complete an online test which assesses knowledge and understanding of assessment and legislative requirements relevant to teaching students with disability.
The purpose of this assessment is to provide students with the opportunity to demonstrate their knowledge and understanding of: (1) legislative requirements and teaching strategies which support students with disability, (2) assessment practices, including interpreting student data, (3) knowledge of strategies to support inclusive student participation, and (4) teaching strategies that are responsive to diverse learning needs through differentiation.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
