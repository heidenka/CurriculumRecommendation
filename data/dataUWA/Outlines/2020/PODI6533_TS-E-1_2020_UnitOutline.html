Unit Details

PODI6533 - Internal Clinical Podiatry Practicum III - TS-E-1-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

The clinical podiatry practicum units train students in a structured manner in the assessment and treatment of the high-risk,
and/or in elective, foot surgical pathologies. Specific instruction in this unit focuses on the further attainment of surgical
skills and knowledge of forefoot surgical procedures.
Outcomes Students are able to (1) demonstrate knowledge of hallux valgus/limitus and lesser metatarsal surgical procedures; (2)
demonstrate an increasing level of surgical knowledge and competence in forefoot surgical procedures; (3) demonstrate
increasing competence in pre- and post-operative assessment and management of forefoot surgical procedures; (4)
demonstrate effective patient and health practitioner communication; (5) demonstrate safe operating room protocol; (6)
demonstrate ability to maintain accurate patient record; (7) display professional behaviour in the educational and clinical
settings; and (8) demonstrate competency in patient education and teaching of the clinical and surgical knowledge to peers.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is a practical unit so all teaching is in a face-to-face format.
Students are expected to learn in a blended learning environment whereby there will be access to online content, pre-reading and face to face discussions/tutorials.
Certain tutorials will be taught in a flipped teaching approach whereby students are expected to self-direct study and discuss findings in a tutorial.
Clinical skills will be examined via DOCS and DOPS throughout the semester and a written paper whereby theory will be assessed at the end of the semester. 

Unit Structure

There are compulsory clinics on Wednesday and Friday mornings (8AM to 1PM , which may finish earlier depending on clinic schedule). 
Wednesday afternoons are designated for workshops/tutorials which will be confirmed closer to date, these dates depend on the availability of industry experts and equipment. Dates will be announced via LMS with ample notice. Students are expected to attend these workshops/tutorials. 
See assessment breakdown on LMS.

Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
