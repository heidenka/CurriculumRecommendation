Academic Information

Content Students develop an ability to read, understand, analyse and apply the law of contract through the study of decided cases and contractual terms. As most of the applicable law studied in Contract is common law case law this material is the primary focus of the unit. Students are also introduced to legislation that impacts on contract law in Australia. The unit begins with an examination of the rules relating to the formation of contracts and concepts of offer and acceptance, consideration, intention to create legal relations, privity and contract formalities. Students then study incorporation and construction of contractual terms and implication of terms. They continue mapping the life of a contract by investigating how contracts come to an end through agreement, performance, breach and repudiation, and frustration of contract. Although contract remedies are studied in a dedicated Remedies unit, students are introduced to restitutionary claims for unjust enrichment. They study the provisions in national consumer legislation (the Australian Consumer Law) that impact on the law of contract, in particular the law of misleading or deceptive conduct. Students analyse case law, extract legal principles from judicial decisions and clearly state them, and analyse and solve hypothetical legal problems relating to the topics covered.

Unit Information

Contracts are fundamental to commerce, our economy and transactions in our everyday lives. Contract law determines which of the myriad of agreements we make each day are enforceable through judicial remedies. In this unit we study the body of core principles of Contract law which have general application to all contracts. There are also principles that apply to the myriad of specialised contracts which you can study in later units which apply, for example, to consumers, employment and insurance. Contract law in Australia is mostly found in the decisions of courts - case law - and therefore this unit is an excellent introduction to the common law method. We also study some instances of where legislation has been introduced to clarify or reform the common law. In many respects the law of Contract is well settled and there is doctrinal certainty about this branch of the law of private obligations. As you will discover in this unit, even settled doctrine can be based on competing commercial and pragmatic considerations and therefore be the subject of debate, divided opinion and reform. 
In this unit you will develop an ability to read, understand and apply the law of contract through the study of decided cases and contractual terms. Although this unit also studies legislation that impacts on contract law in Australia, most of the law is found in common law cases so this is the primary material it focuses on. The unit begins with an examination of the rules relating to the formation of contracts. This includes concepts of offer and acceptance, sufficiency of consideration, intention to create legal relations, capacity, privity, and contract formalities. The unit then examines express and implied terms and construction of express contractual terms. We continue mapping the life of a contract by investigating how contracts come to an end through agreement, performance, breach and repudiation, and frustration of contract. 
To understand Contract law is to understand the ways in which the law supports private ordering between contracting parties while at the same time maintaining the law’s interest to protect individuals on equitable principles and the public interest on other policy grounds. To understand the role of contract law within the law of obligations it is essential to understand what remedies are available for breach of contract and contrast these to the remedies available for a number of non-contractual common law and statutory claims that can arise in a contractual context. In this unit we will consider non-contractual claims for quantum meruit, money had and received and the statutory action for misleading or deceptive conduct. We will not study remedies for breach of contract in any depth: you will study these in the second level JD unit Remedies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The lectures provide an overview of the topics we cover in this unit and detailed examination of selected cases and contract law issues. The material covered in the tutorials will follow on from the lecture topics each week. The tutorials allow for an in-depth analysis of aspects of the law of Contract that will not necessarily be covered in detail in the lectures. The tutorials will be discussion based and provide an opportunity to workshop hypothetical fact problems and policy issues in contract law. The assessment of class participation will develop your ability to participate in discussions of legal materials and issues relating to Contract law both as a speaker and as a listener. The tutorial activities will develop your legal problem solving and legal writing skills, which will be further developed by completing the Contract assignment. The assignment and the final examination will develop your ability to analyse case law and to write a reasoned and concise legal opinion on a hypothetical fact problem. The final examination is open book so you can refer to your study notes. It is strongly recommended that you prepare for the examination as you would for a closed book examination in the knowledge that you do not have to rely on your memory for the names of cases and statutes. The questions on the examination do not call for transcription from your prepared notes onto your examination script or for quotations from primary and secondary sources.

Participation

We believe that the tutorial program is an important vehicle through which you can consolidate your learning about Contract law. For this reason your participation in the tutorial discussions is assessed. Ten percent (10%) of your assessment in Contract is based on your participation in the tutorial discussions.Your tutorial participation mark is calculated by your tutor based on your participation in the tutorial discussions throughout the semester. The objectives of the participation mark are to promote:
•	engagement, 
•	preparation, 
•	understanding,
•	reflection; and 
•	responsible tutorial membership.
As part of your enrolment in the unit you have been allocated a tutorial group in CAS which will remain the same all semester. To enable your tutor to keep an accurate record of your attendance and of your individual tutorial participation over the semester it is important that you attend the tutorial group that you have been allocated. For this reason students will NOT be credited for participation in a tutorial group to which they were not allocated or did not obtain permission from the unit coordinator to attend.
For important additional information about Contract tutorials including attendance and assessment of tutorial participation see the detailed Information about Tutorial Participation in the Assessments and Submissions Area in LMS.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
