Academic Information

Content This unit develops students' understanding of how to effectively integrate Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) into a range of learning areas in secondary school. While focus surrounds the ICT General Capabilities, students are also exposed to elements of the Digital Technologies curriculum. Students are required to design learning activities, lesson plans and programs that focus on introductory and core ICT skills and, in so doing, acquire or improve their own practical proficiency and general knowledge.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The following teaching and learning strategies may be used in this unit: lecture, presentation, small and large group discussion as well as strategies such as self-discovery learning, video tutorials, text & image based tutorials and present and practice to help students develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the weekly lecture and required activities.
The following resources may be used in this unit: video tutorials, pdf tutorials, peer-reviewed articles, ICT and lecturer/tutor created resources.

ASSESSMENT DETAILS
ASSIGNMENT ONE – LESSON PLANNING (PLAN- 20% AND PRESENTATION 20%)
APST Standard 1 - Know students and how they learn - APST Standard 2 – Know the content and how to teach it - APST Standard 3 - Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning
In this assignment, students will create three lesson plans based around a topic presented in class, which focuses on an aspect of the Digital Technologies (DT) Curriculum for Years 7 or 8. These topics will be distributed in week one. For those students that are completing the course as an area of interest may also use the context of their major learning area to assist in teaching the DT skill. The lessons should have at least one digital resource for teaching and use at least one Information and Communication Technology (ICT) tool for the students, to enhance the learning. These lessons should integrate ICT appropriately and it is used as a tool for learning; encourages ICT to be used to develop 21st century thinking skills; address at least one of the DT content descriptions; and engages students in an interesting and exciting way.
Students are then to choose a section of one of the lessons to present to their peers. The presentation is geared to help your fellow students extend their understanding of the workshop content. It is also an opportunity to share your thinking and to practice some teaching skills and try something innovative. The time allocated in the workshop for each presentation is 10 mins and you are to teach as if the tutorial group are the students of that age group. You will also have a chance to explain to the tutorial group the context of the lesson at the beginning of your presentation. You will be assessed on your depth of understanding of the content as well as your presentation skills. 
Please use the Lesson Plan Template to create your 3 lessons. The lesson plans will be submitted through Blackboard. You may also wish to use your website as an online platform to organise the materials you create and for your presentation. 
Please see LMS for more information, including the rubric.

ASSIGNMENT TWO – PROGRAM  (30%)
APST Standard 1 - Know students and how they learn - APST Standard 2 – Know the content and how to teach it - APST Standard 3 - Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning
In this assignment, students will create a series of 6 lessons guiding Year 7 or 8 students through a project or investigation for Digital Technologies. For area of interest students, you may draw on context from your learning areas. This inquiry task must focus on using elements of the Design Thinking Process (see Week 3 notes) which are highlighted by the "process and production skills" section of the Digital Technologies Syllabus. In your task you need to include digital resources for teaching (minimum of 3) that you create for the lessons as well using ICT tools (minimum of 3) with the students. Use the forward planning document to explain your lessons (more information below). When completed upload the forward planning document and materials you create to the "Resources" section of your ICT portfolio. You will also need to submit your forward planning document through Blackboard.
Please Note: 
Your program replaces lesson plans, you will use this in conjunction with a daily work pad (diary), therefore, it needs to be quite specific in terms of the lesson activities that you will include. There will always be an introductory activity, the lesson body activities and a closing activity. Decide how you will assess the students throughout and at the end of the inquiry they conduct.
 
Program Components
•    Overview - An introduction to the series of lessons.  Consider prior learning, what you want students will learn, how they will demonstrate the learning, how they will achieve the learning, and evidence that students have achieved the learning.
•    Weeks - In this column include the week and the lesson label for example Week 1, Lesson 1. Generally, you will have two lessons per week, so in this program you will plan for two lessons for three weeks.
•    Outcome Focus - ( Digital Technologies -  ICT Capabilities)
•    Learning & Teaching Experience- List all the teaching activities that you would teach the students, make sure there is an introductory and closing activity as well as the body activities.
•    Resources - Include text book chapters and create hyperlinks to handouts and online resources, as well as your own resources.
•    Assessment - Outline the assessments that you would do with the students. These may be informal (observation/anecdotal notes/ class quiz) or formal (assessment item that will be marked).
Please see LMS for more information, including the rubric.

ASSIGNMENT THREE – ICT PORTFOLIO (30%)
APST Standard 1 - Know students and how they learn - APST Standard 2 – Know the content and how to teach it - APST Standard 3 - Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning
In this assignment, students will practice their ICT skills and create their portfolio online. The portfolio may also have pages allocated to resources for their lesson plan and program. This assignment will consist of two parts, reflections and an ICT tools.
The reflections section consists of three 500 word reflections. The first reflection is based on the current CoSN Driving K-12 Innovation Hurdles document, where students choose one trend and critically reflect on a school’s ability to move in that direction. The other two reflections are to be based on the following topics discussed in the workshops
• Using ICT for consumption vs creating.
• Using TPACK, SAMR and TIMS  to assist in designing lessons and materials that incorporate ICT.
• Computational Thinking in our classrooms. Is it every teacher's responsibility?
• Big and small data in our schools, where should we place importance?
• What area of Digital Citizenship do you think should be a current focus for schools and why?
Readings provided in the workshops should give students a starting point to write these reflections. It is expected that each reference contain at least 3 references (APA) and contain in text referencing where appropriate. 
The ICT tools section consists of descriptions of at least 5 different tools and they could be used them in a learning area. Students may choose any of the ICT tools used in class or use your own. Each ICT tool should have an in depth description including advantages, disadvantages, examples, use in the classroom and application of appropriate ICT integration models. Examples should demonstrate the student’s competence in using ICT and meaningful integration into a classroom setting. 
The Reflections section is worth 15% and the ICT tools section is worth 15%. Please see LMS for more information, including the rubric.
 
RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
15. Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
