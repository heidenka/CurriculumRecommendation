Academic Information

Content This unit examines the concept of global change and its drivers, and focuses on how global change affects the ocean ecosystem. Students learn about ocean warming, acidification, eutrophication, increased UVB radiation, habitat modification and the emission of pollutants, as well as the complex interactions between them. The impacts on the ocean ecosystem are examined both globally and regionally, with particular attention to Australia, as well as other sensitive regions. The unit also evaluates different strategies to mitigate the impacts of global change on ocean ecosystems. Students are given the opportunity to conduct a research project in small groups, as well as to participate in debates and colloquiums that allow a better grasp on the uncertainties at forecasting the trajectories of marine ecosystems under global change.

Unit Information

SCIE5505 is a level 5 unit which introduces the concepts and drivers of global change and their impacts on the marine environment. This unit is worth 6 credit points.
This unit examines the concept of global change and its drivers, and focuses on how global change affects the ocean ecosystem. In this unit students will learn about ocean warming, acidification, eutrophication, overfishing and other global change processes, as well as their complex interactions. The impacts on the ocean ecosystem are examined both globally and regionally. The unit also discusses different strategies to mitigate the impacts of global change on ocean ecosystems. Students are given the opportunity to lead and participate in debates and discussions that will allow a better grasp of the trajectories of marine ecosystems under global change.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

 
Scientists that are experts in their individual fields will give lectures on various topics. Each topic will therefore include recent research findings as well as give the necessary background to understand the different drivers involved. A discussion will be held after each lecture that will be organised and run by students in the class. Running a discussion will lead students to develop critical reading and review and scientific communication skills. Lectures, discussions and selected research papers from the scientific literature will provide a platform for lecturer and peer facilitated group activities.
On completion of this unit, students will be able to:
-Understand the concept of global change and differentiate this process from previous processes of change in the Earth system
-Identify different drivers of global change and understand their complex interactions
-Compare the relative impact of global change in different marine habitats and regions and discuss the traits that make them more or less vulnerable
-Understand the type of actions needed to mitigate the impacts of global change in the marine environment 

Unit Structure

This unit is taught in 2 blocks of 3 weeks in semester 1. Each week of teaching will have two 3-hour sessions. The unit is based around lectures, discussions and research projects. There are no labs or field trips.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
