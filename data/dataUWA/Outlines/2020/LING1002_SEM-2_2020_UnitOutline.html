Unit Details

LING1002 - Language as a Cognitive System - SEM-2-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

This unit considers language from a cognitive perspective: how language is represented in the human brain, how it is realised by the body in speech and sign, and how language disorders can change all this. We also explore how human language evolved and continues to change, how linguistic ability develops in the human child, and how languages can be represented through writing systems. The unit also introduces basic concepts in the formal modelling of linguistic systems.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit uses a mix of explicit instruction (in lectures), face-to-face student participation (in tutorials), and a flipped classroom model (in which students complete activities outside of class, and then discuss the results in the tutorial).

Unit Structure

This unit comprises lectures and tutorials:
•	There are 2 hours of lectures each week for the full 12 weeks of the semester. The lectures will be recorded and will be available every Monday and Wednesday by 1:00 pm via the Learning Management System (LMS, aka Blackboard), together with the lecture slides.
•	There are 10 tutorial weeks, indicated in the unit schedule. There are no tutorials in Week 1 and Week 10 (the week just after mid-semester break).
NB: The lecture schedule is subject to change depending on student needs.

Participation

•	There are 10 tutorials for this unit. You are required to complete and submit a small assignment (called a ‘tutorial preparation’ or ‘tute prep’) BEFORE the start of your tutorial each week. 
•	You will receive a mark for attending a tutorial and another mark for turning in your tute prep on time. 
•	Your tute prep MUST be submitted by five minutes after the start time of your tutorial (at the latest!) in order to receive the mark. For example, if your tutorial starts at 2:00 pm on a Monday, you will not receive credit for your tute prep if you do not submit it online by Monday at 2:05 pm.
•	Tute prep instructions:
1.) Download each week’s tute prep from LMS (‘Tutorial Stuff’). Preps for the next week’s tutes will be available on LMS after the Wednesday lecture each week. 
2.) Do the exercise, then submit a copy of your results using the designated Turnitin link for that prep (also under ‘Tutorial Stuff’). Any format that is readable on a PC is fine — PDF, DOCx, TXT, or even a photo from your phone. (‘Pages’ files are not readable on PCs. Please do not submit in this format.)
3.) Bring a copy of your completed tute prep to your tutorial so we can all discuss everyone’s answers. It can be on paper or on screen.
•	By the time of your tute, you are also expected to be up-to-date with the previous week’s lectures. In group activities in tutes, other students will be relying on your ability to make informed contributions.

Referencing Requirements

In this course, use Chicago 17th edition author-date referencing style (https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/Chicago_referencing/author-date#Books). 
If you use exact words from any source (the textbook, a website, a TV show, a podcast, etc.), you must put these words in quotation marks and acknowledge the source via a Chicago style reference (see link above). If you paraphrase an idea from another source, you must still acknowledge that you are paraphrasing, and state where you got your idea from (using the Chicago style referencing system again).
One exception: for this course, if you paraphrase an idea from the Fromkin textbook, you do not need to cite it. If you use an exact quote from this textbook (or from any other source), however, you MUST put this in quotation marks and cite it.

Texts

There is an assigned reading most weeks (see unit schedule). These readings will be very important to your understanding of the course material (as well as to your performance on the assignments and exam).
The required textbook for this course is An Introduction to Language by Victoria Fromkin et al. Either the 8th or 9th edition will work great (and in fact, earlier editions will probably be fine too). Just make sure you get the Australia/NZ version, NOT the American version! If you don’t want to buy your own copy, you can find this book at Reid Library. I have requested for it to be put in the High Demand area and for the ebook to be purchased as well. 
Here’s the citation:
Fromkin, Victoria, Robert Rodman, Nina Hyams, Felicity Cox, Rosalind Thornton, and Mengistu Amberber. 2015. An Introduction to Language. 8th edition. South Melbourne, Vic: Cengage Learning.
There will be a few readings from other sources throughout the semester. These will be available as PDFs via ‘Unit Readings’ in LMS.

Other

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
