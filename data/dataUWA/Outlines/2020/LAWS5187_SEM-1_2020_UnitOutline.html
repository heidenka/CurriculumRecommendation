Academic Information

Content This unit examines the law relating to wills, estates, intestacy and family provision. The unit includes analysis of the definition and nature of a will, formal requirements of a will, construction of wills, vitiating factors affecting testators and beneficiaries, revocation, republication and revival of wills, legacies and devises, intestate succession, administration of estates of deceased persons and family provision after death.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is taught by means of a weekly two hour lecture and weekly 45 minute tutorials.
 Students are expected to participate actively in class and prepare for classes by undertaking the required reading in advance and after class. Readings for the lectures and tutorials will be posted to LMS. 

The classes are interactive. Practical, professional questions for budding solicitors will be asked. Student initiative and self-motivation is encouraged and rewarded. Staff have high expectations of students and emphasise the importance of attending lectures and engaging in discussion.

Unit Structure

One lecture and one tutorial per week. Tutorials commence in week 2 of the semester.
While lectures are recorded, students are encouraged to attend lectures to enhance their learning experience and outcomes.
The lecturers are legal practitioners who are interested in engaging with and passing on their knowledge to students.

Participation

Participation at tutorials is marked out of 10%.
The objectives of the participation mark are to promote:
• engagement,
• preparation,
• understanding,
• reflection,
• responsible tutorial membership; and
• quality of contribution to the tutorial.
Engagement will most obviously be in speaking but a student might also be engaged whilst listening. This will be important to note for students who are by nature quieter. They may be engaged, though speak less often.
Preparation might be evident in notes the student has come with or in the quality of answers and comments in discussion.
Understanding of the issues raised in tutorials will most commonly be demonstrated though a student’s overall contribution to the tutorial discussion, including small group discussions within a tutorial, whether by way of answering questions, asking questions, making observations, or listening and responding to other students.
Reflection on the issues (and preparation) might be evident in the student drawing on material outside the material directly related to the tutorial problem, perhaps from other topics in the course and in questions asked during (or even after) the tutorial seminar.
Responsible tutorial membership refers to the respect given to other student’s comments and refraining from attempting to dominate the discussion.
Quality refers to participation being about the quality not necessarily the quantity of contributions (where ‘quality’ means considered and reflective contributions – you are allowed to make mistakes!). The aim of including this component is to encourage unselfconscious discussion amongst students, not an atmosphere of assessment.
In order to encourage and facilitate student engagement and participation, the use of electronic devices is generally not permitted in tutorials in this unit. Students requiring permission to use can help you my darling go to sleep electronic device in tutorials on equity grounds should see the unit coordinator.
The Assessment Criteria for Tutorial Participation:
HD (80% or above) - Student is well prepared for every tutorial; demonstrates good understanding of and insightful and/or original reflection on the issues raised in tutorials throughout the semester; critically engages with the subject matter of each tutorial; and consistently demonstrates responsible tutorial membership.
D (70% - 79%) - Student is well prepared for most tutorials; demonstrates good understanding and reflection on the issues raised in tutorials throughout most of the semester; is engaged with the subject matter of most tutorials; and consistently demonstrates responsible tutorial membership
C (60% - 69%) - Student is well prepared for most tutorials; demonstrates developing understanding of and sound reflection on the issues raised in tutorials throughout most of the semester; is engaged with the subject matter of most tutorials; and demonstrates responsible tutorial membership throughout most of the semester
P (50% - 59%) - Student is not well prepared for tutorials throughout most of the semester; demonstrates little understanding of reflection on the issues raised by the tutorials; is not engaged with the subject matter of most tutorials; and shows little responsible tutorial membership
F (below 50%) - Student is not well prepared for any tutorials; demonstrates little or no understanding of and reflection on the issues raised by the tutorials; is not engaged with the subject matter of any tutorials; and does not demonstrate responsible tutorial membership.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
