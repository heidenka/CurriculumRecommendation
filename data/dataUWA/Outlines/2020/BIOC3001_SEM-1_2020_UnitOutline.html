Academic Information

Content The objective of this unit is to provide students with a broad knowledge and understanding of the concepts and methods used in molecular biology. The unit extends many of the topics introduced in SCIE1106 Molecular Biology of the Cell including DNA, RNA and protein synthesis; gene and genome structure and function, the regulation of gene expression, quality control mechanisms; bioinformatics; recombinant DNA technology and genetic engineering. The practical work familiarises the students with techniques basic to molecular biology.
Unit Learning Outcomes
# Outcome How outcome will be assessed
1 gain an understanding of, and recall and integrate, key knowledge and concepts about the molecular biology of cells and organisms including the molecular biology of gene expression on the transcriptional, post-transcriptional and post-translational level, and the molecular structure and function of genomestwo theory exams (mid-semester and end of semester examination), group assignment
2 gain practical skills in the planning, preparation and execution of gene expression and gene regulation analysis on the molecular level using real time qPCRlaboratory notebook, lab performance, lab assignment and assessment of lab theory content during the end of semester examination.
3 develop generic skills such as data analysis and interpretation and reporting of results in written, oral and other communication formslaboratory notebook, lab assignment and a group assignment, assessment of lab theory content during the end of semester examination.
4 develop interpersonal and teamwork skillsgroup assignment

Unit Details

BIOC3001 - Molecular Biology - SEM-1-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

The objective of this unit is to provide students with a broad knowledge and understanding of the concepts and methods used in molecular biology. The unit extends many of the topics introduced in SCIE1106 Molecular Biology of the Cell including DNA, RNA and protein synthesis; gene and genome structure and function, the regulation of gene expression, quality control mechanisms; bioinformatics; recombinant DNA technology and genetic engineering. The practical work familiarises the students with techniques basic to molecular biology. Student will develop team working skills, research skills and video presentation skills in a group assignment. Students will further learn how to critically evaluate their own work and the work of their peers by participating in peer marking of the group assignment.
Formative questions and feedback will be provided during the first half of the teaching period. This includes a small set of formative question in the style of the mid-semester test and the final exam.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit offers classical face-to-face  lectures as well as laboratories/practicals, interactive tutorials in preparation for the laboratories and workshops during which students view and evaluate science videos prepared by their peers. . 

Unit Structure

Twenty lectures (45 minutes each)
Five laboratories/practical (approx. 7 hours each). In addition to laboratory work, students also participate in a data evaluation session during the final practical
Seven tutorials (45 minutes each)
Five workshops (students attend three after allocation via LMS) (2 hours each). 
One mid-semester test (40 minutes)

Participation

PUNCTUAL attendance at all tutorials, practicals, workshops and the mid-semester test is compulsory and attendance will be recorded. If a session is missed or attended more than 5 minutes late without approved special consideration from the Faculty of Science Student Office, the mark allocated to the missed activity is forfeited.
If a student has a continuing condition or situation that may impact her / his ability to carry out one or more of the labs in this unit, we advise him / her to contact the unit coordinator at the beginning of the semester to discuss what accommodations are possible. There is absolutely no expectation the details of the condition/situation will be discussed, only the potential to accommodate missed work/experience. A student would still need to apply for special consideration if a practical session is missed, but the conversation with the unit coordinator would set out what would be required from the student to complete the unit.
Suitable attire (laboratory coat, CLOSED shoes) must be worn to all lab session and long hair must be tied back. Students are also required to keep accurate lab notes during all lab sessions and these will be assessed. Students need to purchase a notebook for the laboratory notes. 
Please always act in a sensible fashion when undertaking any experimental procedure. Handle all chemicals and samples with care, and wear gloves and safety specs when necessary. Ask a demonstrator if there is uncertainty about the safety of a reagent and where gloves should be worn.
It is advisable to have a calculator at all practical sessions, tests and exams. Please note that all calculators used in assessments at UWA MUST be authorised and labelled with an approval sticker, which can be obtained from the Science Students Office. If a calculator does not have an approval sticker, it cannot be used for any assessment in BIOC3001.

Referencing Requirements

Students are expected to reference any material used for the group assignment and for the lab assignment. References should be provided using the Vancouver citation style (https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/vancouver).

Texts

No single textbook can cover all the topics of this unit. A number of textbooks will be used by the lecturers and students are advised to consult with the lecturer. Students are expected to read and work through the material provided on LMS,  Additional reading material will be referenced in lectures. Please also consult the resources available via the 'Unit Reading' link on LMS.

Other

Students are expected to know how to research the literature. Students need access to software that allows the creation of a short video. Students can prepare videos using only power point but may also like to use their own video equipment (camera, phone, tablet, iPad), video editing software (freeware available on the web) or more sophisticated programs such as those provided by Apple or Adobe. Suggestions will be made during the first tutorial.

Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
Academic conduct
Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.
Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
