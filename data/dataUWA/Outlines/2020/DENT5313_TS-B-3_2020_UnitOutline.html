Academic Information

Content This unit builds on the knowledge and clinical skills including relevant concepts in the disciplines of Orthodontics and Paediatric Dentistry. Students gain understanding on abnormal growth and development and common health problems from birth to adolescence for both healthy children and children with special needs and evidence-based orthodontics treatment for uncomplicated cases. Students formulate and deliver a treatment plan for the individual child in Paediatric Dentistry clinics.

Unit Information

FACE TO FACE and BLENDED LEARNING
This unit equips students with knowledge relating to clinical practice in the following domains: restoration of permanent and deciduous teeth, management of pulp and periapical diseases in the deciduous and mixed dentition; biomechanics of tooth movement; and orthodontic removable appliances.
This unit will equip the students with knowledge and understanding of malocclusion, simple orthodontic diagnosis and management and clinical competencies in diagnosing plaque-induced and developmental dental and skeletal conditions in children and adolescents.
Orthodontic Module:
This unit further exposes students to malocclusion and related topics. Like Growth and Development (Level I and II), this course is self-instructional, but has Lectures on specific topics related to orthodontic treatment.  You will have personal contact with faculty in seminars and the rostered clinics to discuss material you have learned from computer teaching programs and reading assignments.The self-instructional materials can be viewed at any time during the unit. The orthodontics course is in four didactic units in which the self-instructional material is concentrated and spans over the first few months. Superimposed in this time are the Clinical rosters. Level IV introduces students to more complex and Advanced Orthodontics.
Paedatric Dentistry Module:
This module introduces the students to the dental management of child and adolescent patients. It intends to provide the student with the theoretical, practical clinical knowledge on the normal growth and development of the dentition (primary, mixed and permanent), common dental conditions and /or anomalies that occur in a children and adolescents. Furthermore, the student’s will have the opportunity to carry out the clinical techniques commonly used in paediatric dentistry to prevent and/or manage these dental conditions in a clinical environment.

 FACE TO FACE and BLENDED LEARNING
Lectures, Practical Tutorials or Seminars and Clinical Rosters
The teaching and learning approach is a blended model with face to face lectures (on-line 2020), Clinical instruction and training, observational learning and training and an online teaching and learning component. 
The students are expected to review and master all reading material from the textbook, lecture material, Practical Tutorials and Seminars and perform Clinical tasks to a satisfactory level and demonstrate clinical competencies.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
