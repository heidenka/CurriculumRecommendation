ENVT3338 - Land Capability Assessment - SEM-1-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

Understanding how soils and landscapes have evolved provides the key to managing terrestrial environments effectively and making sound decisions for sustainable land use. 
This unit covers the processes contributing to soil formation and degradation such as clay eluviation, precipitation, dissolution reactions and water flow. These processes are presented within a geomorphic and hydrological context. Based on this understanding soil classification is introduced, followed by the definition and mapping of soil and land capability classes.
The broad aim of this unit is to describe how soils influence the behaviour and utilisation of ecosystems and, conversely, to describe the effects that ecosystems and various components of the physical environment (geology, climate, topography) have on the development and properties of soils. You will develop the ability to integrate soil information from field data, remote sensing and geographic information systems in order to derive land capability and land suitability assessments. Selected soils and regolith will be classified, mapped, sampled, analysed and interpreted both for land assessment and to identify and explain the factors contributing to soil evolution. The unit will also integrate soil science knowledge to explain and evaluate soil degradation and management issues such as fertilisers and soil amendments, tillage, organic matter and crop residue management, acidification and liming, salinity and sodicity, waterlogging, erosion, and the functioning of natural and restored ecosystems.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit uses a hands-on approach with lots of practical work in the field and the laboratory. 
There are weekly lectures which provide information to deepen knowledge necessary for understanding laboratory work and overall context. The unit further integrates guest lectures from experienced people. 
You will be working as a team in the field and the laboratory and get partly assessed as a group (30%) but you have to also write your individual assessment reports. 

Unit Structure

The unit will perform a land capability assessment of an area at the UWA farm
The unit is composed of weekly laboratory classes (3 hours) and lectures (2hours). The first 3 weeks will be used to train you for the upcoming field trip (either on the 14th or the 15th of March depending on your group allocation) by introducing you to important field measurement techniques, soil description methods, and ‘how to develop a soil mapping overview map’ based on certain criteria.
The field trip is compulsory and will provide you with important information, which you will need later during the semester. 
Subsequent weeks will use collected samples from the field work in order to conduct necessary chemical and physical analysis. Towards the end of the semester you will be working as a group to perform a laboratory report (=group folder and group assessment) necessary for the land capability assessment. The final 2-3 weeks will be used to collaboratively work on the land capability assessment following official governmental guidelines.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
