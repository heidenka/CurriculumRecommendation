Academic Information

Content This unit focuses upon the development of Western art music in the twentieth century and the beginning of the twenty-first century. It covers the modernist and postmodernist phases of development including such styles as impressionist, expressionist, neo-classical/neo-tonal, twelve-note, serial, indeterminate, minimalist, polystylist, neo-romantic and the new forms of complexity in the last twenty years or so. The works of Debussy, Stravinsky, Bartók, Schoenberg, Berg, Webern, Messiaen, Boulez, Stockhausen, Cage, Penderecki, Reich, Glass, Pärt, Ferneyhough and others form the repertoire to be studied in terms of these trends and characteristics. The unit aims to provide sound historical, technical and cultural perspectives of the repertoire and of the period studied, including its relationship with contemporary trends in the other arts. This is a compulsory unit in the Music Studies major in the Bachelor of Arts degree. It is also open to students enrolled in other majors and degree pathways who fulfil the prerequisites and who have an interest in music.

ASSESSMENTS DESCRIPTION
SHORT ESSAY TOPIC
The period from approximately 1890-1918 saw the rise of modernism and was principally concerned with the overthrow of Classical tonality. Do you agree? In your response, refer to the most important historical, aesthetic, stylistic and technical developments in Western art music during the period, with reference to some representative works.
Length: 1000 words
MAIN ESSAY TOPIC
Critically compare and contrast the concepts of 'modernism and 'postmodernism' as reflected in both writing about the arts and in Western art music since about 1900. (Useful initial discussions and bibliographies relating to both these terms may be found in the second edition of The New Grove Dictionary of Music and Musicians.) Do you discern a significant shift in the fundamental approaches by composers to their art in the so-called 'postmodern' period after about 1970? To illustrate your discussion, compare in some detail a group of three or four works (NOT in NAWM) that together you consider to display a representative range of styles and techniques of musical composition of the last 100 years or so. In light of your analysis of the concepts of modernism and postmodernism, to what extent do these terms apply to the works you have selected?
LENGTH: 3000 WORDS
All essays should be written on a computer/word processor and should be double spaced with a wide margin for comments. Please use a readable font and at least 12-point size.
You should number the pages. Source materials and quotations must be acknowledged on each page as footnotes.and, for the main essay only, a bibliography provided at the end of the essay. 
For details of referencing, see above ('Referencing Requirements')
Please attach a cover-sheet and show your word-count. Essays that exceed the respective word limits by more than 100 words will be penalised

LISTENING TEST
The Listening Test at the end of the semester is based exclusively on repertoire studied during the course (see list above). For this test it is essential not only to be able to identify the music extracts played in the test, but to comment upon the most important technical, stylistic and historical features exhibited by them. It is therefore vital not only to become familiar with this music for study but also to cover the background reading to assist in understanding its basic historical and cultural context.

WRITTEN EXAMINATION
The written examination is held in the formal examination period at the end of the semester and consists of short-answer type questions designed to ensure that a sound level of general knowledge of all areas of the course has been attained. These questions will focus on the most important issues covered in the course - historical, stylistic and cultural contexts - and students will be expected to provide suitable examples from the repertoire studied to back up their general understanding of these various aspects of the course.



Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
