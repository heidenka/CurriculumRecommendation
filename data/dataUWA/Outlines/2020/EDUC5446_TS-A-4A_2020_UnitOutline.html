Unit Information

The Visual Arts Curriculum II Unit builds on your understanding of curriculum, pedagogy, planning, learning strategies, resource use, assessment techniques and classroom processes gained during the first unit and your teaching practice. Our focus is on Year 11 and 12 courses in Visual Art, while consolidating and extending the understandings and skills you developed in Semester One.

The unit aims to develop resourceful teachers who are sufficiently flexible and creative to develop and implement effective teaching and learning programs based on the Courses of Study. It endeavours to make students sensitive to the characteristics and needs of learners as individuals and in groups. It also hopes to encourage you to reassess your philosophy of teaching on the basis of your first school experience, to make a constructive evaluation of your teaching effectiveness and to set realistic goals for the future.

In terms of your generic skills, in this unit we aim to help you improve your communication skills through both written and oral communication; problem-solving and creativity . You will be challenged to think of  appropriate and creative ways to meet the  needs of  children and to work collaboratively with your peers to encourage critical thinking and problem solving.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION: 
 Assessment 1: Curriculum Area Investigation. In this assessment you will explore the key issue of the importance of quality Arts programs and the role of creativity in contemporary Australian education.In answering the question you may wish to focus upon a specific area such as the ATAR stream, General Education, Foundation Education, Vocational Education or International Baccalaureate. You might also consider the need for diverse needs of learners at year 11 and 12 level including EAL/D, special needs including Gifted and Talented, and Indigenous students.This assessment will engage you in investigation, research and examination of best practice, which is an important aspect of your professional career. To do so, read widely in the professional, empirical literature and investigate what is presented to teachers through federal and state organisations, such as the Western Australian School Curriculum and Standards Authority and the New South Wales Board of Studies. Evidence should include books and journal articles reporting the findings of research, and may include the wider published literature, Department of Education policy documents, school level policies and other documents. You may also refer to the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers.Support your analysis by reference to the research literature and to a range of other sources of evidence.Be sure to provide your supported personal views and analysis of your findings.Use APA referencing, with a minimum of five references.Word count: 1,800 to 2,000 
Assessment 2: Using Assessment Data and Processes (in class and take home exercise) Weighting 10%

In class you will be given examples of Year 12 students’ responses a Visual Analysis and a Compare and Contrast task. You will refer to the task sheet and the marking key for both examples when assessing the work and you will assign a grade for each sample.

1. Individually you will assess the work samples. Be sure to annotate the reason and/or justification of why you assigned a particular mark to each criteria within the marking key.
2. In small groups you will then moderate the work samples and reach consensus about the grading of the work samples.
From this experience, you will write a 500 words reflection addressing the following question:
1. From this class exercise, what have you learned about the moderation process and assessment?
2. Your reflection should consider reference to literature on moderation and assessment practice.
· What is the purpose and process of assessment and moderation
· What are characteristics of effective moderation
· What issues arose from this moderation experience
· The implications for your future professional development

In you your reflection be sure to reference academic literature and/or relevant articles to support your findings.

To complete this assignment you are required to watch and analyse the videotape of your lesson made during your second School Experience. In conjunction with your report forms from the mentor and the GSE supervisor, you are to identify your strengths and weaknesses and develop an action plan to assist you in making progress in the areas of difficulty. Your assignment should be completed in no more than 1500 words . 
 Assessment 3: Post Compulsory Curriculum Development                       
Weighting: 60%

This assignment is designed to provide you with an opportunity to develop a package to support your teaching in your a 12 discipline in the early years of teaching. You are to focus of one of the units in the year 12 ATAR courses and develop a 15 week program addressing curriculum requirements, which will include:
· A rationale for the teaching of your upper school discipline – this should also engage with the aims stated in the Syllabus/Courses of Study (see SCSA documents).
· An overview of the structure of the course as per Curriculum documents and how you have developed it to suit your student needs and learning Context
· A Production Task Sheet outlining the project description, time allocation and what the students will need to do.
· A Critical Analysis Task for the unit of work. You are to choose an appropriate analysis framework that will be used by students to complete the task.
· An Investigation Task Sheet that focuses on an art movement or artist.
· A Weekly Schedule of how the unit you have chosen would be broken up over the allocated time (usually 15 weeks). Each lesson needs to be described outlining objectives and outcomes to be achieved. 

Consider that you would normally see the students 4 times within the week and one lesson per week should be dedicated to Art Interpretation. Ensure that you highlight when (Visual language, Visual Influence, Art forms, Media and Techniques, Presentation or Reflection) are being addressed in Art Making and when (Visual analysis, Personal Response, Meaning and Purpose and Social Historical and Cultural context) are being addressed in Art Interpretation.
· Three focussed lesson plans with supporting resources for the unit of work that utilise some of the resources that you have created, ideally for both practical and theoretical classes.

· Support Material and Resources:
· A minimum of 5 resources that support lesson content. At least half of these should be your own original resources ( see recommended resources below) while the others can be resources you have collected during your School Experience, in which case they must be acknowledged.
· An Overview of the Assessment Programme for the course with the weightings for each assessment and the week in which each is due. (as per SCSA guidelines).This should be set out in a table format.
· Marking Rubrics for Production, Critical Analysis and Investigation tasks as per (SCSA documents)
· Evidence of Professional Reading related to programme through a short review of two journal articles related to Visual Arts education in general.

Some Recommended Resources:

1. A worksheet/handout on the chosen artist/art movement for an Investigation task that is informative and provides critical information for students (created by yourself)

2. A power point presentation for students on an artist/art movement or artworks. It can be to related an Investigation task or it can be to support or inspire the Production task.

It is important to remember that quality not quantity is important in this assignment. Merely photocopying pages which you have acquired elsewhere will defeat the purpose of this, which is to encourage you to be reflective, to evaluate, to be selective, to demonstrate creativity and to organise and adapt materials.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
