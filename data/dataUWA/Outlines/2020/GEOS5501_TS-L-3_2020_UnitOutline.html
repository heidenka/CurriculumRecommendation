Academic Information

Content Groundwater modelling is an essential and widely used technique to quantify hydrogeological processes in water resource management. Models may be used to analyse the characteristics of groundwater flow systems (water balance, groundwater flow rates, residence times, etc) and to predict the future response to natural and anthropogenic impacts and management scenarios. This unit teaches the theoretical and practical aspects of numerical groundwater flow modelling. The students will learn how to develop conceptual models of groundwater flow systems and develop corresponding numerical models. Topics include model construction and discretisation, boundary conditions, model calibration, etc. This unit is designed to give students the information to successfully construct their own models and be able to use these skills in the workforce across consultancy, resource exploitation, research and regulation.

A significant portion of this unit will consist of working with software both during and outside of class. You will therefore be required to bring a laptop to class. You will need a Windows operating system in order to use the required software. It is possible to run the software on a Mac laptop. However, this might require the installation of additional software. If you wish to use a Mac laptop, you will need to install a Windows emulator. There are many Windows emulators that can potentially work for you; however, the following two emulators have been tested successfully with the software:
Bootcamp (https://support.apple.com/en-au/boot-camp)
Parallels (https://www.parallels.com/au/)
Please be sure that you have one of the following Windows operating systems installed: Windows 2000, Windows XP, Windows Vista 32-bit, Windows 7 32-bit/64-bit, Windows 8, or Windows 10. Please note that since it may not be possible for you to get the software to work on your laptop, it is essential that you try to install the software before the unit commences. If you are having difficulties, please contact the Unit Coordinator (Dr Adam Siade) and they can help you as much as possible. Some modifications, including the installation of additional software, may be required. To install software you will need to have administrator rights on your computers.
The primary software that will be used in this unit is Processing MODFLOW for Windows, or PMWIN. The version to be used in this unit will be posted in a folder through LMS. The posted version is a “demo” version and is limited to 5000 grid cells and three layers. Alternatively, you can download this demo version of PMWIN here: https://www.simcore.com/download. The “full” version of the software is also installed in a computer lab on campus (location to be announced in class).

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Even though this unit is fully online, students will get the greatest benefit out of this unit if they attend all online workshops in person (via Zoom). The workshops will likely be recorded if possible; however, as these workshops will involve a lot of one-on-one interaction, their respective recordings may be difficult to navigate and extract any desired information. The assignments for this class will be difficult to complete if students do not utilise the opportunity that workshops provide to ask specific questions about their assignments, e.g., model setup and debugging. Additionally, a large portion of each workshop will be dedicated to working individually on in-class exercises that may be part of an assignment. For example, the second assignment will consist of work completed both in, and out of class.

Unit Structure

This unit is conducted in a six-week series of four-hour workshops on Tuesday and Friday of each week. Each workshop will be a mixture of lectures, case studies and exercises.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
