Academic Information

Content This unit provides students with an overview of the humanities and social science learning areas in Western Australia. Students work with a range of school-based, state-based and national curriculum resources to develop the knowledge and competencies needed to devise lesson plans, create programs and develop a variety of learning strategies. Students explore a range of resources and instructional methods and develop and apply appropriate assessment practices. Contemporary issues related to the support of student literacy and numeracy, the use of ICT, values education, global education, and their implication to the teaching of humanities and social sciences are also explored.

Unit Information

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS) Curriculum I (Educn 5466) unit is designed to introduce students to the knowledge, skills, concepts and competencies required for teachers of lower secondary school HASS courses. It focuses on the Knowledge and Understandings; Skills and Key Concepts in the HASS Learning Area of the Western Australian Curriculum and Assessment Outline. The unit also provides opportunities for pre-service teachers to develop competencies which are applicable regardless of the curriculum structure. This is not a unit in social sciences, but it is about teaching social sciences. You will develop an understanding of how students learn most effectively in the HASS Learning Area and be able to use this understanding to plan and implement learning programs. It is important to realise that the outcomes in this learning area encompass concepts from several social science disciplines and therefore you may be required to understand concepts and content outside of your immediate degree studies. This unit aims to develop resourceful teachers who are sufficiently flexible and creative to initiate and implement effective teaching strategies and programs.  It endeavours to encourage pre-service teachers to be sensitive to the needs and characteristics of learners as individuals and in groups. Various assessment strategies and approaches will also be considered.  An understanding of assessment strategies, including informal and formal, diagnostic, formative and summative approaches to assess student learning will be developed. Additionally, student assessments will  be structured to allow students to demonstrate their capacity to organise learning activities and provide clear directions. There will also be formative feedback from peers and group discussions.  The unit hopes to develop in you an enthusiasm for your learning area; an awareness of your own attitudes towards teaching; and a reflective approach to help ensure the on-going effectiveness of your teaching practice.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
