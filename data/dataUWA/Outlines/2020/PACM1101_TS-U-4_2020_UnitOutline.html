Unit Information

Welcome to PACM1101: Communication in Practice.
This unit has been designed to further develop UWA students writing, speaking, critical thinking and research skills. Throughout the semester we will be (1) exploring how to write clearly and convincingly, (2) evaluating and utilising arguments and evidence, (3) creating effective oral presentations, and (3) practising how to work productively and creatively with other people.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit focuses on practical skills-development, so there is great emphasis placed on active engagement. 

Over the course of the semester, students will be putting communication into practice. They will be working on their writing, presenting and critical thinking skills, both individually and in teams. Attendance at the online tutorials is essential. In addition, the weekly lecture-workshops are designed to introduce key concepts in preparation for tutorials and the unit assessments.
Throughout the semester, students will also be keeping a regular 'Reflective Journal'. The journal helps students further develop their knowledge and skills, and track their achievements and progress the unit.
Teaching and Learning Evaluation
You may be asked to complete two evaluations during this unit: The Student Perception of Teaching (SPOT) and the Students’ Unit Reflective Feedback (SURF). The SPOT is optional and is an evaluation of the lecturer and other teaching staff. The SURF is completed online and is a university wide survey and deals only with the unit. You will receive an email inviting you to complete the SURF when it is activated. We encourage you to complete the surveys as your feedback is extremely important and can be used to make changes to the unit or lecturing style when appropriate.

Unit Structure

As a Winter School intensive unit, PACM1101 will run 5 days a week for 5 weeks. Classes run Monday to Thursday at 2pm. Fridays are set aside for independent study.
Each week, there are two Lecture-Workshops and two Tutorials:
(1) Lecture-workshops (Mondays and Wednesdays at 2-4pm): These classes are online and interactive if students are able to join us, but they are recorded if they cannot.
(2) Tutorials (Tuesdays and Thursdays at 2-3pm): These classes are where you get to practically apply what is covered in lecture-workshops.
The tutorials are not recorded. Attendance at these classes is required (see 'Participation' below). This is where students will be engaging in unit content directly, focusing on deepening the understandings gained from the lecture-workshops and assigned readings.

Participation

Participation
Participation in all aspects of this unit is an essential part of the learning process. To successfully complete PACM1101, and to get the most out of the unit, it is expected that you will do these five things every week: 
1) Review the readings and videos in the Weekly Activities folder
2) Attend the lecture-workshop (or watch the recording online)
3) Participate in the online tutorial
4) Do some reflective writing in your Learning Journal
5) Work on your assessments 
In addition, there is a tutorial participation mark for PACM1101.*
A mark of 10% will be awarded based on (1) your engagement in class activities, (2) your willingness to help others learn, and (3) evidence that you have come prepared having reviewed all the necessary materials for that week.
Since the team project is a major component of this unit, you must attend at least 80% of the online tutorials in order to participate in the team project. (The only exception would be if the student has prior approval by the Unit Coordinator.) 
*NOTE: The participation mark does not apply to attendance at the interactive Lecture-Workshops. We strongly encourage you to attend these classes in real-time because doing so enhances your learning; however, if you cannot make it, you can watch the recordings online and do the activities at home.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
