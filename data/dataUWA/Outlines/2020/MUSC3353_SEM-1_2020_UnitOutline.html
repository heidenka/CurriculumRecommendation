Learning And Teaching Strategy

For the in-class portion of the unit, face to face learning via lecture delivery will be complemented by online readings and activities. Some classes will be delivered in a seminar environment. For these classes, students will be expected to read articles and chapters from texts in order to lead discussion. Students will also learn in an extended practicum with an external provider (WASO) and perform as chamber ensembles in a community setting.

Unit Structure

During the semester, students will attend weekly lectures on a range of community music topics, perform in an external venue in the City of Perth, and work with a range of WASO community music projects as part of a practicum. The performance and practicum take place off campus. The practicum includes between 8-10 hours of work with WASO Community Engagement projects in a range of locations in the Perth metropolitan area. Students will be required to arrange their own transport to both practicum and external venue performances.
Assessment in Music in the Community includes an annotated bibliography and article presentation, a small group performance project, and a community music practicum with a reflective essay.

Participation

Active participation will ensure students get the best experience out of the unit. Given that a number of classes require students to meet in their small groups to plan and organise their chamber ensemble, not attending these classes will place undue pressure on other students in the group and risk lower outcomes and results for assessments. 
For the seminar elements of the course, attendance will be strongly encouraged. The lecturer will call on individual students to lead parts of the discussion.

Referencing Requirements

The Chicago citation style (notes and bibliography) is the default referencing style used for Music units at UWA. Students are strongly urged to use the Chicago style for assessments in Music in the Community. However, the use of APA is acceptable upon discussion with the unit co-ordinator.

ASSESSMENT DETAILS
IN-CLASS PRESENTATION (10%): Give a 5-minute presentation on an article of their own choice discussing community music. A rubric for the grading of this element can be found on the LMS site. Due in-class Week 4.
ANNOTATED BIBLIOGRAPHY (25%): Find four articles not previously discussed in class, which demonstrate the benefits of, or discuss exemplar community music projects. For each article, provide a full citation (Refer to referencing in the unit outline) and a 1-2 paragraph description of the article. A rubric for the grading of this element can be found on the LMS site. Due via LMS submission, April 28 at 11:59pm.
ASSESSMENT 2: PRACTICUM OR BUSINESS PLAN (30%)
OPTION A: PRACTICUM
Spend 8-10 hours working with a community music organisation in a mixture of practical, participatory, observational, and administrative roles. A representative of the organisation will provide a report on your work with them, giving you a mark for your contribution. A journal of your experiences MUST be kept as part of this activity. Students selecting this option will be required to complete a creative contract, noting what their roles will be within the organisation and have this approved by a representative of the organisation, and signed by the unit coordinator before proceeding with the practicum.
Practicum must be completed by May 22 and the log submitted via LMS, May 22 at 11:59pm.
Specific notes for Option A
-	You are required to find your own practicum organisation. UWA will provide no assistance in organising these placements
-	You must account for changing circumstances in this placement. Any plan approved by the UC will require back-up plans for any in-person contact required.
OPTION B: BUSINESS PLAN
You will be required to create a 12-minute video lecture where you propose a new community music project to be undertaken in Western Australia. This plan is hypothetical and no action is required for your project. The lecture should include as many of the following elements as you can address accurately in 12 minutes,
a)	The type of community music activity and a brief description of what activities will be undertaken
b)	An explanation of why you are pursuing this project and your interest in this area
c)	A SWOT analysis and a Portersâ€™ Five Forces analysis of the current opportunities in this area within Western Australia
d)	A description of why you are a strong candidate to undertake this project, based on a reflection of the information discussed in (c)
e)	References to relevant literature/research into projects of this type
f)	A description of organisation roles within the activity and who would fulfil them
g)	A brief discussion of inventory and requirements needed to fulfil the project
h)	A brief discussion of budget required, and how you would propose to fund the project
i)	A discussion of the ethos/goals of your project, and how that implicated decisions you made as part of this plan.
This 12-minute lecture needs to be video recorded and use suitable visual aids for support. You will be required to upload the lecture, and a PDF of your visual aid for assessment. You may submit an additional 2-page summary of your plan in written form if you choose to supplement your video.
Due via LMS submission, May 22 at 11:59pm.
REFLECTIVE ESSAY (35%): Write a 1500 word reflective essay, on the topic of "The power of community music." Refer to your own experiences in community music both within and external to this unit, as well as referencing community music literature published in texts and journals. Use your literature references from both in-class examples and outside sources to strengthen your personal experiences and thesis. A rubric for the grading of this element can be found on the LMS site. Due via LMS submission, May 25 at 11:59pm. 

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.
