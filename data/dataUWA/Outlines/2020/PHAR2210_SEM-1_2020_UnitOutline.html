Academic Information

Content This unit provides an introduction to the basic principles of pharmacology, the branch of science that studies the effects of drugs on the human body. Topics, which are delivered in modules, include (1) drug absorption, distribution, metabolism and excretion; (2) toxicology and structure–activity relationships; (3) receptor classification, dose–response relationships and intracellular signalling; (4) chemotherapy; and (5) clinical aspects of drug effects. The material in the unit is the foundation for further study in pharmacology but can be taken on its own. While introducing students to the scientific study of drugs and medicines, emphasis is mainly placed on explaining general mechanisms, processes and theories rather than detailing the complex properties of the many individual drugs that are used and abused in today's society. Summative assessments are held throughout the unit to help students adopt a learning strategy that facilitates long-term understanding and mastery of material.

ASSESSMENT MECHANISM STATEMENT
Attendance
Attendance at laboratories is compulsory for all students enrolled in PHAR2210, and will be recorded electronically on Blackboard. Students will be unable to access data and submit a laboratory report if their attendance is not successfully recorded. No credit will be awarded if a student fails to submit a laboratory report. Students will be required to complete two summative assessments under examination conditions during the semester. 
Written lab reports and assignment - requirements, submission and marking
Requirements - written lab reports and assignment must comply with the prescribed requirements, including formatting (font, font size, line spacing, and page margin) and page/word limit. The Vancouver citation style is the ONLY accepted citation style for all PHAR2210 written submissions.
Submission - students will be required to submit lab reports and assignment to Turnitin for plagiarism detection. Students will be able to view the originality report following a successful submission – be aware that students can re-submit BEFORE the due date, but can only submit once ON OR AFTER the due date.  It is a student’s responsibility to ensure they select the correct file for submission, and that their submission is successful. Students must also retain a copy of the confirmation email from Turnitin for each piece of work as proof of submission – this is the only accepted piece of evidence in the event of a dispute. Submissions by email will not be accepted, and penalty for late submission will be applicable until the work is successfully submitted to Turnitin. Late submissions without special consideration approval will be subject to penalty as specified in the University Policy on: Assessment. 
Marking - anonymous marking will be enabled in this unit, and students must not include any personal information (i.e. student ID number and/or name) in their submissions. Written submissions will be marked against a set of pre-determined criteria, which will be provided to students at the start of semester. 
Calculating PHAR2210 marks
Students are required to complete all assessment tasks in PHAR2210, and will not be exempted from an assessment task. Each PHAR2210 assessment task will be given a raw score out of 100. These raw scores are then converted to a percentage according to their weightings (see the Unit Handbook for details). The final mark for PHAR2210 will be the sum of these percentages. Marks for all intra-semester assessment tasks (i.e. excluding the end of semester exam) will be released to students ONLY via My Grades on Blackboard.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Students are required to attend laboratories and complete assessment tasks in PHAR2210 to obtain credit. While lectures are recorded and made available throughout the semester, students are advised to attend lectures regularly. In the event that atttending lectures is not possible, students are reminded to listen to lecture recordings regularly. Students are expected to login to Blackboard and check their UWA email account regularly for unit update and announcement. Students should make note of the due date for different written tasks, and ensure sufficient time is allocated to complete these tasks.  
Students should revise lecture contents regularly and use the practice questions available for self-assessment. PHAR2210 has a strong emphasis on the development of practical skills. Active participation in laboratories is expected from all students, as you will individually complete a lab skill test at the end of the semester.

Participation

Laboratory attendance is compulsory for all students enrolled in PHAR2210, and will be recorded electronically. Students must seek special consideration approval to waive laboratory attendance, otherwise they will be unable to access data and submit laboratory report. Students must complete the computer lab assessment during their scheduled session. A student will not be exempted from completing an assessment task, and marks assigned to the relevant assessment task will not be re-distributed.  

Referencing Requirements

Students will submit their written laboratory reports and assignment to Turnitin. Upon successful submission, students will be able to view the originality report. Students are strongly advised to submit a finished draft prior to the due date and make use of the originality report in case they need to improve the quality of their work and re-submit. Please note that it is neither appropriate nor practical to determine an acceptable percentage in the context of plagiarism detection, and the unit coordinator will read and review all submissions and make a decision accordingly. The Vancouver citation style is the prescribed referencing style for all written tasks in PHAR2210, and students can refer to the guide in the Unit Handbook and the library guide http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/vancouver for details.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
