Academic Information

Content This unit will consider the scientific and technical challenges associated with renewable energy generation from wind, waves, tides and hydropower schemes. Fundamental concepts for each of these resources and the general principles for energy generation will be outlined. This will include resource assessment, typical design approaches and operational considerations. Cutting edge developments in fluid mechanics, oceanography, structural engineering and geomechanics will be introduced where relevant. The unit will draw from local practical examples (such as the wind and wave energy developments in Albany) to take students through all steps of a project from site identification, through to design and deployment.

Unit Details

OCEN4007 - Renewable Ocean Energy - SEM-2-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

The aim of this unit is to provide a technical introduction to the renewable energy resources associated with hydropower schemes, wind, tidal streams and ocean surface waves, which represent key sources of ocean renewable energy. Students will gain a broad understanding of the scale of these resources, the scientific principles governing generation of power and the technology required to realise Ocean renewable energy. The engineering challenges associated with generation will also be covered, together with a discussion on site selection and project development.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Students are able to (1) participate effectively in teams; (2) communicate effectively in written reports; (3) demonstrate critical thinking and problem-solving skills; (4) choose and apply appropriate theories to estimate the size of ocean renewable resources; (5) predict how much power may be generated by ideal devices; (6) explain the challenges associated with ocean renewable energy generation; and (7) describe the key steps required for site selection and project development.
This is a single semester unit. It covers fundamental knowledge about hydropower schemes, wind, tidal and ocean surface waves, as well as the application of fluid mechanics knowledge to predict how devices may generate power from these sources of energy. The topics covered in this unit include a refresher on the relevant fluid mechanics, operation of hydropower schemes, offshore wind energy, tidal energy, wave energy and aspects of site selection and project development.

Assessment overview
This unit is assessed in the following way(s): (1) assignments, (2) laboratory reports and (3) a final examination. The unit does not have an assigned text book. However relevant references will be advised during lectures.
Assessment: Students will be assessed on the following three components:
o	Assignments
o	Laboratory reports
o	End of semester examination
The students will be assessed according to the aims of the unit (set out earlier). The final mark of the unit is calculated as:
Final mark = assignment mark (total 100) x 0.35 + laboratory mark (total 100) x 0.15 + examination mark (total 100) x 0.50.
The minimum pass mark for the unit is a total mark of 50.
No supplementary examinations will be available for the unit.
Assignment: Assignment sheets will be handed out at some tutorial sessions, and will comprise problems /questions. Answers to these problems/questions are required to be handed back completed within a specified period of time on the assignment sheet (normally between 1-2 weeks). It is the student’s responsibility to collect the assignments from the tutorial sessions and submit the assignments back before the deadline. Late submissions are subject to University guidelines for deducting marks. Special consideration may be granted on medical, personal or other grounds; applications must be lodged with the faculty office as per their current rules and guidelines but normally no later than three working days after the due date for the assessment in question.
Laboratory reports: Requirements for laboratory reports will be handed out prior to laboratory sessions together with pre-reading. It is the student’s responsibility to complete these reports using observations and measurements recorded by the student during the laboratory session. Reports will need to be submitted before the deadline. Late submissions are subject to University guidelines for deducting marks. Special consideration may be granted on medical, personal or other grounds; applications must be lodged with the faculty office as per their current rules and guidelines but normally no later than three working days after the due date for the assessment in question.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
