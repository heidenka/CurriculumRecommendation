Academic Information

Content Students explore a range of computing and mobile technologies, including web 2.0 tools and mobile apps, which are relevant to early childhood and primary education, with a particular focus on relevant pedagogical approaches. Students also learn about the use of new technologies for the advancement of teacher competencies and educational networking. Links are made to the Western Australian Curriculum: Technologies, Early Years Learning Framework for Australia; Australian Curriculum General Capabilities, particularly Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Capability and the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (AITSL), with particular reference to the ICT Elaborations for Graduate Teacher Standards. Students learn to plan for, and integrate, the Technologies learning area (Pre-primary to Year 6) and its two distinct, but related, subjects of Design and Technologies, and Digital Technologies

EDUC5521 - Technologies in the Curriculum - TS-A-1G-2020 - 2020 - Online

Unit Information

This unit has four main aims. It will provide opportunities to (1) develop your knowledge of the Technologies Curriculum and associated teaching strategies, (2) locate and explore a range of digital resources to engage students, (3) create an eportfolio of technology-related evidence to support job applications and ongoing career progression, and (4) increase your personal digital literacies, repertoire and confidence with digital technologies as a teaching professional.
Technologies Curriculum
A major focus will be the Western Australian Curriculum: Technologies learning area, Pre-primary to Year 6 and its two distinct, but related, subjects of Design and Technologies, and Digital Technologies. With a focus on creating preferred futures, you will explore how Design and Technologies can be integrated into teaching and learning to develop children’s design thinking and to generate and produce solutions for authentic purposes. As part of Digital Technologies, you will explore a range of computing and mobile technologies, including web 2.0 tools and mobile apps, which are relevant to early childhood and primary education. Computational thinking, information systems, and pedagogical approaches to support children to define, design and implement digital solutions, will also be explored. Links will be made to the Early Years Learning Framework for Australia; the Australian Curriculum General Capability, Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Capability; and the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (AITSL). As part of this unit, you will complete the nationally accredited University of Adelaide Computer Science Education Research Group (CSER) Digital Technologies - Foundations to 6 MOOC which will provide you with 21 hours of professional learning and certificate.
Practical Skills and hands-on exploration
A key aspect of this unit is to provide you with the practical skills you will need to integrate technologies into your teaching and to become a future technology education leader in your professional context. To support this aim, you will hear from a number of ‘rock stars’ in the field of technology education in Western Australia. These highly experienced guest presenters have been invited to extend your learning experience and provide the latest information about current technology education trends. A major focus will be on providing you with hands-on exploration of a range of resources. As part of an assessment, you will investigate how practicing teachers integrate aspects of the Technologies Curriculum into their teaching and learning.
Digital professional portfolio (Eportfolio)
To provide evidence that you have met the Technologies-related Australian Professional Graduate Standards for Teachers, a key requirement of this unit is the compilation of a digital professional portfolio. This will be a resource that you will continue to add to and modify as you progress through your teaching career. Graduate Standards 2.6, 3.4 and 4.5, along with evidence of your Personal Learning Network (PLN) under Standards 6 and 7, will need to be included. You will draw from on-campus assessments, lessons taught during professional placement, and experience gained through this unit. It is also important to comment on impact on student learning related to the above Standards, where possible. Your eportfolio, based in the Google Suite of programs, will be suitable for use during a job interview and the skills learned will be transferable to your future transition from provisional to full registration.
Increase personal repertoire of digital skills and literacies
By participating fully in this unit, you will also learn about the use of new technologies for the advancement of teacher competencies and educational networking.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is delivered fully online and also as interactive face-to-face tutorials. These two modes will run concurrently during the February condensed study period.

Unit Structure

The unit will run on 3,4,10 and 11 February with two sessions per day running from 9am to 12noon and 1pm to 4pm. Face-to-face tutorials will be held in G.09.
As this unit was originally planned to be run fully online, attendance via a Zoom connection will also be offered. The tutorial sessions will be held in the education building in G.09 and these will be live streamed to any students who wish to attend fully online. The reason the unit has been modified to include face-to-face tutorial sessions is that there were overwhelming requests from students for this to occur. In addition, using, experimenting with and discussing the various resources covered in this unit are major components of the learning experience. Another major component is the group work that, while entirely possible online, has a different quality. These are the reasons that F2F attendance is recommended as the best way to learn about the Technologies curriculum and the associated resources. However, if you are intending to attend as a fully online student, please contact me as soon as possible so that I can make arrangements for you regarding the group collaboration component (which will need to completed entirely in a virtual space). Personal attendance at your group’s presentation is highly recommended. Fully online and F2F students will be graded equitably.

Participation

In order to keep up with this fast-paced unit, it is essential that you attend every tutorial, either online or face-to-face. We have a lot of content to cover in a limited number of sessions, so be prepared to be available in morning and afternoon sessions for at least 2 hours and 45 minutes. On several occasions we will make use of the full three hours, so plan to be in class from 1pm to 4pm each day of the study period.
The series of online lectures (CSER MOOC) and the associated essay should reflect the expectations of AQF Level 9 (described below).
The unit has been designed to be challenging at AQF Level 9: Masters Degree (Coursework). This means that all your work on assignments and weekly participation must reflect the appropriate level of thinking. This level of thinking includes being able to reflect critically on theory and professional practice; using your cognitive, technical and creative skills to investigate, analyse and synthesise complex information, problems, concepts and theories related to technology education; apply established theories to different bodies of knowledge or practice to generate and evaluate complex ideas and concepts at an abstract level, and you should be able to justify and interpret theoretical ideas, methodologies, conclusions and professional decisions to specialist (such as other teachers) and non-specialist audiences (such as parents). At this level you will develop the technical and communication skills to design, evaluate, implement, analyse and theorise about developments in technology education that contribute to professional practice or scholarship. It is expected that you will be proactive in drawing on theory related to both pedagogy and technology education. Your submitted assignment work will be judged and marked with AQF Level 9 expectations in mind.
In terms of your generic skills, this unit aims to help you improve your communication skills as you will be engaging in a lot of written and oral communication; problem-solving and creativity in that you will be thinking of appropriate and creative ways to meet the technology education needs of children; and teamwork in that you will work in groups and teams to present a group assignment, and through your active, collaborative participation during the workshops.  As in almost every unit you undertake in your MTeach, your critical thinking skills will be developed as you will evaluate and critique research. When you start looking for employment, employers are very interested in these generic skills.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
