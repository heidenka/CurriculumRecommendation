Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is an introduction to methods in media and communication research. The methodology aspect introduces you to some key methods. It is run in a seminar format where students share their learning experience and insights into research design and methods with each other.
Some students will do this unit as part of their Honours or Masters dissertations. Naturally, these students are focused on their own dissertation. This unit will work more effectively if students look beyond the specific needs of their dissertation or project and treat the unit as a broader education in research methods. Even if the methodology under discussion is seemingly not relevant to you, each student is expected to come to seminars prepared to imagine and discuss how your approach would develop if you were using each different method and/or why that method would not help you answer your research question.
The responsibilities of the unit coordinator are to provide contemporary relevant resources to students and to ensure the assessments enhance student learning and unit outcomes.
The responsibilities of the seminar leader, or lecturer, is to ensure a safe, inclusive, fair and productive learning environment. This unit is run in a seminar format where students share their learning experience and insights into research design and methods with each other. Some of the material we will be covering is distressing and please alert the unit coordinator of this as soon as possible if it is the case.
Seminars rely on productive engagement between participants, so a responsibility of students is to strive towards productive and respective participation, based on a thorough understanding of the learning materials presented.
This unit operates through close engagement with and reading of texts. Students have a responsibility to remain engaged with the conceptual frameworks underlying the unit and also the concepts underpinning their choice of Case Study. Students must come to class fully prepared, ready to discuss the readings. Careful note-taking is advised.

Referencing Requirements

Work submitted in the unit is expected to adhere to following:
• Use APA referencing style. http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/friendly.php?s=apa
• Use one and a half or double spacing for text. 
• Text should be 12pts and an easy to read font such as Times New Roman, Arial or Calibri. 
• Denote new paragraphs by indenting the first line with the tab key. 
• Do not leave line spaces between paragraphs. 
• Leave one space after full stops (between sentences). 
• Use page numbers to ensure that your work stays in order.

Word Count
Word counts are firm. There is no 10% lee-way. Please include a word count on your assignment. For written pieces, especially essays, word limit counts should include all quotations and in-text references but exclude footnotes and references/bibliography. Information central to your argument should not be in footnotes and abuse of footnotes (i.e. containing long quotes) will be noted.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
