Unit Information

This unit explores the general capabilities: Literacy, Numeracy, Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Capability, Critical and Creative Thinking, Personal and Social Capability, Ethical Understanding, and Intercultural Understanding. The general capabilities play a significant role in the Australian Curriculum. As such, they support the development of knowledge, skills, behaviours and dispositions in a learning area, and the application of these in all aspects of life. General capabilities also offer opportunities to add depth and richness to student learning. Hence, teachers are required to teach and assess general capabilities to the extent that they are incorporated within their learning area content.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:
Assignment 1: Research Report (40%)
There are two elements of this assignment:
Part a – a written paper (word limit 1500 words, not including end-references);
Part b – a poster.
Part a (30%)
Write a rationale for the explicit teaching of General Capabilities in the Australian classroom.
In your rationale, you will need to demonstrate an understanding of:
the purpose(s) of education; 
the philosophical, economic and other drivers that might contribute to the development and implementation of General Capabilities; 
the relevant policies relating to General Capabilities; 
capabilities in the global context; 
the Capabilities versus Standards debate; 
the ways in which capabilities being foregrounded will benefit students.
Your rationale should draw from academic and other appropriate reference sources. 
Part b (10%)
Present your rationale as a poster that would be suitable to display on the wall of your classroom. 
In designing your poster, consider the use and typical features of posters. You might include such things as: title and author; key points; the use of images that complement the subject matter; and the use of a key quote. And make sure that you avoid clutter or visual chaos that would distract the reader.
Word limit
1500 words (not including end-references), plus a poster.
Marking criteria
A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS. 
At the beginning of your assignment please include your student chosen assessment criterion, and the standards of achievement for this criterion.
Assignment 2: Create Teaching Resources (20%)
Create a set of four teaching resources that will develop the General Capabilities literacy, numeracy, critical and creative thinking, and ethical understanding, set within the context of a learning and teaching project.
The project will address specific outcomes from the Australian Curriculum Years 7 to 10.
The learning and teaching context may be either subject discipline specific (i.e. your subject area), or inter-disciplinary (i.e. integrated curriculum, or cross curriculum).
The teaching resources may aim to develop either each of the specified general capabilities separately, or various combinations of two, three or four of the specified general capabilities together. 
For your teaching resources, please include:
an introduction to the project; 
the scope of project – learning area(s), year level(s), AC content covered, outcomes; 
a project outline; 
resources; 
assessment and feedback tools; 
a brief explanation of, or justification for, the place of the resources in the project.
Word limit
500 words, plus four resources.
Assignment 3: Create Lesson plans (40%)
Create a set of four sequential lesson plans that will foreground the development of relevant General Capabilities in the learning and teaching activities. 
Justify your planning by referring to academic sources and other relevant documentation. 
Your lesson plans should:
be well organised so that another teacher could readily implement them;
include a brief overview or outline describing the focus and intentions of the lesson sequence; 
include the relevant lesson outcomes addressing content descriptions from the Australian Curriculum and their elaborations relevant to your lessons; 
identify where and how the General Capabilities of the Australian Curriculum are being developed; 
include a detailed series of lesson steps (including time allocation) indicating the sequence and transitions for activities (use a suitable lesson structure, such as Bybee’s 5Es with timing 1:3:3:1, or similar); 
identify areas for differentiation, including learning support for low achieving students, and ideas for extension and enrichment; 
include all investigations, handouts, worksheets, manipulatives (please include images of these), URLs of websites, e-resources, ICT, and so on, that will be used during the four lessons. 
Word limit
2000 words (indicative, and not including end-references).
Marking criteria
A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS. 
At the beginning of your assignment please include your student chosen assessment criterion, and the standards of achievement for this criterion.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
