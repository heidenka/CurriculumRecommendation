Learning And Teaching Strategy

Learning in this unit is heavily focused towards application of concepts and knowledge, with practical skills, to help
students develop a deep understanding of common Earth materials, focussing on sedimentary rocks and the processes that form them.
Lectures/Workshops provide a blend of conceptual information and interactive work in class to generate a knowledge
framework whereas the workshops and practical classes will focus on application and practical skill development, and
highlighting the importance and relevance of key concepts in sedimentary geology.
Where possible Lectures in the Webb Lecture Theatre are recorded via the Lecture Capture System, but is not available in the Robert's Street Practical Room.

Unit Structure

Lecture/Workshop
This unit starts in the third week of the semester and has initially 4 lecture/workshops on  introductory geology held on Tuesdays (2-5 pm in the Roberts Treet Building ground floor 2nf year lab. The second half of the semester on sedimentary geology. The last 6 weeks of the unit/semester have a 2-hour lecture-tutorial on Mondays held in the Webb Lecture Theatre (Geology-
Geography Building, ground floor), 1–3 pm. In the 2nd half of semester, there is a  Thursday Workshop held in
the 2nd Year lab, Robert Street Building. This class is only one hour of participation but is held over a two-hour period
and is designed to give you more opportunity to develop sedimentary skills through small group work. More
information will be provided during semester. You are strongly encouraged to attend as many lectures and workshops as
you can and especially the Monday class at the start of the week because this is when the topic of the week's work is
introduced. If you miss this class, it is important to catch up the content before the next class, and importantly before
your practical class that week.
Practical sessions
Attendance and participation is expected at one 3-hr practical class per week in the last 6 weeks of the semester
(either Tuesday morning or Thursday afternoon). Note that classes start on the hour - please be punctual so that you do
not miss the lab introduction. Allocation to one of these labs is via CAS or with permission of the Unit Coordinator.
Please come to your allocated lab. Practical classes are held in Robert Street Building 2nd Year Geology Laboratory
(located on the ground floor). Wearing covered footwear in the lab is a UWA safety rule. Please keep this in mind on the
days you have lab classes!! You will not be allowed into the lab without appropriate footwear. Your campus card will
provide you with access to this lab out of class time.
Fieldtrip
Practical classes in the last week of semester (weather permitting) will be a local fieldtrip during the practical class
times. Full details will be provided during semester.

Participation

In this unit, we expect students to attend as many lectures and workshops as possible and to attend their practical class each week (= 6 hours per week with the teaching staff).  This unit starts in the third week of the semester and has 4 lecture/workshops on  introductory geology and the second half of the semester on sedimentary geology.  Each week explores a specific topic and much of the key learning in this unit is through practical work.  There is also an expectation that you will undertake up to 4-6 hours per week in out-of-class work that might include finishing practical work, doing assignments, reading and revising.  Lectures are recorded but keep in mind that accessing captured lectures requires extra time allocation and planning to keep up to date with the work.  The recorded lectures are very useful for revision during semester to supplement your notes and worksheets.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
