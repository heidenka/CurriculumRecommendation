Content This unit introduces students to the understanding and provision of career development in Western Australian secondary schools. It focuses on what may be provided for students in Years 7 to 10 and on the structure of Vocational and Education Training (VET) programs in Years 11 and 12.

Unit Information

The content of the unit is presented in 3 sections - though all three do interconnect throughout.
Section One – Career Development
Students should develop an understanding of Career Development including:
Historical context of Career Development;
Appreciation of the language of the career development;
Understanding of the Australian Blueprint for Career Development (ABCD) including the eleven career management competencies;
Understanding of the Australian Blueprint for Career Development (ABCD) including  the four developmental phases;
Understanding of the Australian Blueprint for Career Development (ABCD) including the performance indicators and local standards;
Appreciation of how the ABCD can be used in secondary school context.
Section Two – Vocational Education and Training
Students will develop:
An understanding of the AQTF;
Understanding of the merging of training into education in a secondary school context;
Relationship between retention and participation and VET in secondary schools;
An appreciation of the importance as a staff member in a secondary schools in understanding the Training sector.
Section Three – Related issues
Students will be able:
To understand the relationship of career development in addressing SAER matters;
To identify good practice of career development in addressing students social, emotional and academic development.

ADDITIONAL ASSESSMENT INFORMATION: 
Three Sequential lesson plans – 40%
This assessment involves identifying areas of the Australian Blueprint for Career Development (ABCD) within a design of three sequential lessons.  The lessons should contain structured lesson plans and associated teaching and learning materials. 
In this assessment you will required to produced three lesson plans form your major learning area. The lessons must also identify throughout the lessons where competencies of the Australian Blueprint for Career Development can be identified and an explanation supportive of that competency must be provided.   Each lesson must also have its attached support material, so if your lesson included a particular worksheet or video resource that item and link must be also provided with comment on how the support resources match the competency identification.
Career Development Practitioners folder – 60 %
The assessment involves the collection and analysis of materials that would be useful for a Career Practitioner in a secondary school content.  The material included needs to cover (printed material, human resources, electronic and audio-visual material and internet resources).
The collected material needs to be professionally presented.  The material will be assessed in line with the ABCD and a written comment on the material included needs to be produced in conjunction with the collection. The analysis of the material needs to identify the competencies covered, the phases of use from the Australian Blueprint for Career Development and comment of how and to what purpose and year groups the material would be used with.
The breadth and depth of material must demonstrate that you have thought about the resources available to support a wide variety of possible needs from a secondary school position including material from every possible year groups use, from Year 7 to 12. The material also needs to demonstrate your appreciation of the differentiation required to support students with special needs, indigenous students and students from diverse ethnic backgrounds. The broader application of the Australian Blueprint for Career Development needs to be also considered when identifying, and rationalising the inclusion of material when addressing overall student health and well being.
It is important that your reasoning for identifying the competencies that you do, is made and argued demonstrating that you appreciate and have an understanding of competencies from the ABCD.  Your interpretation will also show the level of critical and reflective engagement that you have had with the resources and demonstrates that you have reflected on what the competencies actually are trying to define in a practical sense and how The folder must be formatted in such a manner that the material included is indexed or catalogued in relation to the ABCD competencies and target audience. The material needs to be presented professionally demonstrating an appreciation of the professional expectation on the teaching workforce.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.

Special Considerations

If your study has been adversely affected by illness or other significant circumstances outside of your control, you can apply for special consideration. Significant circumstances may include but are not limited to:

  • serious illness or death of a member of the student's immediate family or household or of a close friend
  • serious injury
  • being a victim of a crime
  • breakdown of relationship
  • sudden loss of income or employment
  • serious disruption to domestic arrangements
