Unit Information

Apart from acquiring mastery over the disciplinary knowledge taught in your prior studies and graduate degree, WILG5001 Professional Development Practicum (hereafter WILG5001) provides you with an opportunity to undertake a work integrated learning experience that complements your academic studies and helps you to prepare for your chosen career upon graduation.
Undertaking WILG5001 provides many benefits. Firstly, it gives you an opportunity to put theory into practice and further develop your awareness of workplace culture and expectations. Secondly, you will further enhance your ‘transferable’ skills such as communication, team work, email and report-writing skills, punctuality and attendance. Thirdly, a practicum is your opportunity to develop a practical appreciation of your chosen career and enhance your future employment prospects. Finally, your practicum is an opportunity for an employer to evaluate your potential as a future employee. Research shows that students who undertake a practicum are significantly more likely to be offered vacation work or employment on graduation.
WILG5001 is to be taken in your academic program as an elective unit.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

WILG5001 is unlike any other unit that you have taken in your degree to date as it provides you with an opportunity to undertake a minimum of 100 hours of paid and/or unpaid employment in an actual work environment under the guidance of an external supervisor within your host organisation along with the support of an academic advisor from the Faculty of Arts, Business, Law and Education (ABLE). Thus, by its very nature, WILG5001 will provide you with a learning experience that is uniquely yours and different from the experiences of other students in your degree program.
In terms of having achieved the learning outcomes for WILG5001, you will be able to:
1. Practice effectively as an emerging professional through teamwork, collegial practice, conflict management and the adoption of appropriate professional conventions, as appropriate to the placement;
2. Apply communication and interpersonal skills to a working situation;
3. Apply theoretical knowledge and frameworks in a practical setting and
4. Reflect on and evaluate their performance in a professional context.
As indicated by the above, WILG5001 places primary responsibility upon you for the achievement of these learning outcomes. Whilst the host organisation, host supervisor and your academic advisor from ABLE will contribute to the quality of your work integrated learning experience, your commitment to the practicum placement (or internship) is of the greatest significance in achieving those outcomes.
WILG5001: How is it arranged and undertaken?
Candice Brooking is the Practicum Placement Officer for ABLE and she will have managed you through the process for obtaining a work integrated learning placement.
Undertaking your Practicum Placement
As you commence your placement with your practicum host, it is important to remember some fundamental guidelines about what a placement is, and what it is not:
• A placement is not simply ‘free labour’ for your host organisation; it must provide a genuine learning experience for you and an opportunity to apply the knowledge and skills you obtained from your studies and to further develop those skills.
• In commencing your placement, you should have a clear written or verbal description of the activities that are to be undertaken during the placement. The Academic Coordinator will be available to provide advice if necessary.
• Once you have your placement roles and responsibilities established, you can start work.
On-placement activities
During your placement, please note the following:
• As stated above, your professional practicum placement is not just ‘work experience’, it is an opportunity for you to critically reflect on your learning and how it intersects with the ‘real’ world. To ensure that you maintain adequate documentary evidence to draw upon when drafting your Practicum Report, it is highly recommended that you maintain a daily or weekly diary in which you record those things that have interested, surprised and/or frustrated you. Using the STAR Analysis tool will help you make sense of some of your experiences and contribute to your ability to provide evidence of relevant workplace skills that you have developed.
• It is important to act with a high level of professionalism and integrity throughout your placement. Remember that when you are on placement, you are also representing your student colleagues, the academic program you are studying and the wider University. Hence, completing time sheets, interim and post completion online surveys and interim achievement and planning documents contributes to a professional approach.
• Placements provide you with an opportunity to develop a relationship with a potential employer. However, please note that there are no guarantees that you would be offered employment simply through undertaking your placement.
Undertaking a practicum placement can be a stressful experience. Whilst a moderate amount of stress is a normal feature of working life, if you need extra support, the University offers a wide range of health and wellbeing support services that can restore and balance your life. Information on these comprehensive health and wellbeing support services can be accessed from: http://www.student.uwa.edu.au/experience/health.
Practicum Portfolio
The Practicum Portfolio comprises two parts: the in-placement evaluation tasks (30%) and post-placement tasks and final report (60%). These two parts will be completed in various ways including within practicum activities and the final Practicum and Reflection Report. You are required to complete your Practicum and Reflection Report in accord with the specific requirements for your Practicum Placement and upload this report to LMS. As the Practicum and Reflection Report component of your WIL portfolio accounts for 90% of your assessment weighting it is the critical task for determining the academic outcome (i.e., pass or fail) of your enrolment in WILG5001 Professional Development Practicum 1.
Nevertheless, despite your best efforts, you may find that adverse changes in your health or wellbeing have negatively impacted upon the completion of your practicum placement and/or report. In these circumstances, you may be eligible for ‘special consideration’ which may take the form of an extension for the completion of your practicum placement and/or report. UniAccess is able to provide advice and to assist you to make an application to the Faculty of Arts, Business, Law and Education. However, you must read the conditions attaching to the granting of special consideration before lodging the Application for Special Consideration form. Links to UWA’s policy on the granting of special consideration are as follows: http://www.student.uwa.edu.au/course/exams/consideration and http://www.governance.uwa.edu.au/procedures/policies/policies-and-procedures?policy=UP11%2F23. The electronic version of the Application for Special Consideration form can be accessed from:
http://www.student.uwa.edu.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0009/3007719/Special-Consideration-Electronic-Form-6.pdf
In brief, your Practicum Report should cover the following topic areas:
• A brief description of the placement, including a statement of roles and responsibilities;
• A critical analysis of the placement in relation to your program of study. This may include:
▪ a critical discussion of the host organisation and its position within your intended industry/career;
▪ a critical discussion of how your learning to date will influence your approach to the specific role you will be undertaking, and/or
▪ a critical discussion of how the role might contribute to your learning and career aspirations.
Please note that your Practicum and Reflection Report must be consistent with the requirements set this assessment task. Further information is available from the Practicum and Reflection Report Guidelines document that can be downloaded from LMS.

Assessment overview
The specific assessment tasks to be completed for WILG5001 vary with the type of placement undertaken, the discipline within which a student specialises and how the placement is undertaken (e.g., on a part-time or full-time basis).
Assessment information is provided in the unit outline. Students can enrol in WILG5001 in First Semester, Second Semester or the non-standard teaching period over Summer (November to February) or Winter (June to July).
Satisfactory completion of the unit will result in a Pass grade (no numeric mark), and not contribute toward any major. The Academic Coordinator will provide support to students as they undertake the agreed work integrated learning activity and reflect on their overall experience. To pass the unit, students must not only demonstrate their ability to perform in the workplace but also to critically reflect on the experience and relate it to their academic studies.
More detailed information regarding assessments is available via the Assessments tab in the unit LMS.
The practicum portfolio for WILG5001 includes relevant documents relating to the practicum. Such documents include records relating to career-planning and pre-placement activities; quality assurance tasks and tools (e.g., online surveys, timesheets, achievement and planning milestone reports and STAR analyses) and the Practicum Report.
A student who withdraws from WILG5001 after confirmation of placement with the host organisation will be determined to have failed the unit unless the Faculty is satisfied that there were exceptional circumstances to justify withdrawal.
Unit Schedule
As WILG5001 provides an individualised work integrated learning experience for each student, the typical teaching period unit schedule (e.g.,Semester 1) does not apply. Thus, students must follow a work flow process that enables them to enrol in, undertake and report upon their practicum placement in the teaching period that best matches the timing of their practicum.
Once having their enrolment in WILG5001 approved, a student will engage in the planning and pre-placement activities related to their practicum, undertake a minimum of 100 hours of work experience and complete their practicum portfolio which comprises all the tasks that have been completed during the Practicum and Reflection Report. Whilst it is envisaged that all of these tasks will be undertaken prior to and during the enrolled teaching period, the timing and taking of the work experience may affect when all requirements are to be completed. Hence, while the Practicum Report is normally to be submitted no later than the Wednesday immediately following the last official examination for the relevant teaching period, a final date for submitting the report will be determined in collaboration with and the approval of the Academic Coordinator.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
