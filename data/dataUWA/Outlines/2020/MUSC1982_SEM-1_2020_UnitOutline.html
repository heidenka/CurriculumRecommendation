MUSC1982 - Music Ensemble 2 - SEM-1-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

Building upon the exploration of self-reflection upon musical experience in MUSC1981 Music Ensemble 1, the primary focus of this unit is the exploration of expressiveness and musical performance.
By participating in a music ensemble, students develop instrumental/vocal skills to levels of assured and convincing public ensemble performance.  Such participation results in one or more public performance/s toward the end of semester (either within the University or the wider community).  Music ensembles may be subject to audition*, the same ensembles as previously attended in MUSC1981*, or be new to the student.  Participation allows students to apply theoretical investigation of expressiveness by direct application to their ensemble's performance/s.
 A series of short videos focus upon the development of analytical skills and approaches that can be applied to expressiveness and musical performance, and students apply these to a portfolio of written work and video presentation. 
This unit is an elective unit in the Bachelor of Arts degree. It is also open to students enrolled in other degree pathways who fulfil the prerequisites and who have an interest in music.
* e.g. Symphony Orchestra, Wind Orchestra, Con-Cantorum, Winthrop Singers.
** availability of ensembles for this unit is subject to change. All ensembles may not run in every semester.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit comprises a blend of directed learning (i.e. ensemble rehearsals and performance), and self-directed and self-paced learning (i.e. personal practice of musical part, watching of videos, development of Analysis Portfolio components).  
The Analysis Portfolio components have been devised to be completed at any time, and allow for self-paced learning, and balancing workload with deliverables for other units.  They do not necessarily need to be linked to ensemble rehearsal and performance experience/s within this semester.
The Participation and Performance components are devised and assessed by each ensemble's Director/Conductor, and expectations and requirements will be outlined in the first rehearsal. 

Unit Structure

The unit is designed with two main components: 
1. Ensemble Participation and Performance; and 
2. Analysis Portfolio.
Ensembles
As a guide, you should expect approximately 12 weekly rehearsals of 2-3 hours duration, and a public performance before the end of semester, which is typically during weeks 10-12 but may vary depending upon the ensemble and project/s.  The amount of personal preparation will depend upon your proficiency with your instrument/voice, repertoire, style, etc, but as a very broad guide will usually be about one hour per week.  As the public performance approaches, some ensemble members will typically arrange their own sectional rehearsals as appropriate.
Analysis Portfolio
1. Proposal:  200 words.  The Proposal requires you to choose four works for analysis, a methodology, and reasoning for these choices.  As a guide, you should expect to undertake your reading, listening, and watching early in semester to allow you to develop this proposal (say weeks 1-3 to gather resources, week 4 to develop the proposal, week 5 to finalise and submit - which allows a one week fudge factor).  
Submit by the end of week 6.
2. Video Presentation:  7-minutes.  The video comprises your summary (PowerPoint) slides, and with you presenting to camera as if being recorded while delivering a 7-minute seminar presentation.  As a guide, you should develop some basic video recording and editing skills if you do not already have these (say weeks 1-4), start developing your slides concurrently as you develop your proposal (say weeks 1-4), commence applying your analysis (say weeks 3-7), develop your presentation script (say weeks 6-8), record and edit your script (say weeks 8-10) for submission.  
Submit by the end of week 11.
3. Written Analysis:  1,500-1,800 words.  Your essay presents your cogent argument supporting your choice of musical performances, analysis choice, referencing materials, and summary or conclusions.  As a guide, you would develop this concurrently with your Video Presentation.  
Submit by the end of week 12.

Participation

Ensemble rehearsal attendance
Ensemble work relies on everyone participating fully to the best of their ability. 100% attendance is required for all rehearsals unless a student has been granted approval for any excused absence by the Unit Coordinator, or special consideration in writing by their Faculty Student Office. Failure to attend without a medical certificate or other approved documentation will result in a penalty of 10% reduction in the attendance/participation component of the unit, per unexcused rehearsal absence. Students with more than two unexcused absences will be deemed to have unsatisfactory attendance/participation and this will result in the award of a mark of 0 for the unit.
Ensemble attendance and participation assessment is based upon the following elements: attendance, meeting the expectations of the ensemble director (as provided to each ensemble at the first rehearsal of the semester by the ensemble director), rehearsal preparation, individual contribution to the ensemble, responsiveness to direction, and interaction with other members of the group.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
