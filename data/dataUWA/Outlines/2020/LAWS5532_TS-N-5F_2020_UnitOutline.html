Academic Information

Content This unit examines the law regulating oil and gas agreements in Western Australia. The aim is to consider the key provisions of key contracts important to the commercial development of oil and gas. They include joint venture agreements, transportation and storage contracts and long term sales contracts. The workshop includes contributions from leading industry experts and comprises both plenary sessions and small group sessions analysing problem situations.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is taught intensively. The main teaching is an intensive workshop from 11 to 13 November that in 2020 will be conducted remotely using Zoom. Students should review materials distributed on LMS from the start of the teaching period. During this time they should work on completing the first assessment (Case Study Analysis 1) which is due before the start of the intensive workshop. The intensive workshop consists of a mixture of plenary and small-group sessions. There are approximately 9 plenary sessions for discussion based on the materials. These aim to give broad coverage of the key legal materials and concepts.  Small-group sessions will take up particular problems using case studies for more examination in the light of general guidance given in the plenary sessions. These are held in the afternoon each day and cover the morning's material, giving students the opportunity to test and expand their understanding and analysis of the issues dealt with in the morning. In the small-group sessions students will work in syndicates with industry participants to work up responses to the problems which will then be compared and further discussed in meetings of all the groups together.

Some of the readings included in the materials are drawn directly from legislation, regulations, and reports of judicial decisions.Context and language may be unfamiliar to students who do not have a legal background, but the plenary sessions will aim to clarify the significance and, where necessary, the meaning of this material. Alongside the intensive sessions the other major element of the unit is a research paper. Students from a non-legal back ground will find the following  helpful: UWA Legal Resources for Postgraduates in Law- introductory materials for students without a legal background (this should be available on your LMS homepage).

Participation

Attendance at all sessions in the intensive workshop is required. It is a condition of passing the unit that students have attended all sessions of the unit. An attendance register is maintained and students must sign in and out on this to qualify to meet this condition. In exceptional circumstances attendance at an individual session can be waived by the Unit Co-ordinator.

The reason for this condition is that the workshop is attended by non-students active in the oil and gas industry. Attendance is both an opportunity to increase knowledge and an essential part of the learning for the unit.

Required Assessments
The learning outcomes for the unit are that students are able to: (1) identify the agreements more commonly used to commercialise oil and gas in Australia; (2) identify and explain the fundamental principles of the law regulating agreements for the transportation and sale of gas in Australia; (3) critically analyse and apply those principles in relation to a range of oil and gas agreements; and (4) identify and explain the fundamental principles of the law regulating the marketing of oil and gas in Australia.

The first assessment is an analysis of the first small-group case study considered in the intensive workshop. The word limit is 1600 words. A template for the analysis and a marking scheme will be provided with the problem at the start of the learning period for the unit. The assignment is to be completed prior to the start of the intensive workshop with the prime objective of of giving students the opportunity to consider what they have learned prior to the workshop and prepare for it.

The second assessment is a memorandum of advice to one of the parties in another small-group case study. The word limit is 1600 words.

The third assessment is a research paper on a topic from the list included in the unit materials or within the scope of that list and agreed with the Unit Co-ordinator by the end of the intensive workshop. The word limit is 4800 words.

Further guidance on the formal requirements for the second and third assessments will be provided on LMS, including the case study nominated for the second assessment and the party to be advised.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
