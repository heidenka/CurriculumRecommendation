Academic Information

Content The topics covered in this unit build on those presented in undergraduate units, and reflect cutting-edge developments in biochemistry and molecular biology, providing students with an understanding and knowledge of the discipline at an advanced level. Students develop and present a lecture on an aspect of one of the topics through independent readings, and submit an assignment related to this lecture. The lecture highlights the research that has led to our current understanding of the topic. The main academic objective of the unit is for students to gain an understanding and an appreciation of how the knowledge in a field of biochemistry and molecular biology progresses. Students' abilities in identifying and critically reviewing appropriate sources of the literature, critical thinking, data analysis, and interpretation of results are extended. They also broaden their knowledge base and gain experience in presenting material unrelated to their research project.

Unit Information

Each student has to attend all of the workshops and three of the presentation sessions and attendance will be taken. Student are required to read articles and reviews in preparation for the workshop sessions to complement the material taught and discussed in workshops and to prepare a 10 minute presentation in a lecture style which students will present during the presentation sessions.
PLEASE NOTE: Workshops and presentation sessions will NOT be recorded. 
You need to ensure that you have a firm understanding of the basics of the workshop topics equivalent to a BSc degree in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology at UWA. You can find the basics in most good biochemistry and molecular biology textbooks. If you do not know which textbook to consult, please talk to the individual lecturer. As this is an advanced unit in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology. the topics covered require reading of the research literature (research articles, review articles). 
Formative feedback will be provided to a set of formative, exam style questions within the first half of the teaching period and to the assignment.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is delivered in two parts:
  PART 1: A set of blended model workshops. Students will have to read a set of articles prior to attending the workshops. During the workshops, lecturers will give an overview of the topic and engage students in discussions of the topic.  Student are expected to attend all of the workshop sessions. 
  PART 2: A set of presentation sessions. Students will prepare a ten-minute mini-lecture based on topics related to the four workshop topics. Students will have the opportunity to consult the lecturer responsible for the topic - by appointment. Prior to the presentations, students will submit a written summary of the mini-lecture which has to include a dot-point list of the content, a statement of the expected learning outcome for the audience and a list of peer-reviewed references used for the preparation of the mini-lecture. The summary will be marked (10% of unit marks) and returned to students prior to the presentation to provide feedback. Students will present the mini-lecture during one of the presentation sessions followed by a five minute question session based on the mini-lecture topic. The presentation will be assessed by part of the student cohort and by academics (40% of unit marks). 
In addition to the mini-lecture (40% of marks) and the mini-lecture summary (10% of marks), the unit will be assessed by a written examination (50% of marks). The examination will cover the workshops and presentation session topics and a set of articles which students have to read in advance prior to the examination. More details will be provided via LMS.

Participation

Students are expected to attend all of the workshop sessions and three of the presentation sessions and the written examination. Late or non-attendance will lead to a loss of the marks associated with the activity.
The lecturers will select references to be read for the workshops and the examination.
Students are expected to prepare for the workshop sessions by reading the material referenced on LMS (available during semester) and to actively participate in the discussions during the workshops. 
The lecturers will give an overview of the workshop topics and guide discussions during workshops.
Students are expected to research a topic related to the workshops and to prepare a mini-lecture and a summary of the mini-lecture (assignment). 
The mini-lecture will be presented by students during one of the presentation sessions. 
Students are expected to read a set of publications in preparation for the written examination.

Referencing Requirements

Students are expected to reference any material used for the mini-lecture summary and for the mini-lecture presentation. References should be provided using the Vancouver citation style (https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/vancouver).

Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
Academic conduct
Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.
Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
