Academic Information

Content Students examine and critique research-based approaches to teaching literacy as a general capability and the English curriculum in the primary classroom, including vocabulary, comprehension, grammar, writing/creating, visual literacy and spelling in the context of a range of text types, with a focus on Years 3 to 6. Students also learn how to plan and sequence learning experiences for literacy and English in primary settings, using student data, and use a range of pedagogical strategies to support whole class, small group and individualised literacy instruction.

EDUC5516 - Literacy Across the Curriculum - TS-A-4A-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

Unit Content
Students examine and critique research-based approaches to teaching literacy as a general capability and the English
curriculum in the primary classroom, including vocabulary, comprehension, grammar, writing/creating, visual literacy and
spelling in the context of a range of text types, with a focus on Years 3 to 6. Students also learn how to plan and sequence
learning experiences for literacy and English in primary settings, using student data, and use a range of pedagogical
strategies to support whole class, small group and individualised literacy instruction.
Unit Outcomes
Students are able to (1) demonstrate an understanding of how primary-aged children learn language and literacy; (2)
demonstrate a knowledge and understanding of the English curriculum and Literacy as a general capability curriculum and a
range of pedagogical approaches to teach it; (3) articulate a theory of literacy learning and teaching for the primary years,
based on a critical analysis of research and theoretical literature; (4) create a planning document in English /Literacy that is
appropriate for diverse learning needs; (5) demonstrate knowledge of how to develop children's literacy skills to support
numeracy across the curriculum; (6) create, use and evaluate a range of resources for teaching and learning literacy in the
primary years, including digital technologies; and (7) plan for the use of assessment strategies, including informal, formal,
diagnostic, formative and summative approaches when developing an integrated unit of work focused on English and
Literacy across the curriculum.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:
Assignment 1: Analysis of assessment data and case study (35 marks)
This assignment requires you to analyse/interpret and reflect on a set of spelling assessment data. You will describe how you would use the assessment data to inform your classroom teaching of phonics, spelling and vocabulary (word study). A justification for your proposed teaching strategies should be included, with reference to the literature and the data. You will need to refer closely to the text, Words Their Way: Word Study for Phonics, Vocabulary, and Spelling by Bear, Invernizzi, Templeton & Johnston. There is a 2020 edition of this text but you may use an older edition.
Steps you should take to complete this assignment:
• Analyse the class sets of spelling tests using Words Their Way inventories;
• Complete a class composite;
• Decide how you would group the children into three to five groups. Display your groupings in tabular form with a summary of the children’s scores (a template will be provided). Write a short justification for the number of groups and the composition of the groups. Up to approximately 500 words. Table not included in word count.
• Describe what and how you would teach each group to take them to the next level of phonics, spelling and vocabulary achievement, referring to the Words Their Way Developmental Stages and wider literature. Also, discuss why you would use the stated teaching and learning strategies in a scholarly justification, referring to the data and the research literature. Approx 1500-2000 words
Word allowance: 2,500 words maximum (not counting any tables). In text references and quotations are counted in the word count but end-text references are not counted.
See LMS for detailed rubric and further information.

Assignment 2: Unit of Work/Programme with Rationale (45 marks)
See the Assignment Descriptions, Rubrics and Templates in LMS for further clarification.
For this assignment, you will:
1. Develop an integrated overview for a Year 4 or 5 class for three weeks of teaching. The overview must link three learning areas, which must include English and Mathematics/Numeracy. A set of assessment data will be provided to help you plan appropriately for the diverse abilities and needs of the class. This part of the planning assignment details WHAT will be taught. *You will receive formative feedback on this part of the assignment in class. Use the template provided.
2. Plan a 3-week programme that is based on the overview. This programme will primarily be an English programme but needs to be integrated with at least one other Learning Area as reflected on the overview – for example, English, Mathematics and HASS, OR English and Mathematics OR English and HASS. Make sure you base planning decisions on the assessment data provided for a Year 4 or 5 class and show how you will differentiate for their needs. If you strongly prefer Year 3 or 6, please see me for alternative arrangements. This part of the planning assignment details HOW the teaching and learning, including assessment, will occur. Show how you will integrate Literacy as a General Capability in the learning area/s other than English. Your programme should incorporate approximately 25 hours of teaching in total  (including regular activities such as spelling and handwriting lessons) and must include a good balance of instruction in reading, writing, speaking and listening and viewing. It must be clear from the programme how the children will be scaffolded in their learning. Use the template provided.
The overview and programme components of this assignment may be done in pairs or groups of three. Because of the nature of the overview and programme, there is no set word count.
3. Write a brief rationale (of approximately 1,200 words) to justify your pedagogical decisions (including choices of resources), making references to theoretical and research literature as well as the assessment data you have been provided with. This part of the assignment details WHY you have made certain decisions. Each pre-service teacher must submit an original rationale (this component is not a group assignment) (15 marks).

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
