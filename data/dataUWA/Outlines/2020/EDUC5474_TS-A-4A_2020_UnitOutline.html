Academic Information

Content This unit develops students' understanding of how to teach Information and Communication Technology (ICT) at a secondary level, as well as how ICT can be integrated across a range of curricula. Topics include the Year 7-10 Digital Technologies curriculum (Digital Implementation, Digital Systems, Representation of Data, Collecting, Analysing and Managing Data, Digital Implementation, Creating Digital Solutions) as well as the Years 11 and 12 Computer Science and Applied Information Technology curriculum. Students are required to design learning activities, tutorials, assignments, lesson plans and programs that focus on core and advanced ICT skills and, in so doing, acquire or improve their own practical proficiency and general knowledge. This is a major curriculum unit that emphasises a flexible, adaptive approach to the development and teaching of Information and Communication Technology-based subjects while ensuring that all aspects related to learning outcomes, curriculum design, pedagogical practices, assessment and evaluation are considered fully. It is expected that students are proficient in the use of general application software, such as word processors, spreadsheets and the Adobe Suite, developed during the completion of an undergraduate degree and/or additional information technology-related courses or units and/or relevant practical experience, prior to enrolment in the unit.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assignment 1:  PRACTICAL ASSESSMENT AND MODERATION (30%)
APST Standard 2 – Know the content and how to teach it  
APST Standard 3 - Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning  
APST Standard 5 – Assess, provide feedback and report of student learning
In the first part of this assessment, students will create or modify an assessment task that would run for four weeks (8 lessons), for Year 9 Digital Technologies students, with a marking key and rationale. You need to assign them an activity to complete (it needs to be engaging topic, provide some choice and include some challenges), it is important to not only get students to demonstrate their skills in Spreadsheets AND/OR Databases but it must include the Design Process steps such as investigation, template development and annotation and evaluation. Remember that materials you give the students need to be appealing (e.g. fonts, pictures, space for name, etc). The curriculum points referring to data will be utilised for this task and are to be clearly identified in the task. 
Creating or modifying the assessment
Part 1 of this task will be an "investigation" style task, and Part 2 of this task will be a "validation" style task. The validation part of the component will be set as a test, approximately 30 minutes in length. The test is to be written with no practical component, however, you can include example spreadsheets for them to analyse and correct as well as empty grids for them to draw spreadsheets and include formatting annotations and written formulas. Ensure you clearly identify the requirements of the task you create.
Create the marking key
The marking key should have three main components (Design Process, Application, Independence/Attitude/Problem Solving) you will need to come up with a number of descriptors breaking the three topics down and allocate marks, the best way to do this is a three, two, one allocation or A, B, C for example. (see example provided on LMS). Please remember to reference rubrics heavily used from SCSA, or any other source. They also need to be specific to the task that you will create.
Provide a rationale
Accompanying your assessment, will be a rationale that explains how your assessment meets the Principles of Assessment from the WA Curriculum and Assessment outline; the purpose of the assessment; weighting of the assessment; weighting of the assessment; and work covered prior to students attempting the task (approximately 500 words).
Moderation Task
In the second part of this assessment, students will participate in a moderation activity with their peers using an assessment task and artificial work samples. Upon completing the moderation, students are to complete a 1000 word reflection on their experience. 
This assessment piece is worth 20% and the reflection on moderation is worth 10%. Please see LMS for more information, including the rubric. 
Assignment 2:  CURRICULUM AREA INVESTIGATION (Critical reflection alternative assessment for reflection on Professional Practice A) (30%)
APST Standard 2 – Know the content and how to teach it  
APST Standard 3 - Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning
In this assessment, you will identify and explore the pedagogies, principles and practices of teaching senior students in your specialisation area (in comparison to years 7-10).  
The assessment is based upon the question: What constitutes best practice in the learning and teaching of years 11 and 12 in your major or minor curriculum area (depending upon your enrolment in the unit).
In answering the question, you may wish to concentrate upon a specific area such as the ATAR stream, General Education, Foundation Education, Vocational Education or International Baccalaureate. You might also consider the importance of catering for the  diverse needs of learners at year 11 and 12 level including indigenous, EAL/D, special needs and Gifted and Talented students. 
This assessment will engage you in investigation, research and examination of best practice, which is an important aspect of your professional career. To do so, read widely in the professional, empirical literature and investigate what is presented to teachers through federal and state organisations, such as the Western Australian School Curriculum and Standards Authority and the New South Wales Board of Studies. Do also investigate state based Department of Education sites which can have many interesting and relevant resources. You are also encouraged to refer to the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers and policy documents. 
Please ensure that you highlight the demands of senior school and points of difference between upper and middle school. You may wish to use specific examples and draw upon policy statements. 
The assessment needs to be 1,800 – 2,000 words long.
Criteria:
•	Breadth of literature, research, teaching practice and policy statements demonstrated in at least five rich resources;
•	Detailed explanation of the trends, challenges and opportunities of senior education in your curriculum area;
•	Clear explanation of what constitutes best practice, drawing upon understanding of upper school pedagogy, the demands of upper school and post-secondary pathways; 
•	Clear description of best practice in an aspect of curriculum area, using at least one example;
•	Articulate and professional expression, including APA referencing conventions used throughout the text and in the Reference List. 
This assessment piece is worth 30%. Please see LMS for more information, including the rubric.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
