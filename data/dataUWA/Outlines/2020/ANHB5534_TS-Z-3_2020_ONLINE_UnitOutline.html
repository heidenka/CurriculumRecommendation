Academic Information

Content This unit is to be delivered in six online modules. Students learn about assessment of medical and dental conditions as well as relevant imaging and other techniques to screen patients referred for treatment of sleep disordered breathing. This includes the use of lateral cephalometry and panoramic radiographs. The use of cone beam volumetric tomography and magnetic resonance imaging and evidence supporting their specific uses in sleep apnoea cases are learning outcomes of the unit. At the completion of the unit students have the skills to use evidence-based predictors of treatment outcomes. The unit focuses on discussions of history, examination, clinical tests and treatment planning for a series of cases. A templated approach to development of diagnostic skills and selection of treatment option alternatives are presented for students.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The majority of this unit has been designed with flexible delivery in mind.  It consists of six separate modules.  You are able to work through each module and begin the next module at your own pace throughout the duration of the semester. At the end of each module you must complete an online quiz and/or short written assessment before you can move on to the next module.  All quizzes and written assessment submissions are accessed via links in Blackboard. Module quiz scores will be available to you immediately after completing each quiz. Written assessment marks may take longer to receive due to manual marking by a unit coordinator.
It is recommended that students work through the unit at a pace of 1 module per fortnight if doing only 1 unit or 1 module every week if doing 2 units in a semester.

Unit Structure

This unit runs in non- standard teaching periods (NSTP) semesters Z3 and Z5 and runs for a duration of 12 weeks. The unit is presented in a self paced online format where students are required to complete all unit content and assessments by a 12 week assessment deadline. All unit content and assessments are delivered via the University's learning management system (LMS). The unit comprises of 6 modules, each consisting of between 2-6 lectures as well as a short written assessment and/or online quiz at the end of each module. The unit adopts a continuous mode of assessment delivery whereby students are required to complete the previous module's assessment before being able to proceed to further modules.Two final case reports replacing a typical final exam will be due TWO WEEKS prior to the usual semester end deadline date. Students would have also had 6-12 months lead up time sourcing and preparing these case reports for unit submission. Final grades will not be released until formally released by the University.

Additional information
Students are required to choose a mentor within the first 6 months of commencing the course to help prepare and complete final case assessments for this unit and Advanced Competency in Dental Sleep Medicine (ANHB5535). This mentor will either be a referring sleep physician, general medical practitioner or ENT surgeon with an established interest in sleep. Mentors will not only play a role in helping source patients for case assessments, but will also serve as an assessor for final case submissions for unit ANHB5535. Unit coordinators must be contacted once mentor selection has taken place so communication can be made with the mentor to outline their requirements as a mentor. Unit coordinators should also be contacted if students have trouble sourcing an appropriate mentor for this course requirement.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
