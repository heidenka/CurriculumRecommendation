Academic Information

Content This unit gives an overview of Languages Other Than English (LOTE) in the secondary school curriculum. Principles of, and developments in, language teaching and learning will be discussed, along with the development of micro and macro skills in listening and speaking. This unit also covers the use of teaching aids, including the use of information and communication technology (ICT) resources, text evaluation, student assessment, lesson planning and teaching strategies. Students also reflect on their first school experience through an analysis of lesson segments.

EDUC5471 - Languages Other Than English (LOTE) Curriculum II - TS-A-4A-2020 - 2020 - Face to face / Blended learning

Unit Information

Content
This unit gives an overview of Languages Other Than English (LOTE) in the senior secondary school curriculum. Principles of, and developments in, language teaching and learning will be discussed, along with the development of micro and macro skills in listening and speaking. This unit also covers the use of teaching aids, including the use of information and communication technology (ICT) resources, text evaluation, student assessment, lesson planning and teaching strategies. Students also reflect on their first school experience. 
Learning Outcomes
Students are able to: (1) demonstrate an awareness of the developmental and diverse learning needs of adolescent learners in LOTE; (2) display a repertoire of teaching and learning strategies relevant to the senior secondary LOTE curriculum; (3) display a deep understanding of the current target language curriculum for Years 11 to 12; (4) plan and evaluate varied instruction for effective and engaging learning; (5) critically evaluate and develop instructional resources for secondary LOTE, including a range of educational technologies; (6) apply a range of assessment and evaluation processes and strategies; (7) critically and positively reflect upon own teaching practice; and (8) research established and new theories of learning and best practice as a way to improve own performance.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
ASSIGNMENT ONE – CURRICULUM AREA INVESTIGATION (Critical Reflection Alternative Assessment) (20%)
APST Standard 1 – Know students and how they learn
APST Standard 2 – Know content and how to teach it
APST Standard 3 – Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning
APST Standard 4 – Create and maintain supportive and safe learning environments
APST Standard 5 – Assess, provide feedback and report on student learning
APST Standard 6 – Engage in professional learning
APST Standard 7 – Engage professionally with colleagues, parents/carers and the community
In this assessment, you will identify and explore the pedagogies, principles and practices of teaching senior students in your specialisation area (in comparison to years 7-10).  
The assessment is based upon the question: What constitutes best practice in the learning and teaching of years 11 and 12 in your major or minor curriculum area (depending upon your enrolment in the unit)? 
In answering the question, you may wish to concentrate upon a specific area such as the ATAR stream, General Education, Foundation Education, Vocational Education or International Baccalaureate. You might also consider the importance of catering for the  diverse needs of learners at year 11 and 12 level including indigenous, EAL/D, special needs and Gifted and Talented students. 
This assessment will engage you in investigation, research and examination of best practice, which is an important aspect of your professional career. To do so, read widely in the professional, empirical literature and investigate what is presented to teachers through federal and state organisations, such as the Western Australian School Curriculum and Standards Authority and the New South Wales Board of Studies. Do also investigate state based Department of Education sites which can have many interesting and relevant resources. You are also encouraged to refer to the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers and policy documents. 
Please ensure that you highlight the demands of senior school and points of difference between upper and middle school. You may wish to use specific examples and draw upon policy statements. 
The assessment needs to be 1,800 words long. 
Criteria
•	Breadth of literature, research, teaching practice and policy statements demonstrated in at least five rich resources;
•	Detailed explanation of the trends, challenges and opportunities of senior education in your curriculum area;
•	Clear explanation of what constitutes best practice, drawing upon understanding of upper school pedagogy, the demands of upper school and post-secondary pathways; 
•	Clear description of best practice in an aspect of curriculum area, using at least one example;
•	Articulate and professional expression, including APA referencing conventions used throughout the text and in the Reference List. 
Submission Requirements
This assessment is due on 14 August 2020.  It is to be submitted electronically via LMS by 11.59 pm on the due date.
ASSIGNMENT TWO – PREPARATION OF A SENIOR SCHOOL TEACHING PROGRAM (60%)
APST Standard 1 – Know students and how they learn  
APST Standard 2 – Know content and how to teach it  
APST Standard 3 – Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning  
APST Standard 5 – Assess, provide feedback and report on student learning
You are to plan a Teaching/Learning Program of three weeks duration (12 one-hour lessons) for ONE UNIT from your ATAR LOTE course for Year 11 or 12 (a year-long program is made up of two units – one per semester).
Task
You will need to attend to the following items:
•	Decide upon the unit to be developed in a detailed program.  You will only develop the program of work for part of one unit.  For example, you may choose Year 11 ATAR LOTE but you will only develop the program for Unit 2 (3 weeks).  
•	Create an Assessment Outline for the unit chosen for programming, as per SCSA documentation.  This Assessment Outline must be included with your program of work. 
•	Allocate the assessments, and teaching and learning weeks for the unit you will develop.  You are responsible for ONE assessment task (not the exam), and the teaching and learning activities leading to the specific assessment task.  Please Note: this is to be a PROGRAM OF WORK ONLY and not the lesson plans. 
•	Include a detailed marking key or rubric for the one assessment task.
•	Include the teaching strategies for each lesson to develop the students’ skills and understandings necessary for the assessment task.
•	Resources for each lesson – this will vary depending upon the resource and the number of lessons it relates to, but a minimum total would be 10-12 resources.  This should include teacher and student resources (such as PowerPoints and notes).
Process
There will be an initial class discussion around the planning of the three-week senior school Teaching/Learning Program.  Some class time will be allocated for you to engage in dialogue with your peers/other LOTE students and to commence planning your program.  In particular, you could liaise with peers and your Lecturer for guidance with your assessment development and allocated assessment task. 
Submission Requirements
You will be required to submit the following to LMS:
•	An overview of your unit program: rationale and its connection to the rest of the unit (300 to 500 words).
•	The Assessment Outline for the programmed unit.
•	The three-week Program (including assessment tasks, with a marking key or rubric; teaching and learning activities, and resources), using an acceptable/standard programming format.  ALL sources must be acknowledged individually as well as being part of your reference list.
•	An individual reflection of 300 to 500 words about what you have learned about curriculum development processes, including (but not limited to) interpreting documents, and the unique requirements of Year 11 and 12 courses.  The strongest reflections will refer to professional reading, especially peer-reviewed literature.
•	A reference list using APA referencing conventions.
This assessment is due on 23 October 2020.  It is to be submitted electronically via LMS by 11.59 pm on the due date.  Please note that, by clicking submit you certify that the attached work submitted for assessment is your own and that all material drawn from other sources has been fully acknowledged.  You also acknowledge that you retain a copy of any submitted work.  A cover sheet does not need to be attached to this assessment.
ASSIGNMENT THREE – ASSESSMENT AND MODERATION TASK (20%)
APST Standard 1 – Know students and how they learn
APST Standard 5 – Assess, provide feedback and report on student learning
APST Standard 6 – Engage in professional learning
APTS Standard 7 – Engage professionally with colleagues, parents/carers and the community
You will mark a number of student work samples in class, using the SCSA marking rubrics.  A short written reflection will be completed out of class time.  
Task
Working in small groups with a task sheet, marking criteria (or rubric) and work samples, you will spend one hour (in class) discussing the standard of the work samples in order to reach consensus about the marks/grades to be awarded to them.  These marks will be discussed in class.
From this experience, you will write 1,000 words addressing the following question: From this class exercise, what have you learned about moderation and assessment?  You should consider; a) the purpose and process of assessment and moderation; b) characteristics of effective assessment practices; c) collaboration and reaching agreement (decision-making processes); d) issues arising from this moderation experience; e) the implications for your future professional development; f) considerations based on your engagement with the empirical research (evidence-based reflection).  Please use at least four sources which may include textbooks and may be drawn from the Reading List.
Submission Requirements
Your reflection is due on 6 November 2020.  It is to be submitted electronically via LMS by 11.59 pm on the due date.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
