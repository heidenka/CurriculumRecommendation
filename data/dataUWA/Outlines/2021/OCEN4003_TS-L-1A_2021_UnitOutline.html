Academic Information

Content This unit provides an introduction to strategic leadership and innovation in the context of the oceans. In particular it explores the concepts of leadership and strategy. Students explore how strategy is used within organisations and the strategic planning process. The concept of entrepreneurship is critical in identifying solutions to ocean challenges, and it is explored in terms of what it means and what is it useful for. Understanding the behaviour of individuals and groups within an organisation and their interaction is also an important element of strategic leadership. This is analysed as well as—how managers lead strategic planning and manage strategic vision and change; how innovation can be encouraged and developed and the policy issues associated with this; the differences and similarities between strategic leadership in the private and public spheres; and the interplay of organisations in the ocean context.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit has been designed as a "hands on" learning program in which students work within teams to undertake a group project that will require a multi-disciplinary approach to research and analysis of complex problems. Students will be expected to have undertaken the reading and preparation for the workshops before coming to the classes. There will also be a requirement and an expectation that students will maintain contact with each other in-between the classroom workshops to successfully complete the learning tasks and assignments. A period of approximately 2 weeks has been provided between each teaching workshop block to enable sufficient time to undertake the assignments.
Learning Outcomes
Students can:
1) demonstrate a basic knowledge of strategic leadership, planning, management, and innovation; 
2) display a sophisticated understanding of strategic leadership in the public and private spheres, and how the intersection between these spheres is especially important in the ocean context; 
3) identify and critically assess how an organisation can move forward with strategic leadership, including working with relevant stakeholders to plan innovative strategies for organisational development; 
4) demonstrate an awareness of self and others in leadership; and 
5) demonstrate an appreciation of environmental forces within and outside of an organisation that play a part in its strategic development.

Unit Structure

This is an intensive unit that runs 9:00am - 5:00pm on the dates listed below in the Unit Schedule. Please check the timetable on the UWA website.
Indicative assessments in this unit are as follows: (1) in-class assessment; (2) individual assignment; and (3) group project. Further information is available in the unit outline. These assessments will be distributed as follows:
1) In-class assessment – an individual presentation and supporting notes to the class discussing the student’s own learning journey in relation to the unit and how they feel it has benefitted their understanding of their own leadership and enterprise abilities and how these apply to strategy. Total marks 20%.
2) Group project - this will be a single project focusing on the detailed analysis of a case study that will allow the students to apply the theory and concepts taught in the class. It will be undertaken in two parts. 
Part A will examine the macro environmental strategic context in which the case study firm has and is operating. It will require students to undertake background research of the company and assess its overall corporate strategy and prospects. This Part A component will be allocated 15% of the total 30 marks for the group project. 
Part B will examine a specific area within the case study firm's technology-assets portfolio and undertake a strategic analysis applying strategic management concepts, business model innovation and critical analysis skills. This Part B component will be allocated 15% of the total 30 marks for the group project.
3) Individual assignment - this assignment is also divided into two parts of equal weighting comprising the overall 50 marks. The individual assessment comprises the preparation of two research papers that demonstrate the student's ability to read, understand and apply the theories and concepts taught in the unit via a written assignment.
Part A will focus on the nature of strategic leadership in organisations, with reference to industries that are involved in the marine environment and the impact of the Blue Economy. Students will examine the challenges facing managers, directors, and other business leaders in developing successful strategies within the context of complex, multi-stakeholder environments within a maritime context as highlighted in the Blue Economy concept. This Part A component will be allocated 25% of the total 50 marks for the individual assignment.
Part B will focus on the role of innovation strategy and business model innovation within organisations seeking to maintain competitive advantage in highly uncertain and dynamic environments. Students will research the use of technological innovation to address challenges within maritime environments. They will examine these in the context of the Blue Economy concept. This Part B component will be allocated 25% of the total 50 marks for the individual assignment. 
Supplementary assessment is only available in this unit in the case of a student who has obtained a mark of 45 to 49 and is currently enrolled in this unit, and it is the only remaining unit that the student must pass to complete their course.

It is important that the referencing of any sources used in your written work is done properly, if only to substantiate the points you are making in your assignment or project. The APA style is the preferred used unless otherwise stated by the unit coordinator. There are some notes for guidance which have been prepared by the library staff: ‘APA citation style: Introduction' see: https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa (https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa).

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
