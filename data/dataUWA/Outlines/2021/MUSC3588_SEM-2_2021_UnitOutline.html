Academic Information

Content This practical unit extends MUSC3587 Studio Teaching 1 for students within an instrumental or voice specialisation. Students maintain their intermediate performance skills from Level 2 units, and develop a comprehensive portfolio comprising a studio teaching model, lesson plans, personal resource materials, professional documentation (e.g. financial, insurances, student/parent information sheets), and specific teaching tools and techniques. Students continue to expand their knowledge of theories of teaching and pedagogies, receive practical immersion and discussion with a highly-experienced professional instructor regarding pedagogy, are mentored by the Chair of their specialisation area, and undertake a placement with an instrumental teacher within a school or private studio to observe and participate in instructing other students in one-to-one teaching.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is delivered by a combination of teaching and learning practices:
1. Lectures: Each student will watch a series of online lectures covering basic topics in Instrumental/Singing Teaching. 
2. Discussion Group: each student attends the biweekly Discussion group which allows them to reflect on, and discuss the content of the online lectures
3. Performance: One-to-one lessons: each student in will be assigned a one-to-one tutor and complete 6 hours of instruction per semester, allocated in consultation with your tutor
4. Performance: Workshops/Concert Practice/Repertoire Classes: provide opportunities for students to develop their practical work/acquire performance experience with input from peers and staff. 
5. Teaching: each student will teach a colleague in the class on their own instrument in a studio-based setting. 
6. Pedagogy: each student will be allocated 2-hours with their Chair of Performance to discuss pedagogy and teaching strategies and reflect on their teaching practice as outlined in (5) above.
7. Student’s learning: is supported through LMS, which serves as a resource for course materials, and as a platform for student-student and student-lecturer/tutor online interaction
8. Self-directed study: enables students to acquire knowledge and skills independently and autonomously, whilst taking responsibility for the quantity and quality of their learning.

Participation

Students are expected to attend all examinations, tests, assessments, lessons, and seminars for which they are enrolled, and to remain for the full duration of each activity. Active participation in the discussion sessions is important for all students. If a student is unable to attend a class or activity due to illness or other emergent personal circumstances , they must notify the unit lecturer/tutor (in writing) at least 24 hours in advance, with a medical certificate or other statutory documentation supporting the absence. Students with unsatisfactory attendance/participation may not have their work assessed and this will result in the award of a zero mark for the unit.
Attendance Requirements: Instrumental / Vocal Lessons For all practical studies units, individual composition, instrumental, and vocal lessons are organised between student and teacher/tutor. If you are unable to attend a lesson, notice must be given directly to your tutor at least 24 hours prior to the scheduled lesson time, or the lesson will be considered forfeited. In the case of sudden illness or emergency, notice must be given to both the tutor and Conservatorium Office at the earliest possible time. As a courtesy to your tutor, you should make every possible effort to attend all lessons as scheduled; changes/cancellations to your scheduled times should only be made where absolutely essential. Composition students: note that attendance at the weekly Composition Class/Seminar is mandatory.
Attendance Requirements: Concerts / Workshops / Concert Practice / Collaborative Music
Concerts (5%): It is expected that all music students will make every effort to attend as many concerts as possible, but attendance at 10 or more scheduled Conservatorium concerts (lunchtime, Mainstage etc) is mandatory. All students will receive free admission to Conservatorium of Music concerts. For each concert attended, students receive 5% of the participation component to a maximum of 5 percentage points for the unit.
Workshops (5%): Each performance area will designate workshops/concert practice sessions that meet regularly (usually weekly). For each workshop/concert practice session attended, students can receive up to 5% of the participation component to a maximum of 5 percentage points for the unit.
Collaborative Music (10%): Each performance area will allocate students to a collaborative music ensemble for rehearsal throughout the semester.
Applying for Leave Students must seek permission from the Head of Conservatorium before participating in external activities that are likely to prevent full attendance at classes and/or ensembles. In such cases a leave of absence may be granted for certain external engagements or activities, provided that such absence is assessed as being likely to make a contribution to a student’s overall studies. The support of a student’s lecturer/tutor is required in all cases. Applications for a leave of absence must be submitted to the Head of Conservatorium in writing. Application forms are available at www.student.uwa.edu.au/course/forms. Non-compliance with these regulations may result in failing the unit in question or a penalty in the final assessment.

Hearing protection
Listening to and performing music at high decibel levels can pose a risk to your hearing health. Cumulative and/or prolonged exposure to moderately high decibel levels, or short exposure to very high decibel levels, can adversely affect and permanently damage your hearing. The maximum average safe level of exposure time and sound level per day for ALL sound exposure including your music activities is 85dB for 8 hours—the level equivalent, for example, to playing or listening to unamplified chamber music at close range. Listening to recorded music at high volume (for example using an iPod with headphones) can contribute significantly to risk levels in combination with exposure to live music practice, rehearsal and performance. Students in Music performance units are advised to take precautions to protect their hearing, including the use of musicians’ earplugs when exposed to high decibel levels. Musicians’ earplugs have been shown to effectively filter excessive noise and thereby reduce the risk of hearing damage and are now widely used and promoted in the music industry. It is expected that students who will be regularly exposed to high decibel levels in music practice, rehearsal and performance in Conservatorium units and performance activities will take necessary precautions to protect their hearing. The Conservatorium of Music will provide, free of charge, a single pair of generic musicians’ earplugs per student per year to students enrolled in a performance unit. Students can collect their free musicians earplugs from the Conservatorium Office. Students with consistently high levels of exposure may need to contact a professional audiologist for advice concerning earplugs appropriate for their specific needs, to ensure the maximum musical and health benefit. Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
