Academic Information

Content This unit is for pre-service teachers who plan to teach secondary school English. It provides a theoretical framework for teaching, learning and assessment. Familiarity is developed with current curriculum documents. Through active participation in workshops, students develop an understanding of strategies for effective teaching and learning, skills for planning and programming, development of instructional materials and resources and develop an understanding of issues relevant to English teaching.

Unit Information

In the first teaching period, you developed understandings about the English Learning Area and fundamental issues such as literacy, pedagogy and assessment. You gained an understanding of the Western Australian Curriculum: English for lower secondary and the interrelated Language, Literature and Literacy strands and learned about a range of teaching strategies and the skills for planning learning experiences for students. Finally, you developed an understanding of assessment.

In this unit, the focus is on reflecting on the understandings, skills and knowledge that you have already gained, and then consolidating and extending these. This unit will cover new understandings about  English, General English, Foundation English and Literature in Years 11 and 12. We will consider English teaching pedagogies, contemporary views on teaching reading and writing for upper school students, technology in the classroom, the use and preparation of learning and teaching resources and successful assessment.  The assignments are planned to draw upon your teaching practice experiences, prepare you for future practicums and link to the content of other education units.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is delivered in interactive learning sessions with an online component. The unit is based on interactive learning workshops which includes presentations, collaborative problem solving and student based professional development. These approaches will help you to develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the workshops and required readings and will you the opportunity to practise your teaching and presentation skills. Active learning underpins and you will have the opportunity to draw upon your prior learning and knowledge in practical workshops.

Participation

It is strongly recommended that students attend all lectures and workshops and that there is an expectation that students attend up to 80% of lectures and workshops. Please contact your unit coordinator or tutor prior to a workshop if you are unable to attend. Beginning teachers are often judged on their professionalism. Given the importance of setting and maintaining high standards of professional conduct and commitment throughout your career, this assessment item reflects your recognition of the importance of these standards in your pre-service year. There are two ways in which you can participate in the unit: 

a) Group presentation of one of the weekly topics. You are encouraged to work in a group of three, speak for no more than 15 minutes (as a group, not individually) and prepare a one page summary (7%); b) three reflections – these are worth 1% each. Each week you will be given a topic for reflection and are encouraged to write 200 – 250 words (three entries only). These entries are designed to be largely informal and to engage your colleagues.

Assessments tasks for the unit
 Assessment One: Curriculum area investigation  (40%)
Curriculum area investigation
In this assessment, you will identify and explore the pedagogies, principles and practices of teaching senior students in your specialisation area (in comparison to years 7-10).  
The assessment is based upon the question: What constitutes best practice in the learning and teaching of years 11 and 12 in your major or minor curriculum area (depending upon your enrolment in the unit)? 
In answering the question, you may wish to concentrate upon a specific area such as the ATAR stream, General Education, Foundation Education, Vocational Education or International Baccalaureate. You might also consider the importance of catering for the  diverse needs of learners at year 11 and 12 level including indigenous, EAL/D, special needs and Gifted and Talented students. 
This assessment will engage you in investigation, research and examination of best practice, which is an important aspect of your professional career. To do so, read widely in the professional, empirical literature and investigate what is presented to teachers through federal and state organisations, such as the Western Australian School Curriculum and Standards Authority and the New South Wales Board of Studies. Do also investigate state based Department of Education sites which can have many interesting and relevant resources. You are also encouraged to refer to the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers and policy documents. 
Please ensure that you highlight the demands of senior school and points of difference between upper and middle school. You may wish to use specific examples and draw upon policy statements. 
The assessment needs to be 1,500 - 2,000 words long.  
 Assessment two: Senior English Curriculum Development (60%)
 A Begin by working in a group of 4 or 5, where you will consider one unit for ATAR English, General English, Literature ATAR or Literature General.  This is a strategy to determine how your unit might be structured and does not contribute to the assessment.  Your unit must: Be outlined in a two week programme of no fewer than eight lessons. Please do not include inclass assessments, individual working periods as part of the eight lessons. Each lesson needs to include strategies and activities. 

Learning Programme with: Curriculum links; Learning outcomes or learning intentions; Teaching and learning strategies (outline learning strategies and activities in sufficient detail for the program to be enacted by another teacher); Teaching points (elaborations of content); Resources (ten pages of resources or equivalent); An assessment task and marking key; Incorporation of text(s); All materials and resources other than those produced by you must be referenced. 

Texts must be appropriate to age and syllabus requirements to be included in the unit, and chosen from: A significant text; A text that is part of a series; A multimodal text; A prescribed text from the Literature syllabus; A collection of shorter contemporary texts (such as a collection of blogs, opinionative articles, images on a contemporary issue); Extended media texts such as the TV drama; Texts for production such as creative writing, persuasive visual and written texts and interpretative texts. 

You will submit: An overview of your part of the unit segment: context, rationale (including how this unit relates to adolescent social, emotional, cognitive, physical development) and the secondary English discipline (please use references – you may select sources from the unit Reading List, English Curriculum 1 Reading List, the textbook and DTL). 700 words; Your programme - four pages at least of program; Individual reflection – 500 word statement about the experience (you may wish to refer to the literature here as well). 

Your will present: an overview of your unit segment to the group on November 11 – please speak for 5-7 minutes only; a one page summary of your unit (you will have plenty of material from your program to form the basis of this handout) that your colleagues can add to their resource bank.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
