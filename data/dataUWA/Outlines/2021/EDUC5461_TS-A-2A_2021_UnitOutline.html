Assignment Details
ASSIGNMENT ONE – LESSON PLANNING (30%) 
APST Standards 1-5 
You are required to develop 3 consecutive lesson plans for a year 7, 8, 9 or 10 Language class, each of approximately one-hour in length. You will be provided with a preferred format. 
Please include in your lesson plans: a) organisational details (Year level, topic and focus) b) a brief outline of where the lessons might fit in a program e.g. first lesson of a new topic; c)   links to the curriculum. These include (where applicable) content descriptors, skills, concepts, general capabilities, achievement standards and cross-curriculum priorities, where applicable; d)   specific objectives, written in behavioural terms and derived from the Curriculum Links to provide a focus for each lesson; e) the prior knowledge and skills expected of the students; f)  the materials and resources required, including PowerPoints, (and when and how they will be distributed); g)  a detailed series of lesson steps showing the sequence of input, activities, explanations and instructions, including an introduction/motivation, and conclusion; h) a copy of any content notes you would need for teacher input on an attached sheet; i) a list of resources referred to in the lesson plans along with copies of all the material such as worksheets, maps, diagrams or cartoons that  you will distribute to students during the three lessons. Resources must be presented in a professional manner ready for use in the classroom and their origin clearly referenced; j)  estimated timing of lesson steps; k) how the objectives are to be assessed – this relates to the learning activities; l) space for evaluative and reflective comments. 
Your lesson plans must contain all of the following: a) organisational details (Year level, topic, etc.); b) title or lesson focus; c) links to the Western Australian Curriculum; d) specific objectives that help to implement the curriculum; e) an introduction; f) a motivational activity/task; g) sequence of learning activities that engage students in active learning tasks; h) conclusion; i) timing; j) a list of resources prepared, selected and/or adapted resources, k) questions to stimulate reflection on the lesson.
The lesson must be a) presented in a functional format, containing all the necessary information; b) There must be a professional standard of presentation (including accurate language, grammar, punctuation and headings); c) Be referenced correctly using APA style; d) Links to relevant curriculum documents are clearly identified; e) Objectives are clearly expressed in student behavioural terms; f) Objectives are directly linked to learning activities and resources.  
ASSIGNMENT TWO – PREPARATION OF A TEACHING PROGRAM (50%)
APST Standards 1-5 
You are required to prepare a program of work based on the Western Australian Curriculum, for a year 7-10 class. The program should be for approximately 8 hours, or 2 weeks with 4 x 60 minute lessons per week.  A program is a document that outlines the teaching and learning that is planned for a period of between 2 weeks to a whole term. It provides direction and a time frame to assist with the planning of daily lessons.  Programs are written firstly for the teacher, but are important documents of accountability that may be presented to Heads of Learning Areas and other senior staff, the School Curriculum and Standards Authority, parents and students. Your program conveys your philosophy and approaches to teaching and learning, to students and assessment.
You will be provided with a school and class context on which to base your program development or you may use the school and a class you are working with during your observation. 
Your assignment should include: a) a description of the context for which the program has been constructed – the school context and the particular class – school type, SES, number of students, gender, ability level etc); b) a personal philosophy, outlining your core values, pedagogical orientation both generally and in relation to Languages Education (300-500 words – please use references); c) a rationale to justify the choice of theme, topic, text, etc. (200-300 words – please use references), and how this choice relates to adolescent development (social, emotional, cognitive, physical); d) links to the Western Australian Curriculum; e) specific objectives for student achievement; f) learning activities in sufficient detail for the program to be enacted by another teacher. (Consider including key questions or issues where discussions are involved). Learning activities should incorporate literacy and numeracy strategies, and evidence of the use of ICT to support and enhance learning experiences; g) a list of resources to be used by students and teacher; h) texts, including visual texts, (appropriate to age and syllabus requirements); i) assessment tasks, including the assessment criteria to be used; j) questions to focus future evaluative comments and reflections; and k) approximate timing – number of hours or number of periods.
Remember that this assignment is part of your own learning process. It requires considerable time to locate resources, think through the issues, organise and develop your ideas. You are likely to find it challenging. Please ask me for assistance as you work through this assignment.
ASSIGNMENT THREE – MICRO TEACHING (20%)
APST Standards 1-5
You will deliver 7-10 minutes of a lesson and may build upon the lesson plans prepared for the previous assessment. This means that you may create a lesson segment to take place before or after your segment of three.  Please create an original lesson segment plan. The lesson segment should focus on a concept from the ‘middle school curriculum’; that is, Years 7-10, and may be a lesson aspect such as a general introduction, activity opening, introduction to group work or a lesson evaluation. You will work in small groups and wherever possible your segment will be recorded for your use only. Please take advantage of the opportunity for formative feedback. 
Details
The purpose of this micro teaching activity and assessment is to give you the opportunity for some hands on practical experience in language teaching before heading off on your first practicum.  You will deliver a 7-10 minute microteaching session in groups of 4-5. 
Select an aspect of teaching ‘middle school’ languages. You may build upon an aspect of your lesson plans or you can create something entirely new. 
You need to provide a 30 second explanation of your micro teaching session before beginning with details about: a) The year level; b) The topic; c) The ability level, if relevant; d) The topics/activities that need to be covered. 
After you teach, your colleagues will provide constructive feedback on your micro teaching by describing and evaluating what worked for them and ways in which the teaching could be improved. Micro teaching provides a safe and valuable set of experiences for learning to teach. Watching others teach can provide a springboard for discussion and reflection. 
You must bring all materials you need, including copies of pictures or text. You can expect your learners to have paper and pencil, but you should be able to provide everything else you need for your lesson. Please submit your plan and resources on LMS. 
Reflection
After you co-teach your lesson, you are encouraged to write a 500-word reflection. Your reflection should discuss the following: (1) A description of how you developed your lesson. Give us a sense of what you found hard and what you found easy in designing this lesson. What surprised you? What did you learn?; (2) The thoughts, feelings and emotions you had while you were teaching, and their impact upon the way in which you related to your students; (3) Explain your successes and what worked well; (4) Things that happened during the lesson that stayed with you. What about if the lesson was still in your mind the next day? Of what significance is this?; (5) Plans for the future – what do you need to work on in the future? What have you learned from your course, such as from your Languages Education Curriculum and other studies that might help you in the future?

Other

Pre-service teachers are not permitted to take photographs or videos of students in schools under any circumstances except when collecting evidence specifically for the AfGT during the final professional practice, where strict guidelines apply. Nor are pre-service teachers permitted to take copies of children’s work for use in assignments or the e-portfolio except where formal permission has been secured. However, it is permissible to take photographs of your own work, such as classroom displays and resources. It is permissible to video yourself but no students should be captured in the video.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
