Academic Information

Content Students explore a range of computing and mobile technologies, including web 2.0 tools, social media and mobile apps, which are relevant to secondary teaching, with a particular focus on relevant pedagogical approaches, and embedding ICTs across the curriculum. Students also explore the use of new technologies for the advancement of teacher competencies and educational networking. Links are made to the Australian Curriculum and in particular the third of the seven General Capabilities in the Australian Curriculum (ACARA), namely the 'Information and communication technology (ICT) capability'; the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (AITSL), with particular reference to the ICT Elaborations for Graduate Teacher Standards (AITSL); and, where relevant, the ‘Digital Technologies' and ‘Design and Technologies' subjects (ACARA).

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit runs fully online. Each week, students will view and reflect on asynchronous lecture materials; attend a compulsory 1-hour synchronous tutorial; and complete work on a section of their individual e-portfolio. 

Unit Structure

There are 9 weeks in total (plus an optional 10th week). Apart from the 1-hour scheduled synchronous tutorial, students are able to complete work at any time during a given week. All work should be completed by 5pm on a Friday.

WRITTEN LITERACY REQUIREMENTS
In order to pass the assignments in this unit, all written work must be of a satisfactory standard in terms of written literacy and referencing.  Written texts should be appropriately structured and clear in meaning.  Correct and appropriate grammar, vocabulary and spelling should be used throughout. Referencing should conform to APA style.  Written literacy and referencing expectations will normally be incorporated into assignment rubrics.
ASSIGNMENTS
ASSIGNMENT ONE (Tasks 1A-1C) –  Demonstration of the use of web 2.0/apps to support teaching and learning (50%)
APST Standards 2, 3 & 4
Key focus: you should demonstrate your ability to use ICTs in effective ways to support your teaching and your students’ learning.
In this task you will construct a webpage (one page of your e-portfolio) for each of the following three ICT-related focus areas: Focus Area 2.6 (Information and Communication Technology), Focus Area 3.4 (Select and use resources), and Focus Area 4.5 (Use ICT safely, responsibly and ethically). In each case you should demonstrate your accomplishment of at least the Graduate Level of the focus area, which includes briefly showing your knowledge of relevant theory, critical literature and/or curriculum documents; describing examples of your accomplishment in text; and providing evidence of your accomplishment in text and/or through supporting multimedia artefacts. You may write up to 500 words per focus area, and include as many multimedia artefacts as you wish (though beware of overpowering your text with too many images, videos or widgets). 
ASSIGNMENT TWO (Task 2) –  Construction and representation of a personal learning network (PLN) (30%)
APST Standards 6 & 7
Key focus: you should demonstrate your ability to use your PLN to support your ongoing professional development over time.
In this task you will construct a webpage (one page of your e-portfolio) connected to either Standard 6 or Standard 7, containing a visualisation (e.g., a mindmap) & description of your PLN, which should contain links to both people and resources, and may include face-to-face contacts and analogue resources alongside digital resources. You should ensure that you use a minimum of three digital platforms or tools within your PLN. It is recommended that you include professional organisations such as AATE, ALEA and/or MAWA. In your description of your PLN, explain how you created it; how it has impacted your learning, and ultimately your classroom practice and your students’ learning; how you have used it to contribute to the learning of your peers/colleagues and how this may have impacted their classroom practice and their students’ learning; and how you plan to consolidate & expand it in the future. You may write up to 500 words in addition to your PLN visualisation, and you may include as many supplementary  multimedia artefacts as you wish. 
ASSIGNMENT 3 (Task 3) –  Presentation of an e-portfolio (20%)
APST Standards: All
Key focus: You should demonstrate your ability to present yourself effectively in a multimodal digital text, with your message tailored appropriately to the intended target audience of potential employers.
In this task you will construct a webpage (one page of your e-portfolio) for each of the following: Homepage/Self-Introduction, Teaching Philosophy, Standard 1 (choice of any one focus area), and Standard 5 (choice of any one focus area). Your Homepage should incorporate an introduction to, and overview of, your e-portfolio, and an introduction to your ‘professional self’, which gives a sense of your background and what skills and experiences you bring to the teaching profession. Your Teaching Philosophy page should communicate the key elements of your teaching philosophy and present your pedagogical approach, with reference to relevant Australian Curriculum documents, and should indicate important generic skills you have developed. You may also make brief references to your teaching experience to date. Your Standard 1 and Standard 5 pages should demonstrate your accomplishment of at least the Graduate Level of your chosen focus areas. In each case, you may write up to 500 words. You may include as many multimedia artefacts as you wish. 

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
