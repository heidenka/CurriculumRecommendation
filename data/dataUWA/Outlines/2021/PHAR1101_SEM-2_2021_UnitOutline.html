Unit Information

This unique Elective Unit will show you how pharmaceutical innovation – how we produce new medicines in response to perceived needs – has enormously changed our lives in the last hundred years.
PHAR1101 provides a basic introduction to pharmacology - the branch of science which studies the effects of drugs upon the body. But it also takes a slightly different approach by stepping back to explore drugs in their broader human setting:
- We will find out how key medicines improved our understanding of the origins and treatment of disease, and have produced significant changes in healthcare practices. 
- We will see how ‘miracle drugs’ also paved the way for new research into more tailored and effective treatments.  
- We will look at how the discovery, development and use of drugs is a major contributor to today’s global economy.   
During PHAR1101 we will also explore the downside to such technological innovations: 
- unpredicted side effects, 
- genetic factors that raise the risk of drug toxicity, 
- economic factors that drive funding towards profitable drugs at the expense of safety, and  
- the ability and inability of our healthcare systems to respond to major drug-led changes in health and illness.  
By the end of the course, we hope you will have developed a real appreciation for the dynamic, evolving nature of modern pharmacology.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

PHAR1101 is currently delivered in mixed mode:
- all lectures are online, 
- the tutorials are self-directed and online, 
- the assessment is face-to-face, including the final examination. 
Marks in this unit are cumulative, and students do NOT have to pass the final exam to pass the unit.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
