Academic Information

Content This unit examines key theories, concepts and approaches to leadership and management in health organisations. An important premise underpinning the unit is that leadership is needed at all levels of an organisation, and that leadership takes many different forms. The need for health leadership to be flexible and responsive to different contexts in a rapidly changing world is explored with students. Across the unit leadership is considered from an individual, team, organisation and health sector perspective. Students will examine contemporary leadership issues and challenges facing the health sector, including ethical leadership, leading change, leadership in times of crisis or uncertainty, and the growing imperative to measure and demonstrate outcomes. A feature of this unit is the integration of real world examples and case studies to explore contemporary issues and challenges in health leadership.

Unit Information

The aim of this postgraduate unit is to develop student understanding of contemporary theories of effective leadership and management and how these apply to the health sector and to professionals within health agencies and services.  Leadership in this unit is defined broadly.  A distinction is made between leadership and management, but recognises that both are needed for effective delivery of health related services.  The importance of vision, strategic planning and prioritisation, communication and negotiation are explored.  
The unit provides a working knowledge of topics that are useful to those holding administrative positions in the health system and those working in not-for-profit community organisations including in governance.  Students are given the opportunity to explore contemporary issues in the health system and wider context at the social, organisational and personal levels. 
Unit Learning Outcomes:
After successful completion of the unit, the should be able to:
1. Discuss and critique key theories, concepts and principles of leadership and management relevant to health-related organisations and services
2. Distinguish different dimension and contexts of leadership, including self-awareness, and leadership at the team, organisational and wider health sector level
3. Demonstrate an understanding of challenges and obligations related to governance, accountability and ethical leadership in the health sector
4. Apply evidence-based principles of facilitating and leading change and to leadership in times of uncertainty or crisis
5. Critically analyse contemporary issues in health leadership and management

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is taught through a series of face-to-face lectures and supporting tutorial sessions.
Through the teaching philosophy, students will be exposed to diverse learning experiences, which includes structured lectures, tutorial discussions and experiential learning.  Lectures and tutorials incorporate relevant examples from the real world to illustrate the application of leadership and management principles to a range of health settings, issues and challenges. The readings for each session are an important complement to the lectures and tutorials, so it is important to keep up with these.  
In this unit you will be encouraged to work both independently as well as collaboratively in group settings.  the unit is premised on the adult-education philosophy that each student is an independent and self-motivated learner, with the role of the 'teacher' being one of facilitation and direction rather than the source of all knowledge.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
