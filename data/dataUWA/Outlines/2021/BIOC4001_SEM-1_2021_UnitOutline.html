Academic Information

Content The topics covered in this unit build on those presented in undergraduate units, and reflect cutting-edge developments in biochemistry and molecular biology, providing students with an understanding and knowledge of the discipline at an advanced level. Students develop and present a lecture on an aspect of one of the topics through independent readings, and submit an assignment related to this lecture. The lecture highlights the research that has led to our current understanding of the topic. The main academic objective of the unit is for students to gain an understanding and an appreciation of how the knowledge in a field of biochemistry and molecular biology progresses. Students' abilities in identifying and critically reviewing appropriate sources of the literature, critical thinking, data analysis, and interpretation of results are extended. They also broaden their knowledge base and gain experience in presenting material unrelated to their research project.

The unit covers the topics: Protein interactions, Protein: RNA interactions, Protein phase separation in biology and Organelles
The unit has several interactive workshops guided by a lecturer and a set of workshops during which students will give a lecture style presentation. Each student has to attend all of the lecture guided workshops and three of the student presentation workshops. Attendance will be taken. 
Student are required to read articles and reviews in preparation for the workshop sessions to complement the material taught and discussed in workshops, to prepare a 10 minute presentation in a lecture style which students will present during the presentation sessions and for the exam.

As this is an advanced unit in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology. the topics covered require reading of the research literature (research articles, review articles).

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is delivered in two parts:
  PART 1: A set of blended model workshops. Students will have to read a set of articles prior to attending the workshops. During the workshops, lecturers will give an overview of the topic and engage students in discussions of the topic.  Student are expected to attend all of the workshop sessions. 
  PART 2: A set of student presentation sessions. Students will prepare a ten-minute mini-lecture based on topics related to the four workshop topics. Students will have the opportunity to make an appointment to consult the lecturer responsible for the topic. Prior to the presentations, students will submit a written summary of the mini-lecture which has to include a dot-point list of the content, a statement of the expected learning outcome for the audience and a list of peer-reviewed references used for the preparation of the mini-lecture. The summary will be marked (10% of unit marks) and returned to students prior to the presentation to provide feedback. Students will present the mini-lecture during one of the presentation sessions followed by a five minute question session based on the mini-lecture topic. The presentation will be assessed by lecturers (40% of unit marks). 
In addition to the mini-lecture (40% of marks) and the mini-lecture summary (10% of marks), the unit will be assessed by a written examination (50% of marks). The examination will cover research articles related to the four unit topics which students will have to read, work through and understand prior to the examination. This requires that students know the material taught in the workshops and may require that students consult additional literature resources.

Participation

Students living in Western Australia are expected to attend all of the workshop sessions and three of the presentation sessions and the written examination Face to Face. Late or non-attendance will lead to a loss of the marks associated with the activity.
Student not able to travel to Perth due to COVID-19 travel restrictions can participate in the scheduled workshop and student presentation activities via Blackboard Collaborate. Student not able to travel to Perth due to COVID-19 travel restrictions take the exam via LMS.
The lecturers will select references to be read for the workshops. Students are expected to prepare for the workshop sessions by reading and understanding the material referenced on LMS and to actively participate in the discussions during the workshops. The lecturers will give an overview of the workshop topics and guide discussions during workshops.
Students are expected to research a topic related to the workshops and to prepare a mini-lecture and a summary of the mini-lecture (assignment). The mini-lecture will be presented by students during one of the presentation sessions.
The lecturers will select references for the exam. Students are expected to understand the content of  these references, to understand how the content relates to the material taught in the unit and consult further literature if required.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
