Academic Information

Content This unit is concerned with studying the qualitative and quantitative aspects of nutrition required for people of different ages and physical activities. It deals with the digestion and absorption of macronutrients and micronutrients, and how this is coordinated by the nervous and endocrine systems. It also considers genetic mutations that deal with inappropriate nutrient absorption (lactose intolerance and cystic fibrosis). A detailed analysis of the absorbed nutrients, their assimilation and subsequent metabolism are considered. The role of the endocrine system in metabolic assimilation of absorbed fuel, along with derangements that follow their excess consumption are considered (e.g. metabolic acidosis, metabolic syndrome). Generic components of these endocrine systems responsible for signal transduction that maintain homeostasis are highlighted by examples that use inherited disorders. The neural and endocrine control of appetite and strategies used to manage this are also addressed.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit will be taught via a series of themes by combining small/large group teaching with self-directed learning, laboratory teaching, tutorials and online content. 
Learning Outcomes:

The teaching and assessment in this units is outcome-driven. The specific outcomes are detailed in the unit outline and indicates what students are expected to learn in this unit. They also provide a guideline for self-directed learning. Students are required to be familiar with the specific learning outcomes in this unit. 
Didactic teaching:
The didactic teaching will be delivered over a number of online lectures delivered by staff of the School of Human Sciences and guest lecturers. The students are expected to expand their knowledge and the information given during the lectures through self-directed learning using the specific learning outcomes as a guide. The students are required to source and use peer-reviewed journal articles to enhance their theoretical knowledge. 
Laboratory teaching:

This unit includes teaching of 'in house' (online option available for students with special consideration) and online laboratory sessions. These are compulsory sessions where specific tasks are required to be completed. 'In house' laboratory sessions are under supervision by staff. Students are required to observe the safety policies and regulations that apply to Physiology laboratories (review 'Laboratory procedures' and 'Conduct in laboratory classes' in the Unit guide book, available on LMS). 
Tutorials:

Tutorials are compulsory sessions where the content of the laboratories, the associated lecture to the lab and case studies will be discussed (online option available for students with special consideration). The students are expected to use self-directed learning to prepare the case studies.
