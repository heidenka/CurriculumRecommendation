Academic Information

Content This unit focuses on professional scientific oral communication skills. One tutorial on 'how to present a seminar' is delivered at the beginning of semesters 1 and 2 to cater for the two intakes of honours students. Students attend all presentations in the seminar appraisal and presentation (SCB) seminar series. In the first two-thirds of their honours program, students attend interactive seminar appraisal sessions for four of these seminars, and present a critique to the group. Students then demonstrate their acquired knowledge and skills in a formal presentation of their research at the end of their honours program. The main academic objectives are for students to gain the ability to recognise quality presentation styles and content, and develop skills in critical thinking and analysis. They also learn how to construct an oral presentation that is visually attractive and scientifically robust, and deliver the information in a lucid and logical manner.

Unit Information

This unit focuses on professional scientific oral communication skills. One introductory lecture on 'how to present a seminar' is delivered at the beginning of semester 1. Students must attend all presentations in the allocated seminar critique sessions which span over semesters 1 and 2 and must achieve at least a 70% attendance for the Bayliss seminar series which span over semesters 1 and 2. Students then demonstrate their acquired knowledge and skills in a formal presentation of their research at the end of their honours program in semester 2. The main academic objectives are for students to gain the ability to recognise quality presentation styles and content, and develop skills in critical thinking and analysis. They also learn how to construct an oral presentation that is visually attractive and scientifically robust, and deliver the information in a lucid and logical manner.

Unit Structure

ASSESMENTS:

The introductory lecture for this unit will be held on Friday 5th March 1-2pm in Bayliss G33. This will start with a short lecture on how to present a seminar, followed by an overview of the unit and assessments and upcoming seminars will be highlighted in this lecture. 
The Bayliss Seminars: The seminars are normally held on Thursday and/or Friday at 12 noon, in Bayliss G33. Attend as many of the Bayliss Seminar Series as possible. A sign-in sheet will be provided in the seminar room to record attendance. Students must achieve at least a 70% attendance to the Bayliss Seminars.
The seminar speakers are drawn from across the country and internationally as well as from within the School and University. However, it is not possible for us to have finalised all the travel arrangements for all of the visitors at this stage. The Seminar program will be developed over the course of the year, and as distinguished speakers announce their plans to visit Perth and UWA. We will give as much notice of the seminars as possible, through:
• the seminar mail list https://maillists.uwa.edu.au/mailman/listinfo/sem-scb (https://maillists.uwa.edu.au/mailman/listinfo/sem-scb)
• plasma screens in the Bayliss Foyer
• notices in the Bayliss elevators
• the website http://www.chembiochem.uwa.edu.au/research/seminars (http://www.chembiochem.uwa.edu.au/research/seminars)
Please attend a broad spectrum of talks – you will hopefully be pleasantly surprised at how much you can learn from talks which are oen quite unrelated to the specific focus of your research project.
The critique: Each member of the class will be assigned to a team, within groups. Each team will be assigned to one of the Bayliss Seminars, which they will assess and critique. YOU MUST ATTEND THE ASSESSED SEMINARS IN YOUR GROUP AND THE ASSOCIATED CRITIQUE SESSIONS.
Each team will give a 10 –15 minute group presentation discussing the strengths and weaknesses of the seminar to the class, and submit a written 1 page summary of the critique (please ensure you include the “assignment cover sheet” which is provided on LMS). The group will then have the opportunity to discuss the critique and the presentation aspects of the seminar with a view to gaining and sharing best practice.
To help guide you, a copy of the assessment sheet “Seminar appraisals guide sheet” that will be used for your end of year seminar is included in the Honours handbook, and on the LMS site under Unit Information. You may find it useful to structure your critique on these lines, but it is not essential to do so. Any format that gives a balanced, fair and honest appraisal of the seminar presentation is valid. You will be notified of critique team assignments on LMS once they have been allocated.
End of year Honours research seminar: Students will demonstrate their acquired knowledge and skills in a formal presentation of their honours research at the end of their honours program
