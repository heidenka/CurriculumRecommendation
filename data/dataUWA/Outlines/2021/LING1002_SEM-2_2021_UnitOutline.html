Unit Information

This unit considers language from a cognitive perspective: how language is represented in the human brain, how it is realised by the body in speech and sign, and how language disorders can change all this. We also explore how human language evolved and continues to change, how linguistic ability develops in the human child, and how languages can be represented through writing systems. The unit also introduces basic concepts in the formal modelling of linguistic systems.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit uses a mix of explicit instruction (in lectures), face-to-face student participation (in tutorials), and plenty of practice outside of class (in tutorial prep assignments and larger online assignments). It's really important to attend your tutorial--this is where the hand-on learning takes place, and it's a great opportunity to ask questions.

Participation

•	There are 10 tutorials for this unit. You are required to complete and submit a small assignment (called a ‘tutorial preparation’ or ‘tute prep’) by 2:00 pm every Monday of a tutorial week (weeks 2-8 and 10-12. There are no tutorials or tute preps in weeks 1 or 9). 
•	Each tute prep is worth 1 mark, or 1% of your final grade.
• By the time of your tutorial, you are also expected to have watched the previous week’s lectures and done the associated readings. The other students in your tutorial will be relying on your ability to make informed contributions.
• There is no mark given for tutorial attendance or participation, but I do take roll, because I want to get to know you. I also don't want anyone to fall through the cracks: if someone never attends tutorials, I want to be aware of that fact.
Tute prep instructions:
1.) Download each week’s tute prep from LMS (‘Tutorial Materials’). Preps for the next week’s tutorials will be available on LMS after the Wednesday lecture each week. 
2.) Do the exercise, then submit a copy of your results using the designated Turnitin link for that prep (also under ‘Tutorial Materials’). Any format that is readable on a PC is fine — PDF, DOCx, or TXT. A photo from your phone is also fine, as long as it's legible. (‘Pages’ files are not readable on PCs. Please do not submit in this format.)
3.) Bring a copy of your completed tute prep to your tutorial so we can all discuss everyone’s answers. It can be on paper or on screen.

Referencing Requirements

In this course, use APA referencing style (https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa/introduction_apa7). 
Most of the examples shown on the website above don't include page numbers, but I find this super annoying. Please DO include page numbers. For example, "the corresponding plural form ju-lu was not used by any speakers" (Bavin and Shopen, 1987, p. 171).
If you use exact words from any source (the textbook, a website, a TV show, a podcast, etc.), you must put these words in quotation marks and acknowledge the source via an APA style reference (see link above). If you paraphrase an idea from another source, you must still acknowledge that you are paraphrasing, and state where you got your idea from (using the APA style referencing system again).
One exception: for this course, if you paraphrase an idea from the Fromkin textbook or your lecture notes, you do not need to cite it. If you use an exact quote from these sources (or from any other source), however, you MUST put this in quotation marks and cite it.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
