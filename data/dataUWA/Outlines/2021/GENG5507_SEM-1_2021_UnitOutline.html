Learning And Teaching Strategy

The T&L approach in this unit involves both flipped and traditional learning. There is a video recording in the Unit Admin Folder in LMS called "Course Overview and How to succeed in this unit" that describes the course motivation and design. Please watch this video.
The statistics part of this unit is taught in tradition lecture-practical class sessions.
The engineering content of this unit is delivered in a flipped classroom/workshop. This approach uses technology to move lectures outside the workshop reserving the time inside the workshop for learning activities that connect concepts with practice and involves the students in in-class discussions. In a flipped learning environment it is the responsibility of the students to do work prior to the workshop in the form of readings, pre-recorded lectures and videos so that they are ready to deploy and practice these ideas in the workshop. Obviously this is best done in a face to face environment but the same experience can be achieved by individual students when playing back the recordings, they need to pause and consider what their responses would have been.
This unit makes extensive use of online discussion boards.  These support extensive dialog with the workshop leaders, teaching team and with other students. Take time to work out how these work, for example see the video link mentioned above. You are encouraged to both post questions and help your peers with their questions. Explaining to others is a good way to validate your own understanding. 

Unit Structure

Stats Lectures: 2 hours per week; Stats practical  classes: 1 hour per week; Engineering workshops: ~2 hours per week (note a 3 hour time slot is allowed for in the timetable but usually workshops run 1.75-2.25 hours).
Our expectation is that this unit will require up to 8 hours of time commitment/ week on average. The unit is front end loaded with much of the work happening in the first 8 weeks of semester allowing you time at the end of semester to focus on deliverables from other units in the last 4 weeks.
Face to face contact time includes a:
1. One 2-3 hour interactive Engineering workshop all weeks except weeks 9 and 10. For weeks 9 and 10 you are split into smaller discipline specific groups (each group will have 2-3 hours of  workshops each week, for the 2 weeks)
2. One hour weekly stats practical class (see Timetable for time slots), please register for one practical class [Practice class starts in Week 2].
For "Any time Contact time" please use the Discussion Boards on LMS
Other weekly commitments include pre work (reading and watching recordings), a weekly assessed Engineering quiz, chapters from the Statistics textbook and a weekly assessed Statistic quiz.
There is NO stats practice class in Week 1.
In Week 1 there is an optional baseline stats quiz due. See LMS for details. There are no assessed quizzes due in Week 1.
The first assessed quizzes are due in Week 2. There are separate STATS and ENG quizzes every week (except in week 1), each has a different deadline. See LMS for details.

Participation

To be successful in this environment requires a level of project management (to engage with the pre work and weekly assessed quizzes) and a willingness to participate in peer-peer activities in the workshops. The flipped learning approach for the Engineering workshops requires a high level of preparation and planning from the students. Data collected from previous semesters shows that participation in the pre workshop activities, quizzes and discussion boards is closely related to performance in the unit. A published study conducted in GENG5507 in Semester 2 2013 showed a strong relationship between participation and performance. Further statistics obtained in 2016 also showed this pattern. The median mark in the final exam (worth 65%) for students who attended 4 or less Engineering workshops was 40%. 
The Engineering theme is taught in a flipped learning mode. Weekly Engineering workshop (2-3hrs) is the face to face classroom activity for Engineering topics. Recorded lectures and other supporting material is provided in LMS. The engineering theme requires you to access and absorb content prior to coming to the Engineering workshop. In the Engineering workshop we will do case studies and other interactive and group activities to allow you to use the content you have read. In practice what this means is that early in the week you need to watch the lectures in the pre-work section of LMS, do the Engineering quiz to test you have understood the lectures, and then prepare for the Engineering workshop.
Please be aware that due to the interactive nature of the Engineering workshop that, although it is recorded, it does not record well on the LCS as much of the talking is done by students rather than the academic with the microphone. We do our best to support students who are not able to make the face to face workshops through the discussion boards.
The Statistics content is taught using lectures and practical classes. Attendance at practical classes is strongly recommended as many of the concepts taught need to be practiced and there are facilitators there to assist you.
Assessment overview
This unit is assessed in the following ways: (1) weekly statistical quizzes (15%) and weekly engineering quizzes (15%); (2) a mid term test (1 hour)(15%); and (3) a final examination (55%). 
Assessment in this unit has two aims.
The first is summative to ensure that you have engaged with the content and can apply the principles and tools of risk, reliability and safety in different engineering contexts, summative assessment DOES contribute to your grade. There are only 3 items of summative assessment: 1) the weekly engineering and weekly statistics quizzes, 2) mid term test, and 3) the final examination.
The second is formative in that it seeks to encourage you to engage with the content, and to synthesise elements on the content into a holistic picture for each theme, formative assessment does NOT contribute to your grade.  The formative assessment include 1) in-workshop quizzes held during the engineering workshop, and 2) the base line stats and probability quiz. These are both voluntary.
Summative assessment
Weekly Engineering Quizzes: These quizzes are designed to encourage you to engage with ideas that will be discussed in the weekly 2-3 hour workshop. The workshops have been designed to apply ideas to engineering issues and promote discussion, not for content delivery. In order to get the most out of these workshops it is vital that you have engaged with the content previously. The quizzes are intended to encourage you to do so. You will have 2 opportunities to do the weekly engineering quiz questions; this is to encourage you to reflect on why you got an answer wrong and work out why the right one is right. The first assessed weekly engineering quiz is due in Week 2 BEFORE the Wk2 workshop.  You have a week of time for each quiz.
Please note: There is no grace period or extension available for inability to access ISO 31000 Standard. This Standard needs to be acquired early in the semester and not left to the last minute. There are only 3 SAI Global licences available at UWA so students need to be organised to access them. You will need to have read the Standard to do the week 2 Engineering weekly quiz.
Weekly Statistic Quizzes: As a Master of Professional Engineering student it is vital that you demonstrate the ability to use basic probability and statistical concepts. There are many resources available for you to refresh these concepts and we will provide lectures and practical classes to assist you with ones that are used particularly in risk reliability and safety. In order to encourage you to practice these skills there are weekly statistics quizzes. Like the engineering quizzes there are two opportunities to do these. These are separate from the engineering quizzes and have different deadlines. In week 1 of this course we will be conducting a quiz to establish a baseline of capability across the student group.
Quiz submission: Submission of online quizzes by the due date is the responsibility of the students. Because there is no time limit on the quizzes it is not possible for us to use the automatic submit at the deadline feature in LMS. What this means is that YOU MUST SUBMIT THEM PRIOR TO THE DEADLINE YOURSELF. If you fail to do this, it shows up as an error in the LMS grade book. To correct this error is time-consuming for us. Hence we implemented a policy that says that ONLINE QUIZZES WHICH ARE NOT SUBMITTED BY THE DEADLINE WILL NOT BE CONSIDERED TOWARDS ASSESSMENT; SUCH QUIZZES WILL BE GIVEN A MARK OF ZERO FOR THE PARTICULAR ATTEMPT. This means that even in you have answered a number of questions correctly if it is submitted late or not submitted, it will not be marked, and you will receive 0 marks for the attempt. LMS measures time up to 1/100th of a second. Hence even if you are late by 1/100th of a second it will be considered late and will not be marked. Advice is to submit the quizzes well before the due time. NOTE: The "Late submission of Assignments" policy is not applicable for online quizzes. This approach to quizzes is part of the design of the unit. You get the opportunity to practice your ability to mitigate the risk that you don’t get any marks for a weekly quiz because you did not complete and submit on time. There are many controls for this risk for example, start early (you have a week to do them), use the discussion boards for technical help and so on. Whether you action these controls is up to you. We have told you explicitly here that the consequence of failing to manage the risk is that you will get zero marks for the quiz.
Midterm test: This 1 hour test will be done online and scheduled in week 5 engineering workshop. It will be done virtually by students using LMS. It is ONLY available in the pre-specified hour. Students need to use their own laptop/computer. For students who miss the test or have IT issues, no alternative test will be scheduled, instead 15% of the weighting will be added to the final exam. That is, those who miss (or did not complete due to IT issues) the midterm test, the final exam will be worth 70%. An announcement (in LMS) with more  details of the test will be posted early in the semester. 
Final exam: The final 2 hour examination will be used to provide summative assessment of your achievement of the learning outcomes for the course. It is based on a case study similar to those we do in the workshops throughout the semester.
Formative assessment
Week 1 Base line Quiz - Probability: In week 1 there is one online quiz to be done on probability & data summary. Performance in the baseline quiz does NOT contribute to your final grade. We ask you to do this quiz because this is a whole of Faculty unit with 4th year and MPE students you have all covered different stats/probability topics in various units and therefore there is a range of capability. Week 1 quiz is to establish where you are as an individual and where the group is in terms of stats/prob experience. Distribution of marks on this quiz gives us a baseline and can help us tailor subsequent material appropriately. Please do not copy the answers from others, it serves no purpose as it will only give us the impression you have this knowledge when you don’t. Doing the quiz will help you establish where your understanding is and what pre-reading/ revision you need to do.
Engineering "Workshop" quizzes: Each week there are one or two quizzes done during the Engineering Workshop. Marks in these quizzes DO NOT contribute to your final grade. They are done to capture attendance at the workshop and see if ideas in the workshop have been understood. Please DO NOT do these quizzes if you are not attending the workshop. It serves no purpose and makes our attendance numbers inaccurate.
Please note that in the gradebook LMS does not include a quiz that you never opened in its calculation for your percent performance. For example, there are 10 statistics quizzes so the denominator for calculating the percent performance is 10 x the number of questions in each quiz. However, if you only opened 6 quizzes, LMS thinks the denominator is 6 x the number of questions. If you got 50 answers right then you might think you got 50/60 but in fact you got 50/100.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
