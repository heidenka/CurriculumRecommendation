Academic Information

Content Benefit: Cost Analysis is one of the most important and widely used tools of economics. It supports improved decision making about projects and policies by providing a systematic, logical, transparent, and consultative approach to evaluating and prioritising them. In this unit, students learn how to apply Benefit: Cost Analysis in a rigorous, informative and practical way that recognises the importance of consulting with stakeholders and experts. Examples in this unit focus on agriculture and environmental management, but the material presented is applicable in many other contexts. The unit takes an applied approach, aiming to provide students with all the knowledge and skills needed to conduct a Benefit: Cost Analysis themselves. As part of this, each student will construct their own well-designed spreadsheet to do the required calculations, will use it to conduct a Benefit: Cost Analysis, will conduct an informative sensitivity analysis, and will interpret and explain the results in a written report for decision makers. Challenges in completing a Benefit: Cost Analysis are presented, and strategies to overcome those challenges are provided. The academic objectives are to understand the concepts and principles that underpin Benefit: Cost Analysis and to learn how to apply it to evaluate real projects and policies.

Unit Information

Benefit: Cost Analysis is one of the most important tools of economics, supporting improved decision making about projects and policies. In this micro-credential unit, students learn how to apply Benefit: Cost Analysis (BCA) in a rigorous, informative and practical way that recognises the importance of consulting with stakeholders and experts.
The unit provides students with all the knowledge and skills needed to conduct a BCA themselves. As well as presenting the key concepts and principles that underpin BCA, it has a strong emphasis on practical issues, including: obtaining the required information from various sources, development of a BCA spreadsheet, managing uncertainty through sensitivity analysis and other methods, preparing an effective report for decision makers, and avoiding a number of common errors.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is delivered online, with pre-recorded videos for the lectures and a live weekly workshop delivered online. 
There are two lecture topics each week, and each topic includes 5 to 7 videos, totalling between 40 and 60 minutes' duration for each topic.
For each lecture topic, at least one reading is recommended and in some cases there are optional readings as well, in case you are interested in delving more deeply into the topic. Readings are listed at the end of the page for a Topic and most readings can be accessed directly from there.
There is a workshop each week. The workshop will be conducted live at a time to be determined after consultation with the students. It will also be recorded and made available to students that way. 

Unit Structure

The unit is structured into topics and there are two topics assigned to each week.
Ideally, you would keep up with the schedule of topics.
If you wish to complete topics more rapidly, that is fine.
If other commitments mean that you get behind the schedule for this unit, that is OK up to a point, but there are constraints. You will need to have completed relevant topics or workshops in order to be able to do each of the quizzes and assignments.
Some of the workshops will make more sense if you are up to date with the lecture videos.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
