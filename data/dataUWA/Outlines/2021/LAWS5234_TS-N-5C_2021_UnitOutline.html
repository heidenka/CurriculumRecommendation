Academic Information

Content This unit covers the complex Australian taxation of trusts in relation to both domestic and foreign trusts. Core issues examines are—the nature of a trust; trusts in tax legislation; the statutory scheme in trust taxation; present entitlement; streaming of trust income; anti-avoidance; trust losses; capital gains and trusts; taxation of non-resident trusts and taxation of non-resident trust beneficiaries.

Preparatory Reading
As a minimum students must read the following before the four day intensive:
1. Chapter 17 of Woellner et al – Australian Taxation Law, (31st Edition, Oxford 2021). This chapter covers many aspects of the taxation of trusts in Australia.
2. 24-260 to 24-300 from Chapter 24 of the above book. This covers the Transferor Trust regime.
3. 7-180 to 7-230 from Chapter 7 of the above book dealing with trust related CGT events. You should also follow up relevant cross references in the same chapter.
The above book is available in the library in hard copy and is available as an e-book from the UWA library.
The above is a bare minimum that must be read before the intensive. It is not a huge amount to cover. Ultimately however, good learning would require you to also do the following:
1. Read the core sections of the Income Tax Assessment Act covered in the above readings in the above chapters.
2. Read the commentary on these sections in the Federal Tax Reporter (available through the online library)
3. Read the important cases or summaries of them that are prominent in the commentary discussion.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
