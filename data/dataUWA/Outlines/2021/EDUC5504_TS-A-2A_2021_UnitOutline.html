Academic Information

Content Students examine language and literacy development and learning in young children, as well as a range of practices for teaching and assessing reading and writing, speaking and listening, and viewing. Students also learn about key foundational skills such as phonological awareness and phonics knowledge as well as exploring a range of resources that are important in early childhood literacy learning and teaching contexts, including children's literature, informational texts, multimedia and digital texts, rhymes and songs.

WRITTEN LITERACY REQUIREMENTS
In order to pass the assignments in this unit, all written work must be of a satisfactory standard in terms of written literacy and referencing. Written texts should be appropriately structured and clear in meaning. Correct and appropriate grammar, vocabulary and spelling should be used throughout. Referencing should conform to APA style. Written literacy and referencing expectations will normally be incorporated into assignment rubrics.
ASSESSMENTS
Assignment 1 (25 marks)
Oral presentation
The purpose of this assignment is to give you an opportunity to present on an aspect of the teaching of phonological awareness or phonics. You will present (online video recording) a 10 minute PowerPoint presentation to outline and explain:
1)	How children typically learn the chosen concept - what does the research and theory say?
2)	Teaching strategies and resources to facilitate children’s learning of the specific concept.
The presentation will also be assessed for clear and accurate communication, both written and oral. A detailed assignment description and rubric will be available on LMS.
Assignment 2 Online Quiz (20 marks)
This online quiz assessment assesses: (1)  your knowledge of working effectively, sensitively and confidentially with parents/carers in respect of their child’s language and literacy learning, and; (2) your understanding of moderation processes and making consistent judgements of student literacy learning. 
Assignment 3 – Planning task, digital resource and rationale (55 marks)
a) Create a digital resource to be used by a teacher to support young children's learning of a specific aspect of literacy (NOT phonological awareness or phonics as this would overlap too much with Assignment 1).  The resource should be designed to help a small group of children with similar needs in either Pre-primary, Year 1 or Year 2. 
b)  Write a 1,500 - 2,000 word description and rationale (justification) in which you should: 
Describe the digital resource, its purpose and its features. 250-300 words.
Write ONE lesson plan to show how the resource will be used to help the children learn the specified literacy concept/s. The following should be clear from the lesson plan: 
•	The specific learning objective/s
•	Teaching strategies employed by the teacher
•	Activities the children will engage in
•	Resources to be used (this must include the digital resource that you have created and may also include other resources)
•  How you would assess the children’s learning
The lesson plans are not part of the word count.
Write a scholarly rationale to justify the design of the resource and the way that it will be used in the sequence of lessons, making reference to current and seminal theoretical and research literature. Approx. 1200-1700 words.
In addition, post a 250 word reflection in your e-portfolio – how has engaging in this assignment helped you demonstrate specified APSTs at graduate level?  
*Please see LMS for detailed assignment description and rubric.

RESUBMISSION RULE:
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
15. Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
