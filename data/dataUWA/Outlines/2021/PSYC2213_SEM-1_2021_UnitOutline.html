Academic Information

Content This unit introduces students to key issues and common research methods used in the study of human development. Major theories of development are described and evaluated. Ways in which environmental and biological factors influence development are considered. Descriptions of key changes across childhood and theoretical explanations of these changes are provided in relation to cognition, intelligence, language, literacy, numeracy, social skills, emotion and gender.

Unit Information

The lectures introduce students to changes that occur with ageing as well as changes across childhood. Tutorial exercises introduce students to methods for conducting research on development, including intelligence testing, and engage students in discussions on contemporary developmental issues.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The lectures and associated readings provide comprehensive coverage of the developmental topics. The tutorial classes provide opportunities for interactive learning through discussions of current societal issues concerning child and adult development, evaluations of research designs, and practice in the administration of an intelligence test for children.

Unit Structure

There are 12 two-hour lectures, which are scheduled for Thursdays 12pm-2pm. There are 4 two-hour tutorials, in semester weeks 3, 7, 9 and 11. In the first tutorial (week 3) students are assigned to groups to prepare for oral group presentations. These presentations are then given and assessed in the second tutorial class (week 7). The third tutorial (week 9) is devoted to the study of language development and the fourth tutorial (week 11) to intelligence test administration.
In week 5, a multiple-choice test, covering lecture and tutorial material from weeks 1-4, will be made available on LMS. This optional test provides an opportunity to practice revising unit content and completing an assessment of it. The practice test does not count towards the grade for the unit.
The first piece of assessed work is the group presentation completed in the student's week 7 tutorial class. Each group will develop an oral presentation with associated visual aids to argue for one side of a current developmental issue, with the topic allocated to the group in week 3. This presentation is assessed in the week 7 class and feedback is provided to help prepare for the second piece of assessed work.
The second piece of assessed work is an individual essay which should cover both sides of one of the developmental issues considered in the week 7 tutorial. This piece of assessment must not exceed 2,000 words in length, excluding the reference list.
The third component of assessment is a 2-hour exam held in the University's end-of-semester exam period.
Each component of assessment will be described in more detail in classes and written guides.

Participation

It is important that students attend all of the tutorial classes. In the first tutorial class in week 3, each student will be allocated to a group to prepare for the first component of assessment, which is a group oral presentation held in the second tutorial class in week 7. Each student must contribute in an effective and timely way to the preparation and delivery of their group's presentation to obtain credit for this piece of assessment. Students will be asked to keep a log of contributions of group members and any students who fail to provide evidence of contributing effectively to their group will receive a grade of zero for this assessment component. The log of contributions will be checked by tutors in week 6, i.e., a week before the scheduled group presentation. The tutorials in weeks 3, 9 and 11 also deliver key information on research designs, language development and intelligence testing.  The information from these tutorials will be examined, and since the tutorials are not recorded, it is important that students attend and participate.

Referencing Requirements

The essay assignment should be written according to the format outlined in the Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (7th Edition) and must not exceed 2000 words in length excluding the reference list. Marking will cease once the word limit is reached. The essay assignment is an individual assessment and so care should be taken to avoid overlap with the work of other students, including group members. The essay assignment should be submitted using Turnitin via LMS. Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
