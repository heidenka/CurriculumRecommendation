Unit Information

This unit develops students' expertise in the planning, delivery and assessment of targeted interventions in early childhood or primary education within a 5-day professional practice placement. Students plan a focused intervention project for individual school students or groups of students. The project represents a response to a particular need identified at individual, group, class or school level in consultation with school partners. The unit contains a strong action–reflective practice and research informed dimension. Primary students choose to conduct an intervention in English or Mathematics and Early Childhood students conduct an intervention in Mathematics.
Outcomes
Students are able to (1) develop an evidence based plan of action on the basis of diagnostic assessment or other information (data) about an individual child, or group of children; (2) implement and evaluate a short-term intervention in a selected curriculum learning area; (3) report on the intervention to parents/carers; (4) meet professional ethics and responsibilities; (5) seek and apply constructive feedback from mentor teachers to improve practice; (6) critically review an area of research literature as appropriate to the intervention; (7) write a scholarly research report; and (8) engage in classroom-based research.

WRITTEN LITERACY REQUIREMENTS
In order to pass the assignments in this unit, all written work must be of a satisfactory standard in terms of written literacy and referencing. Written texts should be appropriately structured and clear in meaning. Correct and appropriate grammar, vocabulary and spelling should be used throughout. Referencing should conform to APA 7 style. Written literacy and referencing expectations will normally be incorporated into assignment rubrics.
ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:
Assignment 1: Project proposal
The purpose of a project proposal is to provide an overview of the research to those who are not familiar with the topic. A proposal should address the ‘What and Why’ along with the ‘How’ of the planned research. Remember that this is a proposal, so you cannot identify participants. However, some information about characteristics of the participants to be invited should be included. The proposal will have five main headings: title, statement of the issue, literature review, methodology and references. The length of the proposal is 2,000 words (not including references and the summary of the planned intervention). This is not many words to work with, so please be succinct in your writing. Also, use relevant references. Please see LMS for full description and rubric. Early Childhood pre-service teachers must do a Mathematics/Numeracy intervention. Primary pre-service teachers can choose either Mathematics/numeracy or English/literacy. Please write the word count (excluding end-references) at the top of your assignment. Prior to submitting your proposal, you will do a 10-minute presentation in class and receive formative feedback from your peers. Please see LMS for a more detailed assignment description and rubric.
Assignment 2: Written report. 
In this assignment you are to build on the first assignment to write an extended scholarly plan and rationale for an intervention for a small group of children with a common learning need in either English (or literacy) and Mathematics (or numeracy). This will constitute a substantial elaboration of your initial proposal and will also include a section on how you would plan for a second round of teaching interventions and action research.
The report should be up to 5000 words long, excluding end-references. It should be presented in Times New Roman and1 ½ spacing. Include a footer that has the page number and a header that states your name and student number. Remember to use pseudonyms (for participants and for the school) throughout the report. Please see LMS for full assignment description and rubric. The UWA word count penalty will apply for assignments longer than 5000 words. Please write the word count (excluding end-references) at the top of your assignment. 
See LMS for full assignment description and rubric.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
