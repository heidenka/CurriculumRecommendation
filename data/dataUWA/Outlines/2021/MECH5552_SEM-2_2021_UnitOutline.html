Academic Information

Content The project is a capstone experience in which students integrate and apply knowledge and skills acquired in earlier units to a substantial real world design challenge relevant to their discipline. Students work in teams, typically with four to six members, and are supervised by an academic and/or industry mentor. Teams are responsible for managing their project as well as reporting against milestones and preparing the necessary design documentation. The academic objectives of the project are to lead students through a systematic engineering design and synthesis process and to better prepare them for professional practice as engineers. The focus of this unit is the delivery of outcomes to a professional standard.

Unit Information

The project is a capstone experience in which students integrate and apply knowledge and skills acquired in earlier units to a substantial real world design challenge relevant to their discipline. Students work in teams, with nominally five members, and are supervised by a facilitator (an in-house academic or ex-industry professional with engineering design experience) and mentored by a representative from the industry organisation offering the project. 
Teams are responsible for managing their project as well as reporting against milestones and preparing the necessary design documentation. The academic objectives of the project are to lead students through a systematic engineering design and synthesis process and to better prepare them for professional practice as engineers. The focus of this unit is the delivery of outcomes to a professional standard.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This is the second part of a capstone course and students learn to self-evaluate and reflect on their work through comprehensive milestone assessment rubrics and face-to-face feedback sessions.  Students learn how to build various forms of unwritten knowledge, including tacit knowledge, implicit knowledge, embodied knowledge and procedural knowledge.  All these are essential for design, but not often mentioned in other courses. This unit enables most students to build their design skills by working on an authentic industry project in collaboration with a local company.

A selection of pre-recorded lectures (available through the LMS), weekly 2hr practical classes (face-to-face or online) and periodic (~20min) project-health-check meetings via Zoom/Teams.

Participation

Practical sessions, whether face-to-face or online (e.g. via Teams), are compulsory and you are expected to actively participate. Failure to do so will result in your facilitator reviewing your professionalism and penalising you accordingly. Practical sessions provide you the opportunity to work on your projects whilst interacting with and seeking feedback from your facilitator.
Your Team Report mark will be individually moderated by your individual conduct during practical sessions and at the periodic project-health-check sessions. These sessions, run by the same academic staff member, will gauge cohort performance and provide stimulus to ensure groups and group members make effective and meaningful progress toward achieving professional, industry authentic, outcomes.
Each team member is expected to participate in and contribute to the oral presentations.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
