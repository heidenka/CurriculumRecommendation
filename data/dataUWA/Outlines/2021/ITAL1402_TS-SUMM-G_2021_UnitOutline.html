Academic Information

Content This unit continues the introduction to Italian language and culture provided in ITAL1401 Italian Studies 1. Students are taught to speak, understand, read and write contemporary Italian and study aspects of contemporary Italian culture in Italy and in countries of Italian migration. The unit uses communicative and natural learning methodologies, and authentic print and multimedia texts. Students achieve language proficiency equivalent to Level A1/A2 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (Quadro Comune Europeo di Riferimento per le Lingue). The unit leads to ITAL2403 Italian Studies 3. Level 2 and 3 Italian units not only increase language competency but offer the opportunity to further engage with Italian culture and history (including linguistics, literature and film) and to take advantage of a range of exchange possibilities, including our short-term Bergamo Summer School.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

In this unit we will employ a ‘flipped classroom’ learning approach. Face-to-face classes (tutorials) will be integrated with an online component, which consists of short video lessons and online activities (quizzes, movie clips, forums etc. also referred to as e-tivities). Both in-class and online engagement count towards the final mark.
 
In class, the teacher will work with students on different language topics, helping them to discover the structures of the language and practise in an interactive and student-centred environment. Students are expected to attend all tutorials; if they miss a tutorial, they are required to work on the topics covered in class before attending the next tutorial. All in-class activities are specified in the unit outline. In each tutorial we will build on the topics of the previous ones, so it is crucial that students keep up with the contents of the unit through regular study. 
 
The in-class and online activities are designed in order to allow students to work on short but regular practice sessions rather than in intensive sessions at the end of the course. The reason for this is a pedagogical one, as this structure allows an effective and lasting language learning experience.

1. In-class Tests and Online Tests
In-class tests and online tests will assess you on the content covered until the time you complete them. They will provide a clear picture of your progress in understanding and using the grammar and vocabulary learnt in the previous weeks.
In-class tests - The in-class tests will be closed book. Tests will be marked and returned within one week. All in-class tests run for ~25 minutes each, apart from the final test in Module 4, which will go for 45 minutes.
Listening online test - For this listening test you will be able to listen to the tracks as many times as you like but will only be given one attempt at the quiz (with a 15 minute time limit). Make sure you are in a quiet place and that the videos/audios work on your device. We encourage you to use a computer while taking the test (no mobile devices) and to wear earphones. 
Please note -  If for any reason you are unable to complete one of the online tests you must contact the unit coordinator as early as possible. You will not be allowed to attempt the quizzes and online tests unless you submit a medical certificate.  
3. Oral assessment
The oral will consist of two parts: (1) role play (2) conversation.
1. Role play
This will assess your ability to conduct a conversation with another person in Italian. You will be allocated a partner with whom you will act out short ~3-minute dialogue. You will be given the topic of your role play 15 minutes prior to the assessment. 
Scenarios:
1.	You have just arrived in Italy but your luggage has been lost somewhere on the way there. What happened? What do you do now?  What did you lose?
2.	You sit down on the bus next to an elderly person. They are interested in your hobbies but also in telling you about his/hers when they were young.
3.	You enter a clothing store to buy an outfit for your birthday party but you are indecisive and need the shop attendant’s help.
4.	Your exchange student has just arrived and you are wanting to know more about his/her morning routine (to work out when to have showers etc).
2.  Conversation  
Following the role play, students will be asked to respond to at least one of the following questions.  Students should expect to be asked spontaneous questions by their tutor!
Conversation topics:
1.	I tuoi passatempi.  Sono cambiati da quando eri piccolo/a?
2.	Ti piace vestirti alla moda?  Quanto spendi per i vestiti?
3.	Parlami della tua routine quotidiana!
4.	Che cosa faresti con un milione di dollari?
Students will be assessed on:
•	Ability to conduct a natural conversation for the chosen situation.
•	Use of appropriate vocabulary and expressions.
•	Grammatical correctness.
Students will be given ample time to practise the role play scenarios and the conversation topics during the summer session.  
Note that all your tutors are cheat-proof. We have been listening to and assessing students’ role plays for years now. We have seen students reading hidden behind sunglasses, dubbing and cutting videos, using Google Translate to write scripts etc. With the role play you will show that you can hold a short, simple conversation in Italian with the vocabulary and grammar you learnt in the unit. If in front of the camera you are not as confident as Brad Pitt we have good news: we won’t care 😊

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
