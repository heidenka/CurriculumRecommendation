Academic Information

Content Important areas of podiatric medicine and surgery are explored in detail. These include podiatric surgical management of common foot conditions, musculoskeletal medicine; the role of the podiatrist as a team member in managing sports-related injuries; and the diagnosis and podiatric management of common sporting injuries of the lower extremity. The growth and development of the paediatric patient are explored, together with diagnosis and treatment of musculoskeletal conditions affecting the lower extremity in the young patient. Similarly, the special medical, nursing and social implications of ageing and aspects of geriatric medicine relevant to podiatry are discussed. How to approach disability patients and common conditions affecting them are discussed in detail. Finally, therapeutic and the use of schedule medicine for management of common podiatric conditions will be discussed.

Unit Information

Introduction 
You will achieve confidence and theoretical knowledge as future podiatrists in practicing through a broad approach to podiatric medicine. This is achieved through a practical patient centred approach and teaching using the latest evidence based practical concepts.
 
Unit Description 
In this unit you will further develop your clinical knowledge and skills with regards to assessment and management of wide variety of conditions such as podiatric traumatology, sports injuries, disability conditions, podopaediatrics, and podogeriatrics. The aim of the unit will be to build upon the knowledge and expertise attained in DPM2, integrating into a broad theoretical framework. Finally, personal professional development (PPD) topics on ethics, law and malpractice insurance will be presented.

Participation

Attendance
Attendance at lectures is expected and is compulsory for laboratory and eLearning sessions.  Attendance will be taken at these compulsory sessions. Students must ensure they have signed in for the teaching sessions where roll is kept.  It is not the responsibility of the tutor or demonstrator.  You will be considered to be absent if you have not signed in for the session. 
Students can only miss 1 compulsory session during the semester without a medical certificate.  In the case of illness, a medical certificate (not provided by a family member) must be supplied to the unit coordinator within one week of return to classes.  Any missed tasks will need to be completed by this time. Note that the onus is on the student to make contact regarding absences. If a student misses more than this, he/she will be expected to contact the unit coordinator with an explanation.  A failure to provide a valid explanation, or further missed classes will result in action according to the FMDHS Professional Behaviour Assessment, available on the DPM Community LMS page. 
Lateness
Students who are consistently late to teaching sessions (more than 10 minutes) will be marked as late and an explanation will be sought from the student.  Your fellow students and teachers appreciate punctuality which is part of professional behaviour and courtesy. Multiple late arrivals will be managed according to the FHMS Professional Behaviour Assessment. Late arrival at lectures is also discourteous and unprofessional. If you are unavoidably late, it is important that you do not enter a lecture theatre by the front entrance. 
Adherence to rostered session times
It is critical that where a student is assigned to attend a particular session at a particular time, only that session may be attended. Students are not to attend non-assigned sessions for their convenience. Students doing so will be subject to the FMDHS Professional Behaviour Assessment process.
Patient confidentiality
As podiatry students you are of course not only free to discuss your experiences with each other, but you are actually encouraged to do so. Some teaching may involve discussion of clinical cases. Do not discuss these where you may be overheard by the public. DO NOT upload information or images regarding clinical cases to social media sites.  It is important to remember that any information you learn about a patient should be regarded as a matter of professional confidence.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
