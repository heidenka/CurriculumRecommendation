Unit Information

The analysis of sedimentary basin fills is typically undertaken to interpret Earth history from the stratigraphic record in basin fills, for example reconstructing paleotectonic settings and/or paleoclimate for different regions and for different times in the geological past. Sedimentary basins may contain a wide range of important natural resources including groundwater, energy sources, basin-hosted mineral deposits and building materials.  Increasingly, basins are being explored for new energy sources and their potential for carbon storage assessed. This unit takes a basin-scale to regional approach to analysing basin fills through application of established sedimentological–stratigraphic and geophysical techniques. Learning is experiential with emphasis placed on applying key concepts and developing practical skills for interpreting geoscientific data. Hence basin analysis is a multidisciplinary approach that integrates geoscientific data and interpretations to solve geoscience problems related to sedimentary basin evolution and timing and distribution of key resources. These key elements are incorporated into the unit learning outcomes above.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit provides an opportunity for students to develop deeper understanding of important geological and geophysical techniques that are routinely applied during analysis of sedimentary basins via experiential learning. This means that the unit emphasises learning through practical work with workshops composed of presentation/discussion of concepts and their practical application usually via practical exercises, discussion and feedback during the workshops. Completion of exercises and review of materials for the next workshop are expected between classes. The unit also aims to promote generic skills in research methodology, using scientific literature, scientific communication (written and oral presentation) and teamwork. Project work, undertaken in small teams, is designed to provide opportunity to apply the skills learned in the unit including integration of geological and geophysical data.

Unit Structure

Considerable emphasis is placed on practical skill development in this unit. One 3-hour interactive workshop is held on Mondays at 10 am–1 pm and one 2-hour workshop on Wednesdays at 3–5 pm each  week.  Refer to the UWA timetable and/or CAS as required but note that there is no need for allocations in this unit this year.  You are expected to participate in both workshops per week. The Mapping Lab (G13) is on the ground level of the Geology-Geography Building and can be accessed from the northern end of the building via a short corridor from the building foyer or carpark entrance.
Each class aims to build on the work of previous classes both within a workshop topic and across the unit. Please have pencils, coloured pencils, eraser and a ruler to undertake practical exercises. Workshop notes will be provided and other resources will also be provided in LMS. Formative assessment of each major workshop topic provides an opportunity for individual feedback on learning progress during semester. The group project is a chance to work semi-independently on real-world data as both formative and summative assessment, and is a good introduction to the skills developed in honours and coursework masters research projects in any topic.  There is no end-of-semester examination in this unit.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
