Academic Information

Content People present to clinical psychologists and neuropsychologists with a set of problems for which they are seeking assistance. This unit aims to provide a conceptual framework for the assessment and conceptualisation of problems. Students are introduced to diagnosis (using DSM-V and ICD-10) and diagnostic interviewing, assessment through interview (e.g., history taking and conducting a mental status examination) and psychometric instruments appropriate for the measurement and monitoring of a wide range of psychopathology in both children and adults. Students also learn how to organise clinical material into a case formulation to guide treatment selection and delivery. Consideration are given to comorbidity and cultural diversity and how these factors impact practice.

Unit Information

People present to clinical psychologists and neuropsychologists with a set of difficulties for which they are seeking assistance. This unit aims to provide an introductory framework for the assessment and conceptualisation of these presentations and difficulties. Students are introduced to diagnostic classification systems (i.e. DSM-5 and ICD-10); clinical assessment and interviewing (e.g., history taking, conducting a mental status examination and administering questionnaires); and the use of psychometric instruments for the measurement and monitoring of a wide range of areas of functioning and concerns in both children and adults. Students will also learn how to organise clinical material into a case formulation to guide intervention planning. Consideration is given to comorbidity and cultural diversity and how these factors may impact assessment, formulation and intervention.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This is an introductory/ core unit for clinical psychologists and clinical neuropsychologists in training. The weekly classes are seminar-style, involving a range of teaching and learning strategies, including didactic teaching, small group and whole class discussions or activities focusing on application of knowledge in clinical work (i.e. case studies, videos, role plays). The classes will be interactive, and students are expected to learn from their lecturers and peers and to also share their own experiences, learning and perspectives and learn from one another. Other learning strategies include reading, assignments (both of which are case-based to encourage development of scientist-practitioner thinking and application of new knowledge in case work). A practical/ experiential assignment is also included (psychometric administration video role play), as this is a professional training unit and therefore students are expected to gain knowledge and practice experience.
