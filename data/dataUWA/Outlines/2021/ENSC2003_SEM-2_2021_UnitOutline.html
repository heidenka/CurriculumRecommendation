Academic Information

Content The unit will provide an introduction to the application of the fundamental electrical engineering concepts, theories and abstractions that underpin the design and analysis of analog and digital circuits. The unit also covers: application of circuit theory laws and device modelling to analyse the behaviour of analog circuits; synthesis and design of basic digital circuits, and power analysis of circuits. Throughout the unit, students will explore sustainability challenges in electrical and electronic engineering. Emphasis is placed on practical applications of the above concepts, with laboratory experiments providing opportunities to build and test analog and digital circuits.

Unit Information

The main objective of this unit is to provide the essential electrical-engineering foundation to support the multidisciplinary nature of all engineering disciplines being taught at UWA. In addition, the unit also will provide the background knowledge needed for students who choose to pursue further study in Electrical, Electronic and Computer Engineering.
The unit will provide an introduction to the application of the fundamental electrical engineering concepts, theories and abstractions that underpin the design and analysis of analog and digital circuits. The unit also covers: application of circuit theory laws and device modelling to analyse the behaviour of analog circuits; synthesis and design of basic digital circuits, and power analysis of circuits. Throughout the unit, students will explore sustainability challenges in electrical and electronic engineering. Emphasis is placed on practical applications of the above concepts, with simulated laboratory experiments providing opportunities to build and test analog and digital circuits.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The overall strategy of this unit is to cover the essential theoretical foundation of Electrical Engineering, while providing a solid practical experience. The format of delivery is based on standard lectures followed by problem-solving practical sessions to foster active learning and a highly interactive class environment. 
Lectures will allow you to understand a range of design and analysis aspects of analogue and digital circuits. The unit has been designed with additional interaction time to maximize student-teacher engagement despite the limitations imposed by COVID-19. It is expected that you will pre-read the relevant materials on LMS prior to attending the lectures, information/consultation sessions, practical classes and labs. An “Open-door” policy is applied in this unit. Please feel free to contact us if you have any questions regarding this unit.

Unit Structure

LECTURE-TUTORIAL: The lecture-tutorials will be run primarily face-to-face, with a remote connection available via MS Teams to ensure students not physically in WA can also watch live. The lectures will also be recorded and made available via LMS. These sessions will be interactive and heavy on problem solving and examples. In general, there will be two session per week, each 2-hours in length. In weeks with a test, one of these sessions will be cut back to 1-hour to allow students to attend the test during the remaining hour. For this reason, it is imperative that students do not double-book this test session with other classes.
PRACTICAL SESSIONS: Practical problem solving sessions will be conducted in an engaging interactive format. For these sessions to function in the intended manner, it is essential that students have attempted the problems (provided in the previous week) prior to attending the sessions
LABORATORY WORK: Each Laboratory session is to be completed by students either face-to-face in the lab or online during the selected lab timeslots. 
TESTS: Three tests will take place during the one LecTut time-slot (see timetable). The specific dates of the tests will be announced in the introduction lecture as well as on the LMS. It is imperative that student do not double-book this information session with other units.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
