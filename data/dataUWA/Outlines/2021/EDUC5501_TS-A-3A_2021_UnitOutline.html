Academic Information

Content Students analyse key features of the Humanities and Social Sciences learning area—a broad, interdisciplinary study which focuses on the interactions of individuals and communities with social and natural environments over time. The unit's emphasis is on understanding and developing effective teaching and learning practices (including inquiry and problem solving), competence in planning and implementing quality teaching and learning programs, selecting relevant resources, designing appropriate assessment tasks and implementing both disciplinary and integrated approaches to Humanities and Social Sciences education in early childhood and primary school settings. Students develop content knowledge of a range of Humanities and Social Science subjects areas: history, geography, civics and citizenship, and economics and business.

Unit Information

Students analyse key features of the Humanities and Social Sciences learning area—a broad, interdisciplinary study which focuses on the interactions of individuals and communities with social and natural environments over time. The unit's emphasis is on understanding and developing effective teaching and learning practices (including inquiry and problem solving), competence in planning and implementing quality teaching and learning programs, selecting relevant resources, designing appropriate assessment tasks and implementing both disciplinary and integrated approaches to Humanities and Social Sciences education in primary school settings. Students develop content knowledge of a range of Humanities and Social Science subject areas taught in the primary school years: history, geography, civics and citizenship, and economics and business.
Students are able to (1) use relevant curriculum documents and resources to develop integrated learning programs with a focus on humanities and the social sciences; (2) demonstrate knowledge and understanding of how to embed literacy strategies within the learning area; (3) develop skills to evaluate, use and create resources for teaching and learning including information and communication technology (ICT) and other educational technologies; (4) demonstrate an understanding of a range of approaches to assessment relevant to the learning area; and (5) acquire a broad understanding of how to ensure the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander perspectives are evident when planning units of work in humanities and social sciences.

The lecture content is available to access prior to attending the tutorials.  It is an expectation that students have accessed both the lecture content and unit readings prior to attending the tutorial each week as the tutorial activities provide an opportunity to apply the content covered.

Assessment
*Further detail about each assessment and the rubrics are provided on LMS
Assessment 1: Reflection (10%)
Culturally inclusive pedagogy and practice
The purpose of this assessment is to reflect on some of the important considerations that teachers need to make (when planning, teaching, and selecting and using resources) to be culturally inclusive and embed Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander histories, cultures and perspectives when teaching the Humanities and Social Sciences curriculum.    
A reflection framework is provided on LMS.  
The word limit for this reflection is 1000 words.
Scholarly references must be used to support the reflection.
This assessment is linked to Unit Outcomes: 3 and 5
Assessment 2: Planning task (30%)
This assessment requires pre-service teachers to demonstrate their knowledge and understanding of how to use relevant curriculum documents and resources to plan two sequential lessons to address the History curriculum which includes a piece of children’s literature and embeds literacy strategies. 
There are two parts to this assignment: (1) a justification to explain why the particular piece of children’s literature was chosen and how it will be used in the lessons, and (2) two sequential lesson plans which includes the children’s literature and literacy strategies.
1.	Justification for the choice of children’s literature (600 words).
Provide the publishing details of the children’s literature including the title, author, year of publication and publisher (not included in the word count)
a)	Write a short justification which describes why the piece of children’s literature was chosen
b) Explain how the literature will be used in the lesson sequence. 
Scholarly references must be used to support the justification.  
  
2. Two sequential lessons to address specific content in the History curriculum (F-6) 
a)	Present two sequential lesson plans addressing a part of the History curriculum using the template provided.
b) Clearly explain how the children's literature will be used in the lesson/s
For example, will the piece of children's literature be used to introduce content and spark curiosity in order to promote discussion and inquiry questions, to consolidate and reinforce content previously covered, or will it be used to extend children’s ideas of content previously covered? 
*The piece of children’s literature does not need to be included in both lessons.  
c)	Embed relevant literacy strategies to support the literacy general capability
d)	Include SMART learning goals for each lesson
e)	Include all the resources required to teach these lessons
f) Use an inquiry-based pedagogical approach 
This assessment is linked to Unit Outcomes: 1, 2, 3

Assessment 3: Integrated program (50%)
The purpose of this assessment is to demonstrate knowledge and understanding of how to use relevant curriculum documents and resources to plan an integrated program which focuses on one subject in the Humanities and Social Sciences curriculum (either Geography OR Economics and Business OR Civics and Citizenship) and integration with another learning area (such as Geography and Mathematics, OR Geography and Science, OR Economics and Business and Mathematics, OR Civics and Citizenship and English) as well as links to relevant general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities.  
*A template for the integrated program is provided in LMS.  *There is no word count for this assessment.  
There are four parts to this assignment: (1) a five-week integrated program, (2) resources, (3) a summative assessment task and rubric, and (4) an e-portfolio entry.    

1.	Five-week integrated program
a)	Provide a five-week integrated program which addresses either the Geography, Civics and Citizenship or Economics and Business curriculum with another relevant learning area (English, Mathematics or Science) which also includes relevant integration with general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities.  
*Note that additional time in the school timetable can be used to address/teach some of the relevant content from the other learning area (English, Mathematics or Science) in this integrated program. If this approach is chosen this must be clearly identified in the program.  
*Your program should incorporate approximately one HASS lesson per week and one other lesson per week (relevant to the learning area).  
b)	Include key inquiry questions (big inquiry questions) for the program which will stimulate the children’s curiosity and promote thinking and engagement. 
c)	The five-week program should include an inquiry-based pedagogical approach
d)	Include a variety of learning experiences 
e)	Include diagnostic and formative assessment 

2.	Resources
a)	Provide all the resources for this program.  Ensure that the resources are engaging, age appropriate, aligned with the curriculum, culturally inclusive and of high quality. 
b)	Include at least one ICT tool 
c)	Include embedded URL links, images, handouts and sources with appropriate source acknowledgement. 
d)	Acknowledge resources which have been adapted or modified

3.	Summative assessment task 
a)	A summative assessment task must be included which assesses students’ knowledge and understanding of the curriculum content identified.  Provide the details of the assessment task including any instructions, handouts or information in the appendices. 
*Note that the summative assessment task can be planned to take place after the five-week program.
b)	Provide a rubric or marking key which can be used to provide feedback to the students about their performance on the assessment.  

4.	Eportfolio entry
a)	Provide an eportfolio entry, using sections from the program with additional reflection and annotation, as evidence that you are working towards meeting APST Standard 2.2 or 2.3.  
b)	Further information regarding the eportfolio entry is available on LMS.
This assessment is linked to Unit Outcomes: 1, 2, 3, 4

Resubmission Rule
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
