Academic Information

Content This unit provides training in modules 1-4 of the Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule-2nd Edition (ADOS-2), an internationally recognised gold standard assessment for ASD. Completion of the unit means that the student is able to administer and code the ADOS-2 for all age groups, and is accreditated to use this instrument for clinical purposes. The unit requires (1) extensive pre-workshop introductory reading on the theoretical background of the ADOS-2; (2) preparation for, and participation in, an intensive four-day workshop on ADOS-2 administration and coding; and (3) post-workshop practice in administering and coding the ADOS-2 for a variety of individuals across the liefespan.

By the end of the unit, students will have a thorough understanding of the ADOS-2 and the way in which this tool can be used during autism assessments. Students will understand how to select an ADOS-2 module and demonstrate valid administration of at least one module. Students will understand and demonstrate skills in coding the ADOS-2 and interpreting the algorithm. Finally, students will be able to summarise the observations gained during the ADOS-2 for reporting purposes. 
After the ADOS-2 training course, students will be required to submit the following pieces of work:
1. A video recording of ONE ADOS-2 assessment, completed with an autistic person and a 500 word reflection on the administration
2. Coding protocol for an ADOS-2 assessment that we provide, plus a written justification for a sub-set of the ADOS-2 items
3. 2000 word clinical report
This assessment is graded on a pass/fail basis and is the only assessment for the PSYC5522 unit. Further details are provided in Blackboard about the requirements for this submission. 
The assessment is due on 27th July 2021, 12pm (AWST), but can be submitted at any time prior to this date. 
Note on Assessment:
Formative Feedback: Students will receive feedback on the reliability of their coding through consensus coding discussions, which will occur each day of the course. Students will also receive feedback on their competence in administering ADOS-2 items to volunteer participants, in the small group sessions.
Word Count: The word limit for the reflective essay is 500 words, for the justification of your ADOS-2 codes is 500 words, and for your clinical report is 2000 words.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is delivered in an intensive, 3-and-a-half day workshop which includes didactic teaching, coding and discussing video-recorded ADOS-2 assessments. Students will also be given the opportunity to practice administering select subtests from the ADOS-2 with a volunteer and receive feedback on their administration in small groups. The ADOS-2 trainers will provide ongoing feedback on ADOS-2 coding throughout the workshop. The trainers will also provide feedback regarding your progress and engagement to the unit coordinator throughout the duration of the course.

Participation

Students are expected to:
- complete the pre-training activities, which include pre-reading and watching pre-recorded ADOS-2 assessments
- participate in group discussions during the workshops
- complete all ADOS-2 coding based on videos presented in the training, and contribute to subsequent consensus coding discussions
- prepare adequately for video-recorded administration
- make arrangements to complete the assessment by the due date

Other

Students need to ensure that they have capacity to record themselves administering an ADOS-2 module for the assessment. This means having access to an ADOS-2 kit (please make timely arrangements with reception at the Robin Winkler Clinic test library to borrow one if required), a willing participant and a video recording device with sufficient capacity to store approximately 60 minutes worth of footage. Students should also have the skills to transfer the video file from the recording device to an external storage device or upload the file to Cloudstor. 

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
