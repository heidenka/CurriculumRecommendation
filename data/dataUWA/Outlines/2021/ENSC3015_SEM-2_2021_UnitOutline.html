Academic Information

Content This unit covers the fundamentals of signal and system analysis, focusing on dynamical systems in time and frequency domains, transforms (Laplace, Fourier and Z) used in the analysis and design in signals and systems, sampling and reconstruction of signals, and analog and digital filters. Applications are drawn broadly from engineering including feedback and control, and communications and signal processing.

Unit Information

This unit covers the fundamentals of signal and system analysis, focusing on dynamical systems in time and frequency domains, transforms (Laplace, Fourier and Z) used in the analysis and design in signals and systems, sampling and reconstruction of signals, and analog and digital filters. Applications are drawn broadly from engineering including feedback and control, and communications and signal processing.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit content will be delivered through the lecture notes, you can self-assess your learning through the tutorials and you supplement your learning by accessing the recommended readings texts.
There will be some live overview lectures, but the pre-recorded lecture modules are the main resource to guide you through the content, highlight key results and concepts, and work through demos and problems. There will be small-group practice classes and individual consultation which will supplement the pre-recorded tutorial modules and go through selected questions and explain how to get to the solution.
You will need to access the lectures notes, pre-recorded lecture material and tutorials as indicated by the Preparation column in the Unit Schedule.
Your learning will be complemented by learning how to use the MATLAB / SIMULINK software to analyse systems and signals both as group lab work and individual exploration and discovery. To assist in your learning of concepts through short questions and worked problems there will be summative assessment consisting of two class tests during semester and a final examination. To motivate and engage in the engineering analysis and design there will be summative assessment consisting of group lab work using simulations of systems and signals which you can configure, run and observe. 

Unit Structure

Learning and Participation
There will be no formal lectures or tutorials as content learning will self-guided by accessing the online lecture notes and pre-recorded lecture and tutorial modules as indicated in the Preparation column of the Unit Schedule. This will be supplemented by live overview lecture and Q&A sessions where you can talk to the lecturer, interactive practice class sessions where you can interact with the tutor/facilitator and work together with other students, and scheduled lab sessions where you will work with a team of students to carry out hands-on application analysis and design from your learning under the guidance of the lab demonstrator.
There will be a 2 hour weekly live Face-to-Face (F2F) and/or Teams lecture/Q&A session each week during the 11:00-13:00 Monday Lecture timetable slot, this includes a welcome F2F session in week 1 that all students are expected to attend. Subsequent sessions from week 2 will be live streaming for all students as it is more convenient (unless otherwise requested). These sessions will include an Q&A/Quiz activity and an overview lecture of the weekly learning.
There will be a 2 hour weekly live F2F and/or Teams interactive practice class session each week during the 08:00-10:00 Thursday Lecture/Tutorial timetable slot. These are small-group tutorial and consultation style sessions where a tutor and facilitators will able to provide extra practice on the weekly tutorial questions, address your concerns on the tutorial material and provide extra examples, quiz questions, etc. to assist your learning. These work best if you work in small groups around a table for which the venue has been designed for. 
There will be 3 labs each of 3 hours duration running across 2 weeks during the timetabled Laboratory slots. Please be sure you know which session you need to attend as laboratory attendance is mandatory for the assessment. You need to attend one 3 hour session for each of the 3 labs you will need to complete. Lab instructions will be made available prior to your session so you can prepare and complete the pre-lab exercises before the lab. During the lab you will work with a team of students and after the lab you will work with your team to submit a group lab report.
These live and interactive lecture, practice and lab sessions are your opportunity to connect with the class community, other students and your lecturer/tutor/facilitator/demonstrator, and get the answers to the questions and concerns you have and learn how to apply and demonstrate (to yourself and others) what you have learned.
Assessment
This will mainly consist of the group lab work (30%) where students will demonstrate, analyse and apply their learning of signals and systems through the use of simulation tools; and class test and final exam (70%) where students will apply the theory and modelling of signals and systems through worked problem solutions. There will be two class tests held during the semester for the (20%) class test mark, and 3 lab sessions and group work for the (30%) lab mark. The final exam for the (50%) mark will be held during the end of semester exam period.
Each lab will require submission of a group lab report within 5 working days of your scheduled lab session (please check your timetable for your scheduled session). Specific instructions for the class tests and lab sessions will be provided through LMS in advance. 
There will also be a final examination at the end of semester. Details on how this will be conducted will be announced at a later date.
Face-to-Face (F2F) and Online students
The lab sessions will either be F2F in the computer lab or online interactive. Please be sure you attend the correct session type for your enrolment (as a F2F or online student).
The 11:00-13:00 Monday lecture/Q&A sessions will be live streamed through MS Teams (except for week 1 which will be F2F live). These sessions will be recorded.
The 08:00-10:00 Thursday practice class sessions will be F2F interactive in the venue (from 08:00) and alternate delivered to online students through MS Teams (from 08:30). If there is insufficient attendance the session will revert to MS Teams only. These sessions will NOT be recorded.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
