Teaching contingency plan (In response to Covid19 restrictions)

Unit objectives will not be affected by FTF restrictions, in the event of a Covid 19 related lockdown or social distancing event.

Activities and Assessments will be adapted to support digital / online preparation and development and submission of student work.

Students will be advised to obtain basic tools and materials to support both Drawing + Model-Making activities that may be accomplished at home if necessary.

Site Analysis emphasises both in-person observation, drawing and recording, in addition to digital research, mapping and compiling of information. We will adapt the Site Analysis component of the first assessment, if necessary, to stress digital / online methods.

Learning Structural Concepts will require both drawing and model-making. Activities will be designed to shift to a work-at home workflow if necessary.

Academic Information

Content This unit is concerned with the interrelationship between structures and sites. It provides students with constructional and site manipulation knowledge in relation to architecture and landscape design; an understanding of generic practices in the construction of traditional and contemporary buildings, landscapes and site works; and the applicability of environmentally conscious design in the context of constructed environments. The unit will also address indigenous cultural connections and rights in relation to land, basic drawing techniques, basic technical terminology and an understanding of materials.

Unit Information

This unit is concerned with the interrelationship between structures and sites. It provides students with constructional and site manipulation knowledge in relation to architecture and landscape design; an understanding of generic practices in the construction of traditional and contemporary buildings, landscapes and site works; and the applicability of environmentally conscious design in the context of constructed environments.
The unit will also address indigenous cultural connections and rights in relation to land, basic drawing techniques, basic technical terminology and an understanding of materials.
We will stress the interconnection between systems, forces, structures and materials, at multiple scales, from the scale of the City, Landscape and Site, to the scale of the Architectural Element, Structure and Material Constructions or Assemblies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The Unit will be delivered through Face To Face Lectures, and FTF Tutorials, with the exception of students enrolled in the online tutorial section.
Lectures will be recorded, however Face To Face attendance of lectures is highly stressed for this unit. Both FTF and Online students are expected to review lecture content, ask questions related to content or assignments, and actively participate in discussions of content, methods and assignments.
The tutorials will involve individual and group tasks, workshops and presentations (by internal staff and students), to assist in the understanding of concepts and techniques for learning and applying course material.
Lectures and Tutorial content, and associated tasks, will also develop an understanding of the relation between STRUCTURES + MATERIALS, and their integration with SITE towards the development of basic constructional logics or SYSTEMS.
Detailed "Project Briefs" will be issued in class, with the current or updated schedule and a more detailed description of assessment requirements.
SITE ANALYSIS
Direct observation and recording of Site features, measurements, etc. will be combined with Online or Digital Site Data, Research and Mapping Visualizations, as a means for students to develop analytical, research and documentation skills related to the understanding of what constitutes a SITE.
Each student will be required to visit a designated project site (local Perth Sites), at least once, individually or with other students, to gather necessary information for project analysis and documentation.
Students will use a combination of Hand Drawing  with Digital Drawing + Graphic software to accomplish tutorial tasks assessments.  
STRUCTURAL CONCEPTS + MODELLING
The learning and application of Basic Structural Logics and Systems will utilise intuitive drawing and model-making methods, primarily.

ADDITIONAL POLICIES OR INFORMATION
Self-Plagiarism
The submission of a single piece or body of work to multiple units for assessment constitutes self-plagiarism.  Self-plagiarism is considered a serious breach of academic integrity and is defined by the university as the recycling, copying, or disguising of previous work for assessment in a unit.
Assessment
In the case of folio units, to pass this unit, a student must: (a) achieve an overall mark of 50 per cent or higher for the unit; and (b) achieve the requisite requirements(s) or a mark of 50 per cent or greater, whichever is higher and specified in the unit outline, for the final folio component.
Moderation of marks
Students should be aware that marks can be adjusted in the period of time between a mark being given for an assessment item and the final mark in the official release of results by Student Administration. Students are referred to Part 1, Section 11.4 of the University Policy on: Assessment under Adjustment of Marks. https://www.uwa.edu.au/policy/-/media/Policy/Policy/Teaching-and-Research-Training/Learning-and-Teaching/Assessment/Assessment-Policy.rtf
Criterion based assessment is designed to provide a measure of students' performance that is directly relevant to the learning outcomes. This approach to assessment provides an indication of the specific knowledge and skills each student can demonstrate through an assessment task.  The use of moderation (as currently defined in the assessment policy below) can and should be used where appropriate. 
Moderation is defined in the University Policy on Assessment (the Policy) as “a process to ensure consistent, fair and reliable marking against academic standards” (definitions section). It is “employed to ensure appropriate and consistent marking standards within units, and that assessment outcomes are fair and reliable”.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
