Participation

Students are expected to attend each workshop, and to seek further guidance on content missed when they are not able to attend.

DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assignment 1: Reflective Teaching Practice (50%)
Assignment 1a: Reflective Teaching Practice – Micro-teaching (20%)
Create an activity and/or manipulative that is suitable for developing a mathematical concept for students in senior secondary school.
Justify the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity.
Simulate teaching using this activity and/or manipulative, focusing on questioning strategies and explanation / explication.
Respond to feedback to indicate the effectiveness of the activity and/or manipulative, or how you modified it.
Criteria for marking 
Creation of a learning activity or manipulative, and its suitability for developing a mathematical concept for students in senior secondary school.
Justification for the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity, including documentation and APA style referencing. 
Simulation of the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity. 
Reflection on the learning and teaching simulation, and response to feedback. 
Assignment 1b: Reflective Teaching Practice (30%)
What constitutes best practice in the learning and teaching of senior secondary mathematics?
To address this question, you are asked to identify, explore and debate the pedagogies, principles and practices of teaching the Australian senior secondary mathematics curriculum. Your response may be general in nature, or, if you wish, you may choose to focus on a specific senior mathematics subject (such as Specialist Mathematics and Mathematical Methods; General Mathematics (Mathematics Applications); Essential mathematics; Vocational education; International Baccalaureate); or on a specific aspect of teaching senior secondary mathematics (such as special needs students; EAL/D students; gifted and talented students; Indigenous students; or students in rural and remote educational contexts).
In your response, you are required to refer to a suitable conceptual framework for quality teaching, and to support your argument by reference to literature reporting the findings of research and presenting “best practice” (such as refereed and non-refereed journal articles; and professional sources such MAWA, AAMT, NCTM, departments of education, and curriculum authorities). 
If you wish, you may use a specific example to illustrate your argument, to highlight the demands of senior secondary school, or to clarify points of difference between junior and senior secondary school. 
Please use APA style referencing conventions for in-text references and in the reference list. 
Criteria for marking 
Clarity of explanation of what constitutes best practice in the learning and teaching of senior secondary mathematics; 
Level of insight into significant issues concerning the learning and teaching of senior secondary mathematics; and 
Depth of critical analysis of the literature. 
Please refer to the rubric for further marking guidelines. At the beginning of your assignment response, please include your student chosen assessment criterion with standards of achievement. 
Word limit: 1,500 words.

RESUBMISSION RULE:
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if
(a)	this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b)	they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c)	A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d)	Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e)	A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f)	Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
