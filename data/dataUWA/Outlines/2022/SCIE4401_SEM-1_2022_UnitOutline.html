Teaching contingency plan (In response to Covid19 restrictions)

 All lectures are online and can be watched at a time and location convenient to the student.

If students are unwell, they can miss the workshops without the need to apply for special consideration. But students should keep up with the progress of the unit by watching workshop recordings and submitting assessments online. If they have difficulty submitting on time, they should let the coordinator know and seek their advice on whether they need to apply for special considerations. 

This unit has two instructors and each can teach all the content in the unit. If both staff members are unwell, then material recorded from previous tutorial sessions will be used or the workshops will be taught by other staff members from the School. 

Academic Information

Content The unit introduces students to data collection and analysis to investigate questions relevant to research in the natural sciences. These methods, such as ANOVA and linear regression, are demonstrated and practised in a computer laboratory environment using experimental and observational data. Throughout the unit, scientific interpretation and hypothesis testing are emphasised. Students develop the knowledge to analyse data and explain the data using models. They learn to recognise the appropriate statistical technique for different types of data, and also learn how to obtain results using computer software. This unit equips students with knowledge and skills that they need to undertake research in their honours or master's thesis and beyond in private or public employment.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is taught online with recorded materials provided for lectures. Workshops/tutorials are held online, although students who are on campus can choose to attend a face-to-face version of the workshops/tutorials. The workshops/tutorials will be used to answer questions, explain/discuss statistical concepts, demonstrate statistical procedures and also to allow students to practice in an environment where they can interact with each other and with the instructor. However, note that you can make the most out of these sessions if you come prepared beforehand by having listened to recorded lectures and made effort to practice and solve problems independently.
For a successful learning experience, students need to: (1) Make effective use of learning resources provided (books, handouts, lecture notes, recorded videos, etc.); (2) Come prepared to the workshop/tutorial sessions by doing all required prior reading and preparation; (3) Do ALL tutorial and homework exercises; (4) Participate in discussions during the workshops and on the online discussion boards; (5) Seek help by posting questions online in the discussion board or by visiting instructors; (6) Invest in software skills (R/RStudio) early on; the first two weeks are critical and should be used effectively; (7) Make use of all channels available for learning: tutorials, recorded lectures, textbooks, discussion boards, and internet resources.

Participation

Pre-class preparation quizzes: Each week you are required to complete readings before the online sessions and answers a short list of questions related to your preparation work. These short quizzes are delivered online. The work you need to do is not onerous but will help you the make the most out of the online sessions and help you with future tests and assignment material. The total mark for this preparation work is 5 percent of your final mark.
Computer lab exercises: Each week there are a set of computer exercises for you to complete. The default option for you is to complete the exercises during your scheduled computer lab session, or in the hours following the session. Once you complete the exercises, you need to upload some aspect of your computer lab work to the LMS. See the lab exercise instructions for what you need to upload. Usually, you will be required to upload the R script files and/or results from the lab exercise. The mark given for uploaded lab work is 5 percent of your final mark.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
