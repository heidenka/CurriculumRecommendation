Academic Information

Content This course gives hands-on experience in understanding and obtaining the molecular structure via X-ray crystallography, the gold standard for determining structure in chemistry. Advanced concepts and techniques in chemical kinetics, molecular collision theory, and molecular dynamics simulations of bulk chemical properties are also presented. Concepts and theory will be supported by practical workshops, use of world-class modern equipment, software and visualisation tools. The unit emphasises core STEM skills sought after by employers and invaluable for second-year physical chemistry.

Unit Information

The first part of the unit covers reaction kinetics which concerns the rates of chemical reactions. We will review the basics of kinetics learnt in CHEM1001, and then move on to dive deeper into the mechanisms of reactions. The link between proposed reaction mechanism and the experimentally determined rate law will be established through several examples. Other topics to be covered are the Steady State Approximation (SSA), reaction mechanisms involving pre-equilibria, enzyme kinetics, and the kinetics of unimolecular reactions, polymerisation, chain reactions, and explosions.
The second part of the unit covers solid state chemistry. We will describe periodic crystal lattices before looking at techniques such as X-ray diffraction, which can be used to determine solid structures. Then we will uncover the bonding and properties of solids. We will then look at processes that occur on solid surfaces including adsorption and desorption.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

LECTURES
The best way to learn chemistry is by doing chemistry. Therefore, we have designed activities for you to do in lectures which will help you learn. It is strongly recommended that you attend as many lectures as you can so that you can ask questions to your lecturers and learn from your peers.
ASSIGNMENTS
There are two assignments, the Kinetics assessment and the Solid State Chemistry assessment. Each are worth 10% of the unit mark.
WORKSHOPS
Workshops are non compulsory, however, it is recommended that you attend as many as possible. Problems for the workshop will be posted on the LMS before the class. The lecturer will work with you to answer the problems during the workshop.
LABORATORIES
Laboratories are compulsory and you must attend. There are 5 laboratories in total, each assessment of the laboratory worth 6% of the unit mark. The laboratories are held in two parts. In the first week of labs you will complete the practical. The week after you will be in the computer lab completing the analysis of data and assessment.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
