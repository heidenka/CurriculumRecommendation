Unit Information

Building on the OPTM4103 unit, students will develop an extended understanding and appreciation of scientific research on evidence-based practice and challenges within our health system. Students will encounter more of the fascinating research that has informed our current understanding of the visual system and population health. Students will hone their skills in areas relating to the essentials of reading, writing and appraising vision science research, as well as refinement of skills in teamwork and peer review process. Topics of the unit will explain the need for researchers to possess knowledge of advanced scientific research methods, biostatistics, critical appraisal of publications, ethics approval and grant applications.
OPTM4107 is delivered in a blended-learning environment, which links pre-recorded lecture material with face-to-face seminars. There will also be guest lecturers, according to their expertise, delivering either in-person or online content appropriate to the unit content. The materials for the unit will be provided on a weekly basis in LMS. In 2022, lecturers and teaching staff will facilitate and support fully-online sessions in the instance that face-to-face sessions are not able to be conducted.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

pre-recorded lectures, seminars, team-based learning, journal discussion, and  undertaken quizzes in class consolidate and integrate learning from public health and research lectures. Moreover, guest lectures deliver relevant content from experts in the field of research and public health. 
Up to 3 hours of pre-recorded lectures each week.
11 x 2-hour seminars/team-based learning/journal discussion consolidating and integrating learning from public health and research lectures, including quizzes undertaken in class.
2 x 1.5-hour guest lectures delivering relevant content from experts in the field of research and public health.
Students are expected to dedicate a further 6 hours a week to private study of the subject matter contained in this unit, including weekly readings, private revision and completing take-home assignments and assessments.

Participation

Attendance at all sessions is an important learning experience for all students and therefore attendance is expected. In addition, external experts are invited, and full attendance is expected. All sessions delivered by guest presenters are compulsory. All seminar sessions are compulsory. Students must arrive in time to sign-in prior to the session commencing. Note that it is considered professional behaviour to attend all sessions on time. 
Most face-to-face sessions involve preparation beforehand. Please ensure this preparation is completed. 
If a student is unable to attend a session or complete their preparation, they will need to discuss this with the Unit Coordinator. If a student is unable to attend a session due to illness or for any other exceptional circumstance, the Unit Coordinator must be contacted as soon as practicable. Absences of more than one day due to illness will require a medical certificate to be presented. Failure to meet this requirement may/will result in a report of Professional Misconduct being made. 
Special consideration for missed assessments and absences of greater than two days from scheduled classes must be sought in line with UWA Policy.
Name badges are compulsory for all face-to-face sessions – the tutors and lecturers use a problem-based learning style where you need to participate in discussions in class and it is important that the tutor can refer to you by your name. 
In addition, we encourage you to evaluate through reflection your own skills and abilities. We value you as a member of the unit and we encourage you to learn by:
•	Providing a supportive learning environment that focuses on student needs
•	Documenting transparent learning outcomes
•	Documenting assessment methods clearly and only assessing documented learning outcomes
•	Providing many opportunities for students to receive feedback through formative      assessment
•	Facilitating online learning through:
1.	Providing relevant lectures and related documents in LMS
2.	Promoting online discussions
3.	Providing students with opportunities to evaluate the course and using past student evaluation to develop the course design
All correspondence will occur via the LMS and UWA email.  Please refer to these regularly to stay up to date.  If you are having any problems accessing the LMS please contact the Unit Coordinator.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
