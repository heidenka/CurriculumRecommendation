Academic Information

Content This practical unit draws together students from different levels of musical experience at pre-tertiary level, and consolidates essential musicianship, technical skills and repertoire in either composition or performance. For composers the emphasis is on development of technique and conceptual knowledge. For performers the emphasis is upon development of instrumental technique and performance skills including awareness of psychological, biomechanical/performance health concepts. This unit prepares students for the next unit in the sequence, MUSC1742 Practical Studies B.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is delivered by a combination of teaching and learning practices:
1. Composition Tutorials: each student in composition will receive a combination of group seminars and one-to-one instruction with reference to assigned composition projects
2. Composition: each student attends the weekly Composition Workshop which forms an integral part of the instruction process
3. Performance: One-to-one lessons: each student in performance will be assigned a one-to-one tutor and complete 6 hours of instruction per semester, allocated in consultation with your tutor
4. Performance: Workshops/Concert Practice/Repertoire Classes: provide opportunities for students to develop their practical work/acquire performance experience with input from peers and staff. These classes are optional but HIGHLY recommended
5. Student’s learning: is supported through LMS, which serves as a resource for course materials, and as a platform for student-student and student-lecturer/tutor online interaction
6. Self-directed study: enables students to acquire knowledge and skills independently and autonomously, whilst taking responsibility for the quantity and quality of their learning

Assessment overview
Because of the diversity of backgrounds represented by those enrolled in this unit, an individual program of study for the semester will be designed (a "Creative Contract") in consultation with the instrumental/vocal teacher and Chair of the area. The Creative Contract will outline the work to be undertaken for the semester, and specify clearly articulated examination requirements (Creative Contract template will be emailed to you). Specific details of Assessments vary between different streams and are organised by Chair of areas, but conform to the following.
Assessment Description
PERFORMANCE
Assessment 1 -
Performance (70%)              
A performance examination of approximately 10-15 minutes is required at the end of the semester, normally devoted to technical development. The Conservatorium of Music    will post an examination schedule before the end of the semester. Performance examinations normally occur during the University examination period at the end of each semester.
Assessment 2 -
Online Component (30%)
Completion of online tests and quizzes based upon Readings and texts provided on LMS throughout the semester.
Assessment 3 -
Reflective Practice Journal (0%)
An online Reflective Practice Journal is submitted weekly on LMS. While not marked, these are completed and represent a Pass/Fail component. These must be completed to Pass the unit.
COMPOSITION
Assessment 1 -
Composition Projects (70%)
Three projects assigned during semester devoted to developing compositional technique and stylistic/historical awareness: Each project consists of an original composition and typically an analysis or other related assignment(s). Each project will be developed over the course of four weeks via group seminars and one-to-one instruction.
Assessment 2 -
Collaborative Project (20%)
Each student participates in a collaborative project that is typically, semester-long. The projects can be a variety of activities related to composition. e.g. working with performers to realise a new, experimental and/or conceptual work, or collaborating with another composer on an improvisation project. All projects need to be approved by the Chair of Composition.
Assessment 3 - 
Workshop Participation (10%) 
Compulsory attendance; each student is expected to participate in class discussion within the weekly composition workshops and seminars.

Participation

Students are expected to attend all examinations, tests, assessments, lessons, and seminars for which they are enrolled, and to remain for the full duration of each activity. If a student is unable to attend a class or activity due to illness or other emergent personal circumstances , they must notify the unit lecturer/tutor (in writing) at least 24 hours in advance, with a medical certificate or other statutory documentation supporting the absence. Students with unsatisfactory attendance/participation may not have their work assessed and this will result in the award of a zero mark for the unit. Attendance Requirements: Composition / Instrumental / Vocal Lessons For all practical studies units, individual composition, instrumental, and vocal lessons are organised between student and teacher/tutor. If you are unable to attend a lesson, notice must be given directly to your tutor at least 24 hours prior to the scheduled lesson time, or the lesson will be considered forfeited. In the case of sudden illness or emergency, notice must be given to both the tutor and Conservatorium Office at the earliest possible time. As a courtesy to your tutor, you should make every possible effort to attend all lessons as scheduled; changes/cancellations to your scheduled times should only be made where absolutely essential. Composition students: note that attendance at the weekly Composition Class/Seminar is mandatory. Attendance Requirements: Concerts / Workshops / Concert Practice It is not compulsory to attend additional concerts or workshops (except: Composition Workshop is mandatory for composition students), however, students who do attend such events typically receive a more diverse education and achieve better results than their peers. Excused Absences Students may apply for excused absences in the normal manner directly to the ensemble/class director (it is normal for this to be done in writing - e-mail is acceptable), or through the special consideration process. However, please note the following will not be considered reasons for an excused absence (and will be recorded as an unexcused absence):
1. Applied Music Lesson
2. Working or Teaching
3. Performances with another non-Conservatorium of Music ensemble unless specifically approved in advance by the Chair of the area and the Head of Conservatorium
4. Family events (eg birthdays, birth of niece, family meal, take brother to airport etc)
Applying for Leave Students must seek permission from the Head of Conservatorium before participating in external activities that are likely to prevent full attendance at classes and/or ensembles. In such cases a leave of absence may be granted for certain external engagements or activities, provided that such absence is assessed as being likely to make a contribution to a student’s overall studies. The support of a student’s lecturer/tutor is required in all cases. Applications for a leave of absence must be submitted to the Head of Conservatorium in writing. Application forms are available at www.student.uwa.edu.au/course/forms. Non-compliance with these regulations may result in failing the unit in question or a penalty in the final assessment.

Hearing protection
Listening to and performing music at high decibel levels can pose a risk to your hearing health. Cumulative and/or prolonged exposure to moderately high decibel levels, or short exposure to very high decibel levels, can adversely affect and permanently damage your hearing. The maximum average safe level of exposure time and sound level per day for ALL sound exposure including your music activities is 85dB for 8 hours—the level equivalent, for example, to playing or listening to unamplified chamber music at close range. Listening to recorded music at high volume (for example using an iPod with headphones) can contribute significantly to risk levels in combination with exposure to live music practice, rehearsal and performance. Students in Music performance units are advised to take precautions to protect their hearing, including the use of musicians’ earplugs when exposed to high decibel levels. Musicians’ earplugs have been shown to effectively filter excessive noise and thereby reduce the risk of hearing damage and are now widely used and promoted in the music industry. It is expected that students who will be regularly exposed to high decibel levels in music practice, rehearsal and performance in Conservatorium units and performance activities will take necessary precautions to protect their hearing. The Conservatorium of Music will provide, free of charge, a single pair of generic musicians’ earplugs per student per year to students enrolled in a performance unit. Students can collect their free musicians earplugs from the Conservatorium Office. Students with consistently high levels of exposure may need to contact a professional audiologist for advice concerning earplugs appropriate for their specific needs, to ensure the maximum musical and health benefit. Further information on hearing health for musicians, including sample instrument-specific decibel levels and exposure times, can be found in the hearing module of soundperformers.com: http://author.soundperformers.com/moodle/mod/lesson/view.php?id=52 Access to soundperformers.com is restricted to current students enrolled in a Music performance unit. Students enrolled in MUSC1341 will receive a username and password automatically. Students enrolled in other Music units may request a username and password by contacting Assoc. Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
