Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit applies a blended model of learning and teaching, similar to a "flipped" model. 
Preparation for workshops (viewing of pre-recorded lecturettes, and completing required reading)  is expected. 
The following teaching and learning strategies may be used in workshops to develop deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the weekly lecturettes and required readings: individual, small group and large group activities; small group and large group discussions; modelling of specific learning and teaching strategies.
The following resources may be used in this unit: Textbooks, peer-reviewed articles, video clips, ICT, and lecturer and tutor created resources.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assignment 1: Research Report (40%)
There are two parts to this assignment:
Part a – a written paper (word limit 1500 words, not including end-references);
Part b – a poster.
Part a (30%)
Write a rationale for the explicit teaching of General Capabilities in the Australian classroom.
In your rationale, you will need to demonstrate an understanding of:
the purpose(s) of education; 
the economic, policy and other drivers that might contribute to the development and implementation of General Capabilities; 
the ways in which capabilities being foregrounded will benefit students.
Your rationale should draw from academic and other appropriate reference sources. 
Part b (10%)
Present your rationale as a poster that would be suitable to display on the wall of your classroom for your students. 
In designing your poster, consider the use and typical features of posters. You might include such things as: title and author; key points; the use of images that complement the subject matter; and the use of a key quote. Please do not simply list the General Capabilities. And make sure that you avoid clutter or visual chaos that would distract the reader.
Word limit: 1500 words (not including end-references), plus a poster.
Marking criteria:
rationale for the explicit teaching of General Capabilities in the Australian classroom;
references to the literature, and referencing;
presentation of rationale as a poster;
a student chosen criterion, with standards of achievement (please include your student chosen marking criterion, and the standards of achievement, at the beginning of your response to the assignment task).
A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS.

Assignment 2: Planning for teaching (40%)
Please refer to the Unit Schedule, which lists the General Capability for each of the weeks 2 to 9 of the teaching period. Groups for each week will be negotiated during the workshop for Week 1.
There are two parts to this assignment:
Part a – a collaborative workshop presentation;
Part b – a lesson plan.
Part a – Collaborative workshop presentation (20%)
Your workshop presentation will provide you with the opportunity to work with colleagues to explore a range of learning and teaching ideas and activities related to the development of a General Capability, and to make a 15 minute presentation to the workshop group sharing what you have discovered and created. It is an opportunity to practise some teaching skills and to try something innovative. 
Before the workshop, your group will need to provide a plan for the presentation. This plan should be sent by email to your workshop tutor, and should include: 
the expected outcomes of the presentation;
a brief overview of the general capability, and why it is important; 
some examples of learning and teaching activities illustrating how the general capability may be developed (if these are subject specific, also include ways in which the ideas may be applied in other subject areas);
a brief rationale for the chosen activities, including links to the Australian Curriculum and other literature;
other related notes, ideas, and suggestions for developing the general capability;
references; 
and a clear indication of each individual contribution made to the group presentation.
Time limit for the Group presentation: Please plan the presentation so that the total time is not longer than 15 minutes.
Marking criteria:
the collaborative plan containing the elements above;
the quality of information presented by the group; 
the applicability for each subject area of the ideas and resources presented by the group; 
the creativity of the group presentation; 
individual contributions to the group presentation.
After your presentation, please upload your notes and resources to the appropriate discussion forum on the unit LMS website.
Part b – Lesson plan (20%)
Create a lesson plan in your (major, or minor) teaching subject area that will foreground the development of the General Capability in the learning and teaching activities.
Justify your planning by referring to academic sources and other relevant documentation.
Your lesson plan should:
include a brief introduction, overview or outline describing the focus and intentions of the lesson;
include the relevant lesson outcomes addressing content descriptions from the Australian Curriculum and their elaborations relevant to your lesson;
identify where and how the General Capability of the Australian Curriculum is being developed;
include a detailed series of lesson steps (including approximate time allocation) indicating the sequence and transitions for activities (use a suitable lesson structure, such as Bybee’s 5Es with timing 1:3:3:1, or similar);
specify areas for diﬀerentiation, including learning support for low achieving students;
identify ideas for extension and enrichment;
include copies of investigations, handouts, and worksheets; images of manipulatives; and lists of URLs of websites, e-resources, ICT, and other resources that will be used during the lesson; 
be well organised so that another teacher could readily implement them;
Word limit: 1000 words plus appendices.
Marking criteria:
overall lesson structure, teaching approach, rationale, educational outcomes, lesson aim or key questions, identification of prior knowledge, and lesson timing;
lesson introduction, key learning activities, lesson conclusion, and assessment;
applied knowledge of the curriculum in the learning activities, resources and manipulatives used;
incorporation of differentiation and inclusion of enrichment;
originality and quality of resources used in learning and teaching activities;
a student chosen criterion, with standards of achievement (please include your student chosen marking criterion, and the standards of achievement, at the beginning of your response to the assignment task).
A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS.

Assignment 3: Teaching Resources (20%)
Create a set of three teaching resources that will develop the General Capabilities literacy, numeracy, and critical and creative thinking, set within the context of a learning and teaching project.
The project will address specific outcomes from the Australian Curriculum Years 7 to 10.
The learning and teaching context may be either subject discipline specific (i.e. your subject area), or inter-disciplinary (i.e. integrated curriculum, or cross curriculum).
The teaching resources may aim to develop either each of the specified general capabilities separately, or various combinations of two or three of the specified general capabilities together.
For your teaching resources, please include:
an introduction to the project; 
the scope of project – learning area(s), year level(s), AC content covered, outcomes;
a project outline; 
resources; 
a brief explanation of, or justification for, the place of the resources in the project. 
Word limit 1000 words, plus three resources.
Marking criteria: 
design of learning and teaching project, and its supporting documentation;
creation of three teaching resources;
appropriateness and eﬀectiveness of each of the three teaching resources in developing one or more of the required general capabilities, and with reference to the documentation in #1 above;
a student chosen criterion (at the beginning of your assignment, please include your student chosen assessment criterion, and the standards of achievement for this criterion).
A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass each component of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall.  Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if:
(a)	this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b)	they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c)	A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d)	Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e)	A resubmission is normally due one week a􀀁er being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f)	Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.


Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
