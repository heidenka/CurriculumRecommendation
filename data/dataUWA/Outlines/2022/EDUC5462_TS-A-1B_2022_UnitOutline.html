Academic Information

Content This unit provides an introduction to the knowledge, skills and competencies required by teachers of secondary school mathematics, focusing on the active teaching and learning of mathematics in the secondary school. Students develop an understanding of how students learn mathematics, and explore current best practice for teaching number, algebra, measurement, geometry, statistics and probability. Key themes respond to the Australian Curriculum and the WA syllabus to address mathematical understanding, fluency, problem solving and reasoning in teaching and learning, and the assessment and evaluation of student learning and of teaching practice. Students develop a broad range of teaching strategies and apply their knowledge and skills to prepare lesson plans, topic programs and thematic programs.

Unit Information

This first Mathematics Curriculum unit provides an introduction to the knowledge, skills and competencies required by teachers of secondary school mathematics. It focuses on how best to help school students achieve learning outcomes associated with junior secondary mathematics.
Please see the unit LMS for further information about this unit.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

To help students develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in each topic, the following teaching and learning strategies may be used in this unit: lectures, presentations, small and large group workshop activities, small and large group discussions, cooperative group tasks, online exploration, and online sharing of resources. The following resources may be used in this unit: textbooks, peer-reviewed articles, video clips, ICT, commercially available resources and manipulatives, pre-recorded lectures, and lecturer created resources and manipulatives.

DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assignment 1 Lesson planning (50%) 
1a. Micro-teaching or (perhaps virtual) presentation of a learning and teaching activity (creation and implementation of a manipulative) (20%)
Create an activity and/or manipulative that is suitable for developing a mathematical concept for students in junior secondary school.
Justify the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity.
Simulate teaching using this activity and/or manipulative. 
Respond to feedback to indicate the effectiveness of the activity and/or manipulative, or how you modified it.
Criteria for marking 
Creation of a learning activity or manipulative, and its suitability for developing a mathematical concept for students in junior secondary school.
Justification for the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity, including documentation and APA style referencing. 
Simulation of the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity. 
Reflection on the learning and teaching simulation, and response to feedback. 
Note
This assignment is designed for you to present your learning and teaching activity to a small group of colleagues, and to reflect on the outcome of the presentation; in turn, you will provide feedback to each of these colleagues on their presentations to your group. 
The activity or manipulative may be one that you have used in Assignment 1a Lesson Planning, but different from the manipulative used in Assignment 2 a ii.
1b. Sequence of (three) lesson plans (30%)
Using the lesson plan framework outlined in workshops (and provided on the unit website), you are required to plan three consecutive 60 minute lessons for a class of 30 mixed ability students in Years 7, 8, 9 or 10. 
You may select outcomes from any topic (or topics) from the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics, Years 7 to 10. It is recommended that you choose a topic that allows the development of concepts through exploration, investigation, and the use of concrete teaching aids and manipulatives.
Justify key components of your lesson planning. Your justification should begin with an overview of your developing philosophy for learning and teaching, and a brief outline describing the rationale for and focus of the lesson sequence and where it may fit in a program. 
Each lesson plan should include the following: 
relevant documentation from the Australian Curriculum - Mathematics, WA DoE and WA SCSA curriculum, syllabus and other support material, including the Mathematics sequence of content (“scope and sequence”) document; 
assumptions relating to prior knowledge and skills; 
specific learning outcomes for the lesson; 
a list of resources and materials required; 
a detailed outline of the lesson including key questions, an introduction and closure, the sequence of activities, transitions between activities, and an indication of time allocation; the place in the lesson for learning support, extension, and enrichment; the use of technology, including assistive technology; embedded literacy, numeracy, critical thinking, problem solving activities, and other aspects of the General Capabilities and Cross Curriculum Priorities; 
an indication of any student and/or teacher evaluation; 
all worksheets, handouts, manipulatives (please include images of these), and so on, that will be used during the lesson. 
Criteria for marking 
•	Overall lesson structure, teaching approach, rationale, educational outcomes, lesson aim or key questions, identification of prior knowledge, and lesson timing.
•	Lesson introduction, key learning activities, lesson conclusion, and assessment.
•	Applied knowledge of the curriculum in the learning activities, resources and manipulatives used, and areas of differentiation identified.
•	Incorporation of (at least one of) the proficiency strands, embedding of (at least some of) the general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities, and inclusion of remediation, enrichment and extension.
•	Student chosen criterion, with standards of achievement.
A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS.
Please include your student chosen marking criterion, and the standards of achievement, at the beginning of your response to the assignment task.
Assignment 2 Programming (50%) 
2a. Analysis of a selection of teaching resources (20%)
This assessment item comprises four short tasks. 
Task 1 
Four of the professional organisations available to support teachers of mathematics are known as MAWA, AAMT, AMT, and MERGA. For each organisation, write the full name of the organisation, a description of the aims of the organisation, and a summary of the resources and professional learning opportunities provided by the organisation. Include in your description a discussion of how the organisation can support graduate teachers. 
Membership of MAWA is free for students of initial teacher education programs, and with this membership also comes free membership of AAMT. If you are not already a member of MAWA, you are asked to apply to become a member.
Criteria for marking 
Concise summary of the aims, resources and services of each organisation.
Proof of membership of MAWA.
Task 2
This task requires you create or replicate a manipulative suitable for teaching a mathematics concept to students in junior secondary school and to demonstrate its use to your colleagues.
Criteria for marking 
Creation or replication of a manipulative suitable for teaching a mathematics concept to students in junior secondary school.
Clarity of the demonstration and explanation of its application to teach the mathematical concept.
Note
The manipulative may be one that you will use in Assignment 1a Lesson Planning, but different from the manipulative used in Assignment 1b.
Task 3
This task requires you to collate five (5) of your favourite resources that are suitable for teaching a mathematics concept to students in junior secondary school. 
Your resources should comprise one resource from each of numeracy, number and algebra, measurement and geometry, statistics and probability, and problem solving.
For each of these items, write a brief paragraph describing why it is a suitable learning and teaching resource or activity. Indicate, if appropriate, how the resource could be improved.
You are asked to “post” each of these resources in the appropriate discussion forum on the unit LMS.
Criteria for marking 
Suitability of each resource to teach a mathematics concept to students in junior secondary school. 
Description of each resource and how it is implemented.
Posting of each resource on the discussion forum.
Note
I would like you to make regular contributions to the Discussion Forum. 
Task 4
For this task, you are asked to critically analyse a learning and teaching resource posted by one of your colleagues on a discussion forum on the unit LMS.
Include in your analysis an indication of how the resource may be adapted for varying educational contexts, and how it may be adapted and improved to meet the learning needs of students in an inclusive classroom.
Criteria for marking 
Critical analysis of the suitability of the resource for a learning and teaching activity to develop a mathematical concept for students in junior secondary school. 
Indication of how each resource may be adapted for varying educational contexts.
Indication of how each resource may be adapted and improved to meet the learning needs of students in an inclusive classroom.
2b. Program (topic, or thematic, or cross- or integrated curriculum) (30%)
You are required to prepare a learning and teaching program appropriate for a lower secondary mathematics class and for 10 hours of lessons (that is, presented over approximately 2 weeks).
Your program should demonstrate variety in learning tasks, teaching approaches, resources and assessment items. 
Justify key components of your program. 
Your justification should include: a program rationale encompassing your own teaching philosophy based on sound theory; learning outcomes with links to the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics; cross references to the WA syllabus; references to suitable curriculum support documents; an account of the different teaching and assessment approaches taken; and a description of the students for whom the program is designed. 
Although it is an overall and general planning document, your program should be highly structured, and each lesson should include the relevant statement(s) from the curriculum and syllabus documents and specific objectives for each lesson, and an overview of the learning experiences planned (please include at least one activity that makes use of an appropriate website or software package). 
Lists of examples of materials, resources and ideas that could be used to engage, explore, explain, elaborate, extend, enrich, and evaluate, should be included, either for each lesson, or for the whole program (or for both, with cross references).
Your program should include planning for assessment, including four assessment tasks designed to assess the quality of students’ understanding of content relevant to the program. Note that these four tasks should reflect variety, and should not be just summative in nature. 
Please note that you are not expected to plan 12 individual lessons. 
Criteria for marking 
Overall program structure; conceptual framework and teaching approach(es); rationale and justification for key elements of the program.
Educational outcomes and objectives; resources, ICT, numeracy, literacy, cross-KLA, & documentation; APA referencing style.
Applied knowledge of the curriculum in the learning and teaching activities. 
Program and classroom management, including scaffolding of student learning, remediation, enrichment, extension, assessment and evaluation.
Student chosen criterion, with standards of achievement.
A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS.
Please include your student chosen marking criterion, and the standards of achievement, at the beginning of your response to the assignment task.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach):
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if –
(a)	this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b)	they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c)	A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d)	Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e)	A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f)	Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
