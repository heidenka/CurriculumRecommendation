Academic Information

Content This unit considers the different types of information that investors and analysts use to assess a firm's performance and value. It explores the usefulness, quality and reliability of company reports and other publicly available information sources. Analytical techniques covered include ratio analysis; fundamental analysis; cash flow analysis; pro forma analysis; inventory and non-current asset analysis; and off-balance sheet analysis.

Unit Information

Welcome to FINA5523: Financial Statement Analysis and Business Valuation. I am Dr. Mahmoud Agha, the lecturer and unit coordinator. This unit covers the different types of information that investors and analysts use to assess a firm's performance and value. It explores the usefulness, quality and reliability of company reports and other publicly available information sources. Techniques covered include: valuation models; fundamental analysis; pro forma analysis; evaluating the quality of financial reports; and multinational operations.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The delivery of the unit involves a series of three-hour lecture each teaching week. During the lecture, a broad overview of the topic will be provided,  important issues will be highlighted, and numerical examples will be discussed to enforce your understanding. You are expected to read the assigned readings to bring yourself up to speed on the issues. Participation and interaction during the lecture are encouraged. In addition, you can approach me for help with unit content or queries about the assessments. I am available in office almost every day; please contact me by e-mail to set an appointment if you need any help. At the end of each lecture, you will be given a set of questions to attempt and solve at home. Then, these questions will be solved by the lecturer in the last hour of the next lecture. You are expected to interact with the lecturer, and , of course, ask him to clarify any difficult point. The emphasis of the unit is on developing skills and having a grounded understanding of business valuation.

Unit Structure

The unit is presented over a series of weekly three-hour lectures. The lectures introduce the topic and discuss numerical examples. The last hour of the lecture will be used as a workshop where concepts from the previous week are reinforced by solving a set of questions that cover the concepts discussed in the former lecture. Please refer to the Class Allocation System CAS page for the lecture timetable.
There are three assessment items for this unit: a series of online short tests (4 assessable tests), group assignment, and a final exam. The group assignment (Team Project) is a major assessment item in this unit; therefore, it is very important that you attend the lectures to get familiar with your groupmates and keep in touch with them outside the class to do the group assignment. All members of a group are expected to contribute to the assessment, and if you are absent you will be letting your team mates down. An online tool SPARKPLUS will be used to assess individual contributions to team project. You will provide feedback to, and receive feedback from, your team members. Based on a series of answers from each team member SPARKPLUS automatically produces two weighting factors: your RPF and SAPA. Your individual assignment mark will be equal to the group mark times the RPF score you receive from your groupmates on SPARKPLUS. The RPF score ranges between 0 and 1; therefore, make sure that you do your part of the team project in order to earn the full team mark. You will have an opportunity to value a business in your team project. You will use real data and understand the problems facing the business and use the skills acquired in the unit. The team project is comprehensive in that it involves a topic that enables you to use your skills in working in a team. You will be allocated randomly into groups in week 4 and the assignment will be delivered to you in this week as well.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
