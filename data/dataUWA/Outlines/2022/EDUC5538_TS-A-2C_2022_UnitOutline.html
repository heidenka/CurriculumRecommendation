Academic Information

Content This unit focuses on the development of knowledge and skills essential for establishing and maintaining productive and safe school learning environments. This includes understanding the significance and complexity of relationships with students, parents and colleagues. The importance of teacher behaviour and communication is also a component of this unit. Contemporary legal issues and responsibilities impacting upon the classroom teacher will be evaluated. Student wellbeing in the classroom and broader school environment is also examined.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The following teaching and learning strategies may be used in this unit: lecture, presentation, small and large group discussion, case study analysis and evidence-based strategies. These strategies will allow students to develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the weekly on-line lecture and required readings. The following resources may be used in this unit: textbooks, peer-reviewed articles, video clips, ICT and lecturer/tutor created resources.
It is expected that students will actively contribute to their own and others learning.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:
Assignment 1 - Critical review & Discussion (40 marks)
PART 1: Critical Review (30%) This assignment comprises an extended critical analysis and reflection on a concept, aspect or issue about education, relationships and classroom learning management. Write a critical analysis focusing on the key issues related to this topic. 
PART 2: TWO ONLINE DISCUSSIONS (10%)
You are asked to take part in two online discussions. The discussions are based on practical issues that are designed to make you think about possible scenarios you may encounter in a school. You may include another issue/situation that you may have experienced. Aim for between 200-300 words. When completing your discussion post please write in a formal professional manner. Introduce yourself and your major and minor curriculum areas. After you write each discussion post you should read through other posts from your peers and respond to one post using about 150 words. For example, are there similarities, did you respond the same or differently, did your response provoke another situation, or would you respond differently next time?
Assignment 2 - 40 marks
This unit has a focus on student wellbeing and being aware that there are students who have support needs beyond the average. You are expected to consider what would be required to make your classroom safe and welcoming for students who face difficulties. 
Consider in detail how you would develop a programme to support a positive classroom environment for:
(Choose one from the list)
1.	A student with a poor attention span. (not necessarily ADHD).
2.	A student with poor language skills.
3.	A student who experiences anxiety.
4.	A student who is being bullied by a classmate.
5.	A student from a disruptive family background.
This assignment is to encourage you to think ahead to when you are on practicum and likely to encounter students who have any number of conditions/problems in their lives. No matter what the issues are with the students they are in your classroom and you are responsible for their learning and wellbeing, what will you have to consider? 
Assessment 3 - Law assignment (20 marks): During this in-class, written assessment, students will respond to a scenario which will assess their knowledge of the legal responsibilities of teachers, schools and departments or organisations.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.
Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
