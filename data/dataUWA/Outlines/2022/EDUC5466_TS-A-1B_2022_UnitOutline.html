Academic Information

Content This unit provides students with an overview of the humanities and social science learning areas in Western Australia. Students work with a range of school-based, state-based and national curriculum resources to develop the knowledge and competencies needed to devise lesson plans, create programs and develop a variety of learning strategies. Students explore a range of resources and instructional methods and develop and apply appropriate assessment practices. Contemporary issues related to the support of student literacy and numeracy, the use of ICT, values education, global education, and their implication to the teaching of humanities and social sciences are also explored.

Unit Information

EDUC5466, Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS) Curriculum I, is designed to introduce Master of Teaching (MTeach) pre-service teachers to the knowledge, skills, concepts and competencies required for teachers of Year 7-10 HASS courses. It focuses on the Knowledge and Understandings; Skills and Key Concepts in the HASS Learning Area of the Western Australian Curriculum and Assessment Outline. The unit also provides opportunities for MTeach pre-service teachers to develop competencies which are applicable regardless of the curriculum structure. You will develop an understanding of how students learn most effectively in the HASS Learning Area and be able to use this understanding to plan and implement learning programs. As the outcomes in this Learning Area encompass concepts from Civics and Citizenship, Economics and Business, Geography and History disciplines, you are therefore required to master concepts and content outside of your specialist undergraduate discipline. This unit aims to develop resourceful teachers who are sufficiently flexible and creative to initiate and implement effective teaching strategies and programs. It endeavours to encourage pre-service teachers to be sensitive to the needs and characteristics of learners as individuals and in groups, and as such, various teaching and learning strategies and approaches will be considered. An understanding of assessment strategies, including informal and formal, diagnostic, formative and summative approaches to assess student learning will be developed. Additionally, unit assessments will be structured to allow MTeach pre-service teachers to demonstrate their capacity to organise learning activities and provide clear directions to their students. Formative feedback from the Unit Coordinator, peers and group discussions will help in this respect. It is hoped the unit will develop in all MTeach pre-service teachers, an enthusiasm for the HASS Learning Area; an awareness of your own attitudes towards teaching; and a reflective approach to ensure the on-going effectiveness of your teaching practice.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is delivered on campus but with the option for students to join workshops online. Various teaching and learning strategies may be used in this unit: lecture, student presentation, small and large group discussion as well as various educational strategies to help students develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the weekly workshops and readings. Guest presenters from a number of HASS-related organisations will speak to students and run interactive workshops where applicable.

Unit Structure

Students attend one (3 hour) workshop per week for 10 weeks. Seminars will be delivered on campus on Mondays between 3pm and 6pm. A link to join the workshop online can be found in the weekly content folders.

ASSESSMENT DETAILS
1) Lesson planning (weighting 40%)
You are required to plan Three Consecutive 60 minute lessons for a HASS class of 30, mixed ability students in Years 7, 8, 9 or 10. You are to focus on content derived from the Western Australian Curriculum and Assessment Outline (7-10): Civics and Citizenship, Economics and Business, Geography or History.  Learning Outcomes 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9
2) Learning program (weighting 50%)
You are required to prepare a Two-Week (approximately 8 hours) learning program for either a year 7, 8, 9 or 10 HASS subject. Most schools timetable 4 x 60-minute lessons per week in HASS and a unit of work is normally assumed to be for one term which is 10 weeks in duration, or 40 hours. You will therefore be planning only a section of the unit of work and consequently, you may choose whether to plan for the beginning, the middle or the end. As with Assessment 1, you are to focus on content derived from the Western Australian Curriculum and Assessment Outline (7-10): Civics and Citizenship, Economics and Business, Geography or History.  Learning Outcomes 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9
3) Professional Activities (weighting 10%)
Communicating Concepts Group Oral Presentation - As part of your school experience preparation, in groups of three or four, you will deliver a short 10-minute presentation to your peers. You are to present a humanities concept or event accurately in a novel or creative way to ignite students’ interest in learning more about the concept or event. The purpose of this assessment is to provide you with an opportunity to work in a professional capacity with your peers, develop confidence in your presentation skills and to work with the curriculum documents. RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
