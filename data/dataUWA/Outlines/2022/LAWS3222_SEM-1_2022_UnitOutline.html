Teaching contingency plan (In response to Covid19 restrictions)

TEACHING ARRANGEMENTS

UWA Law School continues to value and prioritise face-to-face learning. However, as detailed below, this unit already has pre-readings, recorded lectures and other materials on the unit webpage. Further, due to the ever-evolving pandemic, protecting our student body and minimising the disruption to student learning, the student experience, and your academic progression is our priority. In light of this, after having considered a range of options, students have been given the option to attend face-to-face OR online tutorials for the duration of this semester.

Students who elect a face-to-face tutorial but are required to isolate for a period pursuant to Government guidelines, will be able to attend an online tutorial for the duration of their isolation.

In the event of future COVID-19 restrictions preventing face-to-face classes, all workshops and tutorials will be delivered online for the relevant period. Students who cannot attend workshops due to Covid19 will be able to dial in online.

In the event that teaching staff are impacted by Covid, they will teach online. If too unwell to teach any of the scheduled workshops or tutorials, recordings or rescheduled online workshops/tutorials will be arranged as soon as possible.

COVID PROTOCOLS

The Law School has developed protocols to deal with those instances where a student has been in a lecture or tutorial and, following the class, is required to isolate and undergo a COVID test under Government guidelines.

1. The student is expected to inform their Unit Coordinator/tutor/lecturer that they are undergoing a COVID test for any reason - they are symptomatic, are a close contact, were at an exposure site, etc.

2. All other students/staff that were present in that class will be informed of this fact, ensuring the student's/ identity and circumstances are kept confidential.

3. Under current Government guidelines the other students/staff are not required to isolate or be tested but they may wish to do so.

4. The student is expected to inform their Unit Coordinator/tutor/lecturer of the outcome of the test who will in turn inform others in the class.

5. If the student tests positive, all members of the class must follow government guidelines regarding testing and isolation.

Unit Information

This unit gives students a basic understanding of how the Australian taxation laws apply to a wide variety of business and financial transactions. The first part of the unit focuses on income tax. The unit then explores GST, FBT and taxation administration.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is comprised of 12 online learning modules which each cover a different area of taxation law. The modules can be accessed through the unit website on UWA's Learning Management System at www.lms.uwa.edu.au. Each online learning module is designed so that you can learn at your own pace and from the comfort of your own home! It is suggested that students complete the online learning modules in accordance with the unit schedule set out below.
Each online learning module is broken into four sections:
What is this module about?
This section gives a general overview of what will be covered in the module.
Watch it!
You should start each module by watching the video clips which are intended to guide you through the relevant legislation, case law and readings. Please note that the video clips are not intended to cover every aspect of the course in detail but, instead, are designed to explain key concepts and highlight important issues.
Read it!
Once you have watched the video clips, you should complete the required readings which are listed in this section of the module. The readings are intended to cover the finer points of the concepts which may have been discussed in the video clips. These readings will form part of the assessable content for the unit. Most of the readings come from the compulsory text book. However, other readings (including rulings and additional materials) are noted under the module.
Apply it!
Once you have watched the video clips and completed the readings, you should consolidate your learning by attempting to answer the questions listed in this section of the module. Once you have answered the questions, you can then check how you went by comparing your answers to the Answer Guide. You will also have the opportunity to apply what you have learnt in each module by attending the interactive workshops and tutorials. 
In addition to the online learning modules, there are a series of interactive workshops which are designed to support you in completing your assessments in this unit. There are two workshops to help you complete the assignment and four workshops to help you prepare for the final exam. Note the workshops that are to help you complete the assignment have been scheduled to ensure that you begin your preparation for the assignment earlier in the semester. Please ensure you have a draft of the assignment completed (to the extent possible) for the assignment workshop. 
The interactive workshops are not like "traditional lectures" where there is no student and lecturer interaction. Instead, the workshops will be hands-on, practical and directly relevant to your assessments. The interactive workshops will involve you working together in groups (with the assistance of the lecturer) to complete a variety of tasks relating to the assessments. Given that the interactive workshops are based almost exclusively on group work, recordings will only work to an extent. You are therefore strongly encouraged to attend all workshop sessions. If you have a laptop, you are encouraged to bring it with you to the interactive workshops as some activities may involve an online component. More detail about the interactive workshops and what you will be doing in them is available on the LMS site for the unit.

Participation

Tutorial Participation
There are 11 tutorials in total and you are strongly encouraged to participate in these tutorials. While they are not directly assessed, the tutorials will materially indirectly help you with your assignment and your exam. Tutorials commence in Week 2 and will run each week for 45 minutes.
How do I enrol in a tutorial?
Please ensure you have enrolled in a tutorial in the Class Allocation System (http://www.cas.uwa.edu.au). The Class Allocation System allows you to select your preferred class time. The system will then assign you to a tutorial based on your preferences. You may then be given an opportunity to change your tutorial time (if an alternative time is available). Unfortunately, the unit coordinators are not able to take requests from students to be allocated to a specific tutorial or to move students to a different class once assigned as we do not have access to the relevant system. If you have a question about your tutorial enrolment, please lodge an enquiry through AskUWA at www.ask.uwa.edu.au and Student Administration should be able assist you.  
Can I attend a different tutorial for a week?
Students are required to attend their assigned class.  Part-time work, social or other commitments are not grounds for attending an alternative class.  In exceptional circumstances (eg due to Covid19), you may attend an online or other alternative tutorial provided that you have first checked with the tutor for that tutorial.
Where can I find the tutorial questions?
The tutorial questions are available on the tutorial section of LMS.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
