Academic Information

Content This unit provides an introduction to abstract methods in a continuous setting. Topics include topology and geometry from a constructive perspective in dimensions two, three and four. Students investigate, via concrete examples, homeomorphisms and diffeomorphisms of manifolds; curvature; vector bundles; and elements of the machinery for describing space-time in physics. Alternatively, topics may include Banach spaces, Hilbert spaces, projections, compact, self-adjoint, normal and unitary operators, Riesz representation theorem, the Fredholm alternative and applications to solving integral and differential equations from physics.

Unit Information

The course sets the foundations of topology and mathematical analysis. We take a fairly classical approach and work our way into the following topics (tentative programme):
- Metric spaces
- Topological spaces: topology, open sets, closed sets, continuity, compactness, connected and arc connected sets, homeomorphic spaces, product topology, quotient topology.
- Smooth manifolds: smooth maps and manifolds, charts, tangent spaces and tangent maps, regular values.
- Normed vector spaces: normed spaces, Banach spaces, bounded linear operators.
- Inner product spaces: Hilbert spaces, Fourier coefficients, orthonormal bases, Riesz representation.
- Some applications: compact operators and Fredholm’s alternative for integral operators.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is run in a classical style. There will be 3 lectures and 1 workshop session per week (to begin in week 2).
Participants will be challenged to reflect and submit 3 assignments during the semester.
A two-hour final exam will take place during the official end-of-semester examination period.

Unit Structure

This unit comprises 3 hours of lectures and 1 hour of workshop per week (to begin in week 2).
The lectures cover the bulk of the material; definitions, theorems, applications and examples. Lectures are recorded and available on the LMS.
During workshops, certain topics from the lecture notes will be discussed in more details, some more examples will be given. Attendance at practical classes is not compulsory, but participation is highly encouraged. These sessions will not be recorded.
The assessment for this unit consists of 3 assignments and a final examination. The assignments, which contributes 42% to the overall assessment (14% each), will be handed out in week 3, 6 and 9. They will be due in week 5, 8 and 11 respectively, i.e. 2 weeks to reflect and write out solutions in formal mathematics. Marks for the assignments will be made available to you though the csmarks system, which may be accessed via https://secure.csse.uwa.edu.au/run/csmarks.
The final examination, which contributes 58% to the overall assessment, will be held during the official examination period at the end of semester. The time and location of your final examination will be provided to you via studentConnect, NOT the lecturer. It will have two hours working time, and will cover material from the entire unit.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
