It deals with the logic and methods of social enquiry, particularly the application of social research methods to a range of contexts and social problems. Students learn how to develop a research proposal for academic and applied purposes using a range of methods and perspectives.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This intensive unit is available for Honours, Masters and early stage PhD students from a range of different disciplines. As such it brings together a wide variety of students at different levels of study and disciplinary knowledges and experiences, all of whom are developing social research projects. The learning and teaching strategy aims to make the most of this diversity by encouraging collaborative engagement, while recognizing students will receive one on one advice from supervisors.
Students are expected to be active participants in classes. While there is some content which it is necessary to convey through a lecture style, students are encouraged to ask questions and contribute to the discussion. There will be time in most classes for group discussion and/or working on activities.
Some additional recorded short lectures showcasing specific methods will be available for students to access online through LMS.
The textbook includes online quizzes, extra resources, and some online videos at https://edge.sagepub.com/creswellrd5e  which will be of value for students to reinforce and test their understanding of relevant concepts and skills. Students are encouraged to use these.
As an intensive unit, it is important that students keep up with the reading. Please ensure you allocate at least 10 hours per week to the unit (on top of contact time).

Unit Structure

This unit is taught intensively, and consists of two 2.5 hour seminar sessions per week for 5 weeks (Mondays and Wednesdays) beginning 28 February. You are required to attend both sessions each week.
The seminar is supported by online materials including readings, videos, PowerPoints and other content, available through the Learning Management System (LMS) site under 'Weekly learning materials'. This unit is designed to be taught in an interactive seminar format. Please note that given this format, enrolled students are required to attend all the sessions (if an absence is necessary, please inform the unit coordinator). Topics and linked materials can be found in 'Weekly learning materials' and 'Unit readings'. There will be regular opportunities to discuss your specific research topic and approach.
This unit is taught face-to-face and as an online option. The online option is open to all students, regardless of enrolment status. If you are unable to be on campus due to Covid-19 or some other general illness, you can attend the lectures online. However, students who can make it are expected to be in class in person.
Starting this year, UWA has switched to Teams as the online interface. Links will be posted in the LMS system for everyone to access. If you are unfamiliar with Teams, please visit this link: https://www.uwa.edu.au/library/Help-and-support/Student-email-and-collaboration-tools.

Participation

Students are expected to attend and contribute to classes. Absences will only be accepted on medical or other unavoidable grounds. Participation is included in assessment. Tutorial participation is not an assessable component of the unit but is required in order to complete assessment items. Consequently you are required to attend the seminars in order to be in a position to complete the assessments for this unit.

You are required to come to class having:
1) Listened to, watched, or attended the lecture (depending on all our abilities to come to campus)
2) Completed any other components contained within the weekly module
3) Completed the required reading and tutorial preparation.

Referencing Requirements

Referencing
It is important to note that you need to reference any material you draw on when writing your essays, whether you have directly quoted or whether you have paraphrased. 
Students must use an author/date system of referencing listed in the UWA library referencing guides.
This means you have a choice of either APA or Chicago Author/Date format.
• APA: https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa
• Chicago Author/Date https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/Chicago_referencing
While you can choose either referencing style, you MUST CHOOSE ONE and be consistent in your use of it.
Make sure you reference the page number as well as the author and year, even when you are paraphrasing.

Other

Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
Academic conduct
Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.
Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
