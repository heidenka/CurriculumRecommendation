Academic Information

Content This unit introduces students to processes of innovation and entrepreneurship. What is innovation, what are its characteristics, how does it compare to business as usual. What is entrepreneurship, what is the difference between start-up entrepreneurship and corporate entrepreneurship. The course covers an array of concepts, e.g. technological innovation, ambidexterity, transformational innovation, entrepreneurial orientation, characteristics of entrepreneurs (both within and outside the corporate context) and antecedents and outcomes of innovation and entrepreneurship more generally. Throughout the semester students are familiarized with concepts that are meaningful for building a toolkit for dealing with applications of innovation and entrepreneurship in later coursework.

Unit Information

This unit introduces students to the processes of entrepreneurship and innovation. Different perspectives on start-ups and corporate innovation are discussed and students apply the theories of the lectures to exercises they conduct during the tutorials over the duration of the course. This course is practical in its content approach which is why you will have 1 hour lectures each week but 2 hour tutorials (in 50-person tutorials). This gives you an opportunity to conduct all exercises under supervision of the tutor and takes away the need to plan in and coordinate out-of-hours group meetings. 
This unit will give you a foundation to excel in the E&I Bachelor of Business but also offers a great opportunity to those (from other programs) seeking to test their interest in the field of entrepreneurship and innovation. Note that the E&I major can be chosen as a second major by anyone studying at UWA and this option would be particularly relevant for students in e.g. engineering, computer science, medicine and agriculture.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

It is anticipated that students who undertake this unit will find it enjoyable, challenging and intellectually stimulating. The focus of learning within the unit will be based upon investigation and analysis of real life ventures and their associated processes and deliverables. Online lectures and face-to-face tutorials will be held to provide a framework for the unit, however, there will be a strong emphasis placed upon student participation as the most effective learning takes place when students are required to apply their learning or teach others. You will get the most out of this unit if you are prepared to read the relevant materials and listen to lectures and question the content. Please come prepared to the tutorials (having watched the lecture and read the weekly materials) ready to participate in the class discussions and apply your knowledge within the assignments.
Teaching and Learning Evaluation
You may be asked to complete two evaluations during this unit: The Student Perception of Teaching (SPOT) and the Students’ Unit Reflective Feedback (SURF). The SPOT is optional and is an evaluation of the lecturer and the unit. The SURF is completed online and is a university wide survey and deals only with the unit. You will receive an email inviting you to complete the SURF when it is activated. We encourage you to complete the forms as your feedback is extremely important and can be used to make changes to the unit or lecturing style when appropriate.

Unit Structure

Please refer to the Class Allocation System http://www.cas.uwa.edu.au for the lecture and tutorial timetable.
This course is organized along the rationale that combining theory and practice offers the highest learning outcome. Applying the theories and frameworks that are offered in the lectures to real life ventures, helps you to understand why these theories and frameworks are shaped the way they are. It also allows you to form an understanding of the make-up of other companies, especially newly founded ventures.
This course does not use a textbook, but selected readings are allocated for each week.
To facilitate the learning process, lectures will cover the principles of varying textbooks and articles. In this unit, we adopt an interactive format and it is very important that you are open to participation in the lectures and the seminars, as this will greatly enhance your learning experience.
Through cases and readings, we will address questions such as:
What is entrepreneurship?
Why do risk and uncertainty matter so much for those setting up a new business or pursuing corporate innovation?
What is lean development?
What is a value proposition?
Why is customer segmentation so important?
What is an MVP?
How do I set up distribution
How do I create functional customer relationship strategies
How do I balance customer acquisition cost with life time value
What kind of metrics can I consider to aid company building?
The bottom line of this unit: We want to teach you how to use commonly available resources to build viable, productive, enduring businesses that create real value.

Participation

For each of the tutorials, we expect all students to participate fully. This means you are encouraged to actively engage in your team's exercise every week during the tutorials. You are also expected to actively co-create business solutions as presented by other teams, try to add to their rationale, be critical but constructive!
Be ready to give your opinion about choices your team made as well as to comment on choices of other teams. You get full marks (1 out of 1) for each tutorial where your participation is deemed satisfactory. If you fail to participate in discussions, comes unprepared to tutorials or participate negatively in your team, you will score 0 out of 1 for participation for that session.
Participation is marked for weeks 2 through to 11, for a total of 10 weeks where each satisfactory week gets you 1 point and ten weeks of satisfactory participation will get you 10/10 for full marks on this component.
 
Prior iterations of this course has shown that most teams are able to score 100% for this component, just do your work, come prepared and ready to participate in our class dialogue!
 
As stated above participation is marked and constitutes 10% of your final grade for the module MKTG1342!

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
