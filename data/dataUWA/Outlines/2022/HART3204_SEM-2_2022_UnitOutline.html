Content This unit gives students in the Curatorial Studies minor the opportunity to undertake a supervised placement within a gallery, museum or an appropriate art organisation. Student will undertake a project supervised by their host institution and focused on a specific area of curatorial practice. On occasion there will be opportunities for students to complete their internship overseas, interstate or at a regional organisation.

Unit Information

This unit gives you the opportunity to undertake a minimum of a 100 hour supervised placement within a gallery, museum or an appropriate arts organisation. The internship allows for the critical reflection of curatorial practices and the acquisition of essential knowledge and skills to enhance your employment and career prospects in the sector. The opportunity to develop your skills in a 'real-life' situation is an invaluable workplace experience which, coupled with your University-based studies, provides a unique opportunity for those seeking to broaden their base of practical and professional competencies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

HART3204 is a unique unit that provides you with the opportunity to undertake a minimum 100 hour supervised placement in a work environment under the guidance of an external supervisor within your host institution and with the support of the unit coordinator and the UWA Work Integrated Learning Placement Coordinator. 
The learning and type of work that is undertaken in this unit will be different for each student and will depend on a range of factors such as the type of institution that you are working in and your role/s and responsibilities within the organisation. The very nature of the HART3204 internship is such that each student is individually responsible for achieving the learning outcomes of the unit. These learning outcomes are as follows: 
1. apply communication and interpersonal skills to a working situation
2. demonstrate a high level of initiative and the ability to work independently when required
3. perform tasks set by an employer to a high standard
4. critically reflect on experience in the workplace.    

Unit Structure

There are no formal lectures, tutorials or seminars for HART3204 as the unit is structured around internships undertaken within a host institution. 
There is a whole of class mid semester progress meeting in Week 6, that is, in the week beginning Monday 29 August. The meeting will be scheduled at a date and time that suits everyone enrolled in the unit. In Week 4, that is, in the week beginning Monday 15 August, you will receive a Doodle Poll meeting request which will allow you to advise of your availability for scheduling the meeting in Week 6. 
As there are no regular lectures, tutorials or seminars for HART3204, you are more than welcome to contact me on 6488 2114 or at clarissa.ball@uwa.edu.au Please be aware that I do not respond to student emails outside of working hours.

Referencing Requirements

It is of the utmost importance that all sources used in your written work are referenced and that they are referenced correctly. In so doing, your work will have greater academic and intellectual integrity. 
As the written requirements for this unit comprise reflective writing and a record of work based activity, it is possible that your written work will not draw on secondary sources and as such, there will be no need to provide references. However, do be vigilant in ensuring that your secondary sources are acknowledged when you deploy them. The School of Design prefers the Chicago Style, and there are some notes for guidance which have been prepared by library staff. 
https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/Chicago_referencing

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
