Academic Information

Content This unit introduces fundamental concepts and applications of essential finance topics across a range of perspectives. The unit provides a solid foundation of financial theory with an understanding of when and how to apply those theories in financial decision making. Topics include financial mathematics and the time value of money, investment decisions and capital budgeting, valuation, the cost of capital, capital structure and the risk and return relationship.

Unit Information

This unit will introduce you to the fundamental principles of finance, starting with time value of money, and applying that key principle in the valuation of assets such as stocks and bonds.  The important relationship between risk and return will be covered and extended into the study of cost of capital.  We next focus on corporate financial policies and decision making, presenting important topics such as capital structure and payout policy.
As the name of the unit suggest, you will learn essential knowledge of finance that will enable you to analyse and make sense of practical cases in finance and the confidence to delve into higher level of studies.  My goal is to try my best to capture and explain difficult concepts in a clear and amenable manner to you to support and facilitate your learning of finance.
The required assessments of this unit comprise a number of components that are designed to emphasize problem solving and reiterative exercises to reinforce your learning of key concepts.  These are in the form of weekly online quizzes and in-class multiple choice quizzes to validate your understanding of the lecture material as we progress in the semester.  These numerous quizzes are important to provide feedback to you and to me on whether you have grasped the material and ensure you have reached a good understanding when the final exam comes around.
There is one assessment that will require you to undertake online training module on Bloomberg at your own time during the semester.  If you complete the training and pass the final quiz, you will obtain a certification from Bloomberg which will be very useful for including in your curriculum vitae.  There will be more information at the start of the semester and on LMS regarding how you can access this training module.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The curriculum for Finance Essentials has been designed to expose students to various learning environments. Students are expected to attend the weekly seminars where the Unit Coordinator will discuss the lecture material and explain key concepts and calculations using a mix of mini case studies and quantitative problems.   To gain the most benefit from the unit, students should read the lecture materials (including all prescribed pre-reading) prior to attending seminars. Understanding of financial concepts will be tested through weekly online quizzes and in-class MCQ quizzes. These assessments will help to reinforce and strengthen students' knowledge and assure an excellent competency in fundamentals of finance. 
Teaching and Learning Evaluation
You may be asked to complete two evaluations during this unit: The Student Perception of Teaching (SPOT) and the Students’ Unit Reflective Feedback (SURF). The SPOT is optional and is an evaluation of the lecturer and the unit. The SURF is completed online and is a university wide survey and deals only with the unit. You will receive an email inviting you to complete the SURF when it is activated. We encourage you to complete the forms as your feedback is extremely important and can be used to make changes to the unit or lecturing style when appropriate.

Unit Structure

Students must commit a minimum of three hours to the weekly seminar which comprises two hours of lecture and one hour of discussion.  For face-to-face students, this three hours seminar will take place in one session on the prescribed time and day of the week.  Online students are expected to watch the lecture recording on LCS and attend an online seminar of one hour to review and discuss the lecture material.
Students are expected to complete pre-reading and other prescribed exercises outside the scheduled seminars. Link to the CAS page.

Participation

Participation in class, whether it is listening to a lecture or getting involved in other activities, is an important part of the learning process, therefore it is important that you attend classes. More formally, the University regulations state that ‘to complete a course or unit students shall attend prescribed classes, lectures, seminars and tutorials’. Where a student, due to exceptional circumstances, is unable to attend a scheduled class, they are required to obtain prior approval of the unit coordinator to be absent from that class. Any student absent from class without having had such absence approved by the unit coordinator may be referred to the faculty for advice and may be required to withdraw from the unit.

Referencing Requirements

It is important that the referencing of any sources used in your written work is done properly, if only to substantiate the points you are making in your assignment or project. UWA prefers the APA 7 referencing type, unless otherwise stated by the unit coordinator. Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
