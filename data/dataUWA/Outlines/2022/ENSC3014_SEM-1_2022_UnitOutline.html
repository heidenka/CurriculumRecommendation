Content This unit provides an understanding of electron behaviour as particles and as waves; properties and carrier dynamics of electrons in solids, particularly in semiconductors; the behaviour of pn junctions; and the operation, modelling and design of diodes and transistors.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Lectures: It is expected that you will pre-read the relevant material for each lecture. Lectures will be used to highlight important sections and for discussion and activities. It is very important that you keep on top of the material in this unit by pre-reading lecture material. 
Examples and exercises will be used to reinforce the concepts and test understanding. Solutions to exercises will be provided. It is best that you avoid looking at the answers until you have made every effort to answer the questions by yourself by searching through the notes.
Laboratories will be used to introduce measurement concepts relevant to electrical engineering
Groups will be used to deepen the understanding of concepts and engineering practice. Self-selection of group membership is not permitted.  You will be allocated to a group to develop your interpersonal skills in a more realistic engineering environment
Students are expected to spend on average 10 hours per week during semester  reviewing the learning material, completing exercises, laboratories and assignments,
Some past examination papers will be provided but solutions to these papers will not be provided

Unit Structure

36 hours of lectures, 3  hours per week
3 by 3 hour laboratories (summative reports in total contribute 20% to the unit score).  You can select laboratory sessions using CAS.  Reports are due two weeks after the laboratory has been completed.
1 group assignment that requires an individual report (summative report which contributes 20% to the unit score).  The assignment will be given on the 28th of March and the report will be due on the 9th of May.
1 Mid-semester test (formative) has been arranged for the 29th of April. It is not necessary to sit the test and no deferred test will be given
1 Final Examination (summative and contributes 60% to the unit score.  You need to achieve 30% in the Final Exam to pass the unit)

Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
