Academic Information

Content This unit presents an overview for audiologists of speech and language development, from early childhood through to adulthood, and relates it to difficulties associated with impaired hearing. Alternative communication systems (e.g. sign language) are also presented. Topics include the production of speech sounds, basic physiological linguistics, linguistic organisation, acoustic phonetics, phoniatrics, sonograms, normal loudness, pitch and timing perception, masking in speech perception, developmental milestones, speech and language development in normal and hearing-impaired children, auditory perception (in children and adults), psychological effects, effects on reading and general education, artificial speech, Makaton, Auslan and signed English.

Unit Information

This 6-point unit is completed during the second semester of the course. This unit presents an overview of speech and language development, from early childhood through to adulthood, and relates it to the difficulties experienced by hearing-impaired individuals. Topics include speech and language development in normal and hearing-impaired children, speech and language pathologies in children and adults, the production of speech sounds, basic physiological linguistics, acoustic phonetics, phoniatrics, and spectrograms. Alternative communication systems are also presented.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is delivered through lectures and workshops presented during the 5 week intensive teaching block in the first part of the semester. These are supplemented with tutorials during the clinical placement block.

Unit Structure

This unit comprises of 15 hours of lectures with the theoretical components being consolidated with a further 3 hours of workshops. Two tutorial sessions are offered during the semester.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
