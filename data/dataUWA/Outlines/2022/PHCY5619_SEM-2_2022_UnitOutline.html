Academic Information

Content This unit consolidates concepts introduced in PHCY5618 Pharmaceutical Chemistry and Analysis as well as PHCY5610 Physical Pharmacy and Biopharmaceutics. It focuses on the relationships between chemical structure and biological activity of various drugs. Emphasis is on basic principles, broad structure–activity relationships and the mechanisms of action (where known) of a range of important drugs in a number of classes (e.g. antibacterials and antivirals, anticancer drugs, cholinergics and adrenergics, steroids and analgesics) as well as the chemical strategies that have been adopted to optimise their activity. These concepts also require an appreciation of the chemistry of proteins and nucleic acids as these macromolecules constitute common drug targets. Further, the unit introduces students to the drug development process and explores sources of lead compounds as well as strategies of lead optimisation. Additionally, technical and analytical skills in methods commonly employed in drug isolation, identification and analysis are acquired and refined.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

In response to student feedback, the lecture content of this unit will be delivered online in form of pre-recorded lectures. To allow for interaction with staff and fellow students and also as a means to clarify anything from the recordings, a weekly face-to-face Q&A session is also scheduled. During this timeslot quizzes that provide feedback on your ongoing learning and mastering of various lecture topics will also be run. Lecture topics will also be reinforced in hands-on activities as part of the unit’s laboratory exercises. 

Unit Structure

The teaching of this unit will be organised as a mixture of lectures/tutorials and a number of practical exercises (commencing in Week 4). There will also be a prelab session where the various lab exercises will be discussed.

Laboratory Classes:
Each student will be allocated to a laboratory group. You will be notified of your groupings in the first week of semester. You will also be provided with a schedule of laboratory experiments that need to be carried out by each group. It is essential that you attend your laboratory class well prepared, so please read through the respective lab exercises in the lab manual scheduled for your group on the day, refer to information on the various experiments provided in the pre-lab session and also read up on any theoretical background as required.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
