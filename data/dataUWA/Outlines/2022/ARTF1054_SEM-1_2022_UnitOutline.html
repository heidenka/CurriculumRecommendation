Academic Information

Content This drawing unit welcomes students from all disciplines and is structured in such a way that it caters to those who have no or limited prior drawing experience as well as to those who wish to advance their existing skills. This unit explores drawing as a fundamental process in the development and communication of ideas. Undertaking structured studio workshops supported by online learning content, students will acquire the foundational skills necessary to begin developing a competent and engaging visual language. The unit introduces a range of practical methodologies for observation and recording as well as experimental, conceptual, process-based and non-representational approaches to mark making. Students produce a folio of in-class and independently produced drawings followed by a dedicated drawing project with supporting research and critical documentation. This unit may involve working at off-campus locations so that students have the opportunity to learn ‘in situ'. Drawing Foundations is a complementary unit for students enrolled in the Fine Arts major.

Unit Information

LMS learning content and practical activities in this unit explore drawing predominantly within the context of contemporary visual arts practice. In addition, students are invited (though not required) to investigate connections with other disciplines, areas of personal interest and fields of study.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

In class contact time for this unit involves a 45-minute weekly live online interactive and a 3-hour weekly on campus practical workshop. Students must also complete a series of homework activities each week. A full description of homework is available in the LMS workshop content for each corresponding week.
WEEKLY ONLINE INTERACTIVE (45-MINUTES)
The weekly 45-minute live online interactive contains a guided explanation of LMS learning content, homework and assessment information, presented by the Unit Coordinator with the opportunity to discuss the content and ask questions. The online interactive sessions are the primary avenue for students to ask the Unit Coordinator questions about assessments, learning content and other issues relevant to the unit. 
PLEASE NOTE: Students who do not attend or view the weekly online interactive session will miss critical learning content and should not expect questions around unit content to be answered, if they have missed information due to absence. If you are not present for the online interactive in real time (live) you must catchup and view the recording in your own time.
The weekly online interactive will be recorded and uploaded to LMS, however students are expected to attend the online interactive in real time (live) on MS Teams at the allocated time in which they are enrolled.
The Unit Coordinator will present an abbreviated description of LMS learning content in the live online interactive each week, however students are required to read through and view the entire weekly LMS learning content in their own time. LMS learning content incorporates video, audio, image and text references, exploring diverse drawing approaches and examining the work of contemporary artists. This content assists students to engage with and contextualise the ideas and practical skills introduced in the workshops.
WEEKLY ON CAMPUS PRACTICAL (3 HRS)
The weekly 3-hour on campus practical is a hands on workshop containing practical demonstrations, with students undertaking a range of drawing activities and creative investigation. 
It is very important that students attend ALL live online interactive and on campus weekly sessions.

The submission of a single piece or body of work to multiple units for assessment constitutes self-plagiarism.  Self-plagiarism is considered a serious breach of academic integrity and is defined by the university as the recycling, copying, or disguising of previous work for assessment in a unit.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
