Unit Information

This unit provides students with an introduction to the fundamental principles of the transport of mass and momentum in fluid flows. Topics discussed include the fundamental properties of fluids, hydrostatics, the general equations of fluid motion, dimensional analysis, fluid friction and the forces acting on objects in a flow, pumps, pipe systems, flow in porous media and the flow of compressible fluids in pipes. Students will develop fluid mechanics analysis skills via a combination of assignments and laboratory exercises.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Lectures
In this course, the lectures will expand on assigned reading (see “Reading Assignments” below). They will provide extra focus on important and/or additional topics, and may include worked examples and additional (interactive) discussion. 
The lectures will be delivered in face-to-face mode. The Lecture Capture System (LCS) will record the lectures for students taking the unit online. If government direction, university policy, or the lecturer being forced into isolation necessitate fully online delivery, the lectures will be pre-recorded and posted to the LMS and LCS in the usual scheduled timeslot.
There will be two distinct sessions each week – one on Tuesday, one on Thursday. The unit coordinator will lead all lectures (unless there is an unavoidable commitment requiring his absence, in which case a substitute will lead the session or a pre-recorded lecture will be provided).
For those able to attend face-to-face, participation will enhance the lectures – questions and discussion are a valuable part of the lecture, and enhance the lecture for all (including the video audience). Live note-taking is an important skill in professional practice, and participating in the lectures will enhance your skills. For those reviewing the lectures online, it is important that you review the lectures as close to the time they are scheduled as possible, to give you time to consider concepts before the practical classes – and you should also practice live note-taking while reviewing the captured video!
Practical Classes
Practical Class sessions will be held on Friday each week of the semester (with the exception of Good Friday). The student body will be divided into 3 face-to-face streams and one online stream (with allocations managed through CAS). The online session will be conducted using MS Teams (Meeting connection details will be made available to students enrolled in the online session in the first week of the semester).
At the practical classes, the unit coordinator will be available to discuss course materials and assignment questions with students, and will lead group discussion of key issues and examples. Some of the lecture material may also be revisited and expanded upon.
Attendance at the practical classes is not recorded or assessed, but experience has shown that students who consistently attend tutorials are generally more successful in this unit. The prac classes will not be recorded on the LCS.
If government direction, university policy, or the lecturer being forced into isolation necessitate fully online delivery, all sessions will be conducted using MS Teams
Office Hours
Except where unavoidably called to other appointments, the Unit Coordinator will maintain office hours between 1:00 pm and 4:00 pm each Monday. During that time, the unit coordinator will be available at his office (Room 1.55 in the Monadelphous Integrated Learning Centre) to answer questions (appointments are not necessary during office hours). A MS Teams session will run in parallel for students not able to attend in person..

Students will submit a report for each laboratory. Lab reports for all students will be due at 5:00 pm on the Friday falling the week after the last session of each lab. The due dates are;

The lab reports will be marked and returned via the LMS as marked up pdfs. Marks will be provided via the returned assignments – it is important that the marked reports be reviewed by students, as the feedback is intended to assist student learning and preparation for the final exam.

Safety will be a point of emphasis during the face-to-face laboratory sessions. Students who arrive at the lab improperly attired will be required to leave. Any student required to leave the laboratory due to improper attire will not be rescheduled, and a mark of zero will be recorded for the lab.

Students who miss their scheduled session without grounds for special consideration will not be rescheduled, and a mark of zero will be recorded for the lab. Assignments - Reading and Written
Reading Assignments
The reading assignment (notes) for each week will be made available at the unit web site no later than 5:00 pm on the preceding Friday. Students are expected to complete the reading assignment prior to attending the lectures, so that they are properly prepared.
To assess the preparation of students for the lectures, each week students will complete an online quiz dealing with the reading assignment. This quiz will be administered via the Learning Management System (LMS), and must be completed by 11:55 pm on Monday evening (ie before the lectures for the week commence). The quiz questions will be selected randomly from a bank of questions, so each student will see a different quiz, and may include multiple choice and calculation questions.
The weekly quiz will commence in week 2 of the semester (Calendar week 10), and continue throughout (with the exception of the week of the mid-semester break; there will be quizzes in the week after the mid-semester break and in the last week of the semester). A trial quiz will be provided in Week 1 that is intended to familiarize students with the process, and which will not count for assessment.
Written Assignments
During the course of the unit, students will complete 5 assignments (roughly one every 2 weeks). Each assignment will contain 8-12 questions. Students will submit answers to 2-4 designated questions from each assignment for assessment. The submission time and date will be listed on each assignment as it is posted – in general the submission time will be 5:00 pm on a Monday. Assignments will be submitted to the LMS – the assignments will predominantly be written, and the assignments must be submitted in a single pdf file to the LMS (scanning the written assignment into a pdf is the required approach). PDF file sizes will be strictly limited to 10 MB – penalties up to 5% will be applied for exceeding this limit.
The answers to the questions that are not to be submitted will be posted on the LMS so that students can check their work and understanding prior to submitting the assessed questions. The answers to all questions will be posted once the last acceptable time for late submission has passed.
Assignments will be marked and returned via the LMS as marked up pdfs. Marks will be provided via the returned assignments – it is important that the marked assignments be reviewed by students, as the feedback is intended to assist student learning and preparation for the final exam. 
Note that professional presentation of assignment submissions is essential, and deductions up to 5% will be applied if submissions have pages upside down, rotated, or out of order, or are simply unacceptably poorly presented. Submissions that are partly or entirely illegible will only have the legible portions marked. Submissions must be in pdf form – a penalty of 5% will be applied to a non-pdf submission IF it can be converted to pdf form for marking (if it cannot be converted, it may not be possible to mark the assignment and a mark of zero will apply). The submissions must also be in a single pdf file – if multiple files are submitted, only the first file will be marked.
Exams
At the end of the semester, students will complete a two hour final exam. At this time, it is planned that the exam will be delivered online via the LMS for all students. The exam will be open book.
The University is currently reviewing invigilation arrangements for exams, and it has not been determined whether invigilation will be mandatory (at the time of writing). The details of the exam arrangements will be communicated to students when they are confirmed.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
