Unit Information

To establish and articulate core pedagogical understandings, you will explore theory and scholarly research relating to child, adolescent and young adult development. A critically reflective framework will be employed to support understanding of learning and cognitive emotional, physical and social development. This unit includes micro-teaching using the mixed reality platform known as SimLab.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The teaching and learning approach used in this unit is known as a blended/multimodal model. There will be a mix of online recorded lectures, face-to-face or online tutorials and a weekly online component. Instruction includes educational theories, planning for teaching, and the overall role of the teacher as a professional. Your responsibilities and expectations as a preservice teacher are to engage professionally in all activities, collaborate with peers/colleagues in the unit and to engage respectfully with the requirements of the micro-teaching simulation experience.

Unit Structure

Your 3 hours of weekly contact time will typically be split between 40 minutes of online lecture content, a 120 minute tutorial and a 20 minute online quiz on the weekly readings. Engagement with each of these components of the unit, including attendance of tutorials each week is expected.
An exciting element that has been included in the unit again this year is the simulated mixed reality microteaching experience that you will all participate in later in the teaching period. This simulated microteaching, using the SimLab platform, will give you your first taste of teaching a class of students and will be excellent preparation for your professional practice placement in Teaching Period 3. SimLab will be discussed over the coming weeks and each week in your tutorials you will be supported to develop aspects of the lesson you will teach. Your participation in this experience will be necessary for you to complete Assessment 3.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:
Assessment One: Unit reading quizzes (10%)
You will complete 5 multiple-choice quizzes, each worth 2 marks, to reinforce key concepts from the unit readings. These quizzes are designed to encourage you to examine key evidence, concepts and theories related to developmental domains (physical, emotional, social and intellectual) and stages which extend from childhood to adolescence, and young adulthood.
Assessment Two: Essay (30%)
This essay (1400 words) will focus on educational psychology theories of learning and development covered in the unit. To complete this task, you will analyse key theoretical and scholarly research evidence relating to physical, emotional, social and intellectual development from childhood to adolescence and young adulthood. This essay task is designed to encourage you to engage with the unit content on a more sophisticated level, and to assist you to become an agile thinker in terms of translating theory into practice. 
The essay will include:
(a)	A referenced rationale as to why it is necessary for teachers to have an understanding of development and associated learning theories. 
(b)	An evaluation of the major educational theorists (covered in the first 5 weeks of the unit), their broad contribution to contemporary teaching and learning, and how knowledge of these learning theories enables teachers to address the physical, emotional, social and intellectual needs of learners from childhood to adolescence and young adulthood. You will draw from the unit materials and scholarly sources (not webpages).
(c)	A personal reflection on how the field of educational psychology will impact your own classroom practice, including catering for diverse learners.
Assessment Three: Critical reflection (60%)
This assessment will comprise a series of three multi-modal critical reflections based on educational psychology theories of teaching. A critical reflection is a process of identifying and evaluating your assumptions about aspects of teaching. For this task, you can use unit material (lectures, readings, discussions, etc.) and your practical experience (micro-teaching) to examine any bias you might hold, compare theories with actions, search for causes and triggers, and identify any potential problems. Essentially, you will synthesise and evaluate what you have been learning in this unit, how this has influenced your thinking and how it is likely to impact your future choices/actions as a teacher.
Your critical reflections will draw from what you know about the theory of teaching and your micro-teaching experience. 
To complete this task you will:
(a)	Create an instructional resource to support a 6-minute microteaching lesson (undertaken in Week 6) on a topic of your choice. The instructional resource and microteaching episode will not be graded. Marks for this assessment task will be drawn from your critical reflection – See part (c) which can include how you used the instructional resource in the micro-teaching lesson, and how you were able to bridge the gap between teaching theory and the practical act of teaching. Important note: Planning of this part (a) will be strongly scaffolded in class each week in Weeks 1-5.
(b)	Reflect on your microteaching experience in relation to the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (APST) in the following three areas:
1.	How can instructional resources be used to engage students in their learning? APST 3.4.
2.	What strategies can teachers use to be responsive to the needs of students with diverse backgrounds and abilities? APST 1.3.
3.	How can interpersonal relationships, and verbal and non-verbal classroom communication, be fostered by teachers to support student engagement? APST 3.5.
(c)	Create three multi-modal (visual with a recorded narration) critical reflections of 3 minutes each (to a total of no more than 9 minutes) using Flipgrid. You are encouraged to integrate other digital storytelling and/or video creation platforms of your choice to enhance your message. You can explore a practice Flipgrid task here (join with Microsoft). All the Flipgrid tasks can be found at: https://flipgrid.com/e19260c2.
The three critical reflections should:
•	draw from what you learned about teaching theories in the unit and from your micro-teaching experience, and synthesise these into three distinct multi-modal critical reflections on the topics of (1) instructional resources (20%), (2) catering for diverse backgrounds and abilities (20%), and (3) interpersonal skills/communication (20%). 
•	be suitable to include in an eportfolio of accomplishments against the APSTs identified above. 
•	showcase your thinking and understanding of educational psychology theory at Masters level: AQF Level 9. 
Links to your three completed Flipgrid videos will be submitted to LMS (a template for this will be provided) and the written transcripts will be submitted to Turnitin. Further details about submission will be provided in the coming weeks.

AUSTRALIAN PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS FOR TEACHERS (APST)
1.	Analyse key evidence, concepts and theories related to child and adolescent development. (APST 1.1, 1.2)
2.	Synthesise the relationship between developmental theory and the practice of teaching students 5 to 18 years of age. (APST 1.1, 1.2)
3.	Develop, use and evaluate instructional resources, including the use of contemporary technologies, for teaching and learning with students up to 18 years of age. (APST 1.1, 1.2, 3.4)
4.	Demonstrate an understanding of the importance of acknowledging, and responding to, the strengths and needs of students from diverse backgrounds and students across the full range of abilities. (APST 1.3)
5.	Examine and apply interpersonal skills, verbal and non-verbal communication, to support student engagement. (APST 1.5, 3.5)

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
