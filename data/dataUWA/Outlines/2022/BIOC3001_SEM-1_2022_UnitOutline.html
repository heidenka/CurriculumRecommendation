Academic Information

Content The objective of this unit is to provide students with a broad knowledge and understanding of the concepts and methods used in molecular biology. The unit extends many of the topics introduced in SCIE1106 Molecular Biology of the Cell including DNA, RNA and protein synthesis; gene and genome structure and function, the regulation of gene expression, quality control mechanisms; bioinformatics; recombinant DNA technology and genetic engineering. The practical work familiarises the students with techniques basic to molecular biology.

Unit Information

The unit will be taught in a flipped classroom mode to encourage an active learning experience. Students engage with online learning materials (e.g. videos, readings) in preparation for activities in seminars and laboratories, which will be continuously assessed throughout semester. 
The objective of this unit is to provide students with an understanding of the concepts and methods used in molecular biology and to prepare students to apply this knowledge to solve problems, as well as to analyse and evaluate experimental results. The unit extends many of the topics introduced in SCIE1106 Molecular Biology of the Cell and BIOC2001 Biochemistry and Molecular Biology of the Cell. These topics include DNA, RNA and protein synthesis; gene and genome structure and function, the regulation of gene expression, cellular quality control mechanisms in RNA and protein synthesis; bioinformatics; recombinant DNA technology, membrane-less organelles and genetic engineering. In practicals, students will learn manual skills required to prepare and perform a gene expression analysis using qRT-PCR. Students will learn how to document and report experimental outcomes. Students will further learn how to analyse and critically evaluate experimental results and how to avoid and troubleshoot experimental problems.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The theory content of the unit will be - in parts - taught in a flipped classroom mode to encourage an active learning experience. Students engage with online learning materials (e.g. videos, readings) in preparation for activities in compulsory seminars and laboratories, which will be continuously assessed throughout semester. In addition, students work in teams to prepare a video related to the theory of the unit. They will present and assess the videos in workshops. 
The unit is delivered via:
- a weekly face-to-face theory seminar for which students have to review videos and other material prior to attending the seminar.
- Five laboratory session. During the laboratories, lecturers assist students in their learning activities. Students have to attend practical seminars in the week of their laboratory sessions. During these seminars, students ill learn about the theory underlying the practical work.
- in group work, students will research a topic related to the unit and prepare in team work a short video. Students have to attend a video seminar and two workshops. Videos will be presented and marked during the workshops.
. PLEASE NOTE: Laboratory and workshop and seminar attendance is compulsory. Seminars, laboratories and workshops are not recorded.

Participation

PUNCTUAL attendance at all seminars, workshops and practicals is compulsory and attendance will be recorded. If a session is missed or attended more than 5 minutes late without approved special consideration from the Student Office, the mark allocated to the missed activity is forfeited.
The seminar mark makes up 10% of the unit final mark.
If a student has a continuing condition or situation that may impact her / his ability to carry out one or more of the labs in this unit, we advise him / her to contact the unit coordinator at the beginning of the semester to discuss what accommodations are possible. There is absolutely no expectation the details of the condition/situation will be discussed, only the potential to accommodate missed work/experience. A student would still need to apply for special consideration if a practical session is missed, but the conversation with the unit coordinator would set out what would be required from the student to complete the unit.
Suitable attire (laboratory coat, CLOSED shoes) must be worn to all lab session and long hair must be tied back. Students are also required to keep accurate lab notes during all lab sessions and these will be assessed. Students need to purchase a notebook for the laboratory notes. 
Please always act in a sensible fashion when undertaking any experimental procedure. Handle all chemicals and samples with care, and wear gloves and safety specs when necessary. Ask a demonstrator if there is uncertainty about the safety of a reagent and where gloves should be worn.
It is advisable to have a calculator at all practical sessions, tests and exams. Please note that all calculators used in assessments at UWA MUST be authorised and labelled with an approval sticker, which can be obtained from the Science Students Office. If a calculator does not have an approval sticker, it cannot be used for any assessment in BIOC3001.

Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
