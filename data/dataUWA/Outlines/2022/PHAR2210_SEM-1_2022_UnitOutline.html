Academic Information

Content This unit provides an introduction to the basic principles of pharmacology, the branch of science that studies the effects of drugs on the human body. Topics, which are delivered in modules, include (1) drug absorption, distribution, metabolism and excretion; (2) toxicology and structure–activity relationships; (3) receptor classification, dose–response relationships and intracellular signalling; (4) chemotherapy; and (5) clinical aspects of drug effects. The material in the unit is the foundation for further study in pharmacology but can be taken on its own. While introducing students to the scientific study of drugs and medicines, emphasis is mainly placed on explaining general mechanisms, processes and theories rather than detailing the complex properties of the many individual drugs that are used and abused in today's society. Summative assessments are held throughout the unit to help students adopt a learning strategy that facilitates long-term understanding and mastery of material.

Assessment Mechanism Statement
Laboratory attendance
Laboratory attendance is mandatory for all students enrolled in PHAR2210, and will be recorded electronically on the LMS, unless approved in the presence of supporting documentation. Approved absence at a laboratory session does not exempt a student from completing the associated assessment task. A student will be able to access lab data and complete lab assessment only if their attendance is successfully recorded, unless an alternative arrangement was approved. A student will receive no credit without the successful submission of lab assessment.
Completion of assessments
A student will be required to complete each assessment task to obtain a score that will contribute to the final mark for this unit. A student who missed an assessment task due to acceptable ground must seek Special Consideration in a timely manner as per the University Policy. Alternative arrangements (including the date, time, venue and format) will be at the Unit Coordinator’s discretion upon the receipt of a Special Consideration approval. Exemption from completing an assessment task and re-distribution of marks is not offered in this unit.
Written assignments - requirements, submission and marking
•	Requirements - Written assignments must comply with the prescribed requirements. The Vancouver citation style (https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/vancouver) is the ONLY accepted referencing style for all PHAR2210 written submissions.
•	Submission - students will be required to submit written assignments to Turnitin for plagiarism detection. Students will be able to view the originality report following a successful submission - be aware that students can re-submit BEFORE the due date but can only submit once ON OR AFTER the due date. It is a student’s responsibility to ensure they select the correct file for submission, and that their submission is successful. Students must retain a copy of the confirmation email from Turnitin for each piece of work as proof of submission - this is the only accepted evidence in the event of a dispute. Submissions by email will not be accepted, and penalty for late submission will be applicable until the work is successfully submitted to Turnitin. Late submissions without special consideration approval will be subject to penalty as specified in the University Policy on: Assessment. 
•	Marking - anonymous marking is enabled in this unit, and students must not include any personal information (i.e., student ID number and/or name) in their submissions. Written submissions will be marked against a set of pre-defined criteria, which will be provided to students at the start of semester.
Summative Assessments
Students will complete two invigilated computer-based summative assessments during the semester. These two summative assessments are exclusively linked to lecture contents and serve to assess if a student has achieved the intended learning outcomes.
Calculating your final mark for PHAR2210
Each PHAR2210 assessment task will be initially given a raw score out of 100. These raw scores are converted to a percentage according to their weightings as outlined in the table below. The final mark for PHAR2210 will be the simple sum of these percentages. Marks for all intra-semester assessment tasks (i.e., excluding the final exam) will be released to students ONLY via My Grades on the LMS. As per the University Policy on: Assessment, marks for assessment tasks will be reviewed to ensure consistency.

Active participation in laboratories is expected from all students, as you will individually complete a lab skill test as part of Lab Assessment (II) at the end of the semester.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
