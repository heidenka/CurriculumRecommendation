Academic Information

Content This unit provides an understanding of individual and group behaviour in organisations, with an emphasis on similarities and differences in different cultures. Topics that deal with individuals in organisations include personality, perception, motivation, ability, satisfaction, decision making and stress. Topics relevant to group behaviour include interpersonal relations, group dynamics, group decision making, leadership, conflict, power, ethics, social justice and social responsibility. The application of theory and research findings to management practice in organisations is addressed in the coverage of these topics.

Unit Information

This unit provides an understanding of people's behaviour in organisations. It considers both groups and individuals, with an emphasis on similarities and differences in different cultures. Topics that deal with individuals in organisations include decision making, motivation, satisfaction, stress, personality, perception and ability. Topics relating to group behaviour include group dynamics, leadership, power, ethics, group decision making, social justice, social responsibility, conflict and interpersonal relations. As we cover these topics, we will focus on applying related theory and research findings to management practice in organisations.
Studying human behaviour is fundamental to understanding organisations and how effective they are, because organisations are made up of people. The concepts studied in this unit are relevant to all forms of business and they help us to understand life beyond the classroom and the workplace.
In addition to introducing students to the study of human and organisational behaviour, this unit aims to educate students on how this knowledge is applied to improve the management of organisations and the well-being of those who work in them.
Unit Learning Outcomes:
Students will be able to: 1. Explain organisational behaviour theory and concepts across cultures and nations; 2. Describe the importance of an evidence-based approach to management and organisational behaviour; 3. Interpret organisational problems in terms of individual, interpersonal and group processes; 4. Analyse organisational problems using a number of established theoretical frameworks; 5. Create solutions to managerial problems through the application of well-researched organisational behaviour principles, and 6. Develop oral and written communication skills.
Students will be encouraged to:
*Critically evaluate and develop solutions to organisational behaviour problems.
*Develop their communication skills during class discussions.
*Develop a desire to question accepted wisdom and be open to exploring new ideas through discussions that involve critical analysis of existing theories.
*Build independent learning skills through the completion of assigned work throughout the course.
*Develop competencies for working effectively in teams 
*Gain awareness of the ethics of organisational behaviour

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit focuses on understanding key concepts and developing practical skills through discussions and active engagement. Over the course of the trimester, students will be putting their critical thinking, research, writing and presenting skills into practice; both individually and in teams. The unit is presented as a hybrid/hyflex model. Students are encouraged to be on campus if able.

Unit Structure

This unit contains 13 weeks which are made up of 12 teaching weeks, and one revision week before exams. Each week has six contact hours broken up into 3-hour classes. Preparation and extension work will be provided outside of class.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
