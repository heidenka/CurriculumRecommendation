Unit Information

This unit seeks to prepare you for today’s and tomorrow’s marketing world by linking the latest developments in marketing technologies to tried and tested marketing theories. An understanding of both will be vital for a career in this field.
You will learn about a new digital mindset, new digital business models, and new digital strategies. Specifically, the unit will focus on the front-office engagement initiatives and the back-office data analytics possible on the key pillars of digital marketing: content, social media and search. The unit balances theory and practice, giving you the opportunity to design and deliver a content marketing campaign for a local organisation, and encouraging you to build your own personal brand online.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Strategy
This unit is bang up to date with what is happening in marketing today. Students will have to get involved in using new technologies to get first-hand experience with digital marketing. This will, of course, be complemented by seminars and the use of practical reading materials. You will complete a series of learning activities each week and work on project-based assessments. These activities and assessments support the development of digital marketing knowledge and skills by facilitating hands-on experience and reflection.
Evaluation
The Student Experience of Learning and Teaching (SELT) survey enables us to seek student feedback on their experience of learning and teaching in the unit. You may be asked to complete evaluations of the unit and your instructors' teaching (lecturer and tutor). You will receive an email inviting you to complete the SELT survey when it is activated. We encourage you to complete the survey as your feedback is extremely important and can be used to make changes to the unit or teaching style when appropriate.

Unit Structure

This unit is based on the delivery of weekly 3-hour seminars. Each seminar consists of a lecture on a weekly topic and a workshop that complements the previous week's lecture with activities and discussions and allows time for team project work. Your levels of preparation and participation will be assessed weekly, so it is important you always come to class prepared by having completed the weekly readings and assigned tasks. Please refer to the LMS for weekly activitity details and resources.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
