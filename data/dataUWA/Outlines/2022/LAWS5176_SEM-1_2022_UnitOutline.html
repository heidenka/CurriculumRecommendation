Academic Information

Content This unit offers an introduction to finance law. It focuses on the law and regulation of financial transactions undertaken in Australia. Topics covered include sources of finance law, forms of business organisations, lending and security, investing in companies, corporate fundraising, corporate takeovers and insolvency, financial market misconduct and legal obligations of financial intermediaries

Unit Information

The unit provides an introduction to Australian financial transactions law and regulation. Topics covered will include the Australian legal system, the law of contract, torts law (with a particular focus on the tort of negligence), the law relating to the most commonly used business structures and relationships, the law relating to secured lending and insolvency, the general law and statutory obligations of financial advisers, and the regulation of financial market misconduct, corporate fundraising and takeovers.

Unit Structure

For students enrolled in this unit in face to face mode (F2F) all the teaching in this unit will be structured around a series of weekly seminars. There will be 12 three-hour seminars, one for each teaching week of the semester. The seminars will be held every Thursday throughout the semester (except for the week of the 'Study Break') between 5.00 PM and 8 PM in LAWS: [106] (this is the so called 'Law Lecture Theatre' and is located on the first floor of the main Law School building, overlooking the Law Courtyard). Each seminar will include both a formal lecture and a more interactive 'tutorial' or 'workshop' component (except when indicated otherwise in this Unit Outline or on the unit LMS page). In the workshops and tutorial classes active engagement and in-class participation will be expected of all students and each student will be individually assessed and awarded 'participation' marks on the basis of their engagement and participation in those classes throughout the semester.
All lecture slides, prescribed and recommended readings and other relevant learning materials will be posted on LMS well in advance of the seminar/lecture to which they relate. In order to give you ample time  for preparation the topics/questions for discussion in each tutorial will be posted on LMS one week in advance of the class to which they relate. 
All formal lectures given in this unit will be recorded and posted on LMS. All students, whether enrolled in this unit in F2F or Online (OL) mode, will be able to access and listen to the recorded lectures.
So that they do not fall behind in their studies, all students enrolled in this unit in online mode should ensure that they listen to each set of the weekly recorded lectures within no more than 48 hours after the date on which those lectures were delivered. In addition, all online students will be required to attend and participate in a weekly online tutorial. The online tutorials will be made available only to students who are enrolled in this unit in online mode. Full details regarding the time and date of the online tutorials, and how to access them, will be released in due course.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
