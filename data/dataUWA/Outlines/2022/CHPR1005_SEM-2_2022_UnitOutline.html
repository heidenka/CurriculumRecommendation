Academic Information

Content Mass and energy balances are a cornerstone of chemical engineering. This unit introduces students to the theories and tools required to formulate and solve material and energy balances for chemical process systems. Students learn how to conduct and analyse material balances for multi-unit process systems with and without chemical reactions, including systems that incorporate recycle and purge streams, and to formulate energy balances based on the first law of thermodynamics. Analysis of these systems develops an understanding of and the ability to calculate the thermodynamic properties of gases, liquids and vapours.

Unit Information

This unit develops the student’s ability to formulate and solve material and energy balances for chemical process systems. Students analyse material balances for single and multi-unit process systems, both with and without chemical reactions. Analyses are developed for systems that incorporate recycle and purge streams. 
An understanding of, and the ability to calculate, the thermodynamic properties of gases, liquids, and vapours is developed. This understanding is applied in material and energy balance analyses.
Energy balances based on the first law of thermodynamics are formulated for systems with and without reactions.
Students who successfully complete the unit should be able to:
•	Formulate and solve analyses of chemical process systems; 
•	Locate and/or calculate the thermodynamic properties of fluids and simple two-phase mixtures;
•	Apply technical communication skills through assignment submissions;
•	Develop and execute Hysys models.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit is structured to encourage you to learn independently (using the reading material, lectures, examples, and the text book) and also through interaction with your fellow students and teaching staff (during practical classes and laboratories). 
As you progress through the unit, weekly reading assignments are provided to help you prepare for the upcoming content, and three written assignments are provided for you to gauge and consolidate your understanding of the core concepts and ability to apply them to solving engineering problems. 
Weekly lectures will summarise and highlight key concepts contained in the weekly notes.
Weekly practical classes will allow you to develop problem-solving strategies, clarify concepts and topics, and participate in a peer-to-peer learning environment. 
The Hysys laboratory assignments will introduce you to a chemical process simulator environment and allow you to connect the concepts and methods you learn with a physical model.
The Practical Lab will connect hands-on measurements of water saturation and ideal gas expansion with fundamental concepts.

Unit Structure

Lectures
In this course, the lectures are designed to illuminate and expand on the material in the weekly notes. This will take the form of reviewing key concepts, worked examples, and additional discussion. Students are strongly encouraged to attend lectures in person. Lectures will be delivered in person on Monday and Tuesday. Recordings will be available on the unit LMS page.
Practical Classes
Practical Classes will be held on Thursday each week. There will be a single face-to-face session, and a single online session (the online session is only for remote students). At the practical classes, the lecturer will be available to discuss course materials and assignment questions with students, and will at times lead group discussion of key issues. The lecturers may also take the opportunity to answer questions lodged prior to the sessions via e-mail. The practical classes will not be recorded.
Consultation and Office Hours
The Unit Coordinator and Lecturer will be available for consultation by email request. Enquiries will be responded to within two working days. Remote students can request a video meeting to discuss questions in person.
Reading Assignments
Reading Assignments consisting of a set of notes for the coming week (“the Weekly Notes”) will be made available at the unit LMS site no later than 12:00 noon on the preceding Friday. Students will be expected to complete the Reading Assignment prior to reviewing the lectures, so that they may actively engage with the lecture material.
To ensure that students are prepared for the lectures, each week students will complete an online quiz dealing with the Reading Assignment. This quiz will be administered via the unit learning management system (LMS), and must be completed by 12:00 noon on Monday (i.e. before the lectures for the week). The quiz questions will be selected randomly from a bank of questions, so each student will see a different quiz.
The weekly quiz will commence in week 2 of the semester, and continue throughout. A trial quiz will be provided in Week 1 that will not count for assessment, but it is intended to familiarize students with the process. 
Written Assignments
During the course of the unit, students will complete three (3) assignments spaced out through semester. Students will submit answers to designated questions from each assignment for assessment.
The submission time and date will be listed on each assignment. Assignments will be submitted to the LMS. The assignments will predominantly be written, and the assignments must be submitted in PDF format according to the following guidelines:
- include your name, student number, assignment title, and date at the top of the first page; 
- the assignment should be professionally presented, with working clearly labelled and pages in the correct orientation and sequence; 
- the PDF must be clear and of sufficient resolution to be marked;
- the assignment PDF should be submitted as a single document (not multiple files);
- the PDF file size should not exceed 15 MB;
- the PDF filename should be of the format "SURNAME_STUDENTNUMBER_ASS_XX.pdf".
Penalties up to 10% will be applied for not following the guidelines above. Illegible submissions will not be marked. Submissions must be in PDF format – a penalty of 10% will be applied to a non-PDF submission IF the submission can be converted to PDF form for marking (if it cannot be converted, it may not be possible to mark the assignment and a mark of zero will apply).
Assignments will be marked and returned via the LMS as marked-up PDFs. It is important that the marked assignments be reviewed by students, as the feedback is intended to assist student learning and preparation for the final exam.
Marking of the assignment will proceed as follows:
• One of the submitted questions will be marked in detail, using the style of marking scheme that will be used in the final exam. The score for the “detailed” questions will make up the majority of the overall assignment mark.
• The remaining submitted questions will be marked using an “overview” approach. The focus in marking the “overview” questions will be identifying that the correct elements have been provided, that the correct approach has been taken, where a critical error has occurred (if there is an error), and that all answers are correct. 
Students will not know in advance which of the submitted questions will be marked in detail and which will be marked in overview.
The answers to the questions that are not to be submitted will be posted on the LMS so that students can check their work and understanding prior to submitting the assessed questions. The answers to all questions will be posted once the submission time for the assessed questions has passed.
Hysys Laboratory Assignments
Three computer lab sessions will be scheduled over the course of the semester. In each session, students will work through assignments designed to introduce Hysys and develop their modelling skills. Participation the laboratory sessions is required for students to receive credit for these assignments. Face-to-face sessions are scheduled, with simultaneous online sessions for remote students.
Each student will be given one of a set of similar assignments to complete, and at the end of the laboratory session will submit their work for assessment. This will be done by exporting model results in PDF form, and submitting the pdf to the LMS. Submission must be completed within 15 minutes of the end of the laboratory session – assignments submitted after this time will not be accepted. Assignments will be marked and returned via the LMS as marked up PDFs. Marks will be provided via the returned assignments and the LMS.
Practical Lab
Students will complete a practical lab where they will measure the saturation conditions of water vapour and ideal gas expansion and compare the results with theoretical predictions. Participation in the lab session is required for students to receive credit. Online students will be provided with material to complete the learning activity.
Exams
At the end of Week 7, students will complete a mid-semester test assessing the material covered in Weeks 1 to 6.
At the end of the semester, students will complete a two-hour final exam. This exam will focus on the material covered in the second half of the unit, but will also include elements from the first 6 weeks.
NOTE: The exam will assess all elements of the course, so all assignment questions, including those that are not submitted for marking, deal with examinable material. All material presented in the lectures and Hysys lab sessions is also examinable.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
