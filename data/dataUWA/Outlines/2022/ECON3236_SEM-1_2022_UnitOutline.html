Learning And Teaching Strategy

The teaching format is a mix of video lectures and face to face (in-class) lectures. Every week, approximately one hour of video lectures will be uploaded online. Lecture material in the form of presentations will be made available. This will be followed by a one-hour in-class lecture that will improve the student's grasp on the topic by discussing relevant examples or case studies. Students are expected to go through the video lectures and background reading before coming to the in-class lecture to maximize their understanding of the unit material. Apart from video lectures and in-class lectures, tutorials are also an important component of learning and assessment in this unit. 
Note: Depending on how the COVID-19 situation evolves, the one-hour in-class lecture and the face-to-face tutorials may have to be offered online.
Teaching and Learning Evaluation
You may be asked to complete two evaluations during this unit: The Student Perception of Teaching (SPOT) and the Students’ Unit Reflective Feedback (SURF). The SPOT is optional and is an evaluation of the lecturer and the unit. The SURF is completed online and is a university wide survey and deals only with the unit. You will receive an email inviting you to complete the SURF when it is activated. We encourage you to complete the forms as your feedback is extremely important and can be used to make changes to the unit or lecturing style when appropriate.

Unit Structure

This unit is spread over 12 weeks. Every week approximately 1 hour of video lectures will be complemented by 1 hour of in-class lecture. This unit has no mid-semester exam. Instead, the focus is on regular assessments through online quizzes (in weeks 3, 5, 7, 9, and 11) and online assignments (in weeks 7 and 11). The regular assessments will be followed by feedback through tutorials which will also happen once every two weeks starting from week 4. In total, there will be 5 tutorials (weeks 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12). Each tutorial will be 2 hours in duration. There will be a non-teaching study break after week 7 (18-22 April). There will be no lectures or tutorials this week.
Note: Depending on how the COVID-19 situation evolves, the one-hour in-class lecture and the face-to-face tutorials may have to be offered online.

Participation

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
