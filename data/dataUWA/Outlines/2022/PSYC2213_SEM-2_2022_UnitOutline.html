Academic Information

Content This unit introduces students to key issues and common research methods used in the study of human development. Major theories of development are described and evaluated. Ways in which environmental and biological factors influence development are considered. Descriptions of key changes across childhood and theoretical explanations of these changes are provided in relation to cognition, intelligence, language, literacy, numeracy, social skills, emotion and gender.

This unit is taught through a series of lectures and tutorial classes. In the lectures, students will gain in-depth knowledge of the changes that occur across childhood as well as changes that occur with normal ageing. The tutorial classes will introduce students to methods for conducting research on development, including intelligence testing, and engage students in discussions on contemporary developmental issues. Students will work in small groups in their tutorial classes and will learn practical skills relevant to a career in developmental psychology. Attendance at all classes is expected.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The lectures and associated readings provide comprehensive coverage of the developmental topics. The tutorial classes provide opportunities for interactive learning through discussions of current societal issues concerning child and adult development, evaluations of research designs, and practice in the administration of an intelligence test and a reading assessment for children. The assessment for the unit is designed to be formative by including a group debate followed by an individual essay, which most students choose to complete on the same topic. A small ungraded online quiz during the first half of semester will provide a guide to student progress in learning the unit content.

Unit Structure

Lectures: 12 x 2-hour lectures (Thursdays 12pm-2pm, recordings will be made available on the LMS shortly afterwards)
Tutorials:  4 x 2-hour tutorials (Weeks 3, 5, 7 and 11 - see Notes column in the Unit Schedule below)
Group work: Approximately 20 hours of group work outside of lectures/labs
Assessment Items 
Mid-Semester Quiz (optional): In Week 5, a multiple-choice quiz, covering lecture and tutorial material from Weeks 1-4, will be made available on LMS. This optional quiz provides an opportunity to practice revising unit content and completing an assessment of it. The practice quiz does not count towards the grade for the unit.
Group Presentation: In the first tutorial (Week 3), students are assigned to groups to prepare a group oral presentation in the form of a debate on a current issue concerning child and adult development. The debate will be presented and assessed during the tutorial classes in Week 7. Students must attend the first tutorial class in Week 3 in order to be assigned to a group. Each group will develop an oral presentation with associated visual aids to argue for one side of a current developmental issue, with the topic allocated to the group in Week 3. At the end of the presentation, groups must include a slide which lists who contributed to different aspects of the group presentation. This can include but is not limited to contributions to an analysis of the topic, searching and evaluating the peer-reviewed literature relevant to the topic, development of the argument, project management, and/or preparing visual materials or slides. Note that everyone in the group will get the same mark, and this information is not considered as part of the marking. This final slide is simply included to make your individual work more visible and make you all more accountable for your overall group success.
This presentation is assessed in the Week 7 tutorial class and feedback is provided to help prepare for the second piece of assessed work. Completing the Group Presentation assessment in this unit requires attendance in your tutorial class in Week 7.
Individual Essay: The individual essay will cover both sides of one of the developmental issues considered in the Week 7 debates. The essay should follow the guidelines set out in the Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (7th Edition), and must not exceed 2,000 words in length, excluding the reference list. Marking will cease once the word limit is reached. The research report is an individual assessment and should not contain material taken from the group debate slides. The essay should be submitted via LMS and will be marked by your tutor.
Exam: A final 2-hour exam will be held in the University's end-of-semester exam period.
Each component of assessment will be described in more detail in classes and written guides

Participation

It is important that students attend all of the tutorial classes. In the first tutorial class in Week 3, each student will be allocated to a group to prepare for the first component of assessment, which is a group oral presentation held in the third tutorial class in Week 7. Students who do not attend the tutorial in Week 3 will not be allocated to a group. Each student must contribute in an effective and timely way to the preparation and delivery of their group's presentation to obtain credit for this piece of assessment. Students will be asked to keep a log of contributions of group members and any students who fail to provide evidence of contributing effectively to their group will receive a grade of zero for this assessment component. The log of contributions will be checked by tutors in Week 5. The tutorials in Weeks 5 and 11 also deliver key information on research designs, language and reading development, and intelligence testing. The information from these tutorials will be examined, and since the tutorials are not recorded, it is important that students attend and participate.
Group Presentations
Presentations are conducted in the tutorial class in Week 7. 
To obtain a grade, students must:
• contribute to preparing the presentation
• attend the lab class in which the presentation is delivered.
While we encourage students to practice their presentation skills and contribute to delivering their presentation, it is not compulsory for every student in the group to deliver the presentation. Issues with this assessment should be emailed to to the unit co-ordinator as soon as possible, and preferably before the end of Week 5.
What if there is a problem?
The Guide to Working in Groups and the Group Work Log will help you prevent any problems in the group work process, and will enable you to tackle problems early should they arise. However, sometimes issues occur which cannot be resolved without assistance from the Unit Coordinator. If this is the case, you need to report this to the UCs at least 10 days before your scheduled group presentation (email PSYC2213-SPS@uwa.edu.au), to ensure there is sufficient times to resolve these issues. Keeping records of commitments and meetings in the Group Work Log will be invaluable to working towards a satisfactory solution of group problems. Tutors will ask to see your Group Work Log in Week 5 to help ensure your group work is on track. In addition, if any issues arise, the UC will also ask to see the Group Work Log to help resolve these issues with the group.

Referencing Requirements

The essay assignment should be written according to the format outlined in the Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (7th Edition) and must not exceed 2000 words in length excluding the reference list. Marking will cease once the word limit is reached. The essay assignment is an individual assessment and so care should be taken to avoid overlap with the work of other students, including group members. The essay assignment should be submitted using Turnitin via LMS. Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
