Academic Information

Content Students will work as a language assistant in a primary or secondary school under the supervision of a qualified teacher. Prior to the placement they will participate in professional learning provided by the Department of Education (this will be delivered in an intensive format prior to the beginning of semester 1). The placement will involve roles negotiated with the supervising teacher, for example facilitating group work. Students will develop a language learning resource/activity in consultation with the mentor teacher. Over the course of the placement, students will attend three online check-in sessions with the unit coordinator, in which they will report on the progress of their placement and reflect on their workplace learning. The academic objectives of the unit are to apply the practical skills and theoretical learning from their language major in this particular workplace setting and to identify and demonstrate transferable skills applicable to any workplace. This unit is not available for self-enrolment and is only available to high-performing students. Students wishing to apply for the unit must contact the unit coordinator and submit an expression of interest (a form will be supplied for this purpose).

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This is a work-integrated learning unit which is intended to be delivered and experienced fully face-to-face, with the exception of the online check-in sessions. A shift to online may be required in the event of COVID restrictions.

Unit Structure

This work-integrated learning unit consists of one week of intensive professional learning delivered by the Department of Education (prior to semester), followed by 80 hours of work placement as a language assistant in a government primary or secondary school (usually spread over 12 weeks). In addition, students are required to attend three online check-in sessions with the Unit Coordinator in weeks 3, 7 and 11 of the semester.

Participation

Participation is required for all activities: professional learning intensive, online check-ins and, of course, your placement. If you are unwell you must seek special consideration and inform your mentor teacher and unit coordinator as soon as possible.

Referencing Requirements

Any sources consulted in the preparation of the language learning resource/activity and/or the reflection must be fully documented in accordance with the Chicago conventions. If you prefer to use a different referencing style, please contact me ahead of submission to discuss.

Other

Like most work-integrated-learning units, WILG3003 will be assessed on a pass/fail basis. You will, however, receive a result for each of the assessment items below, and feedback on your language learning resource/activity and your reflection.
Successful completion of each of the three assessment items is necessary to pass the unit. Note, however, that successful completion of these items alone will not guarantee a pass in the unit – you will also need to complete all placement hours (timesheets) and surveys as well as submitting work of a pass standard.


Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
