Unit Information

This unit explores professional practice in paediatric exercise rehabilitation focusing on the assessment, design and implementation of exercise rehabilitation programmes for children with difficulties participating in physical activity.   You will be introduced to approaches in assessment and exercise rehabilitation and acquire knowledge of current best practice in paediatric exercise rehabilitation.   Through lectures you will gain a broad understanding of conditions, diseases & disorders associated with disability in childhood, and develop an understanding of workplace roles and responsibilities of an accredited exercise physiologist working within paediatrics.  Though your experience in the iFit programme you will obtain experience in the client centred approach to optimise client outcomes following exercise rehabilitation interventions.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Paediatric Exercise Rehabilitation uses a blended learning approach, the theoretical underpinnings of the unit are applied within the practical component.
LECTURES: The unit involves 11 lectures; all recorded and will be available on Lecture capture system.   The lectures cover all the theoretical content of Paediatric Exercise Rehabilitation.
SEMINARS:  Six live seminars will be conducted over the semester - these seminars are specifically designed to enhance your learning this semester and will involve specialist guest lecturers (which may include case sensitive material, and therefore unethical to record), coursework updates, review of assignments and unit content, and opportunity to interact with teaching staff and student peers.
LABS: The unit involves 4 lab classes; which cover the fundamentals of teaching within the iFit program, including, goal setting, developing individualised assessments, strength training in paediatrics, community integration strategies,  and managing behaviour and motivational teaching. 
PRACS: Within the unit students will complete a 10-week placement in the iFit program.  The program is run once a week for 1.5hours.  Students will deliver an individually tailored program designed to develop a child's ability to participate in their recreation/physical activity goal.    The iFit program complements the theoretical components of the course, with students having the opportunity to plan and execute an intervention to improve a child's capacity to engage in physical activity.

Unit Structure

This Unit involves weekly lectures, Live Seminars (check timetable), Laboratory classes, and practical classes.  The Lectures recorded and available online and cover the theoretical content of the unit.  The Seminars will be conducted live (and live streamed via MS Teams) and give students the opportunity to interact with the teaching staff around course content. The Labs are delivered weekly in the first 4 weeks of semester, and develop students' core skills and knowledge to deliver a client centred goal based exercise intervention for a client in the iFit program. In iFit prac, students get the opportunity to put their theoretical learning into practice, working one-on-one over a 10 week period as an exercise coach of a child.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.
