Teaching contingency plan (In response to Covid19 restrictions)

This course is already delivered online, and as such, there will be no changes to unit delivery in the event of a lock-down. Staff will stay at home if unwell with COVID-19, as directed by UWA Health advice. This might mean that some lectures will be delayed or delivered by a different lecturer. We appreciate that this is not ideal, and see this as a temporary measure put in place to handle this extraordinary circumstance.

If students contract COVID-19 and are too unwell to attend lectures or complete assessments, it is advised that they seek special consideration through their Student Advising Office (see https://www.uwa.edu.au/students/My-course/Exams-assessments-and-results/Special-consideration). If students are feeling well, though need to self-isolate due to a positive COVID-19 test or identification as a close contact, students are still expected to complete unit requirements, due to its online delivery method.

Thank you for your understanding and patience as we try to bring you the best educational experience that we are able to.

Academic Information

Content This unit will focus on equipping students with the knowledge needed to carry out thorough, accurate and clinically useful assessments for which Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD) is a possible differential diagnosis. Assessing for relevant differential diagnoses will also be discussed. The unit includes teaching development of discipline-specific skills. There is a focus on developing skills needed to work in a multidisciplinary or interdisciplinary team.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

All Collaborate sessions will be presented on Blackboard Collaborate. These sessions will be recorded and made available to students through LMS the following day. Students should not rely on the recorded content for learning, as their online presence for the Collaborate sessions is required in order to contribute to class discussions and group activities. Participation in course content will be monitored weekly, in the form of Quizzes and Discussion forums (non-assessed) or Journal Entries (assessed).

This unit is run during a non-standard teaching period, from 25th July 2022 to 9th September 2022. There will be a weekly 'Collaborate Session' run Wednesday's or Thursday's 15:00-18:00 AWST, where students will be able to watch the lecture in real time, participate in class discussions, and have the opportunity to ask questions about lecture content.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
