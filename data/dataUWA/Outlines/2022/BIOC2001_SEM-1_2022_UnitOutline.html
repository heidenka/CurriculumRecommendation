Academic Information

Content This is an introductory unit that explores the molecular basis of life at the cellular level through a flipped classroom, active learning experience. Students will engage with learning materials; e.g. videos (i.e. recorded lectures) and readings, in preparation for activities in workshops and laboratories, which will be continuously assessed throughout semester. The theoretical content of the unit is divided into four major themes: (1) DNA, with a focus on recombinant DNA technology; (2) information flow from DNA to RNA, with an emphasis on how this is regulated at the transcriptional and post-transcriptional levels; (3) the protein life cycle, involving an examination of protein synthesis and catabolism pathways; and (4) proteins as functional molecules, where the fundamentals of enzyme action are highlighted. Detailed content includes (1) role of DNA polymerases in DNA replication, tools of the molecular biologist, cloning, genetic recombination and DNA sequencing; (2) transcriptional regulation of genes, post-transcriptional processing and methods to quantify mRNA transcripts; (3) protein synthesis in prokaryotes and eukaryotes, control of protein synthesis, post-translational modifications, protein targeting, protein catabolism and antibodies as tools of discovery; and (4) proteins as enzymes, enzyme kinetics, proteolytic enzymes, and enzymes as tools of discovery. The laboratories introduce students to modern technologies used in commercial and research laboratories, which include fundamental techniques of homogenisation, centrifugation, spectrophotometry, polymerase chain reaction (PCR), and enzyme kinetics.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

In this unit students will experience a teaching and learning model which is considered blended, interactive, and known to be a more engaging way to learn with the potential to dramatically improve their learning experience. All Theory and Laboratory content follow the same Prepare-Do-Review structure. Students will be required to "prepare" for class using supplied materials (pre-recorded videos/"lectures", explanatory or animated videos, readings, practice questions) to introduce them to the main concepts of the Theme topic or Laboratory experiment. "Do" - For Theory content, each week a workshop is timetabled where students will discuss and apply concepts from the pre-class materials. Workshops are intended to be interactive, and may involve problem solving, case studies, small group work, online quizzes or polls, and practice questions. For Laboratory content students will conduct an experiment and complete questions based on their findings. Finally, "Review", for Theory content (post-workshop) students should engage in revision and self-reflection of their learning, the content, and assess their knowledge using supplied revision questions, then complete the related assessment. For Laboratory content (post-laboratory discussion session), the class will discussion the experiment findings and related questions, followed by completion of related assessment. 
> What does this mean for how students study?
This structure of teaching and learning provides guidance to students on what is expected of them to undertake before timetabled classes, then time in workshops and laboratories can be used to clarify understanding and establish concrete knowledge of the topic. These classes are designed to be interactive and to reinforce theory concepts from supplied learning materials. Academics will facilitate these classes and will be able to clarify any misunderstandings and answer student questions. Students are highly encouraged to participate in the in-class activities ("Do"), chat to peers, and share their understanding in a collaborative approach to learning.

Unit Structure

Theory 
The theory component is divided into four (4) Themes (see "Theory Overview" on LMS) (20% of final mark). Each Theme will have sub-topics. As described above, students will be expected to engage in preparation for workshop sessions by watching recorded lectures or videos, completing readings and/or other supplied learning materials - this engagement with the learning materials will be monitored and marked. Workshops will be interactive, attendance compulsory, and participation expected and marked.
At the completion of each Theme, student knowledge of the theme will be assessed via online quizzes, approximately one (1) question per Theme sub-topic. At the end of semester, there will be an examination (40% of final mark) which will consist of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ) and Short Answer Questions (SAQ). Check the LMS nearer the end of semester for revision material and specific exam information. 
Laboratories
Laboratory and Post-Laboratory Discussion sessions occur in alternate weeks. 
Note: A Pre-laboratory Online Safety Induction Quiz must be completed in order to gain access to the first laboratory classes Pre-laboratory quiz.
Week A – Pre-laboratory (Students complete online in own study time ahead of scheduled laboratory). Watch the pre-recorded video/lecture covering the theory and information about the laboratory (on LMS site and LabArchives eNotebook). Revise using the practice pre-laboratory quiz online (formative assessment) before completing the assessed pre-laboratory quiz online. Note: Quizzes close before the laboratory practical session.
Week B – Laboratory session (Laboratory attendance is compulsory – 3 hours). Students will need to access the unit's LabArchives electronic laboratory notebook and review the laboratory details. Then complete the experiment component and the relevant Results Sheet page and questions within the 3 hour class. 
Week C – Post-laboratory discussion and quiz session (Attendance is compulsory).
The laboratory component is worth 40% of the overall mark, comprised of the laboratory marks (35%) and an oral presentation (5%). Important note:  The 10 marks for each laboratory are scaled to a mark out of 7 for the final grade.
Please read “Laboratory Overview” and "How will I be assessed?" on the LMS site for further details.

Participation

Satisfactory performance in both the laboratory and theory components of the unit is required to pass the unit. Completion of the laboratory requirements (attendance and all assessment tasks) is compulsory.
Theory
Engagement and participation are key to ensure an enhanced learning experience in a blended, flipped classroom. As such, in this unit there are multiple opportunities for students to engage with the theory content, such as watching recorded videos and answering embedded questions, answering online quiz questions and participating in workshop activities, and completing online assessed (summative) quizzes for each Theme. Student engagement will be monitored and rewarded.
Workshop Participation
Theme Quizzes
Missed a Workshop
If you are going to miss a workshop, please contact the Unit Coordinator in the first instance as you may be able to attend an alternative scheduled workshop session. If you do not have special consideration approved, and cannot attend you timetabled session or the alternative scheduled session, you will forfeit any associated mark for the missed workshop session. 
If you are unable to undertake the online theme quizzes within the allocated time (~1 week), and receive special consideration approval, please inform the Unit Coordinator promptly so alternative arrangements may be organised for you to complete the assessment.
Laboratories
The practical laboratories are a compulsory component of this unit. If you have a continuing condition or situation that may impact your ability to carry out one or more of the labs in this unit, you are advised to contact the Unit Coordinator at the beginning of the semester to discuss what accommodations are possible. 
Detailed information about the laboratories can be found online  on the LMS in a document titled "Laboratory guide".
Pre-laboratory quiz (2 marks)
It is critical that you understand the underlying theory and the aims of the laboratory before you undertake the actual practical work. You must attempt the online pre-laboratory quiz questions. Note that quizzes close before the start of the first laboratory for a particular experiment (see “Laboratory Structure and Schedule” on the LMS for dates and times). Important note: All online activity is recorded during the quiz assessment procedure and can be retrieved at a later time if required.
Laboratory session (1 mark)
It is imperative that the laboratories start on time as important health and safety information is delivered in the first few minutes of the practical session.  If you are more than five minutes late, you will not receive any marks for that particular laboratory. 
Attending the Laboratory Session is a prerequisite for the corresponding Post-laboratory discussion session and quiz. If you fail to attend a laboratory session you will not receive any marks for the post-laboratory quiz for that particular laboratory.
Documenting laboratory procedures and data are an essential skill and will be assessed. 
Post-laboratory discussion session and quiz (7 marks)
It is important that the discussion sessions start on time.  If you are more than five minutes late, your quiz will be marked out of 5 instead of 7 (i.e. the maximum will be 5 marks). If you do not attend the post-laboratory discussion session you will not receive any marks for the post-laboratory quiz. 
Missed a laboratory
If you miss a laboratory, but you do not have special consideration approved, you may take the laboratory (and the associated quiz) at another scheduled session, or at the end of semester. However, you will not receive a mark for the laboratory, and the post-laboratory quiz will be marked out of 5 (instead of 7). If you have undertaken the laboratory, but have missed the quiz session, then you can retake the quiz at a later time, but the quiz will be marked out of 5.
If you have a clash for any assessment task, including a laboratory session, please inform the Unit Coordinator before the due date, and alternative arrangements will be considered. 
Laboratories are compulsory and there are penalties for failure to attend and these will be enforced. There are also penalties for failing to complete laboratory components. See more details on the LMS.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
