Academic Information

Content This unit is run by the Centre for Aboriginal Medical and Dental Health and Aboriginal organisations in the community. It is directed at students who are interested in learning more about Aboriginal health and helps improve their understanding of the position of Aboriginal people in contemporary Australian society as a basis for working with Aboriginal people during their health career. Taught predominantly by Aboriginal people, the unit addresses historical and cultural issues that underlie current Aboriginal health problems, and explores specific health issues affecting Aboriginal people. The unit also explores in more detail interactions between Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal service providers in a range of settings and the implications of these experiences on attempts to improve Aboriginal health.

Unit Information

This unit is intended for students that are interested in learning more about Aboriginal health and social matters. The unit provides students the opportunity to learn about health and social issues predominantly from the perspectives of Aboriginal people and Aboriginal Community Controlled Organisations (ACCO).
It is directed at students who are interested in improving their understanding of the position of Aboriginal people in contemporary Australian society, and as a basis for working with Aboriginal people in their future career.
This unit is designed to provide a firm basis of consolidating theory with a practical application in an ACCO. Students can develop skills for working with Aboriginal people during the remainder of the course. Students will have the opportunity to work alongside Aboriginal people in clinical settings, community and organisational level within the Aboriginal health and wellbeing sector.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

To understand the process of shared teaching and learning, as a two-way process is central to effective engagement with Aboriginal peoples and Organisations. This concept underpins the way in which non-Aboriginal organisations or health professionals can work collaboratively with Aboriginal Community Controlled Organisations or communities.
At the completion of this unit students are expected to:
1.	Describe how the organisation started and why.
2.	Discuss with staff the different ways the organisation works with Aboriginal families, communities or other organisations or agencies.
3.	Describe the links between what the organisation provides and the impact on health from a broad-based perspective. 
4.	Demonstrate their involvement in a variety of activities within the organisations as appropriate. 
5.	Describe the process undertaken to how best to contribute to the organisation that will be of benefit for staff, students, or clients of the organisation. 
6.	Reflect, in writing, on how the organisation meets the needs of the Aboriginal community and in what way this may be different from mainstream and non-Aboriginal organisations. 
7.	Reflect, in writing, on what you have learned during the placement and how this may influence your future role as a health care provider within the Aboriginal community.

Unit Structure

The placement involves students being placed with an Aboriginal Community Controlled Organisation for the equivalent of one-half day (3-4 hours) per week for the duration of semester 2 under the direction and supervision of employees of the placement organisation
There are no formal teaching sessions throughout the semester for this unit, however, students will be expected to attend an introductory session at the commencement of the semester and two face to face sessions with CAMDH staff during the placement. These will occur in week one, week four and week eight.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
