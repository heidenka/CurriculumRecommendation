Unit Information

This unit is designed for pre-service teachers who plan to teach secondary school Music. It provides a theoretical framework for teaching, learning, and assessment. Students will gain familiarity with the current upper secondary Music courses, specifically ATAR Music and Music General. Through active participation in workshops, pre-service teachers acquire strategies for effective teaching and learning, gain skills for planning and programming, learn about instructional materials and resources, and develop an understanding of issues relevant to Music teaching.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Sessions will be run in a variety of formats, including lectures, tutorials, seminars, workshops, laboratories, practical classes, forums, and masterclasses. Sessions will cover a range of topics that address both theory and practice. Sessions are interactive, involving musical leadership and participation.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assessment 1 - WACE Unit Planning (40 marks)
Create a three-week (12 periods) segment of work for any of the ATAR Music units. 
Your programme must,
• Be outlined in a three-week programme of no fewer than twelve lessons. Please do not include in-class assessments or individual working periods (e.g. “private instrumental practice”) as part of the twelve lessons. Each lesson needs to include strategies and activities.
• Include:
- Curriculum links to the ATAR Syllabus
- Learning outcomes or learning intentions for each lesson
- Teaching and learning strategies (outline learning strategies and activities in sufficient
detail for the program to be enacted by another teacher)
- Teaching points (elaborations of content)
- Resources (ten pages of resources or equivalent)
- An assessment task and marking key
- Incorporation of a designated work or works
All materials and resources other than those produced by you must be referenced.
Works discussed must be appropriate to age and syllabus requirements to be included in the unit, and chosen from:
• A significant work from the genre (e.g. a Mozart piano concerto, a piece from Miles Davis’ Kind of Blue or a song from The Beatles’ White Album).
• A designated work from any of the ATAR contexts (please note, if you are select Unit 1 or 2, please use the Year 11 designated works, if you select Unit 3 or 4, please use the Year 12 designated works).
  
      
        
  
You will submit:
• An overview of your part of the unit: context, rationale (including how this unit relates to adolescent social, emotional, cognitive, physical development) and the ATAR Music discipline (please use references – you may select sources from the unit Reading List, Music Curriculum 1 Reading List, or any other resources). This overview should be approximately 700 words, and
• Your programme – including all the points stated above, and
• Reflection – 500-word statement about the experience (you may wish to refer to the literature here as well)
Assessment 2 - Curriculum Investigation (30 marks)
In this assessment, you will identify and explore the pedagogies, principles and practices of teaching senior students in Music (in comparison to years 7-10).
The assessment is based upon the question:
What constitutes best practice in the learning and teaching of years 11 and 12 in Music?
In answering the question, you may wish to concentrate upon a specific area such as the ATAR stream, Music General, a Certificate course or International Baccalaureate. You might also consider the importance of catering for the diverse needs of learners at year 11 and 12 level including indigenous, EAL/D, Special Needs and Gifted and Talented students.
This assessment will engage you in investigation, research and examination of best practice, which is an important aspect of your professional career. To do so, read widely in the professional, empirical literature and investigate what is presented to teachers through federal and state organisations, such as the Western Australian School Curriculum and Standards Authority and the New South Wales Board of Studies. Do also investigate state-based Department of Education sites which can have many interesting and relevant resources. You are also encouraged to refer to the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers and policy documents.
Please ensure that you highlight the demands of senior school and points of difference between upper and middle school. You may wish to use specific examples and draw upon policy statements.
          
          
        
  
  
Present your research as a presentation in class. You should present for 8-10 minutes, including time for questions.
Assessment 3 - Set Works Analysis Documents (30 marks)
Based on the example produced by ASME/UWA, and the requirements in the ATAR Music syllabus, present an analysis of the following,
a) One set work from any of the genres in the Western Art Music syllabus (with the exception of four works analysed in the ASME/UWA book), OR
b) Two set works from any of the eras in the Jazz syllabus or the styles in the Contemporary syllabus. Both of these set works should come from the same Year group and era/style.
Your analysis should address the main points listed in the ATAR syllabus in relation to the work(s), including:
- Genre, Composers, Era/Period, and Musical characteristics of the work for Western Art Music, or
- Overview of historical stylistic influences on the works, overview of the artist and the performers, how the piece is representative of the era, and Musical Characteristics of the works for Jazz, or
- Musical characteristics of the works, overview of historical influences on the works, and context of how the works fit into the development of contemporary music for Contemporary Music
Your total analysis package (either one work for WAM or both works for Jazz/Contemporary) should be approximately 12 pages. You may choose to add some questions, or links to online resources for “further reading” for students within this document.
After submitting this work, please share your analysis documents with your colleagues to be used as resources for teaching ATAR Music in the coming years.

RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
