Academic Information

Content This highly interactive experiential unit aims to enhance students' personal effectiveness and to develop foundation leadership capabilities. The unit is underpinned by positive psychology principles as well as a contemporary model of work behaviour that identifies the importance of mastery, agility and proactivity for success in today's complex and changing organisations. In Part 1—Leading Self, reflective activities are used to develop students' personal mastery (e.g. knowing one's strengths/limitations, goal setting, time management), agility (e.g. coping with change, being resilient) and proactivity (e.g. being creative, taking charge, design thinking). In Part 2—Leading Others, practical activities and simulations are used to enhance students' ability to promote mastery, agility and proactivity in others via transactional and transformational leadership.

Unit Information
“When we experience the world as “too complex” we are not just experiencing the complexity of the world. We are experiencing a mismatch between the world’s complexity and our own at this moment. There are only two logical ways to mend this mismatch—reduce the world’s complexity or increase our own." —Robert Kegan & Lisa Lahey, 2018.
The Volatile, Uncertain, Complex and Ambiguous (VUCA) environment requires a different approach to leadership to facilitate the shift to 21st Century leadership. To be able to not only survive the disruptive, volatility, uncertainty, complexity and uncertainty but to thrive and flourish balancing profits, people and planet.
Much of what worked before is no longer good enough or even appropriate. Rapid transformation and evolution is required to build life affirming organisations designed to unleash human potential. This Unit blends horizontal leadership development approaches with a focus on skills and competencies (enables us to become more skilful and adept at things we already do) with vertical development that enhances our capacity to shift our mindsets and beliefs to be able to respond creatively through increased complexity of mind. Vertical development aligns more with capacity, horizontal developmental is more about competency.
Leaders benefit from an approach that connects vertical and horizontal approaches because adding to a knowledge base and responding in ever more complex ways, means they increase their capacity to respond generatively and effectively to the challenges of modern life. The “vertical” movement is one where individuals develop a deeper, broader and more dynamic worldview through personal transformation. The “horizontal” movement is about expanding what you know. In this Unit, you will have the opportunity to develop both horizontally as well as vertically.
Unit goals:
In this unit, you will:
(1) Become conscious of and learn how to shift action/ mental/ emotional habits that are limiting effectiveness;
(2) Develop your capacity to address complex problems, through increasing your emotional intelligence and systems thinking;
(3) Be provided with the opportunity to develop your authentic leadership including reaching beyond yourself, heightening self awareness, courage, purpose, agility, values and strengths; and
(4) Build capacity to lead from the inside out through a process of personal development and leadership transformation in order to elevate impact and influence on individuals, teams and organisations.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

As Herbert Otto astutely commented, personal development and growth will only happen if you are willing to try new behaviours: “change and growth take place when a person has risked himself and dares to become involved with experimenting with his own life”. Throughout Leading Self and Others, we encourage you to try new behaviours and be open to experimenting.
This unit is probably not for you if you believe that "good education equals good grades". It is fine to want good grades and we encourage you to work hard to do your best. But we also believe that good education is about personal growth and learning. We have designed this unit to be a pass/fail unit so you can fully concentrate on your personal development.
But just because the unit is pass/ fail, please don't assume it is an easy ride! We expect a lot from you in this unit because we know that full engagement and participation is the only way to progress your development.
The Unit has been carefully designed to provide you with the opportunity for personal transformation. Theories of personal transformation support the idea that personal transformation is an internal creative problem solving process occurring at the level of the unconscious but sparked by an interactive dissonance between environment and individual affective/ cognitive processes. Critical to the transformative learning process appears the confrontation of a disorienting dilemma or feeling of being “stuck”. It is the experience of failing or being stuck no matter which path you choose that prompts a willingness to pause, look elsewhere, and reflect. The common principles of the transformation learning process appear to follow a set of stages (Walinga and Harris). 
a) A disorienting dilemma or problem causing
b) a threatening and challenging opportunity for reflection, problem solving and expansion of consciousness at which point the individual must
c) make a deliberate choice to confront the conflict or dilemma by
d) question assumptions 
e) release old ways of knowing, becoming receptive to new ways of viewing the self, and reinterpreting experiences in a new context resulting in
f) a new level of consciousness or insight which unites the mind and heart to form a new self-definition  and express a more inclusive, differentiated, permeable and integrated meaning perspective Transformation is followed by
g) feelings of excitement, satisfaction and freedom as well as sadness associated with loss of the old self.  Finally, transformation is
h) enduring change in attitude and behaviour. Once transformation has never returns to the old perspective.
Therefore your participation in this Unit is highly likely to challenge and disorient you especially if you have not engaged as yet with any personal development work. Based on previous experience and observation, you are generally likely to respond in two different ways - either through a courageous deep engagement with the opportunity to develop and grow within a learner centred environment - or not! You will require time and energy and commitment to your own growth in order to reap the full benefits from this experience.

Unit Structure

Leading Self & Others consists of three components: (1) Leading Self, (2) Leading Others and (3) True North Groups.
In Leading Self, the unit aims to support your development so you can deal with challenges associated with “leading oneself”, such as knowing your strengths and being able to cope with setbacks and disappointments. Research shows that MBA students often struggle with these types of issues within their career. A 30 Day Challenge will allow you to practice Habit Change to increase your personal mastery. You will have the opportunity to identify your Motivators, Purpose and Values and to explore the role of mindfulness in teaching you to pay attention to the present moment, recognising your feelings and emotions and keeping them under control, especially when faced with highly stressful situations. When you are mindful, you’re aware of your presence and the ways you impact other people. This lays the foundation for the development of your authentic leadership and living and leading in alignment with your values and according to your True North. This will allow you to "Wake Up, Grow Up, Show Up" (Google, Search Inside Yourself Leadership Institute).
In Leading Others, the unit will introduce you to vertical leadership approaches and how to identify developmental opportunities to expand your complexity of mind so that you can ensure that your leader/action logic is able to meet the complexity of the environment and that you are not operating from yesterdays logic. We shall explore the importance of leadership culture and how this impacts organisation evolution. You will be introduced to the coaching approach and have the opportunity to practice the methodology.  You will also be provided with frameworks, skills and competencies on how to hold caring, courageous performance conversations.
The True North Leadership Development groups draw upon the work of Bill George and Doug Baker from Harvard University. “At various times,” George and Baker write, “a True North Group will function as a nurturer, a grounding rod, a truth teller, and a mirror. At other times the group functions as a challenger or an inspirer. When people are wracked with self-doubts, it helps build their courage and ability to cope.” You will join a group with meetings held fortnightly throughout the Unit with the option of extending these throughout the year to continue to build the cohort experience and to allow you to continue your leadership journey.

Participation

Participation in class, whether it is listening to a lecture or getting involved in other activities, is an important part of the learning process, therefore it is important that you attend classes. More formally, the University regulations state that ‘to complete a course or unit students shall attend prescribed classes, lectures, seminars and tutorials’. Where a student, due to exceptional circumstances, is unable to attend a scheduled class, they are required to obtain prior approval of the unit coordinator to be absent from that class. Any student absent from class without having had such absence approved by the unit coordinator may be referred to the faculty for advice and may be required to withdraw from the unit.
All students will be required to actively participate in all of the prescribed activities including online lecture sessions, surveys, discussion boards, leadership development groups sessions held fortnightly and completion of all of the reflective leadership development journal entries in order to receive an ungraded pass for this unit.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
