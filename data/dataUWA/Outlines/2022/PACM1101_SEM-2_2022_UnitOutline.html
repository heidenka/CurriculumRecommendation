Unit Information

Welcome to PACM1101: Communication in Practice!
PACM1101 promotes excellence in academic and professional communications, critical and creative thinking, and the most effective approaches to learning and collaborating with others. This unit is for all UWA students no matter where you currently are on your student journey.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit focuses on practical skills-development, so there is great emphasis placed on active engagement. Over the course of the semester, students will be putting communication into practice. You will be working on your academic, reflective and professional writing, presenting and critical and creative thinking skills, both individually and in teams. Attendance at tutorials is therefore essential. In addition, the weekly lectures are designed to introduce key concepts in preparation for tutorials and the unit assessments.

Unit Structure

Each week there is a two-hour lecture-workshop and a one hour tutorial.
The lecture-workshops are on Wednesdays 12pm-2pm. There are three options for this class: (1) you can attend in-person, or (2) you can live-stream the lecture and interact online via MS Teams, or if you are not able to join us on Wednesdays (3) you can watch the recording. 
Attendance at tutorials is required (see 'Participation' below). This is where students will be engaging in unit content directly, focusing on deepening their understandings gained from the lecture-workshops and assigned readings. 
Please note: If you are enrolled in the online version of this unit, you must have regular access to a computer with a camera and microphone in order to engage with others in the class.

Participation

Participation
Participation in all aspects of this unit is an essential part of the learning process. To successfully complete PACM1101, and to get the most out of the unit, it is expected that you will do these four things every week:
1) Review the readings and videos in the Weekly Activities folder on the LMS
2) Attend the lecture-workshop in-person or online, or watch the recording
3) Actively participate in tutorials
4) Work on your assessments
In addition, there is a tutorial Participation mark for PACM1101*.
A mark of 15% will be awarded based on (1) your engagement online and in-class activities, (2) your willingness to help others learn, and (3) evidence that you have come prepared having reviewed all the necessary materials for that week.
*NOTE: The participation mark does not apply to attendance at the interactive Lecture-Workshops. We strongly encourage you to attend these classes in real-time because doing so enhances your learning; however, if you cannot make it, you can watch the recordings online and do the activities in your own time.
Attendance and the Team Project
Since teamwork and the development of interpersonal skills is a major component of this unit, participation in the Team Project is dependent upon satisfactory engagement in tutorials over the semester. All students must attend a minimum of 4 out of 5 tutorials before Week 6, and at least 7 out of 9 tutorials before Week 11, in order to participate in the Team Project. 
Any students who do not meet these conditions and do not have a valid exemption (e.g. Special Consideration or a UniAccess Academic Adjustment Plan) will not be able to complete this assessment, and no alternative assessments can be given.

Referencing Requirements

It is important that the referencing of any sources used in your written work is done properly, if only to substantiate the points you are making in your assignment or project. 
UWA prefers the APA 7 referencing type, unless otherwise stated by the unit coordinator. You can find resources on using APA 7 on the University Library website: https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa
The EndNote software package is a really good system for building up a database of references. Not everyone will want to invest the time in using this system but you should consider it if you intend to build up resource materials or plan to undertake extensive research in a particular area. The library staff have also developed a tutoring package: ‘A Quick Guide to Using EndNote’ which provides the basics for using EndNote with an essay http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/endnote

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
