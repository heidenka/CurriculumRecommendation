Academic Information

Content This unit explores the structure and trading practices of financial markets. It examines the roles of market participants, price discovery processes and the impact of regulation and transactions costs. The unit provides an opportunity for students to gain practical experience in the trading of financial assets.

Unit Information

Welcome to FINA5524 Trading and Markets.
The main aim of the unit is to provide insights into the complex workings of financial markets and boost your confidence when discussing issues associated with financial markets and trading. The unit will benefit students who are interested in working in the finance industry and will be of particular interest to those planning careers in portfolio management, equities and foreign exchange trading and financial market regulation.
In the unit, you will be introduced to the theory and practice of securities trading on organised exchanges. We will discuss how prices reflect information about fundamental value and how the design of markets may affect price efficiency. We will examine the role of different traders and discuss the determinants of market liquidity. We will also examine how and why some traders consistently profit from trading while other traders lose. During the semester, you will experience how trading occurs through your participation in a series of trading games and simulations. In the trading games, we will explore the effects of information, market rules, and trader composition on liquidity and price formation.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

FINA5524 Trading and Markets is delivered using 3-hour seminars and trading games/simulations. Learning in this unit is achieved by a combination of lectures, problem-sets, simulations, readings, and discussions.
During class, a broad overview of the topic will be provided and important issues will be highlighted. You are expected to read the assigned chapters and readings to bring yourself up to speed on the issues. Subsequently, you will be given the opportunity to discuss and explore these issues. The lecture slides will be made available in advance and hard copies will not be provided.
Attendance and participation for the trading simulation sessions is required. You will not be able to submit the trading reflection report if you have not attended the trading sessions and so you may lose up to 30% of total assessment for the unit. The trading sessions will draw on the ideas and issues discussed in class. It is important that you reflect on these issues when constructing your trading reflection reports.

Unit Structure

The unit will generally be presented as a series of three-hour seminars once per week. The exceptions are Week 6,  when you will have a mid-semester test, and the weeks when you will participate in the trading simulation.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
