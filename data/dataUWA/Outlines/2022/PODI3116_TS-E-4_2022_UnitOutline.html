Academic Information

Content In this unit, students develop their patient history taking, communication, professionalism, reflective practice and manual dexterity skills by engaging in experiential learning in the UWA Podiatry Clinic to provide care to low risk patients. Students also engage in simulated work place learning to develop their understanding and application of safe practice management by demonstrating skills in clinical record keeping, gaining consent, patient confidentiality, podiatric assisting and explaining relevant mandatory policies and protocols in podiatric practice. Students are exposed to the application of therapeutic management of podiatric patients here. Students also develop clinical assessment skills, diagnostic principles and are able to justify assessment techniques to diagnose foot and ankle conditions and implement evidence based conservative treatments, while applying the principles of infection prevention and control protocols. Students learn the application of biomechanical principles to orthotic therapy and fundamentals of clinical assessments and conservative care, to then learn how to design,develop and manufacture a custom made foot orthoses to support and treat patients with foot & ankle pathologies with foot orthoses. Students must abide by the Professional Behaviour Procedure set in out in the experiential work-place learning environments.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit has been designed to accommodate all the students as students have varying levels of knowledge in podiatric medicine. This unit builds on the skills obtained in semester 1 units.
PODI3116 aims to help students develop the overarching inherent qualities required as a graduate podiatrist.
- Develop psychomotor skills in orthotic modifications
- Develop and obtain competency in basic clinical skills
- Undertake self-evaluation
- Develop critical thinking and reflection

Unit Structure

Our philosophy is to allow students to apply what they have learnt. The combination of theoretical and practical aspects of this unit is to help you get the basic skills required to approach more advanced clinical skills required in the later years. We hope that this unit will provide foundational skills as a graduate endorsed podiatrist. This unit will be taught by combining lectures, practical exercises, and self-directed learning.
Content of the unit is available in WEEKLY CONTENT on LMS. The delivery, content and order of the unit material may vary throughout the semester and will be indicated up to one week prior on LMS announcements.
Order of lecture/tutorial sessions or material are subject to change and will be notified to students at least 48 hours prior; exceptional circumstances such as injury or sickness will require rescheduling or default to pre-recorded materials.

Participation

100% attendance is expected at all lectures and practical sessions. If you are ill or have an appointment that causes you to miss a session, please email Dr Catherine Crabb and Dr Madison Champion ASAP: catherine.crabb@uwa.edu.au and madison.champion@uwa.edu.au
Attendance
Attendance at lectures is expected and is compulsory for laboratory and eLearning sessions.  Attendance will be taken at these compulsory sessions. Students must ensure they have signed in for the teaching sessions where roll is kept.  It is not the responsibility of the tutor or demonstrator.  You will be considered to be absent if you have not signed in for the session. 
Students can only miss 1 compulsory session during the semester without a medical certificate.  In the case of illness, a medical certificate (not provided by a family member) must be supplied to the unit coordinator within one week of return to classes.  Any missed tasks will need to be completed by this time. Note that the onus is on the student to make contact regarding absences. If a student misses more than this, he/she will be expected to contact the unit coordinator with an explanation.  A failure to provide a valid explanation, or further missed classes will result in action according to the FMDHS Professional Behaviour Assessment, available on the DPM Community LMS page. 
Lateness
Students who are consistently late to teaching sessions (more than 10 minutes) will be marked as late and an explanation will be sought from the student.  Your fellow students and teachers appreciate punctuality which is part of professional behaviour and courtesy. Multiple late arrivals will be managed according to the FHMS Professional Behaviour Assessment. Late arrival at lectures is also discourteous and unprofessional. If you are unavoidably late, it is important that you do not enter a lecture theatre by the front entrance. 
Adherence to rostered session times
It is critical that where a student is assigned to attend a particular session at a particular time, only that session may be attended. Students are not to attend non-assigned sessions for their convenience. Students doing so will be subject to the FMDHS Professional Behaviour Assessment process.
Patient confidentiality
As podiatry students you are of course not only free to discuss your experiences with each other, but you are actually encouraged to do so. Some teaching may involve discussion of clinical cases. Do not discuss these where you may be overheard by the public. DO NOT upload information or images regarding clinical cases to social media sites.  It is important to remember that any information you learn about a patient should be regarded as a matter of professional confidence.

Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;
Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
