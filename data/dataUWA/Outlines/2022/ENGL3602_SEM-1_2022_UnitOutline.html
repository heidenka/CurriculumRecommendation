Teaching contingency plan (In response to Covid19 restrictions)

My approach is to try and run as much in-person as circumstances allow. All lectures will either be recorded or pre-recorded. Our tutorials are set up as face-to-face classes, with the capacity for online attendance for individuals studying externally. If we go into lockdown, or if a staff member has to isolate, then these will convert to online classes. If a student has to isolate, then you will be able to join an in-person tutorial remotely. If you have any concerns about this strategy, particularly in terms of managing your own health in the context of the pandemic, please contact me and we will facilitate your needs!

Academic Information

Content This unit explores Shakespeare's original texts as the basis for understanding creative decisions that shape filmic versions of Shakespeare's plays and theatre performance. Focusing on literary and dramatic elements which underpin Shakespeare's complex plays and their realisation in performance, intellectual, historical and cultural contexts of Shakespeare's ideas and different approaches to performing Shakespeare throughout history, the unit encourages students to think critically about individual directorial approaches to issues supported by Shakespeare's works, and about the works' continued relevance in our world.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Online lectures (45mins) and face-to-face tutorials (1 x 1.5hrs pw). In the event of COVID restrictions imposed, tutorials will go online via Collaborate on the LMS.
Lectures will introduce the texts and performances that we will work through in the unit, before we open to further discussion and exploration in tutorials. The LMS provides access to the relevant weekly readings, the texts and performance recordings via Digital Theatre + and Kanopy. You will be expected to source other performances and filmic renderings of the texts yourselves. 
I will expect all students to work with me in the tutorial space to develop an environment for conversation based on inclusion, informed engagement and enthusiasm for our pursuit. (I mean, it's pretty easy to be enthusiastic about Shakespeare, right?)

Unit Structure

Twelve weeks semester, including ten weeks of lectures/podcasts (with recordings available for all which are in-person); ten weeks with tutorials; and two reading weeks (Weeks 7 & 12), creating space for students to prepare assessment material. 
It is expected that students will be prepared for all classes, having read the required material beforehand and completed any necessary activities.

Participation

Participation is graded as 20% of your final mark. Students are expected to listen to all lectures/podcasts, to attend all tutorials (either on-campus or joining online), and participate actively in the tutorial activities and general discussions each week. Students are expected to have listened, read and understood the assigned weekly materials. The participation mark is based on active engagement in tutorial discussions and activities, and evidence of thorough engagement with the unit materials, including readings and lectures. This mark will be assessed by your tutor over the course of the unit.
Participation in class, whether it is listening to a lecture or getting involved in other activities, is an important part of the learning process. It is therefore important that you attend classes. More formally, the University regulations state that ‘to complete a course or unit students shall attend prescribed classes, lectures, seminars and tutorials’. Where a student, due to exceptional circumstances, is unable to attend a scheduled class, they are required to obtain prior approval of the unit coordinator to be absent from that class, or present evidence for special consideration. Any student absent from class without having had such absence approved by the unit coordinator may be referred to the faculty for advice and may be required to withdraw from the unit.
In the event of a COVID lockdown or the isolation of a staff member, tutorials will revert to on-line learning. Students will still be expected to listen to all lectures/podcasts, and to attend all on-line tutorials (via Collaborate on the LMS).

EXPLICIT MATERIAL
The unit proceeds on the basis that students are adults and the texts that are explored do contain adult material. If you feel that a text contains material that may be upsetting, please let your tutor or unit coordinator know and an alternative arrangement will be discussed. You are never compelled to watch or read a text if you do not want to for personal reasons, though you may be expected to complete alternate work in this instance.
Because these issues are personal, it is very hard to predict in advance for all students if certain performed scenes or themes may be upsetting. We therefore empower students to take responsibility for exercising discretion and encourage you to take reasonable precautions if you feel you are vulnerable; for example, by Googling texts and checking that they are not likely to contain material that you find upsetting.
SEEKING SUPPORT IN THE UNIT
Learning can be strenuous, and regularly life interferes in the learning process. We encourage all students who are finding the unit difficult, for any reason, to talk with the teaching team, so that we can best facilitate your needs in completing the unit. Please do not hesitate to email your tutor or talk to them in class.

Again, please be in touch with me.


Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
