Unit Information

CONTENT: This unit develops the skills necessary to teach Health and Physical education in the secondary school. It focuses on practical and theoretical skills with an emphasis on effective and ineffective behaviours. The unit has a lecture, tutorial and practical format. Major areas covered include the development of organisational, personal, planning and management skills, and the formation of a personal philosophy towards the teaching of Health and Physical Education.
Students continue to develop professional skills based on teaching practicum experience.  There is an emphasis on camp planning and delivery, and preparation for further teaching practicum.  Outcomes in transferability (communication, lifelong learning), science as a human endeavor (innovation, social context), international quality (research-informed teaching, leadership) are emphasized.  
OUTCOMES: Students are able to (1) demonstrate an awareness of the developmental and diverse learning needs of adolescent learners in HPE; (2) develop a repertoire of teaching and learning strategies relevant to the upper school HPE curriculum; (3) develop sound specialist content knowledge in Health Education, Physical Education and Outdoor Education; (4) apply a range of assessment and evaluation processes and strategies; (5) critically and positively reflect upon own teaching practice; and (6) research established and new theories of learning and model best practice as a way to improve own performance.
ASSESSMENT: Indicative assessments in this unit are as follows: (1) camp—planning, development and delivery, teaching program; (2) issues in HPE: report; and (3) assignment: feedback rates in teaching.
CONTACT HOURS: lectures: 30 hours; tutorials: as arranged

Learning And Teaching Strategy

M.Teach students are preparing for a professional vocation that demands high standards of teaching, professionalism and conduct.  Students should assume that they are part of this profession, and should conduct themselves accordingly.  An appropriate standard of punctuality and behaviour is expected.

Specific Objectives
	At the conclusion of this unit, the student should be able to:
•	Write lesson plans incorporating clearly stated outcomes, introductions, skill development and culmination phases of the lesson.
•	Understand and utilise student outcome statements (or equivalent).
•	Implement a variety of organisational skills to assist in class management, discipline and safety.
•	Acquire a positive, varied and enthusiastic approach towards the teaching of Physical Education.
•	Adopt a positive approach towards the need for appropriate dress and presentation in teaching Physical Education.
•	Implement appropriate teaching strategies to cope with the varying conditions, levels and classes found in secondary schools in Western Australia.
•	Promote maximum participation and use of equipment by students, including its efficient distribution and collection in Physical Education lessons.
•	Issue instructions which are clear, precise and complete, delivered with a varied use of one's voice and manner.
•	Implement suitable procedures for non-participants, roll checks, change room supervision and inclement weather.
•	Introduce effective management procedures to enhance learning outcomes in a safe classroom environment.


 Major Areas to be covered in the Unit
•	Developing a personal philosophy towards the teaching of Physical Education and personal fitness.
•	Developing a range of organisational skills including methods of
o	Gaining attention
o	Defining the activity area
o	Teacher positioning
o	Equipment distribution
o	Use of appropriate formations
o	Effective use of concluding
•	Developing personal skills which will enhance the teaching of Physical Education:
o	Appearance
o	Use of voice
o	Use of gesture
o	Use of eye contact
o	Appropriate movement around class
o	Posture
o	Focusing
•	An introduction to lesson planning in Physical Education including:
o	The writing of outcomes (or equivalent)
o	The writing of suitable Introductions, Skill Developments and Culminations
•	A study of the Curriculum Framework so that students are aware of its content areas and teaching strategies
•	Development of teaching strategies using Mosston & Ashworth’s (2002) categories of:
o	Command style
o	Task style
o	Reciprocal style
o	Small group styles of teaching
o	Self-check style
o	Guided-discovery style
•	Investigate management procedures which effectively increase activity time and decrease instructional and organisation time.
•	Develop appropriate types and rate of feedback plus correct ratios of positive to negative feedback.
•	Preparation for teaching practice.

ASSESSMENT
You must pass all assessment components to obtain a pass in the unit.  You will only be able to demonstrate all National Professional Teaching Standards by passing every assessment task.  If you fail any component, it is your responsibility to see the unit coordinator to discuss possible resubmission of the component to upgrade a fail mark to a pass.  You may only resubmit one assignment in unit.  A 50% pass grade only will be recorded for resubmitted work that meets a pass standard.
This is a professional unit.  If an absence is unavoidable, you have a responsibility to let your lecturer and your peers know, in advance, so that your absence can be accommodated.  If you have missed so much work as to be inadequately prepared for teaching practice, you will not be allocated a school for Professional Practice units.  Unsatisfactory attendance can result in exclusion from further study under University and Faculty Regulations.
All students are required to provide their own whistle and have it available at all times.
Classes will be both theoretical and practical in nature.  Students are required to bring activity attire to all classes.

LATE WORK AND EXTENSIONS
The ability to meet deadlines and manage your time to fulfil administrative and teaching obligations is a vital professional skill.  Schools are intensely busy places that require teachers to juggle many tasks simultaneously.  Your ability to submit assignments on time will demonstrate an ability to fulfil professional responsibilities.  Assignment submission dates indicated in the unit outline are final.  Extensions will only be given with the presentation of a medical certificate or in dire, extenuating circumstances.  Do not ask for an extension for difficulties with managing your time, for work commitments or lifestyle and other commitments; you will be directed to simply submit the work that is done at the time.
Late assignments will receive a penalty of 10% per day.  After 5 days the best mark possible will be 50%.  All assignments must be submitted and passed in order to satisfactorily complete the requirements of this unit. •	Punctuality
•	Appropriate dress: clean, sun-smart etc.
•	Asking considered questions – facilitating class discussion.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
