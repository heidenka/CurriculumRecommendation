Academic Information

Content By participating in one of a range of music ensembles—some subject to audition (e.g. symphony orchestra)—this unit enables students to develop ensemble skills to a level of competent group performance. A series of seminars focus on (1) essential contexts and concepts in relation to musical ensembles in general; and (2) reflective practice as a research methodology applied to music ensemble participation. All ensembles perform at least once in public during the semester, either in the University or in the wider community. This unit is an elective unit in the Bachelor of Arts degree. It is also open to students enrolled in other degree pathways who have an interest in music.

Participation

The ensemble component consists of two marks, allocated to you by your ensemble teacher:  a.   	Public Performance (30% of the final grade).  
b.  	Rehearsal Attendance and Participation (30% of the final grade)
Attendance will be taken at rehearsals and seminars. 
Your Rehearsal Attendance and Public Performance Marks will be 0% under the following conditions:
You have an unexplained absence from a Performance.
You have an unexplained absence from a Dress Rehearsal.
You have more than three unexplained absences from normally scheduled rehearsals.
Unexcused absences from rehearsal will attract a penalty deduction of 10% per absence up to three absences.  You should submit medical certificates for absences, or apply for special consideration through your home faculty student office.

Other

The Participation Mark is a mark out of 30, often expressed as a percentage (so 75% would Mean a mark of 22.5/30).    This mark is allocated to you by the ensemble director.
The Participation Mark is a mark allocated by effort as well as skills development.
The Participation Mark IS NOT an attendance mark.   Just because you went to every class, do not expect a mark of 100% for participation.
Effort
Effort is measured in many ways.   It can be the way you interact with the students around you.  I can be the way you offer suggestions at the appropriate time in classes.  It could be a desire to improve and ask lots of questions.
In short, the easiest way to get a good mark for effort is bringing yourself to the attention of the ensemble director in a positive way.   Arrive early.  Pack up.   Help your fellow students.   Ask questions.
Skills Development
All of the instrumental groups will require you to develop skills.   As in any music activity, the best way to acquire skills is to practice.   To take the time between classes to learn the material you have studied.  
You need to accept that almost all of these classes will require an effort - that will need you to be able to confidently and without error execute complex skills. That is best acquired through practice.   Ensemble directors will all tell you that it is easy to identify those who are practicing - regardless of the entering skill level.
The Performance Mark is also a mark out of 30, also often expressed as a percentage.
As, in many of our ensembles, it is difficult to identify individuals in the performance, it is quite often the case that the group receives the same or similar mark for performance.   This is altered by solos, or particular effort that is perceived by the director.
The best way to get performance marks is to be ready to play.  In other words, to have both the skills and the mental preparation to perform. 

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
