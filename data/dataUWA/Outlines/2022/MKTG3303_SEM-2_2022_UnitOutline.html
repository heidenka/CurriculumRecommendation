Academic Information

Content This unit focuses on new product development (NPD) approaches and frameworks practiced within established small and large firms. The first part of this unit concentrates on product development (idea generation, crowdsourcing, business analytics, screening, and concept testing). This is followed by intellectual property protection and market development (beta testing, product launch, NPD branding strategies, portfolio management, and market entry). This unit combines the elements of strategic management, strategic marketing, business analytics, and marketing research, but has a specific focus on NPD.

Unit Information

Welcome to MKTG3303 New Product Development and Commercialisation!
Not to innovate (not to introduce a new product), is to die. Firm’s market longevity is contingent on the success of their new/modified products. This unit focuses on new product development (NPD) approaches and frameworks practiced within established firms to give students an opportunity to execute each NPD step via a series of practical group project tasks.
This unit combines the elements of strategic management, strategic marketing, business analytics, and marketing research. It is split into two parts, which will give you a clear understanding of the processes involved in NPD and commercialisation:
-	Part 1: Weeks 1–8: product development which covers: idea generation, crowdsourcing, screening, and concept testing
-	Part 2: Weeks 9-12: market development, which covers: beta testing, product launch, NPD branding strategies, and market entry. 
If you are interested in start-ups and new product development within start-ups, it is recommended that you enrol in MGMT3342 Entrepreneurship unit. 
Students enrolled in this unit need to watch 1.5 hours pre-recorded lectures prior to attending workshops. Workshops will be carried either face-to-face or online (check your enrolment mode).

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is based on problem-based learning via doing. This unit's format consists of a weekly online lectures consisting of short videos and a hands-on workshops to offer practical application of theory. You need to watch all videos designated for each week before you attend the workshops. Please see LMS for a more detailed week-by-week plan (Road Map). 
In workshops, we will cover the concepts mentioned in lectures in more depth and you will be working on your gorup project for 10 weeks in teams. Each week, you will progress to a new stage of NPD and will complete an NPD-related task during or outside the workshops. You will be also required to compete certain tasks prior to attending workshops (please refer to LMS for more detail). 
Learning and the tasks are approximated to the real-life NPD process in the existing firms where business intelligence is gathered, analysed, discussed in boardrooms and weekly meetings. 
Teaching and Learning Evaluation:
You may be asked to complete two evaluations during this unit: the Student Perception of Teaching (SPOT) and the Students' Unit Reflective Feedback (SURF). The SPOT is optional and is an evaluation of the lecturer and the unit. The SURF is completed online and is a university wide survey and deals only with the unit. You will receive an email from the SURF office inviting you to complete the SURF when it is activated. We encourage you to complete the forms as your feedback is extremely important and can be used to make changes to the unit or lecturing style when appropriate. This feedback is very valuable, is accepted without prejudice and has been instrumental in improving teaching and learning in this unit.

Unit Structure

The unit progression is approximated to the real-life business environment where you are required to perform various NPD business analytics on your own or as part of a team, deliver short presentations in a boardroom setting and meet internal deadlines. Online lectures introduce the key processes involved in NPD, which are then practiced and applied in more depth in each workshop. Workshops and teaming up start in Week 1 – please make sure you do not miss the first workshop. 
Please refer to the Class Allocation System (https://www.cas.uwa.edu.au/) page for the workshops timetable.
Group project:
There is no SPARK evaluation for the team project in this unit. If any team experiences issues with group work, it members should reach out to the lecturer or the tutor and request an “Individual contribution Diary” assessment where each team member will be required to complete a one-page diary to details her/his contributions. This diary will be used to assess the quality and quantity of students’ contributions to the common goal. The lecturer/tutor reserves the right to award a lower grade to a student whose contribution to the team project was not equitable to other team members or when there are inconsistencies in submitted diaries. Please make sure to contact your tutor at least 3 days before the assessment is due to request this diary. The grade of the student in question will be calculated as an average (as per the attached file) rating given by all team members.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
