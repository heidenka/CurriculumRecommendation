Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is taught through participation in teaching and learning contexts modelled in seminars and workshops. Sessions will cover a range of topics that address both theory and practice. Sessions are interactive, involving musical leadership and participation.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:
1) Essay (DUE 11/11/2022)
Students should write an essay of 2000 words on a topic related to the field of ensemble education. A selection should be made from one of the following titles:
a) The use of vocalisation in instrumental teaching and learning
b) Teaching theory and aural through instrumental music
c) The benefits of learning an instrument
APST: 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 1.5, 1.6, 2.1, 2.2, 2.5, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, 6.1, 6.2
2) Portfolio of Plans and Materials (DUE 25/11/2022)
Part 1: Plans and Reflections
You are required to complete at least 5 hours active involvement in instrumental or vocal teaching. This time may include some observation, and should also comprise sessions in which you actively teach.
 You are responsible for making and documenting evidence of your own arrangements for this experience, though you are entitled to seek the support and advice of the lecturer in devising how it will best be achieved.
 Your log should show an accurate record of each session and how you were involved.
 Your file MUST include the following:
 1. All observation notes of lessons that you observed.
2. Detailed plans for lessons (or sections of lessons) that you taught. Use the standard lesson format as a guide.
3. Reflections of lessons you taught
PART 2: Materials
Present a portfolio of instrumental/vocal resources. This portfolio should be comprised of:
 A critique of five method books (not repertoire books) for your instrument or specialisation. The books reviewed should be able to be used by students in the first two to three years of their learning experience.
 You need to present a summary of each book, indicating:
a. Reasons for selection
b. The strengths of the method book including any underlying philosophy.
c. The weaknesses of the method book
d. How the book differs from other method books.
e. An overall conclusion
 The conclusion of the review should include your personal opinion as to the suitability of the material for its stated purpose. This conclusion should be supported by the previous areas outlined above, and any other pertinent information. Some comparisons and advocacy for your own preferences could be included in a final summing-up.
You need to clearly include the name of each book, the author and the publisher.
APST: 1.4, 1.5, 1.6, 2.1, 2.5, 3.3, 3.5, 2.2, 2.3, 3.2, 3.4, 3.6, 6.2, 6.3, 2.6, 3.1, 4.5, 5.1, 5.3, 5.4

3) Presentation (DUE 6/12/2022)
You are to present a 15-20 minute presentation on an aspect of one of the following topics:
 
Student motivation in instrumental music;
Aural development;
The myth of 'tone deafness';
Teaching intonation;
Developing student musical expression and musicianship
Incorporating ICT in instrumental music
Managing the challenges of the Primary-Secondary and/or Middle-Senior School transitions in instrumental music;
OR a topic agreed with the Lecturer.
 
The purpose of this task is to illustrate leadership in working with colleagues. You may include interactive activities as part of your presentation. The presentation is intended to be useful to the class. Please note that your chosen presentation topic should be different from your research essay topic.
 
The presentations will be scheduled during class on either 6/12 or 13/12
APST: 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 1.5, 1.6, 2.1, 2.2, 2.5, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, 6.1, 6.2

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
