Unit Information

This unit enables the practical application of immersive simulation techniques across a variety of health professional settings. Students apply the theoretical basis, design, planning, implementation and evaluation processes related to advanced simulation settings.
Learning Outcomes
At the end of the unit, students will be able to:
(1) apply skills in course design to the advanced simulation setting; 
(2) develop and apply simulation training in specific teaching and learning contexts; 
(3) develop and use clinical scenarios for use in simulation training to assess competency of clinical skills; 
(4) examine and practise communication skills required in simulated settings; 
(5) discuss ethics, feasibility, limitations and practical implications of simulation equipment and training settings; 
(6) design tools to assess and evaluate simulation training; and 
(7) discuss how to manage crises and incidents during simulated learning settings.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit uses interactive learning strategies and a flipped classroom approach via a combination of online self-directed activities followed by a two-day workshop. All self-directed  content will be available via the LMS for asynchronous delivery, with no requirement for students to participate online at specific times. The workshop is scheduled on Tuesday 29 and Wednesday 30 November, and relevant content will be available at that time.  
We believe you will learn best through reflection on, and application of, your own teaching and learning experiences. We value you as a member of the unit and will encourage your learning through providing: 
• a supportive learning environment that centers on student needs;
• transparent learning outcomes as the foundation for learning materials and assessment items;
• learning materials that are recent, evidence based and recommended by experts in the field (including suggested readings, presentations developed specifically for IMED5831 and high quality videos curated from external sources);
• facilitated discussions (via face-to-face workshop and online forums) to share information and generate ideas;
• clear and authentic assessment items to simulate core HPE processes;
• opportunities for students to receive feedback through peer review and formative assessment; and 
• mechanisms for students to provide feedback on the unit and using these evaluation findings to revise the unit to ensure it meets current and future HPE needs. 

Unit Structure

This unit is divided into three modules: 
• Module 1: Simulation Scenario Design and Facilitation (Learning Outcomes 1 to 7) 
• Module 2: Non Technical Skills (Learning Outcomes 4 and 7)
• Module 3: Managing Complexity in Healthcare Simulation (Learning Outcome 5 and 7)
The two-day workshop will expand upon the LMS activities and provide an opportunity to engage in interactive learning within a simulation suite. Assessment 2 will occur during the workshop and will involve facilitating and observing team simulation-based activities.  
In addition, learning materials will be available in folders for each module on the Learning Management System (LMS). 
Modules can be completed at a flexible pace with learning materials remaining accessible throughout the semester (until after the final assessment is submitted).

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
