Academic Information

Content This unit provides students with an overview of the humanities and social science curriculum in Western Australia. The curriculum documents in economics, geography, history, and politics and law are used as a basis for programming and lesson planning, focusing on the development of appropriate learning strategies, selection of resources and the development of assessment programs. A range of cross-curricular topics related to supporting effective literacy and numeracy development, differentiation and inclusive schooling and the use of information and communication technology (ICT) to support student learning are also explored.

Unit Information

This unit provides students with an overview of the Senior Secondary Humanities and Social Science Curriculum in Western Australia. Curriculum documents are used as a basis for programming and lesson planning, focusing on the development of appropriate learning strategies, selection of resources and the development of assessment programs. A range of cross-curricular topics related to supporting effective literacy and numeracy development, differentiation and inclusive schooling and the use of information and communication technology (ICT) to support student learning are also explored.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit is delivered in interactive online learning sessions with weekly workshops run through Collaborate Ultra. Various teaching and learning strategies may be used in this unit: lecture, student presentation, small and large group discussion as well as various educational strategies to help students develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the weekly workshops and readings. Where possible, guest presenters from a number of HASS-related organisations and schools will speak to students in relation to the Senior Secondary Humanities and Social Science Curriculum in Western Australia.

Unit Structure

The unit runs over a nine-week consecutive period. The workshops are scheduled for three hours per week and are designed to acquaint pre service teachers with the Senior School curriculum in their specialist HASS subject as mandated by the Schools Curriculum and Assessment Authority in Western Australia. The focus is on planning and pedagogy for Years 11 and 12 as well as mapping progress from years 7-10 to years 11 and 12. The assessment process in the Senior School is studied in detail including the types of assessment, grading, recording and reporting, and moderation. Effective use of ICT in the Senior School will also be a focus for this unit.

ASSESSMENT DETAILS
Assessment 1: Senior School Curriculum Area Investigation
Due date: 4th November
Weighting: 30%
You are required to write a 2000-word report (excluding in-text references and the reference list) that identifies and examines pedagogies, principles and practices of teaching in Years 11 and 12 for your HASS specialist discipline.
Your response will be based on the following question:
What constitutes best practice in the teaching and learning of Years 11 and 12 in your specialist HASS discipline?
You may address the overall signature pedagogies for your discipline or concentrate upon a specific Year 11 and 12 course such as ATAR, General, or Foundation. You should also consider how you will cater for students with diverse needs including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander, EAL/D, Gifted and Talented, and Special Needs students. Your report should highlight the points of difference between Senior School and Lower School. You may use specific examples and refer to curriculum and policy documents to do this.
 The assessment will engage you in research and examination of best practice in your specialist HASS discipline, which is an important aspect of your professional career. You must read widely in the professional and empirical literature as well as exploring the relevant state and federal curriculum and policy documents. The Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (AITSL) should also be consulted when considering best practice.
Assessment 2: Senior School Technology Presentation Due date: 15th November
Weighting: 10%
For this assessment you will work with peers from your HASS specialist discipline area (up to four) to identify an ICT strategy that you can utilise in the Senior School Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS) classroom. The strategy can be specific to your HASS discipline or generic. If it is the latter, you must demonstrate how it can be applied to your HASS discipline. You will present your ICT strategy to your peers on the date shown above. The object of the assessment is to share ICT strategies with your peers that they can subsequently use in the Senior School HASS classroom.
Instructions
Sign up to a group for your specialist HASS discipline. A signup sheet will be available in Week 1.
Work together to identify the ICT strategy that you will present. This could be a strategy that you or one of your group members has observed on school placement.
Link the strategy to a specific unit of work and specific content and skills. This can be yours or one of your group members' chosen unit for Assessment 3. 
Demonstrate how you will use your identified ICT strategy to support teaching and learning in the unit.
Presentations should be a maximum of 15 minutes in length. This includes questions. 
Assessment 3: Senior School Program
Due date: 5th December
Weighting: 60% 
For this assessment you will be developing curriculum ideas and documents to create a Senior School program for your specialist Humanities and Social Sciences discipline. You are to draft a unit overview and plan in detail a learning program of two weeks duration for one unit from an ATAR or General HASS discipline. 
For the program of work, you will:
• Decide upon the unit of work that you will create the overview and program for.
• Create a unit overview for the whole of the unit (usually 16 weeks). You may use the SCSA sample unit outline and any school unit outline you may have as a guide, but you must clearly state that your unit overview has been informed by these documents and list them in your reference list.
• Create an assessment outline for your chosen unit as per SCSA guided by the SCSA example assessment outline documentation. You may use SCSA sample assessment outline for the unit and any school assessment outline you may have as a guide, but you must clearly state that your unit overview has been informed by these documents and list them in your reference list.
• Produce one assessment task (not the exam), the format of which should follow the SCSA sample assessment tasks. It should be professionally presented and ready for classroom use including a cover sheet with the relevant details on it such as task number, assessment type, weighting, total number of marks, and any information the students require to complete the assessment. A detailed marking key following the SCSA example marking keys should be included.
• Create a two-week program of work that details the teaching and learning activities which feed into the assessment task. You must include curriculum links, Specific objectives, diagnostic, formative and summative assessment (where applicable), resources, and differentiation. Use a format of your choice.
• Create and or adapt five sophisticated resources that you will use when you teach the program.
• Include a referenced rationale and a reflection on the curriculum development process.
Please refer to the criteria and marking rubrics in the assessment folders for further details.
RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.


Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
