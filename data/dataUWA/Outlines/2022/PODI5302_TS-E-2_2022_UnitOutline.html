Teaching contingency plan (In response to Covid19 restrictions)

The University has an over-arching plan for the management of student interaction in the event of public health disasters such as pandemics (ie: COVID-19). Key to this plan is the individual discipline plans for distance learning, impacts of health department orders, and emergency enforcement of public health and safety.

In the event of students being unable to attend campus physically due to pandemic restrictions, alternate plans will be advised in relation to lectures, tutorials, laboratory education sessions and assessments. The impost of time available for clinical practicum and work integrated learning clinical placements will be managed in accordance with University and external accreditation bodies (ie: AHPRA) to ensure students complete sufficient clinical hours to be considered clinically competent to practice in accordance with the registering bodies.

LMS will be the primary communication for changes to course content as impacted by pandemics.

Students are encouraged to maintain consistent vigilance on their University emails for any directions made by the University executive, in response to Federal or State legislation or emergency regulations (ie: Health Worker (Restrictions on Access) Directions).

Academic Information

Content This unit consolidates clinical practice in podiatry in preparation for the student to enter independent practice upon graduation and registration as a podiatrist. All aspects of the assessment and treatment of common foot conditions, performing minor surgery and the administration and prescription of scheduled medications are covered.

Unit Information

This unit consolidates clinical practice in podiatry in preparation for the student to enter independent practice upon graduation and registration as a podiatrist.
Students consolidate their theory from previous years into practice via clinical placements, case discussions and assignments. Students will have the opportunity to explore critical and clinical thinking skills with a variety of supervisors while on placements. 
The clinical skills and topics taught in PODI5309 will also be assessable in this unit. 

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Students will attend internal and external placements to gain real-life exposure of the current profession. During these placements students will consolidate theory and be assessed in different modalities.
Students are expected to learn in a blended learning environment whereby there will be access to online content, pre-reading and face to face discussions / tutorials.
Certain tutorials will be taught in a flipped teaching approach whereby students are expected to self direct study and discuss findings in a tutorial.
Through assignments, students will learn different concepts and modules such as the ESM pathway and prescribing / imaging modalities in a Podiatric setting.
Clinical skills will be examined throughout the semester via competencies during placements and OSCE's.

Participation

All sessions are important learning experiences and therefore attendance is expected. All lectures delivered by guest lecturers are compulsory. Students must arrive in time to sign-in prior to the session commencing. All tutorials, laboratories, workshops, site visits etc. are compulsory.
If a student is unable to attend a session due to illness or for any other exceptional circumstance, the Unit Coordinator must be contacted as soon as practicable. Absences of more than one day due to illness will require a medical certificate to be presented to the Academic Services Officer (admin-podiatry@uwa.edu.au) within seven days. Failure to meet this requirement may/will result in a report of Professional Misconduct being made. Medical certificates must be provided by a medical doctor or surgeon. Medical certificates provided by pharmacists will not be accepted.
If a student wishes to be away for any period during their course, it is the student’s responsibility to apply for Short Leave. It is also the student’s responsibility to inform their Unit Coordinators and/or clinical teachers and tutors about any possible absences, so they are aware of the approved leave. It is the student’s responsibility make arrangements to catch up with any work missed, including any assessments. This is a condition of short leave being approved. Students can apply for short leave of 1 or 2 days by emailing the Head of the Discipline of Podiatry, clearly stating the following:
 Full Name and Student ID
 Year level
 Dates of absence
 Detailed reason for absence
 Any assessments that may be affected by the leave
 Whether this has been discussed this with Unit Coordinators
Students will be given a response in writing as to whether the requested leave has been approved or not. Please note that short leave has the capability to inconvenience supervisors/unit coordinators greatly if there are assessments due during the period of leave. Please consider this when applying for short leave and decide whether the proposed absence is more important than the assessment.
If 3-10 days is required, any leave request must go to the Head of the Discipline of Podiatry and the Manager of Student Services and Engagement. A maximum of 10 days is allowed per calendar year.

Referencing Requirements

EndNote is a referencing software that assists in creating a database of references from readings that can be organised via the use of keywords. In academic writing, entries from this database can be inserted as in-text references and a bibliography can be automatically created following a chosen formatting style (ie: Harvard, Vancouver).  Access to the EndNote program is free to all UWA students and can be downloaded on a personal device (ie: laptop or home computer). The UWA Library runs regular workshops and training sessions on the use of EndNote and students are expected to familiarise themselves during the first trimester of study. Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
