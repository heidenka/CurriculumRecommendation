Academic Information

Content This unit develops students' understanding of pedagogy, teaching and learning and provides the opportunity to explore an area of need or interest within their curriculum area(s). The unit explores contemporary approaches to dialogic and collaborative classroom learning and examines active, flexible and online learning for the twenty first century. Specific pedagogic approaches of key sectors such the International Baccalaureate and Vocational Education and Training are examined. Students will investigate and reflect upon these key aspects in a self-directed curriculum project and will apply relevant strategies for flexible and online learning using active, contemporary dialogic and collaborative learning principles. Its focus on pedagogy and practice is designed to build students' classroom readiness. Generic skills that are developed in this unit through online discussions, written assignments and workshops include both oral and written communication skills; problem solving and creativity; teamwork; time management; and critical thinking through evaluation and critique of educational research.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Generic skills that are developed in this unit through online discussions, written assignments and workshops which help to build both oral and written communication skills; problem solving and creativity; teamwork; time management; and critical thinking through evaluation and critique of educational research. The unit is delivered through a hyflex model for those students unable to attend. 
Students are able to (1) apply an understanding of dialogic pedagogies for the classroom; (2) evaluate and apply collaborative learning approaches for the classroom; (3) evaluate and apply contemporary active learning, flexible delivery and online strategies for curriculum areas and for the classroom; (4) investigate sector specific approaches to learning in the International Baccalaureate, Montessori and Steiner and other sectors; and (5) examine the delivery of vocational education and training within secondary schools.
This unit is based upon workshops, online participation and an active learning approach. In this unit, students will experience a range of learning strategies that are both designed to engage post graduate students and to model strategies and activities for learning in the school.
 

Unit Structure

There are nine workshops of two hours duration each. The workshops are intended to be practical and to build your 'craft' as a teacher. It is really important that you come to workshops prepared by doing the reading(s) and also any preparation activities and students will be able to participate in some components of the unit online. 
There will also be a drop in session in each week when an assessment is due.

Participation

Students may attend workshops online if they are unable to attend face to face for individual workshops and students are expected to attend online for the full duration of the workshop. If you are unable to attend a workshop at all, please make sure to let the unit coordinator know in advance. Students will need to attend for a full online workshop to be recorded as having attended. 
Students are encouraged to participate in the unit by a) view recorded mini lectures; b) preparing for workshops by completing readings and activities; c) attending workshops; d) taking part in online activities. 
Weekly readings will consist of:  a) a general introduction to the topic; b) an example of research in the field; c) examples of practice in schools and links to professional and industry bodies.

Assessment One:  Curriculum Area Project - due week five Friday 3rd June 
The 21st century educational landscape is one of change as secondary schools adapt to the challenges posed by the global pandemic, online and flipped learning, a world where knowledge and information cannot be taken for granted and where our young people increasingly live in a global and connected world. 
This assessment gives you the opportunity to think about your curriculum area (or one of them) as they are learned and taught now and in th future. In doing so, you are to: 
a)	select a curriculum area and a stream in that area and also one or two of the following pedagogic approaches – collaborative and group work; dialogic learning; personalised adaptive learning; direct explicit instruction;  
b)	Provide an explanation of your chosen pedagogy/ies that is evidence based, that is, it draws upon recent research; 
c)	Explain why the pedagogy is useful for your curriculum area and specific stream; 
d)	Design two activities for students using 1-2 of your chosen strategies and present with information about a) cohort; b) purpose; c) the activity; d) resources; e) specific instructions as you would present to students;
e)	A conclusion explaining how your activities enact the pedagogy. 
The assessment overall should be between 2,500 and 3,000 words including all resource materials.

You may draw upon some of the material produced previously in the course; however, it must pass the Turnitin originality test and be accompanied by all resources. RESUBMISSION RULE
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if—
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
