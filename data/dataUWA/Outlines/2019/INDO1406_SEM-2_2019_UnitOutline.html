Unit Information

This unit follows on from INDO1405 Indonesian 5 and leads to INDO2407 Indonesian 7. It is designed to allow students to develop an advanced competency in written and spoken Indonesian, and achieve 'basic vocational proficiency' in Indonesian or Level 3 of the International Second Language Proficiency Ratings, meaning they can perform effectively in most formal and informal situations. 

Materials used include short stories, magazine articles and popular videos. Students' translation skills are also enhanced. Graduates attain a relatively high level of fluency, enabling them to communicate effectively in Indonesian in social and employment contexts. Particular emphasis is placed on developing an awareness of social and cultural contexts of Indonesia using the Indonesian language. This includes broad social–cultural topics, as well as some specific focus on political contexts which are of importance to Indonesia today. To this end a wide variety of materials and language styles (genres) are explored and students are expected to explore, understand and articulate those contexts.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

A blended model with face to face classes and an online component. Students are expected to fully participated in the activities.

Viewing & Listening 
There is a variety of viewing and listening material used in this unit.  
1.	Prior to class most weeks you are to watch at least one video on Moodle answer vocabulary and comprehension questions about it and be prepared to discuss the material in class.  This part of the unit is one of the most demanding parts of the unit, as the language is authentic, fast and sophisticated both in content and linguistically. I will check that you have done these exercises each week and they will count towards your participation mark but they will not be handed in for correcting.
2.	In Weeks 5 & 6 we will watch a feature film Long Road to Heaven which, like the interviews, is demanding. As well as considering its language, you will need to watch this film for its social & political themes and style. You will be required to report on the theme of this film (see Assignment Details, below).
3.	Other audio-visual materials will be used when appropriate. 
Readings
As well as notes and exercises, the Reader contains a variety of reading material.  Some readings have glossaries and comprehension questions; some do not.  You are required to read all materials before coming to class.  You must be prepared to discuss the topics/issues that these materials cover, and to express your considered opinion.  The content will overlap with that of other materials and you are expected to recognize and articulate these overlaps.
Each week the class covers a specific theme, such as environmental problems, the lives of women or ethnic relations in Indonesia. Students are exposed to a range of authentic audiovisual and written materials related to the topic, allowing them not only to develop listening and reading skills but also to learn in some depth about aspects of Indonesian society and culture.

Additional Study and Practice
You should be spending at least 7 hours a week outside of class to study and practice for this unit. Take every opportunity to practice your Indonesian and to listen to it being spoken!  There are many effective learning techniques (see information on language learning strategies in Helpful Websites below).  First of all, listen to/watch as much of the material on LMS as you can. You can also listen to Indonesian radio via the internet (see Helpful Websites below) and Indonesian TV news is on SBS at 12.05pm weekdays. Find a native speaker (there are plenty on campus!) and discuss the social, cultural and political issues with him/her. Read aloud to yourself. Varied learning methods are helpful and they avoid monotony. If you are having trouble with some aspect of the language, let me know. You are welcome to come and see me during or outside consultation hours.

Participation

Students need to participate actively for the whole semester and follow the guidelines.

	Late submission of Assignments
A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.
For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).
If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.
If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course.

	Penalty for exceeding word limit
Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

	Academic conduct
Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarize themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.

Translation exercises will be from Indonesian into English. Marks will be given for completeness and accuracy, as well as for sensible, appropriate English. This means that your English must be logical, natural, understandable, accurate and contextually correct. This means you need to recognise and incorporate the style and purpose of the original writer and translate similarly.  Do not translate literally and present inaccurate, unnatural, nonsensical English! For guidelines on how writing exercises will be marked, see notes under Essay.

Presentation: Budaya, masyarakat dan politik Indonesia (Week 9)
In order to expand your own and your classmates’ knowledge about the culture and society of Indonesia, you are to research an issue relating to Indonesian culture or society (see examples below), and present your findings to the class. You will be required to submit your topic on Wednesday Week 3 or earlier, to avoid duplication of topic with other students. The presentation will require oral and visual input, a single summary sheet for each member of the class, and a few questions to ask your audience to make sure they were listening and understood your presentation.  

The following are a few suggestions for a topic. However, you may choose something different if it interests you more, as long as it is not something you have done in a previous class.

Perdagangan ternak hidup; Penyelundupan orang dari Indonesia ke Australia; Komite Pemberantasan Korupsi (KPK). Banjir; Masalah kemacetan/ transportasi di Jakarta; Isu Badan Penyelenggara Jaminan Sosial (BPJS) Kesehatan, Kartu Indonesia Pintar (KIP); Sistem pendidikan Indonesia; Masalah Papua Barat; Pembakaran hutan Kalimantan dan/ atau Sumatera; Penelitian tentang tokoh terkenal di Indonesia (misalnya di bidang kebudayaan/ politik/ agama/ sastra/ militer); Kepercayaan tentang ilmu gaib di Indonesia; Kisah wayang; Hubungan Indonesia dengan negeri lain; Komedi di Indonesia: Waria; Korupsi; Musik; Budaya pop/ remaja; Narkoba; Kebebasan media; Perempuan di Indonesia; Islam; Sepak bola/ olahraga lain; Trauma 1965 dan dampak jangka panjangnya; Hubungan antar kelompok etnis di Indonesia; Masalah pendidikan bahasa; Dunia film Indonesia (misalnya: meningkatnya popularitas film Islam); dan lain-lainnya. 

See also Inside Indonesia for ideas: http://www.insideindonesia.org/articles-by-topic/articles-by-topic 


The Presentation
1.	Oral presentation time: 15 minutes maximum. The presentation must be in Indonesian, of course! Your oral presentation should be relaxed, intimate, and communicative. Talk to the other students, not to your notes! Do not simply read your notes. You are also encouraged to use visual props to engage the interest of other students.
2.	Use illustrations/media during your presentation; e.g. pictures, maps, diagrams. These may be in hard copy such as posters/artefacts/etc, on OHP/whiteboard, on Power Point, or others.  They must illustrate the points you are making.  Make sure they are clear, understandable and relevant. (Note: PowerPoint and other visual aid should supplement and support your presentation and not simply replace your hand held notes!)
3.	Written summary handouts (single A4 sheet) in NOTE form are required for each class member (including the teacher)
4.	Prepare a few questions to ask the audience to make sure they are listening to your presentation and understanding it.  

Essay 	Due: Week 12
During Week 5 and 6 we will be reviewing the film Long Road to Heaven. In addition to the regular discussion of content and language, you will be required to submit a 700 word essay based on the film. The essay should include a brief summary and comments/critique on the theme of the film. You may wish to compare/contrast it with other literary work or movies. You should put your discussion in context and indicate what you consider to be the cultural and/or social significance of the film. 
You are also required to interview at least one Indonesian person on what they think about the issue that you will be discussing in your essay. You will need to incorporate the findings from your interview into your essay.
Marks will be given for:
1.	cohesion of the essay, 
2.	accuracy in presenting a description of theme(s),
3.	clarity of expression,
4.	quality of the argument in your commentary on the cultural/social significance of the film, 
5.	language quality, accuracy and expression.
