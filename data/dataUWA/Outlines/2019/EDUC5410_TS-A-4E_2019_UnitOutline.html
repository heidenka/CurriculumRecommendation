Academic Information

Content This unit examines how knowledge is constructed in different subject specific discourses and what types of literacy and numeracy support can be offered in classrooms to enhance students' opportunities for learning across the range of curriculum areas. This unit explores theories related to vocabulary development, reading, and writing as well as the nature of numeracy and the extent to which it relates to mathematical concepts, skills, and thinking. In addition, the unit focuses on classroom-based applications so that ITE students develop the skills and strategies needed to support literacy and numeracy in the classroom.

The following teaching and learning strategies may be used in this unit: Lecture, presentation, small and large group discussion as well as strategies such as jigsaw, graffiti wall and quickwrites to help students develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the weekly lectures and required readings.

ASSIGNMENT 1A: SUPPORTING STUDENTS’ LITERACY IN LEARNING AREA LESSONS:
[Word limit: 1200 words.]
Weighting: 35%

For the following task, you are to focus on EITHER reading and writing aspects of literacy only, OR listening and speaking aspects of literacy only.

1. Decide on a topic for a Year 9 lesson in your specialist learning area, and using the Australian Curriculum locate and record information pertaining to: 
•     the content description; 
•     elaboration/s relevant to the topic of your lesson; 
•     the General Capabilities - Literacy (EITHER reading and writing only, OR listening and speaking only) linked to the identified content description; 
•     topic specific vocabulary (you need to identify this yourself). 

2. Construct a specific learning goal (or goals) for the lesson (that is, a statement that clearly indicates what the students will know or be able to do as a result of the lesson). 

3. Provide a brief overview (~200 words) of the main steps of a lesson that you might implement to address the curriculum content identified. The lesson needs to: 
•     involve EITHER reading and writing only, OR listening and speaking only, as the main modes for students’ learning; 
•     Incorporate EITHER a specific (evidence-based) strategy for reading and a specific (evidence-based) strategy for writing, OR a specific (evidence-based) strategy for listening and a specific (evidence-based) strategy for speaking (strategies are instructional practices that reliably supports students’ learning).

4. Provide precise descriptions (~400 words each = ~800 words) of EITHER each of the reading and writing components of the lesson, OR each of the listening and speaking components of the lesson, ensuring to incorporate the specific strategies used to support the development of students’ effective literacy skills in EITHER reading and writing, OR listening and speaking, and the achievement of the stated learning goal(s).

Present the assignment using the following headings:

1.	Learning area, year level, curriculum information and vocabulary (present in table as shown below)
2.	Learning goal / s
3.	Lesson overview
4.	Listening component lesson
5.	Speaking component of lesson
6.	References

Note: Reference (in-text and in reference list) the Australian Curriculum Content and other documents used to source lesson content, strategies or other information.

ASSIGNMENT 1B – ANALYSIS AND DEVELOPMENT OF NUMERACY STRATEGIES.
[Word limit: 750 words (not including end-references), plus concrete learning and teaching aid.]
Weighting: 35%

The aim of this assignment is to create an evidence-based concrete aid to support the learning and teaching of a numeracy concept in your teaching area.

1. Synthesise what the literature describes the nature of numeracy to be in your subject area(s). (~250 words) 

2. Analyse the theory and practice of using concrete learning and teaching aids to develop numeracy concepts, with an emphasis on your subject area if possible. (~250 words) 

3. Create a manipulative that may be used in a learning and teaching activity to develop a numeracy concept in your subject area(s). Please include an outline of the context and rationale for why you have designed or created your manipulative, and provide a brief description of how it is to be implemented. (~250 words, plus concrete learning and teaching aid.)

In your response you may wish to distinguish between research-based literature and literature presenting “best practice”. 

Please hand deliver your concrete learning and teaching aid to the Unit Coordinator or to Reception (2nd floor, Education building) before 4 pm on the due date. 

Note: A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS. Please include your student chosen assessment criterion, and the standards of achievement, at the beginning of your assignment.

ASSIGNMENT 2: A RATIONALE: ADDRESSING LITERACY AND NUMERACY GENERAL CAPABILITIES THROUGHOUT THE TEACHING OF YOUR SPECIALIST CURRICULUM LEARNING AREA
[Word limit: 1000 words (not including end-references), plus a poster.]

There are two elements of this assignment:
•	a written paper (word limit 1000 words –not including end-references); 
•	a poster. 

1. Write a rationale for addressing literacy and numeracy general capabilities throughout the learning and teaching of your specialist curriculum learning area.

A rationale is basically a set of reasons for a particular belief. It provides the justification or articulation of reasons for the belief which in this case is, ‘‘it is important to address general literacy and numeracy capabilities in your specialist curriculum learning area.” A rationale answers the question, ‘why?’ and does so by making reference to theoretical or research-based evidence and other appropriate documents such as policy and curriculum documents.

Your rationale should draw from academic and other appropriate reference sources. It might include such elements as: 
•       state and briefly explain the topic; 
•	highlight the importance of the topic; 
•	outline goals related to the topic; 
•	present and explain different points of reason or justification; 
•	provide practical examples that assist to clarify your points. 

2.	Present your rationale as a poster that would be suitable for display on the wall of your classroom. In designing your poster, consider the use and typical features of posters. You might include such things as:
•	a title and author; 
•	key points; 
•	the use of images that complement the subject matter; 
•	quotes; 
•	avoid clutter or visual chaos that would distract the reader. 

Note: A rubric will be made available on the unit LMS. Please include your student chosen assessment criterion, and the standards of achievement, at the beginning of your assignment.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
