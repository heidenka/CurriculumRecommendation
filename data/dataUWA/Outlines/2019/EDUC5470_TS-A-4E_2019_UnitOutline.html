Academic Information

Content This unit is for pre-service teachers who plan to teach secondary school English. It provides a theoretical framework for teaching, learning and assessment. Familiarity is developed with current curriculum documents. Through active participation in workshops, students develop an understanding of strategies for effective teaching and learning, skills for planning and programming, development of instructional materials and resources and develop an understanding of issues relevant to English teaching.

Unit Information

In first semester, you developed understandings about the subject English and fundamental issues such as literacy, pedagogy and assessment. You gained an understanding of the Western Australian Curriculum: English for lower secondary and the interrelated language, Literature and Literacy strands. You developed an understanding of a range of teaching strategies and the skills for planning learning experiences for students. Finally, you developed an understanding of assessment.

In this unit, the focus is on reflecting on the understandings, skills and knowledge that you have already gained, and then consolidating and extending these. This unit will cover new understandings about ATAR English, General English, Foundation English and ATAR Literature in Years 11 and 12. We will consider English teaching pedagogies, contemporary views on teaching reading and writing for upper school students, technology in the classroom, the use and preparation of learning and teaching resources and successful assessment.

Assessments in this unit are designed to develop the skills and competencies required for teaching English. The assignments are planned to draw upon your teaching practice experiences, prepare you for future practicums and link to the content of other education units.

This unit is delivered in interactive learning sessions with an online component. The unit is based on active learning workshops which includes presentations, collaborative problem solving and student based professional development. These approaches will help you to develop a deeper understanding of the concepts and theories presented in the workshops and required readings.

PROFESSIONAL PARTICIPATION
Beginning teachers are often judged on their professionalism. Given the importance of setting and maintaining high standards of professional conduct and commitment throughout your career, this assessment item reflects your recognition of the importance of these standards in your pre-service year. There are two ways in which you can participate in the unit: 

a) Group presentation of one of the weekly topics using materials from the Reading List. You are encouraged to work in a group of three, speak for no more than 15 minutes (as a group, not individually) and prepare a one page summary (7%); b) three weekly reflections – these are worth 1% each. Each week you will be given a topic for reflection and are encouraged to write 200 – 250 words (three entries only). These entries are designed to be largely informal and to engage your colleagues.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:

ASSIGNMENT ONE

IMPROVING PRACTICE: RECORDING ANALYSIS, RESEARCH AND REFLECTION (20%)

APST Standard 1- Know students and how they learn; APST Standard 3 - Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning; APST Standard 6 – Engage in professional learning

In this assignment you will reflect upon your teaching practice using the recording obtained during your first practicum. To do so, you will select one lesson and focus upon one segment of that lesson and analyse it in depth. You are encouraged to follow the following process: View your recording several times; Select a 5-7 minute segment of the tape which is relevant in some way (shows an issue or a problem that you would like some peer help with). It might be your introduction, a questioning sequence, giving of instructions, interaction with an individual student, explanation of concepts, introducing or analysing a text, building literacy skills, engaging students with content, establishing and monitoring group work or supporting student(s) experiencing difficulties. Your segment needs to be specifically relevant for English teaching. 

During workshops in week one, you will work in small groups to analyse the recorded segment as preparation for the first assessment task.

On Monday July 22, and in a small group, each person will introduce their video by providing essential background details (context) and justification for the choice of footage. The 5-7 minute segment will be shown. The recording will be briefly discussed with a focus on ways of improving practice. You may find it useful to work with a colleague to dissect and discuss your recording. Your group may wish to brainstorm suitable research topics. 

Your analysis should reveal strengths and weaknesses. Some questions are provided to help you with your analysis. You do not have to answer all questions. They are a thinking tool. You may include discussion of other aspects of your teaching that is not evident in the recording. From your analysis and the group discussion write a 2,000 word paper. The paper will follow the structure of a research report: An introduction (What is the main research question you are examining in this piece of research); Background and context (A description of the school/class context, using pseudonyms and preserving confidentiality); Literature review (What does the literature tell you about this area/issue?); Research method (What tools/approaches have you used to examine your recording, what kind of data have you collected? What are your initial interpretations of your session). You may also use feedback from your mentors, supervisors and your own reflections; Findings and discussion (What did your research show you? How does this relate to the literature?); Implications for future practice (How will you apply what you’ve learned to improve in this area? Be specific – you may attach the Action Planning Sheet to help demonstrate this); Conclusion.

•	Appendix- you may include 1) your lesson reflections as attachments 2) the Action planning Sheet  - attachments are not included in the word count 

You need to read widely in the professional and preferably the empirical literature (literature that is based on classroom research and observation) using a minimum of five journal or book sources. The quality of your selected resources will be assessed. The unit reading list may be helpful as a starting point for some topics and you are welcome to use one source from the list. 

ASSIGNMENT TWO

SENIOR SCHOOL CURRICULUM DEVELOPMENT (60%)

APTS Standard 1 - Know students and how they learn; APTS Standard 2 – Know the content and how to teach it; APTS Standard 3 - Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning; APTS Standard 7 – Engage professionally with colleagues, parents/carers and the community 

Begin by working in a group of 4 or 5, where you will consider one unit for ATAR English, General English, Literature ATAR or Literature General.  This is a strategy to determine how your unit might be structured and does not contribute to the assessment.  Your unit must: Be outlined in a two week programme of no fewer than eight lessons. Please do not include inclass assessments, individual working periods as part of the eight lessons. Each lesson needs to include strategies and activities. 

Learning Programme with: Curriculum links; Learning outcomes or learning intentions; Teaching and learning strategies (outline learning strategies and activities in sufficient detail for the program to be enacted by another teacher); Teaching points (elaborations of content); Resources (ten pages of resources or equivalent); An assessment task and marking key; Incorporation of text(s); All materials and resources other than those produced by you must be referenced. 

Texts must be appropriate to age and syllabus requirements to be included in the unit, and chosen from: A significant text; A text that is part of a series; A multimodal text; A prescribed text from the Literature syllabus; A collection of shorter contemporary texts (such as a collection of blogs, opinionative articles, images on a contemporary issue); Extended media texts such as the TV drama; Texts for production such as creative writing, persuasive visual and written texts and interpretative texts. 

You will submit: An overview of your part of the unit segment: context, rationale (including how this unit relates to adolescent social, emotional, cognitive, physical development) and the secondary English discipline (please use references – you may select sources from the unit Reading List, English Curriculum 1 Reading List, the textbook and DTL). 700 words; Your programme - four pages at least of program; Individual reflection – 500 word statement about the experience (you may wish to refer to the literature here as well). 

Your will present: an overview of your unit segment to the group on November 11 – please speak for 5-7 minutes only; a one page summary of your unit (you will have plenty of material from your program to form the basis of this handout) that your colleagues can add to their resource bank. 

ASSIGNMENT THREE

MODERATION TASK USING DATA AND ASSESSMENT PROCESSES AND REFLECTION (IN CLASS EXERCISE: 1 HOUR) (10%)

Working in small groups with a task sheet, marking criteria (or rubric) and work samples, you will spend 1 hour (in class) discussing the standard of the work samples in order to reach consensus about the marks/grades to be awarded to them. These marks will be discussed in class. From this experience, you will write 750 words addressing the following question:

From this class exercise, what have you learned about moderation and assessment?

You should consider: the purpose and process of assessment and moderation; characteristics of effective assessment practices; collaboration and reaching agreement (decision making processes); issues arising from this moderation experience; the implications for your future professional development; considerations based on your engagement with the empirical research (evidence based reflection). Please use at least four sources which may include textbooks and may be drawn from the Reading List.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
