Academic Information

Content This unit provides students with an overview of the humanities and social science curriculum in Western Australia. The curriculum documents in economics, geography, history, and politics and law are used as a basis for programming and lesson planning, focusing on the development of appropriate learning strategies, selection of resources and the development of assessment programs. A range of cross-curricular topics related to supporting effective literacy and numeracy development, differentiation and inclusive schooling and the use of information and communication technology (ICT) to support student learning are also explored.

Unit Information

This unit builds on your understanding of curriculum, pedagogy, planning, learning strategies, resources, assessment techniques and classroom processes gained during first semester and your teaching practice. The main focus this semester is on the Year 11 and 12 units, in your particular discipline, while consolidating and extending the skills you developed in semester one. The teaching and learning strategies in this unit are designed to develop the skills and competencies required for teaching in the following Year 11 and 12 Units of Study: Economics, Geography, Ancient and Modern History, and Politics and Law. It is assumed that you have a strong background in your degree in at least one of these humanities and social science disciplines. This is a unit about teaching the courses based on the Humanities and Social Sciences Learning Area. The unit aims to develop resourceful teachers who are sufficiently flexible and creative to implement effective teaching and learning programs based on Year 11 and 12 Units of Study. The unit will encourage you to reassess your philosophy of teaching on the basis of your first school experience, make a constructive evaluation of your teaching effectiveness and set realistic goals for the future

Teaching and Learning Strategy

This unit is delivered in interactive learning sessions.

Multi-mode with face to face classes and an online component.

Information is provided to students in a variety of ways including workshop/seminar presentations, small group/whole class discussion, and guest speakers. A range of teaching, learning and assessment strategies are explicitly modelled to students as part of their learning experience. These include cooperative learning strategies, programming, formal and informal assessment strategies, including a focus on questioning, the provision of feedback and examination construction.

RESUBMISSION RULE:  

Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach) 

15. Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if— 
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit; 
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission. 
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component. 
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23). 
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator. 
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
