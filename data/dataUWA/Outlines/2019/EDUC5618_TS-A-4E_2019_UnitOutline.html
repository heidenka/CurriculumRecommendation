Academic Information

Content Students explore a range of computing and mobile technologies, including web 2.0 tools, social media and mobile apps, which are relevant to secondary teaching, with a particular focus on relevant pedagogical approaches, and embedding ICTs across the curriculum. Students also explore the use of new technologies for the advancement of teacher competencies and educational networking. Links are made to the Australian Curriculum and in particular the third of the seven General Capabilities in the Australian Curriculum (ACARA), namely the 'Information and communication technology (ICT) capability'; the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (AITSL), with particular reference to the ICT Elaborations for Graduate Teacher Standards (AITSL); and, where relevant, the ‘Digital Technologies' and ‘Design and Technologies' subjects (ACARA).

Unit Information

Over recent years, ICTs - Information and Communication Technologies - have come to play an increasingly prominent role in education.

This unit, EDUC 5618: Embedding ICTs Across the Curriculum, will give you the chance to explore ICTs and consider how to integrate them into your teaching as well as your own learning. You will also have plenty of opportunity to gain hands-on experience of e-learning and m-learning (mobile learning) tools. 

This course is designed for secondary pre-service teachers in Australia. It provides an overview of the rapidly changing forms and educational uses of ICTs, including both web-based technology (especially web 2.0 tools) and mobile app-based technology. It consists of ten tutorials, which address key themes and introduce pre-service teachers to key digital tools.

Links are made to:  
• the Australian Curriculum and in particular the third of the seven General Capabilities in the Australian Curriculum, namely the "Information and communication technology (ICT) capability";  
• the new Technologies subject, covering Digital Technologies in particular (but also with some reference to Design and Technologies); 
• the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers, with particular reference to the ICT Elaborations for the Graduate Teacher Standards.

During the course, participants will turn their developmental e-portfolio, on which they've been working for 18 months, into a showcase e-portfolio which demonstrates their achievement of the Graduate level of the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers; demonstrates their ability to integrate ICTs into their teaching and into the presentation of their e-portfolio; and demonstrates the establishment of a digital Personal Learning Network (PLN).

The unit is offered in blended mode, comprising flipped videos, face-to-face classes, and the development of a showcase e-portfolio, including providing commentary on the e-portfolios of peers.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:

ASSIGNMENT ONE –  Demonstration of the use of web 2.0/apps to support teaching and learning (40%)

APST Standards 2, 3 & 4

Key focus: you should demonstrate your ability to use ICTs in effective ways to support your teaching and your students’ learning.

In this assignment you will construct a webpage (one page of your e-portfolio) for each of the following three ICT-related focus areas: Focus Area 2.6 (Information and Communication Technology), Focus Area 3.4 (Select and use resources), and Focus Area 4.5 (Use ICT safely, responsibly and ethically). In each case you should demonstrate your accomplishment of at least the Graduate Level of the focus area, which includes briefly showing your knowledge of relevant theory, critical literature and/or curriculum documents; describing examples of your accomplishment in text; and providing evidence of your accomplishment in text and/or through supporting multimedia artefacts. You may write up to 500 words per focus area, and include as many multimedia artefacts as you wish (though beware of overpowering your text with too many images, videos or widgets). 

ASSIGNMENT TWO –  Construction and representation of a personal learning network (PLN) (40%)

APST Standards 6 & 7

Key focus: you should demonstrate your ability to use your PLN to support your ongoing professional development over time.

In this assignment you will construct a webpage (one page of your e-portfolio) connected to either Standard 6 or Standard 7, containing a visualisation (e.g., a mindmap) & description of your PLN, which should contain links to both people and resources, and may include face-to-face contacts and analogue resources alongside digital resources. You should ensure that you use a minimum of three digital platforms or tools within your PLN. In your description of your PLN, explain how you created it; how it has impacted your learning, and ultimately your classroom practice and your students’ learning; how you have used it to contribute to the learning of your peers/colleagues and how this may have impacted their classroom practice and their students’ learning; and how you plan to consolidate & expand it in the future. You may write up to 500 words in addition to your PLN visualisation, and you may include as many supplementary  multimedia artefacts as you wish. 

ASSIGNMENT THREE –  Presentation of an e-portfolio (20%)

APST Standards: All

Key focus: You should demonstrate your ability to present yourself effectively in a multimodal digital text, with your message tailored appropriately to the intended target audience of potential employers.

In this assignment you will construct a webpage (one page of your e-portfolio) for each of the following: Homepage/Self-Introduction, Teaching Philosophy, Standard 1 (choice of any one focus area), and Standard 5 (choice of any one focus area). Your Homepage should incorporate an introduction to, and overview of, your e-portfolio, and an introduction to your ‘professional self’, which gives a sense of your background and what skills and experiences you bring to the teaching profession. Your Teaching Philosophy page should communicate the key elements of your teaching philosophy and present your pedagogical approach, with reference to relevant Australian Curriculum documents. You may also make brief references to your teaching experience to date. Your Standard 1 and Standard 5 pages should demonstrate your accomplishment of at least the Graduate Level of your chosen focus areas. In each case, you may write up to 500 words. You may include as many multimedia artefacts as you wish.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
