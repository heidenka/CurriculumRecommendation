Academic Information

Content This unit aims to equip students with appropriate research skills to be able to analyse and develop possible solutions for a real world business problem. It comprises a strategic analysis of real world business problems; and a business advisory consulting report. Students learn to think strategically and apply key course theories and tools to identify key challenges and opportunities; formulate plausible solutions and initiatives consistent with organisational goals; make informed recommendations regarding the optimal path for the organisation for sustainable performance and apply strategy models, frameworks and tools to strategically transform an organisation. Students apply their strategic management and analytical research skills to a real business problem in the completion of the business advisory consulting report. This report is completed in teams and students work on a project identified by a private or public company, a not-for-profit organisation or an industry association. Students are required to present the results of their analysis to both the client and faculty.

Unit Information

Hi All - Welcome to the Strategic Analysis & Consulting Project unit. In this unit we'll engage in two work streams - firstly a work stream to study strategic management from both academic and practitioner perspectives - and secondly complete the business consulting project itself - which is a proxy for a real-world management consulting engagement. Along the way we sincerely hope that you enjoy the learning experience - and that by the end of the unit you have all added to your skill-sets in both the strategy and management consulting areas. Experience from case studies across many business sectors will help our learning along the way.

Unit content
Please read the following formal description of the unit: This Strategic Analysis & Consulting Project unit is a distinct and unique feature of our UWA Business School MBA Full Time in a number of ways, forming a capstone to your studies. For example:

There are 2 distinct components to this unit a Strategic Management component and a consulting project component.

The Strategic Management/Analysis component comprises the content of a traditional capstone Strategic Management unit which is typically offered within MBA programs. Consequently, this part of the unit will help you to pull together your previous MBA units and learning into an integrative whole. Consequently, through undertaking this component it is envisaged that MBA students will be able to take a generalist perspective when analysing an assortment of organisations and their different strategic contexts.

The consulting project component acts as a bridge between your MBA projects and the real-world of strategic-level business decision-making. It comprises a detailed project proposal for your groups chosen client project which will be presented in both verbal and written form and then a final project presentation and written submission.

Although the Strategic Management component is distinct from the consulting project one, they may also be interlinked. For example, you may be able to choose and apply strategy models / frameworks / theories learnt from the Strategic Management part in order to help you to better understand and analyse the project topic you are investigating for your client. Indeed, as it is envisaged that as the projects investigated will be both strategic and multi-disciplinary in nature, it is likely that your project will make much progress at this time through applying the learning from the Strategic Management part.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
