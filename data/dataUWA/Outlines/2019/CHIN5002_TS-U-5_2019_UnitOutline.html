Unit Information

This postgraduate level language unit is a continuing unit to CHIN5001 Chinese for Professionals 1. It provides students further knowledge and understanding of basic Chinese grammar and vocabulary in everyday and professional contexts at beginner level.  Focusing on developing communication skills required in varied professional settings, the unit further develops confidence in listening, speaking, reading and written Chinese. The unit is useful for broadening students' international outlook in professional contexts, and for developing independent learning skills to be applied in a professional context where the use of a foreign language and appropriate non-verbal intercultural communication skills are required. Further learning is supplemented by a number of contextual seminars in addition to language seminars, aimed at to preparing students for further independent language study.

Participation (20%)
It is very important for you to actively participate in all classes. Regular preview and review will help you successfully complete group and individual tasks in class. You will give a PPT presentation on your essay topic in week 3 and this will make up 5% your participation mark.

Oral language performance (20%)
In Week 6 you will give an oral performance as part of a small group of two or three people. It should involve dialogue between all involved in more or less equal measure. The topic is up to you, but I would suggest that you draw on the vocabulary covered in the unit. In order to make it fun, feel free to use props (clothes, hats, weapons, food, back projection, whatever!)  The performance should last about 3 minutes per person (i.e. 3 people = 9 Minutes). Please make an effort to learn your lines. 
Each of you will be given a mark based on the content of your conversations (20%); fluency (20%); the correctness and sophistication of your language i.e. grammar, vocabulary range, syntax (50%); and how well you interact with one another – including your ability to be spontaneous (10%)

Test (30%)
The test will have listening, reading, grammar and cultural communication sections.

Written assignment (30%)
Select one of the following topics or one of your own (after checking with the unit coordinator), and write 1500 words in English about its significance to understanding contemporary China: 

1. The Chinese language
2. Cultural difference they may cause miscommunication

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Home Study

Non-contact or home study consists of the following:

Preparation for Contact Sessions

You are required to prepare all the exercises and readings listed in your Unit Outline and announced by lecturers BEFORE each session. It is essential that you prepare for each class in order to participate in and derive maximum benefit from the activities. Your degree of preparation will be noted by the lecturer and tutor and will contribute to your mark for participation. Where there are specific exercises to prepare, these are mentioned in the outline. This preparation is assessed.  As you are preparing for class, note down any questions you have that you can ask the lecturer about in class.  A good principle to remember is that if you don’t understand something, you will not be the only one. All questions are good questions!

Study for Tests and Quizzes 

This unit is intensive so you have to keep working at it if you want to keep up. It is very important that you study for your quizzes and tests from Week 1. You will need to maintain sound study habits throughout the unit. Language learning requires ongoing, steady learning. Studying in bursts places too much strain on the memory and is, therefore, far less efficient and effective than working at it continuously.  Remember that your marks in continuous assessment will reflect the effort you make and that it represents a large component of your overall assessment for the unit. Tests will be held in class time. 

Homework 

Every week there is homework set, but it is intended to be for your own benefit and is not directly assessed. Materials will be uploaded on LMS. I strongly recommend that you listen to these each week because they will help your listening skills and your pronunciation. 

Additional Study and Practice

You need to master the language elements presented in the course. This means you need to be able to understand them in print and spoken form and to reproduce them in writing and speech. While the unit is quite demanding, it will help you to do additional independent study. Our textbooks provide additional activities that will help to consolidate the language patterns and vocabulary. 

You should listen to or watch the news, podcasts, or other relevant materials in Chinese for 15-30 minutes every day. I highly recommend you to join ACYA (Australia China Youth Association) and get a 'study buddy' and practice Chinese on a regular basis for sustained improvement in language learning.

Unit Structure

Most of the sessions focus on language learning but there will be a one-hour seminar on society and culture every other week.

The two-hour classes on Mondays will normally introduce vocabulary and phrases associated with particular situations such as ‘asking for directions’. We will then focus on group work, expanding on and practicing the new grammatical points. The classes will involve a range of activities including exercises, guided conversations, listening, reading, interviews and role-play.

Participation

Our tutorials are designed to be interactive in nature, and will provide ample opportunities for all to participate and make a contribution. Each session is graded as follows:

Fail (0-49%) - No show, or completely distracted on social media etc/asleep.

Pass mark (50%) - Attends passively, no meaningful contribution or evidence of preparation.

Credit (60-69%) - Demonstrates some evidence of prior preparation, participates actively in class discussions to expected level.

Distinction (70-79%) - Demonstrates clear evidence of thoughtful engagement with the learning objectives and tasks set for the tutorial session, active participation and thoughtful contribution to class discussions.

High distinction (80%+) - Demonstrates high degree of critical engagement with learning materials, makes original and highly relevant contributions to class discussions and tasks set for the tutorial session.

Note: Supplementary assessment is not available in this unit except in the case of a bachelor's pass degree student who has obtained a mark of 45 to 49 and is currently enrolled in this unit, and it is the only remaining unit that the student must pass in order to complete their course.

Note: There is no scheduled exam for this unit during the official exam period. 

Note: To apply for assignment extensions a student should submit a Special Consideration application to their Allocated Advising Student Office. Late Penalty apply to submissions without approved special consideration.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
