Academic Information

Content This unit examines the key principles of the income taxation and other relevant tax of a foreign state. It also examines the tax administration system and relevant legal and equivalent institutions. The primary focus is on the taxation of inbound investment, outbound investment and trade by the state. The operation of double taxation agreements is also covered. Core concepts studies are residence and sources rules; corporate tax rules; taxation of passive income flows and anti-tax avoidance rules.

Unit Information

This unit examines the regulatory framework for tax in the EU. It analyses in detail the case law of the Court of Justice of the EU in relation to direct taxes. The course is interactive with student involvement throughout at an individual level and a team level. Cases are divided between the participating students so that each student gains an expertise in 7 or 8 key cases. When these cases are discussed, there will always be one student in the room who can help explain the case in detail and make an individual presentation. Students are encouraged to prepare the cases in groups/teams to assist with accelerated learning. All classes are interactive and involve discussion of the cases, rules and concepts of EU tax law learned on the course. 
Please note as this is an intensive teaching unit, it is more than advisable not to miss any classes. This unit will not use Lecture Capture System/recording.

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The learning approach will involve a mixture of face to face lectures all involving classroom discussions and student presentations. Each student will get the opportunity to present each day on the course one or more of the cases under discussion.

Unit Structure

The course is divided into advance reading and preparation of student presentations and delivery over an intensive four-day period. The unit will end with a course paper of 5000 words (max) with a deadline of two weeks after completion of the course teaching. Two weeks after that there will be examination based on what students have studied and prepared on the course.

Participation

The cases on the reading list are divided between the students so that each student prepares 7 or 8 cases. In class students will present these prepared cases in the powerpoint format specified in the instructions to students. Students are expected to participate in classes through discussion of the cases they have prepared and by asking questions and answering questions in class.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
