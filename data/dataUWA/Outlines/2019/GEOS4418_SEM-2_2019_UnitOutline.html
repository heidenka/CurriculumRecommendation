Unit Information

The analysis of sedimentary basin fills is typically undertaken to interpret Earth history and/or for the exploration of basin-hosted resources such as petroleum reservoirs, groundwater aquifers and mineral deposits. This unit takes a basin-scale to regional approach to analysing basin fills through application of established sedimentological–stratigraphic and geophysical techniques. Emphasis is placed on learning key concepts, development of practical skills and on integration of geoscientific data. Hence basin analysis is a multidisciplinary approach that emphasises  integration of geoscientific data and interpretations to solve problems related to sedimentary basin evolution.

Teaching and Learning Strategy

This unit provides an opportunity for students to develop deep understanding of important geological and geophysical techniques that are routinely applied to understanding sedimentary basin evolution via experiential learning.  This unit emphasises learning through practical work with formal classes organised into face-to-face workshops composed of presentation/discussion of concepts and their practical application usually via practical exercises during the workshop. The unit also aims to promote generic skills in research methodology, using scientific literature, scientific communication (written and oral presentation) and teamwork.  Project work, undertaken in small teams, is designed to provide opportunity to apply the skills learned in the unit including integration of geological and geophysical data.

Unit Structure

Considerable emphasis is placed on practical skill development in this unit and therefore taught via workshops.  One 3-hour workshop is held on Mondays at 10 am–1 pm and one 2-hour workshop on Wednesdays at 3–5 pm in G13 (Mapping Lab) in the Geology-Geography Building.  Refer to the UWA timetable and/or CAS as required but note that there is no need for allocations in this unit this year.  Lecture capture is not used in this unit.

Each class aims to build on the work of previous classes both within a workshop topic and across the unit.   Please bring pencils, coloured pencils, eraser and a ruler to workshops.  Workshop notes will be provided and other resources will be provided in LMS.  Formative assessment of each major workshop topic provides an opportunity for individual feedback on learning progress during semester.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
