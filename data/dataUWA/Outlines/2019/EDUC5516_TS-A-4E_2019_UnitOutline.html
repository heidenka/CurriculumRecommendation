Academic Information

Content Students examine and critique research-based approaches to teaching literacy as a general capability and the English curriculum in the primary classroom, including vocabulary, comprehension, grammar, writing/creating, visual literacy and spelling in the context of a range of text types, with a focus on Years 3 to 6. Students also learn how to plan and sequence learning experiences for literacy and English in primary settings, using student data, and use a range of pedagogical strategies to support whole class, small group and individualised literacy instruction.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:

Assignment 1: Analysis of assessment data and case study

This assignment requires you to analyse and reflect on a set of spelling assessment data.  You will describe how you would use the assessment data to inform your classroom teaching of phonics, spelling and vocabulary. A justification for the teaching plan described should be included, with reference to the literature. You will need to refer closely to the text, Words Their Way Word Study for Phonics, Vocabulary, and Spelling by Bear, Invernizzi, Templeton & Johnston. There is a 2016 edition of this text but you may use an older edition.

Steps you should take to complete this assignment:
•	Analyse the class sets of spelling tests using Words Their Way inventories;
•	Complete a class composite;
•	Decide how you will group the children into three to five groups. Describe and justify your grouping. (Who will be in each group and why?) Approx 800-1000 words.
•	Describe how you will teach each group to take them to the next level of phonics, spelling and vocabulary achievement, referring to the Words Their Way Developmental Stages. Approx 1500-1700 
        words.
•	Justify your teaching plan, referring to the data and to the research literature (research and theory).
 
Word allowance:  2,500 words in total

See LMS for detailed rubric and further information.

Assignment 2: Unit of Work/Programme

For this assignment you will be required to:

1.  Develop an integrated overview (unit of work) for a Year 4 or 5. The overview must link three learning areas, two of which must be English and Mathematics. Plan for 3 weeks of teaching.  Some assessment data will be provided to help you plan appropriately for the class. This part of the planning assignment details WHAT will be taught. I will give you formative feedback on this part of the assignment in class.

 2.  Plan a 3-week programme that is based on the overview.  This programme will primarily be an English programme but needs to be integrated with at least one other learning area as reflected on the overview – for example, English, Mathematics and HASS, OR English and Mathematics OR English and HASS. Make sure you base planning decisions on the assessment data provided for a Year 4 or 5 class and show how you will differentiate for their needs. If you strongly prefer Year 3 or 6, please see me for alternative arrangements. This part of the planning assignment details HOW the teaching and learning, including assessment, will occur. Show how you will integrate literacy across the curriculum area/s other than English. Your programme should incorporate approximately 25 hours of teaching in total (including regular activities such as spelling lessons).

 3.    Write a rationale (of approximately 1,200 words) to justify your pedagogical decisions (including choices of resources), making references to theoretical and research literature as well as the assessment data you have been provided with. This part of the assignment details WHY you have made certain decisions. 
See LMS for detailed rubric and further information. You will have the chance to examine and discuss exemplar programmes in class.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
