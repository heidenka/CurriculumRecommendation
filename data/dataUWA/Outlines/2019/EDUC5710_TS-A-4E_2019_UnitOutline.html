Academic Information

Content Students study advanced theory, research, content, pedagogy and assessment with a special focus on a specific learning area (Mathematics/ Numeracy or English/Literacy). The unit helps prepare students for specialist teaching in the focus learning area (Mathematics or English) in Primary schools (P–6) and helps Early Childhood specialists enhance young children's early numeracy or literacy learning. Students also increase their knowledge of assessment and differentiated teaching in the focus area, which includes teaching children with diverse needs and disabilities. The perspectives and needs of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children and families are investigated with particular emphasis on the focus learning area (Mathematics/Numeracy or English/Literacy).

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:

Assignment 1: Planning document

Students provide plans for a sequence of three lessons to address either the English or Mathematics curriculum to match the diverse learning needs of students within an inclusive classroom. Differentiation needs to be clearly identified throughout each lesson as well as strategies to support inclusive student participation and effective classroom communication.  Students are required to show a clear link between assessment data provided and how this informed decisions regarding differentiation for the three lessons and the assessment task.  A summative assessment task and rubric must be provided which cater for the diverse learning needs of all students.  

The purpose of this assessment is to provide you with the opportunity to demonstrate your knowledge and understanding of differentiation and how teachers can proactively plan to cater for the diverse learning needs of students in inclusive classrooms. There are two parts to this assignment: (1) planning a sequence of three lessons which include purposeful and relevant differentiation to accommodate student diversity and (2) a summative assessment task and rubric.  

•	You will be provided with a class profile which includes pre-assessment data (readiness information) for each student as well as information pertaining to each student’s interests and learning profiles. 
•	Interpret the pre-assessment data and plan a sequence of three lessons to address an aspect of either the WA English or Mathematics curriculum for a year level of your choice. 
•	Identify when differentiation is planned and for which students.  
•	Incorporate a specific instructional strategy which makes differentiation manageable, e.g. RAFT, Menu or Choice Board.  
•	Consider the use of assistive/adaptive technology to remove barriers and increase opportunities for students to truly demonstrate what they know and understand. 
•	Consider inclusive practices and effective classroom communication throughout your lesson sequence.  
•	Provide elements of student choice and ensure that all choices have elements of interest and challenge.  
•	Plan a summative assessment task which allows all of the students in the class profile to demonstrate their knowledge and understanding of the content covered in the lesson sequence. Provide all of the instructions, handouts and details of the assessment task. 
•	Plan a rubric which allows the teacher to provide feedback to the students about their performance on the assessment task. 

Assignment 2: Professional Learning Plan

Students reflect on and modify a previous English or Mathematics lesson plan to take into account student diversity based on what they have learnt about differentiation in regards to content, process, product or learning environment and inclusive practices. Students justify why they have modified their lesson in the manner that they have, pulling on relevant literature. Students develop a plan of action for professional learning by identifying further learning needs in their relevant specialisation, identifying relevant sources of professional learning to develop their identified needs, and provide a referenced rationale of why it is important for both new and experienced teachers to have ongoing professional learning.

There are four parts to this assignment: (1) modified English or Mathematics lesson plan, (2) a reflection and justification on the modifications made, (3) a plan of action for professional learning in the specialist curriculum area and (4) a referenced rationale for ongoing professional learning.
(1) You must include one prior lesson plan, either from one of your professional practicums or from a previous assessment in your specialist curriculum area and provide modifications (for example using different coloured text) to demonstrate your knowledge and understanding of differentiation and inclusive practices.  Clearly identify when your lesson plan was created.  
(2) You then need to include a reflection and justification (approximately 500 words) which outlines why you have made the changes in light of your new knowledge and understanding.  Include at least three peer reviewed references in this section.  
(3) The third component of this assignment is where you outline a plan of action for future professional learning in the specialist curriculum area in light of identified gaps in your curriculum knowledge.  As part of this plan of action you need to clearly identify specific content descriptions or strands in your specialist curriculum area where you need some further professional learning.  You are also required to identify relevant sources of professional learning which you could potentially access in order to improve your knowledge and understanding.  Include relevant details of professional learning which may be suitable for you to access in the future.  *Please note that for this assessment you are not required to access the professional learning that you identify as part of your action plan.  
(4) The final component of this assignment includes a referenced rationale (approximately 1000 words) where you outline the importance and value of ongoing professional learning for both graduates and experienced teachers.  At least five peer reviewed references must be included in this rationale.  

Assignment 3: Test

Students complete an online test which assesses knowledge and understanding of legislative requirements relevant to teaching students with disability and assessment and moderation practices

RESUBMISSION RULE:  

Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if -
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit. 
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission. 
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component. 
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23). 
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week after being approved by the unit coordinator. 
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
