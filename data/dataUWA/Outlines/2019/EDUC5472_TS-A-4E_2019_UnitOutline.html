Academic Information

Content This unit focuses on the active teaching and learning of mathematics in secondary school, building on the knowledge, skills and competencies required by teachers of secondary school mathematics which were developed in EDUC5462 Mathematics Curriculum I. Students explore current best practice in teaching both higher-level content knowledge to more able students and general mathematical proficiency to lower achieving students. A key theme in the unit is teaching for growth through reflective teaching practice and improving teaching quality.

Unit Information

This unit further examines the knowledge, skills, and competencies required by teachers of secondary school mathematics. There is a focus on reflective practice and improving teaching quality to produce exceptional learning gains for both high achieving and low achieving students in Years 11 and 12. Students will have opportunity to familiarise themselves with the mathematical content for all mathematical subjects in the Senior Secondary Curriculum of the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics and its translation into the Western Australian senior secondary education context.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:

Assignment 1: Reflective Teaching Practice (Video Analysis & Reflective practice)

A draft of this assignment is due in the workshop on Monday 5 August.

Final copy is due on Monday 12 August. 

Videotaping is a powerful tool that can help you develop as a teacher. Although the process of being videotaped can be threatening and viewing the tape can often be uncomfortable, the advantages are well worth the effort and any embarrassment. It is one of the few ways you can closely examine your own teaching. There is also much to be gained in sharing observations and reflections with your peers: as they are experiencing similar challenges, they will have good insights into issues that you might otherwise overlook.

The first assignment requires that you watch and discuss the videotape of your lesson made during School Practicum Experience, focusing on one or two aspects of your teaching that your believe need improvement. You are to examine the quality of your teaching during public time using the Teaching of Mathematics Observation Schedule (ToMOS) or similar conceptual framework, referring to journal articles reporting the findings of research and “best practice”, and you may use software and feedback from colleagues to support your analysis.

Your essay (approximately 2000 words) will be assessed according to the following criteria: a) depth of analysis; b) level of insightfulness into significant teaching and learning issues; and c) demonstrated understanding of how to improve teaching quality.

Please refer to the rubric for further marking guidelines. At the beginning of your assignment response, please include your student chosen assessment criterion with standards of achievement.

Assignment 2: Planning for quality teaching in senior secondary mathematics units

A draft if this assignment is due in the workshop on Monday 28 October.

Final copy is due on Monday 4 November. 

This assignment comprises two parts: a) quality teaching for higher performing mathematics students in senior high school; and, b) quality teaching for typical and lower performing mathematics students in senior high school.

Part 1 planning documents (approximately 1250 words not including appendices) are to include: a) A review of a published research article relating to a mathematical concept covered in a Mathematics course for higher achieving students (Mathematics – Applications, Methods or Specialist); b) A sequence of three lesson plans introducing or reinforcing this concept. In the presentation of the lesson plans, please provide a brief overview of the key lesson components for each lesson, then choose one of the lessons to provide greater detail of the lesson components: c) An explanation of how the lesson plans illustrate quality teaching.

Part 2 planning documents (approximately 1250 words not including appendices) are to include: a) A review of a published research article relating to a specific maths concept or skill that students have difficulty learning and is required or covered in a Mathematics course for typical or lower achieving students (Mathematics – Essential or Foundation); b) A sequence of three lesson plans reinforcing this concept and/or skill. In the presentation of the lesson plans, please provide a brief overview of the key lesson components for each lesson, then choose one of the lessons to provide greater detail of the lesson components; c) An explanation of how the plans illustrate quality teaching. At the beginning of your assignment response, please include your student chosen assessment criterion with standards of achievement.

Assignment 3: Professional Conduct: collaborative workshop presentation, and collaborative moderation task.

(i) Collaborative workshop presentation  – presentation dates as negotiated (10%)
(ii) Collaborative moderation task – Using data and assessment processes (10%) 

Group task in workshop 6 November.

Individual response due Wednesday 13 November.

Work samples related to Senior Mathematics will be used to moderate an assessment task (the material will be derived from a past WACE examination).

First, the assessment task will be marked individually. Second, small groups will work with a task sheet, marking criteria or rubric, and the previously marked work samples to discuss the standard of the work samples. Students will need to reach a consensus about the grading of the work. From this experience, students will write an individual reflection addressing the following question: a) From this class exercise, what have you learned about moderation and assessment? B) Your reflection should consider, with reference to the literature: c) the purpose and process of assessment and moderation; d) characteristics of effective assessment moderation practices; f) issues arising from this moderation experience; and g) the implications for your future professional development.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
