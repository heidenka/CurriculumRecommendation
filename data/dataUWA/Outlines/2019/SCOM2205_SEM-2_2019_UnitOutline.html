Unit Information

Welcome to SCOM2205 Science Presentations. In this unit you will share your enthusiasm for science and practice your oral presentation skills by giving several talks, both individually and in groups. You will also learn how to produce eye-catching and effective visual aids (e.g. PowerPoint) to help engage your audience during your presentations.

The aim of this unit is to increase your skills and confidence when speaking to groups who may or may not share your knowledge or enthusiasm for a scientific topic.

This unit focuses on using oral communication to communicate science to a range of audiences. It is NOT about presenting to your peers or a scientific audience. While all material presented is based on a theoretical foundation this unit is very practical in nature. Expect to get up and give talks and put lecture content into practice.

You will learn how to effectively communicate complex information on scientific topics of your choice, as well as develop skills in self-reflection and peer feedback.

This unit builds on the presentation practice that you may have done in SCOM1101 Communicating Science.

Participation

Weekly participation in tutorials is compulsory.

It is a unit requirement that you attend ALL tutorials. Most tutorials will incorporate class discussions and activities that are obviously more valuable if you participate in person. The tutorials will not be recorded. Attendance will be taken and students who miss tutorials without an approved reason (i.e. without approved special consideration or attendance at another session) will be awarded a failing grade for the unit.

If you miss a tutorial, you should first attempt to attend any other tutorial class as a replacement (unless you are missing scheduled assessments, you do not need to email the tutor or unit coordinator or apply for special consideration to attend a different class to normal; just introduce yourself to the tutor when you arrive). The full list of tutorials and room locations is available on the LMS. 

If you are going to miss a scheduled assessment (e.g. the group talk), or you are unable to attend any makeup tutorial in the week, you must apply for special consideration.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
