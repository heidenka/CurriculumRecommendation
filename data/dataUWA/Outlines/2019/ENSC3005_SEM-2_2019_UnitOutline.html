Academic Information

Content This unit develops the student’s ability to formulate and solve material and energy balances for chemical process systems. Students analyse material balances for multi-unit process systems with and without chemical reactions, including systems that incorporate recycle and purge. Analysis of these systems develop understanding of and the ability to calculate the thermodynamic properties of gases, liquids and vapours. Energy balances based on the first law of thermodynamics are formulated for systems with and without reactions.

Teaching and Learning Strategy

In this unit, students will master the unit material through a combination of lectures, readings, practical classes and written assignments.

To maximise preparation for the lectures, weekly notes will be posted no later than the Friday before each week, with students completing quizzes on the assigned reading before the first lecture of the week to ensure that they are best placed to capitalise on the lecture sessions. Practical classes will focus on the material in the written assignments, and developing the skills needed to successfully complete the written assignments. Attending all lectures and practical classes will give students the best opportunity to master the unit material

Mastery of the unit material will be demonstrated through completion of the assignments, a mid-semester exam, and a final exam.

Please note that a more complete description of the unit (in particular the assessments) than is provided for in the prescribed format of this unit outline is provided in the LMS under the "Unit Administration" link

Unit Structure

Reading Assignments & Weekly Notes

Reading Assignment consisting of a set of notes for the coming week (“the Weekly Notes”) will be made available at the unit LMS site no later than 12:00 noon on the preceding Friday. Students will be expected to complete the Reading Assignment prior to attending the lectures, so that they may actively participate in the lecture.

To ensure that students are prepared for the lectures, each week students will complete an online quiz dealing with the Reading Assignment. This quiz will be administered via the unit website in the learning management system (LMS), and must be completed by 10:00 am on Monday (i.e. before the lectures for the week commence). The quiz questions will be selected randomly from a bank of questions, so each student will see a different quiz.

The weekly quiz will commence in week 2 of the semester (Calendar week 32), and continue throughout. A trial quiz will be provided in Week 1 that will not count for assessment, but it is intended to familiarize students with the process.
 
Lectures

In this course, the lectures are designed to illuminate and expand on the material in the weekly notes. This will take the form of worked examples and additional (interactive) discussion, with students having the opportunity to ask questions about the lecture notes and assignments. 

Given the adaptive and interactive nature of the lectures, student attendance is important. The audio and any tablet projection from each session will be made available via the LMS, but the discussions and examples may use the white boards, which will not be captured by the LCS (there are no lecture cameras in the lecture theatre)

There will be two distinct sessions each week – one on Monday, one on Tuesday. Dr Leggoe will lead the lectures during weeks 1-6, and Dr Stanwix will take over for weeks 7-12.

Practical Classes

Practical class sessions will be held on Friday each week. There will be a single practical class stream. At the practical classes, the lecturers will be available to discuss course materials and assignment questions with students, and will at times lead group discussion of key issues. The lecturers may also take the opportunity to answer questions lodged prior to the tutorial sessions via e-mail. The practical classes will not be recorded.

During that time, the Dr Leggoe will be available at his office (Room 1.55 in the Monadelphous Integrated Learning Centre) to answer questions (appointments are not necessary during office hours). 

Dr Stanwix will also maintain office hours between 1:00 pm and 4:00 pm each Monday unless unavoidably called to other appointments. Written Assignments

During the course of the unit, students will complete 6 assignments (one every 2 weeks). Students will submit answers to designated questions from each assignment for assessment. 

The submission time and date will be listed on each assignment– for most assignments the submission time will be 5:00 pm on a Monday. Assignments will be submitted to the LMS – the assignments will predominantly be written, and the assignments must be submitted in pdf form to the LMS. Scanning the written assignment into a single pdf file is the required approach – if the assignment is submitted as multiple files, only the first one will be marked. PDF file sizes will be strictly limited to 10 MB – penalties up to 10% will be applied for exceeding this limit.

Assignments will be marked and returned via the LMS as marked up pdfs. Marks will be provided via the returned assignments – it is important that the marked assignments be reviewed by students, as the feedback is intended to assist student learning and preparation for the final exam.

Marking of the assignment will proceed as follows:
•	1-2 of the submitted questions will be marked in detail, using the style of marking scheme that will be used in the final exam. The score for the “detailed” questions will make up 2/3rds (66.67%) of the overall assignment mark
•	The remaining questions will be marked using an “overview” approach, where each question will be marked out of 5. The focus in marking the “overview” questions will be identifying that the correct elements have been provided (1 mark), that the correct approach has been taken (3 marks), where a critical error has occurred (if there is an error), and that all answers are correct. Note that 1 mark will be reserved for having correct answers – if any answer is wrong, 4/5 is the highest mark that can be awarded. The score for the overview questions will make up 1/3rd (33.33%) of the overall assignment mark.
Students will not know in advance which of the submitted questions will be marked in detail and which will be marked in overview.

The answers to the questions that are not to be submitted will be posted on the LMS so that students can check their work and understanding prior to submitting the designated questions. The answers to all questions will be posted once the submission time for the marked questions has passed.

Note that professional presentation of assignment submissions is essential, and deductions up to 10% will be applied if submissions have pages upside down, rotated, out of order, or are otherwise unacceptably poorly presented. Submissions that are partly or entirely illegible will only have the legible portions marked. Submissions must be in pdf form – a penalty of 10% will be applied to a non-pdf submission IF the submission can be converted to pdf form for marking (if it cannot be converted, it may not be possible to mark the assignment and a mark of zero will apply).

Hysys Laboratory Assignments

3 computer lab sessions will be scheduled over the course of the semester (in weeks 4, 8 and 11 of the semester). In each session, students will work through assignments designed to develop their Hysys modelling skills. At least one of the lecturers will be present at each session to issue the assignments and provide guidance.

The assignments will only be made available at the Lab sessions – meaning that attendance at the laboratory sessions is required for students to receive credit for these assignments. A roll will be taken at the sessions, and only students present will have their submissions assessed.

Each student will be given one of a set of similar assignments to complete, and at the end of the laboratory session will submit their work for assessment. This will be done by exporting model results in pdf form, and submitting the pdf to the LMS. Submission must be completed within 15 minutes of the end of the laboratory session – assignments submitted after this time will not be accepted. Assignments will be marked and returned via the LMS as marked up pdfs. Marks will be provided via the returned assignments and the LMS.

Exams

In week 8 of the semester, students will sit a one hour mid-semester exam. This exam will deal with the material covered by Dr Leggoe in weeks 1-6 of the semester (and in Assignments 1, 2 and 3)

The mid-semester exam will be held in ELT2 on Friday the 20th of September, starting (tentatively) at 8:45 am (timing will be confirmed once room bookings are confirmed), and continuing during the normal practical class timeslot.

At the end of the semester, students will complete a two hour final exam. The final exam will focus predominantly on the material covered by Dr Stanwix in weeks 7-12 of the semester (and in Assignments 4,5 and 6). Note that since the material covered in weeks 7-12 builds on the material in weeks 1-6, students should prepare for all elements of the unit to potentially be covered in the final exam.

Scheduling of the final exam will be confirmed when the final exam timetable is released.

Both exams will be closed book with written answers (the question style will be similar to the assignments). Students will be permitted to bring an approved calculator and writing implements into the venue. No notes will be permitted.


NOTE: The exams will assess all elements of the course, so all assignment questions, including those that are not submitted for marking, deal with examinable material. All material presented in the lectures and Hysys lab sessions is also examinable.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
