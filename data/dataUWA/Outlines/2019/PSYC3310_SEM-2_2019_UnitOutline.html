Academic Information

Content This unit is taught by seminar presentation and a small-group problem-based research project. The seminar topics are offered in areas of staff expertise and are based on a set of target papers drawn from the current literature. Student seminar presentations consider the theoretical context of the research and evaluate the hypotheses examined, the research methodologies used, the results obtained, and the conclusions drawn in the research being considered. The small-group research project is carried out in the topic area of the seminar and actively engages students in formulating and implementing an experimental approach to a research question. The unit gives students the opportunity to explore issues of contemporary psychology in depth and to acquire skills in areas such as research design and written and oral communication that prepare them for advanced study in psychology.

Teaching and Learning Strategy

This unit is designed to expand the opportunities for research-based learning and to further support the development of research skills at the undergraduate level. Active and inquiry-based learning is promoted through discussion, information gathering, problem solving and analysis. You will work closely with a member of academic staff and your peers throughout the semester in order to review the literature relevant to your chosen topic area, design and conduct or participate in an experiment, analyse the results, and prepare a research report based on the findings. You will also be expected to work in groups to prepare a research proposal, an ethics application and to present the outcomes of your research. Active participation in all aspects of this unit is strongly encouraged.

Unit Structure

Research proposal: The research proposal should briefly outline the proposed research. It should be no more that 1200 words in length (excluding the abstract (max 200 words), any figures or tables, and the reference list) and is a joint product of the research group (i.e., one research proposal submitted per group). Marking will cease once the word limit is reached. One group member will need to take responsibility for submitting the  research proposal on behalf of the group via LMS. The research proposal will be assessed by your seminar leader and all group members will receive the same mark for the research proposal.

Ethics application: The ethics application needs to be completed using the appropriate form (available on LMS) and should detail the proposed experimental procedures and responses to any ethical concerns or issues. Appropriate information sheets and consent forms should also be included. The ethics application should be completed by the research group and submitted via LMS (i.e., one ethics application submitted per group). The ethics application will be assessed by your seminar leader and all group members will receive the same mark for the ethics application.

Research report: The research report should be written according to the format outlined in the Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association, 6th Edition (guide available here http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa) and must not exceed 2500 words in length excluding the abstract (max 200 words), figures, tables, and the reference list. Marking will cease once the word limit is reached. The research report is an individual assessment and should not contain material taken from the research proposal. The research report should be submitted via LMS and will be marked by your seminar leader.

Research presentation: During Week 11, you will have the opportunity to present the details of your research to students in PSYC3302. While each group member is expected to contribute to the preparation of the presentation, group members will decide how best to deliver the final presentation, which may involve only two or three group members as speakers. All group members are required to attend the research presentation, regardless of whether they are speaking or not. If you do not attend the research presentation with your group, you will receive a zero grade for the research presentation. All group members who attend the final presentation will receive the same mark for the presentation. The research presentations are usually a maximum of 15 minutes in length + 5 minutes of question time, but this is dependent on the number of 3302 classes available and is subject to change.

Seminar/Lab participation: You are expected to participate actively in all class discussions, reviews of key papers, and group work, in both the seminars and the lab classes. Your seminar leader will review the criteria against which the participation mark will be determined with you in class.

Group work: This unit involves working together in a group to design and conduct a study, and to analyse and present the outcomes of that research. Learning to work with others is a valuable skill that you will need once you enter the workforce, but it is also an opportunity to form strong bonds with your peers as you work towards your Honours year. You are expected to contribute to all group-based project work. The contribution of each student to group work will be monitored by the seminar leader and tutor. If you feel that someone in your group is not contributing their fair share, you should inform the tutor and/or seminar leader at least 10 days prior to any group work assessment deadline. This will enable the tutor/seminar leader to address any issues with the group prior to  the deadline. Students who are not deemed to be contributing to their group project may be asked to provide evidence of their contribution.

Participation

Attendance at all seminars and lab classes is expected. In the seminars and lab classes, you will be involved in reviewing and discussing relevant background literature, designing and conducting or participating in an experiment, and analysing and reporting on the results of an experiment. Much of this work will be conducted in the seminar and lab classes in small groups. Due to the number of different topics and the active nature of the class activities, the seminars are not recorded. Failure to attend the seminars and lab classes will result in you missing key aspects of the design and analysis process and will impede your contribution to the group work components. You are expected to actively participate in discussions and activities. 10% of the total marks will be allocated to active participation and will be assessed throughout the semester in both the seminars and the lab classes. Special consideration is required if you are unable to attend one of your seminars or lab classes.

Referencing Requirements

You must have completed the Academic Conduct Essentials unit, available through your LMS account. You are expected to be familiar with the University's guidelines and policies regarding academic conduct, available at http://www.student.uwa.edu.au/learning/resources/ace/conduct Please note that the research proposal is a group-based assessment and the research report is an individual assessment. As such, you should be careful to avoid using material from the research proposal in your individual report. Excessive similarity between the research proposal and your individual report will be identified by the plagiarism detection software and reported to the academic misconduct advisor. Some similarity in the Method and Results sections is expected across reports and will not be identified as problematic, however, even these sections should not be identical to those in another student's report. Referencing should be in accordance with APA 6th edition. A library guide for using the APA 6th edition style is available at https://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
