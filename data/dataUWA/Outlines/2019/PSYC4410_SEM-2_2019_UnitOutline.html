Teaching and Learning Strategy

The unit will employ a variety of teaching and assessment techniques. There will be lectures and seminars where students give brief ‘elevator talks’ about their honours projects. Students will attend school colloquia and evaluate the presentations. Students’ presentation at the Honours Conference will be the main item of assessment.

If you choose not to attend lectures, it is expected that you will listen to the online lecture promptly, as lectures will contain important assessment information.

There will be one compulsory seminar, during which you will give short “elevator talks” on your honours research project, i.e. 3 slides in 3 minutes. These will be marked in a combination of peer-marking and marking by the unit coordinator (worth 20% of the grade). Use CAS to select your classes: www.cas.uwa.edu.au

You need to attend two school colloquia; specifically, two of the four colloquia on offer (attendance worth 10% of the grade; attendance marks are awarded for attending and actively paying attention to evaluate the delivery). There will be a cap on how many students can attend any given colloquium, and students will need to sign up online (via sign-up link on LMS). The four colloquia will take place Tuesdays, 4-5pm in GPB3 Simmonds LT - dates are Aug 6, Aug 27, Sep 24, and Oct 15 (dates may be subject to change).

Participation

Attendance and active participation at the honours conference, as well as the seminar and the two colloquia that a student has signed up for, is compulsory. Not attending and participating will result in loss of marks (unless special consideration has been granted, of course).

Referencing Requirements

Assignment should be APA formatted. Format of oral presentations will be discussed in class.

Texts

There is no set core textbook for the course. Some suggested readings will be provided online. All slides used in the lectures will be available online.

Other

Assessment Details

Oral presentations at seminar (“elevator talk”) and at honours conference:

You will be required to give a 3-minute presentation on your honours project. The focus should be on creating interest, so you should explain the project's aims and significance, while only touching on methodological details. You can refer to results if they are available but this is not expected. This presentation should be designed for a general lay audience, and you can only use three slides. You can look at example presentations online (e.g., http://threeminutethesis.org/).

You will be required to formally present your Honours project to your peers and academics attending the Honours Conference. Presentations will be marked using a variety of criteria by two markers. Marking criteria will be freely available and will be discussed beforehand in class. There will be a strict time limit (e.g., 12 minutes), plus time for questions (responses to questions will not be marked). Supervisors can only provide feedback once on the Honours Conference presentation—that is, there can only be one "dry-run". Conference talks will be recorded. 

Attendance at school colloquia (two semester-2 colloquia):
	
Colloquia are research presentations by visiting academics. These are usually 45 minutes plus 10-15 minutes question time. Students attend 2 colloquia of their choice during Semester 2. Students need to sign-up for specific colloquia, and there will be a quota, i.e. a maximum number of students allowed at each colloquium. Attendance marks are awarded for attending and actively paying attention to evaluate the delivery. Questions to the speaker are encouraged.

Written evaluation of attended colloquia:

Students evaluate the two attended colloquia on a standardized form, provided by the unit coordinator at the beginning of the semester. APA formatting rules apply. Submission via turnitin.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 10 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 30 out of 50 (a mark of 5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "acting with the values of honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility in learning, teaching and research". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.

If your study has been adversely affected by illness or other significant circumstances outside of your control, you can apply for special consideration. Significant circumstances may include but are not limited to:
