Academic Information

Content This unit examines key concepts in environmental protection law in Western Australia in its national and international contexts. It focuses on the law relating to pollution control and environmental impact assessment. The unit analyses a range of legal techniques and principles applied to environmental protection, including an introduction to the new regulatory challenges of reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Unit Information

This unit examines key concepts in environmental protection law in Western Australia in its national and international contexts.  It focuses on the law relating to pollution control and environmental impact assessment. The unit analyses a range of legal techniques and principles applied to environmental protection, including an introduction to the regulatory challenges of reducing greenhouse gas emissions to mitigage climate change.

Lecture Capture System

In accordance with UWA’s policy on lecture capture all lectures* in enabled venues will be recorded and made available to stream and download by enrolled students via the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for the duration of the unit (including Supplementary and Deferred Examinations period).

* Please note that in some limited circumstances lectures may not be recorded due to:  ethical and/or security related issues; the use of commercially sensitive material; the use of third-party copyright teaching material; or culturally sensitive content. Students will be advised, in advance, when this applies.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit will be conducted in the format of a weekly 2 hour lecture and fortnightly 2 hour tutorial with participation assessment. Lectures and tutorials will commence in Week 1 of Semester. These class hours equate to 3 hours per week for students across the semester. The exception to this is that there will be no tutorial meetings for Tutorial 3 (weeks 5 & 6) while students work on the written Tutorial Paper, which is due at the end of week 6. 
In 2023, the post-COVID-19 circumstances bring a return to standard face-to-face teaching. All lectures will be delivered in person and recorded in accordance with the UWA lecture capture policy unless a change in circumstances leads to delivery by lecture recording only. All tutorial groups will be face-to-face unless a change in circumstances leads to some or all tutorials being conducted on-line by Teams. 
It is advisable to do some pre-reading before attending lectures or listening to lecture recordings. It will be essential to prepare for tutorials. Students must attend the tutorial group to which they have been assigned by the Class Allocation System unless the Unit Coordinator has given prior approval for their attendance in another group.  Failure to attend your allocated group means you will lose tutorial participation marks.  
All unit materials will be delivered electronically, either through the LMS or by reference to on-line databases. 
Please see the Unit Structure and Unit Schedule below for an outline of the substantive content of the unit and the weekly progression through the six topics. The climate change law theme of regulation of greenhouse gas emissions will be explored through both the lectures and tutorials across the semester and included in assessment exercises.

Unit Structure

The unit explores six topics structured into three parts reflecting the three key elements of Environmental Law. 
Part 1: Environmental Governance: covering Topic 1 on the key definitions, institutions and objectives of Environmental Law; 
Part 2: Environmental Regulation: covering three topics - 
           * Topic 2 on policy and information instruments for managing cumulative harm; 
           * Topic 3 on the controls on particular activities causing pollution and environmental harm; 
           * Topic 4 on environmental impact assessment; and 
Part 3: Environmental Liabilities: covering two topics - 
           * Topic 5 on criminal liabilities and Topic 6 on civil liabilities for causing pollution and environmental harm. 
Please see the Timetable for details of class times and venues.  The tutorial allocations can be found in the Class Allocation System (CAS). 
In respect of assessment, students who wish to do the research essay instead of the final exam will be asked to make this choice in week 7.

Participation

Tutorial Participation Assessment: participation is mainly by demonstration of listening and oral communication skills during the tutorial. The Tutor will record attendance and assess participation. 
The objectives of the participation mark are to promote: engagement; preparation; understanding; reflection and responsible tutorial membership. Participation is about the quality not necessarily the quantity of contributions.
Students must attend the tutorial group to which they have been assigned by CAS unless the Unit Coordinator has given prior approval for their attendance in another group. Failure to attend your allocated tutorial group without the Unit Coordinator's approval means you will lose tutorial participation marks. 
PLEASE NOTE: As tutorial attendance and participation is being assessed for all tutorials, students who are unable to attend a tutorial for medical or other circumstances should contact the Unit Coordinator in a timely manner and provide satisfactory medical or other documentation to explain their absence. If possible, attendance at an alternative tutorial will be arranged. As a matter of courtesy, the student should also inform the Tutor.

There is a set of edited reading materials provided for each of the six topics. However, with the growth in easy access to on-line materials, this set of materials has not been updated since 2015. Statutes: The unit focuses on selected provisions from two main pieces of legislation and the relevant case law. 
* Environmental Protection Act 1986 (WA) [as amended in 2020], with the Environmental Protection Regulations 1987 (WA) and the Environmental Impact Assessment Administrative Procedures made under the Act.
* Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 (Cth).
Special Consideration
Late submissions of assessments granted through Special Consideration will only be granted for a maximum of 21 days from the original due date (including weekends and public holidays). The University’s Special Consideration policy application deadlines and other rules apply, as usual.


Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
