Unit Information

Welcome to PSYC5550 Organisational Consultancy Case Study. 
This unit provides a capstone experience in the Master of Business Psychology course, allowing students to integrate and apply learnings from preceding units in the degree to realistic organisational cases. In addition, it provides the opportunity for students to engage with literature that is critical to the knowledge base they acquire in the course. In this unit, students are first required to read and produce a learning journal on critical readings on Business Psychology. Next, in an intensive mode, students are required to carry out, in a group, a simulated organisational consultancy project that aims to diagnose and solve an organisational problem. Students work in groups under the guidance of academic staff in the unit and present a group response to the case, students then produce individual reports in the case incorporating feedback on the group presentation.
Unit Learning Outcomes:
Students are able to (1) critically analyse an organisational problem; (2) clearly and concisely present their diagnosis of the organisational problem; (3) reflect on critical readings in business psychology; and (4) illustrate appropriate solutions to organisational consultancy problems to clients.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

You will learn about a broad range of business psychology topics, including many “classic” and “modern classic” readings selected by staff in the whole business psychology postgraduate program. These readings, in combination with your learning from other units, will prepare you to tackle an intensive week (3 days on campus) where you will be required to provide solutions to problems described in a realistic case. This case analysis and initial response will require teamwork, communication, and interpersonal skills. You will then be given the chance to individually reflect on the case and the initial proposed response to it in more detail over the remaining weeks of the semester.
The approach in this unit is what’s known as a flipped classroom: Prepared self-paced learning via reading followed by more free-form and activity-based classwork. 
As a post-graduate professional unit, there is great value in the collective knowledge of experienced students, all of whom have degrees and many of whom have jobs and, in some cases, substantial work experiences. It is critical in postgraduate units to be receptive to and constructively critical of ideas from your classmates and teachers, to ask questions, to share experiences, and to learn from the knowledge of your classmates. 

Unit Structure

In this unit, students will prepare for the on-campus workshop days by reading and writing a learning journal on assigned readings in the early weeks of the semester. Students will then attend two on-campus intensive workshops on the dates and times below:
1. Wed 3th May 10am-4pm
2. Wed 10th May 10am-4pm
Please note that the timetable also includes a session scheduled on the ANZAC day public holiday, which will not be used because it is a university holiday. 
Students will be given case studies and assigned to groups 1 week before workshop 1 in order to work on in groups, which they will then work on in their own time and in workshops 1 and 2. They will develop a group response to their assigned case with access to guidance from the unit coordinator in workshop 1. The group will present a response to the case in workshop 2. Students will use feedback provided on their group presentation to refine or revise their response to the case in an individual written report.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
