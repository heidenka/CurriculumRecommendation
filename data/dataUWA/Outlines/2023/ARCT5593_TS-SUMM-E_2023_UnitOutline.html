Academic Information

Content This unit asks students to engage with the idea that the objects they design have a ‘life-cycle' and are therefore always implicated in networks. As they develop and prototype a complex piece of furniture, designed to be suitable for commercial production, students are asked to engage critically and pragmatically with concepts of sustainable design and life-cycle-analysis. As well as traditional workshop production skills, participants are given instruction in rapid-prototyping, CAD/CAM machining, and other computer-mediated production processes. The unit is built on a basis of ‘research through making' as it investigates the way furniture design draws materials, technologies, designers, the media, consumers, regulatory agencies, and users into complex networks of production, distribution, and consumption.

Unit Information

UNIT CODE: ARCT5593 
UNIT TITLE: FURNITURE : FROM PROTOTYPE TO PRODUCTION 
PROJECT SUMMARY: PACK STACK FOLD
Over the course of the Summer School program you are required to design, prototype and prepare full design documentation of a complex item of furniture. As part of the design task, you will also design product graphics / instructions describing the product assembly process, as required.
The program is effectively divided into two parts, carefully read the Summer School program:
•	The first part involves research, concept development, model making and testing.
•	The second part involves design development, full design documentation and full-scale prototype production of the furniture item and assembly instruction graphics.
In summary the following items comprise the full assessment requirements and must be submitted on the due dates as indicated in the unit schedule:
•	A full set of design documentation / general arrangement (GA) and part / component drawings; include specification of materials, finishes and ‘supplier’ information.
•	Full size, working prototype of the furniture item.
• Furniture item, sustainability / 'life cycle analysis' statement (visuals and graphics) plus  product graphics / instructions describing the product   assembly process, as required.
PROJECT METHOD: PACK STACK FOLD
Working through this project you will explore and develop ways of generating multiple ideas and concepts, expanding and developing the idea, and selecting design concepts in response to the project outline. The process of design and the ability to produce multiple ideas that demonstrate originality and creativity will be under review. 
The design content should evolve from a carefully considered investigation moving through a staged design process. It is intended the development of ideas will be through a combination of drawing, sketching, 3d model making and experimentation working directly with materials. 
You are required to prepare and present concept proposals to the teaching staff and peer group during the early sessions of the program in the ‘tutorial’ sessions.
Please document and record the design development process by keeping all sketches, drawings and models (or photographic record of physical 3d models) of the design process, and prototype construction / making process. 
The final piece is to be an expression of the designer / makers ideas and approach to design and demonstrate a sensitive and appropriate use of materials and resources.
In common with contemporary thinking and following the unit guidelines the investigation and resultant design will focus on; commercial production, effective and economical distribution, sustainable design principles and trends in product consumption.   
PROJECT TASK: PACK STACK FOLD
FURNITURE DESIGN
The item of furniture you select to design is your choice. The materials and processes required to realise the design must be available to you and be fit for purpose in relation to the design. The design must be suitable for industrial mass production or batch production, as opposed to a one-off craft based object.
Once you have selected the furniture item to be designed, it will be necessary to write an item specific design brief. This is an important first step of your design process.
The furniture piece must be designed to meet the following criteria:
•	STACK or FOLD. 
Note only one of these is required. By ‘stackable’ we mean that multiples of the same item can be stacked up for storage and / or transporation (eg. Charles and Ray Eames, DSS Chair). By ‘foldable’ we mean that the piece can be folded down for storage or transportation (eg. Tom Skeehan Hup Hup Chair).
•	PACK / TRANSPORTATION
Regardless of which of the above criteria is chosen for your design, consideration must be given to the design’s ‘packability’ for distribution and transportation. Note that not all designs are required to be completely ‘flat pack’but there must be a considered plan regarding how the product would be packed for shipping in the most economic way.
Things to consider will include:
How effectively does the furniture 'pack down' or 'fold' to afford the most economical (and sustainable) packing for transportation purposes.
How many boxes / packages will fit on a pallet?
How will the boxes / packages be stacked most efficiently for transportation?
•	DESIGN PRODUCTION. 
In common with contemporary thinking (and following the unit guidelines) the investigation and resultant design will focus on; commercial production (being either mass or batch production), effective and economical distribution, sustainable design principles in the product design while addressing trends in product consumption and markets.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
