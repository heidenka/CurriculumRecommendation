Unit Information

Welcome to ACCT5633 Accounting for Planning and Control. This unit aims to introduce management accounting information for planning, control, and decision making within business organizations. This unit covers three principal areas. The first section is cost accounting fundamentals, including an introduction to cost terms and cost accumulation systems. The second section is tools for planning and control, including a master budget, flexible budgets, variances and management control, inventory costing, and capacity analysis. The last section is the cost of information for decisions and advanced topics in management accounting. Topics in this section include cost-volume-profit analysis, decision-making, relevant information, inventory management, and pricing.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

The seminars in this unit are interactive. Students will be grouped into 3-4 people for seminar activities and collaborative learning project, a team project. The primary objective of using teams is to encourage students to develop life-long learning skills, such as learning to learn via peer and self-teaching and learning. In each seminar session, students are encouraged to participate actively in seminar activities. The goal is to foster a learning environment where all students feel comfortable asking questions to clarify an issue or concept. In class, a combination of lecture presentations and team-based work will be used.

Unit Structure

Students will be grouped into teams of 3-4 people for seminar activities and collaborative learning project (see below). These teams will remain fixed for the semester. Students are not allowed to change their team members without prior consent from the lecturer-in-charge. 
Seminar Activities (15%):
Seminar activities consist of questions that will be discussed in each seminar session. These questions include multiple-choice type questions, problem-type questions, and/or cases and are available on the Unit LMS site. These seminar activities aim to provide students an opportunity to (1) improve their understanding and technical competence of the management accounting concepts and techniques; and (2) to develop their generic skills in the following areas: critical thinking, problem-solving, communication, and interpersonal skills. The students' performance in seminar activities will be assessed based on their active participation and contribution to seminar activities. Suggested solutions to the weekly seminar activities will be placed on the Unit LMS site on the Friday of the week concerned. In addition, self-study questions are also assigned each week. Suggested solutions to these self-study questions will be available on the Unit LMS site at the commencement of the semester. Students are strongly advised not to consult the suggested solutions before attempting the assigned self-study questions each week. 
Collaborative learning project (35%):
The collaborative learning project consists of case studies and/or problems. One of the case studies/problems will involve preparing a master budget using a spreadsheet software program (e.g., Microsoft Excel). The collaborative learning project aims to allow students to work in teams, improve their peer-teaching/learning skills, and enhance their generic skills in a team-based setting in the following areas: problem-solving, critical thinking, and interpersonal skills. In addition, each individual student is required to submit a reflective report. On completion of the assignment, students are required to complete a peer evaluation assessment via online SPARKPLUS for each of their team members. SPARKPLUS details are provided in LMS. 
Final examination (50%):
The final exam will comprise both theoretical and practical questions. The materials covered in the final examination will be drawn from the seminar topics covered in weeks 1 to 12. More details will be provided closer to the assessment.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
