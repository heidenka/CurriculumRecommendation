Academic Information

Content This unit is concerned with organisational change and transformation, and focuses on the major challenges facing organisations in a rapidly changing, technologically challenging and increasingly complex and competitive global business environment. Topics covered may include organisational life cycles, longevity and effectiveness; managing and changing organisational cultures; leading and managing change; organisational (re)structure and (re)design; change management tools and interventions; evaluating change management strategies; creativity and innovation as drivers of change; organisational learning and the Learning Organisation; managing knowledge and intellectual capital; new technologies as drivers of change in organisations; and new and emerging organisational forms.

Organisational Change and Transformation – An Overview
The fast pace of change, uncertainty and volatility is inherent in all our personal and work lives. This is no more evident than right now with the impact of the pandemic, which is not only disrupting our lives but is also reshaping the global economy. I don't think any of us, need to be persuaded of the importance of becoming more adaptive to change....whether we like it or not!! 
In my other life as a Change Consultant, I work with corporate, public sector and not for profit organisations. From this experience, I find that irrespective of the type of organisation that the change is occurring within, the emotional reaction to change appears to be universal and an emotionally charged issue. With this in mind, there is no one change model and / or guaranteed universal steps for leading change. Therefore, the purpose of this unit is to take you on a journey and equip you with a range of models, tools and strategies to lead change.
I will bring my past and current experience to this Unit, however I also encourage you to share your wisdom in all our discussions. I invite you into this 5 day intensive unit therefore, not only as learners but importantly as co-creators in the discussions around change, transition and transformation. This will be both as an individual and within your Syndicate Learning Team, which will be self-selected on Day 1- 3rd February 2023.
The 5 day program is spread over 3 distinct periods:
Days 1 & 2 - 3rd & 4th February: Change –The Paradox of Change…..Change as the New Normal 
Days 3 & 4 - 17th & 18th February Transition – Heading into the Unknown..The Emotions of Change
Day 5 - 3rd March Transformation – The Future State...A Different Way of Doing Things
During the sessions your Syndicate Learning Team members are also required to submit a series of ‘Decision Points’ in the form of a Team Check-in Report to monitor your progress and understanding of the case study assignment (allocated on Day One - 3rd February).
I also encourage you to keep a personal reflective journal to document the key points from each week and record your insights or further questions that may emerge. Please note the journal is not part of the Unit assessment, therefore not compulsory (but highly recommended!).
Another key factor in this Unit is the manner in which you all work together. Your Syndicate Learning Team, is a very important part of the program in how you co-create your ideas and solution-focused responses to the questions and challenges presented during the 5 days. Working as ‘team’ therefore is bound by your understanding of your own team ‘Charter’ which involves developing a Team Vision; Purpose; Values and an illustration of your collective ‘Signature Strengths’ profile (please complete your individual Top 5 Strengths by accessing the free VIA Survey http://www.viacharacter.org/www/Character-Strengths-Survey. Please share your own top 5 Strengths with your team members to determine and plot the collective diverse strengths that reside within your team. Your completed Team Charter and your collective Team Strengths are required by the end of Day 2 - 4th February.
As you will read in my first ‘post’ on the ‘Announcement’ page, it is CRITICAL that you come prepared to each session, having completed the readings that I have specifically allocated for the particular session. Remember your Syndicate Learning Team will be reliant on each member to be prepared to be able to deliver the expected check-in ‘Decision Points’ at their due dates.
Please also note, the first Assessment (Quiz 1 - 30% assessment grade) opens Monday 13th February 9am and closes Tuesday 14th February 5pm and involves a range of questions that focuses on Planned Change & Transition readings and class discussions from Day 1 & 2. 
Finally, my commitment to you is to provide a creative learning environment that encourages creative thinking and excitement around change; transition and transformation.
I look forward to meeting everyone on Friday 3rd February at 9am sharp.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct
