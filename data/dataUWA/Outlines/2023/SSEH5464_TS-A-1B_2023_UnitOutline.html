Academic Information

Content This unit develops the skills necessary to teach Health and Physical Education in the secondary school. It focuses on practical and theoretical skills with an emphasis on effective and ineffective behaviours. The unit has a lecture, tutorial and practical format. Major areas covered include the development of organisational, personal, planning and management skills, and the formation of a philosophy towards the teaching of Health and Physical Education in a school setting.

Unit Information

This unit develops the skills necessary to teach Health and Physical Education in the secondary school. It focuses on practical and theoretical skills with an emphasis on effective and ineffective behaviours. The unit has a lecture, tutorial and practical format. Major areas covered include the development of organisational, personal, planning and management skills, and the formation of a philosophy towards the teaching of Health and Physical Education in a school setting.
Students are able to (1) display an awareness of the developmental needs and interests of adolescent learners in Health and Physical Education; (2) develop an understanding of different orientations and theoretical frameworks of the subject; (3) demonstrate a repertoire of teaching and learning strategies relevant to the secondary Health and Physical Education curriculum; (4) display a detailed understanding of the current Western Australian Health and Physical Education Curriculum plan; (5) evaluate various instructional sequences (lesson plans and programs) for effective and engaging learning; (6) evaluate instructional resources for secondary Health and Physical Education, including information and communication technology (ICT) and other educational technologies; (7) acquire an understanding of effective assessment and evaluation processes practice in Health and Physical Education; (8) develop an awareness of diverse student learning needs through the planning of differentiated learning and the selection of differentiated resources; and (9) demonstrate a developing proficiency in engaging students from diverse social and cultural contexts and with diverse literacy and numeracy capabilities.
Indicative assessments in this unit are as follows: (1) lesson planning - peer teaching, lesson planning and development; (2) research report on ALT in Physical Education classes; and (3) curriculum readings and class quizzes. Further information is available in the unit outline.
Student may be offered supplementary assessment in this unit if they meet the eligibility criteria.

Major Areas to be covered in the Unit
•	Developing a personal philosophy towards the teaching of Physical Education and personal fitness.
•	Developing a range of organisational skills including methods of
o	Gaining attention
o	Defining the activity area
o	Teacher positioning
o	Equipment distribution
o	Use of appropriate formations
o	Effective use of concluding
•	Developing personal skills which will enhance the teaching of Physical Education:
o	Appearance
o	Use of voice
o	Use of gesture
o	Use of eye contact
o	Appropriate movement around class
o	Posture
o	Focusing
•	An introduction to lesson planning in Physical Education including:
o	The writing of outcomes (or equivalent)
o	The writing of suitable Introductions, Skill Developments and Culminations
•	A study of the Curriculum Framework so that students are aware of its content areas and teaching strategies
•	Development of teaching strategies using Mosston & Ashworth’s (2002) categories of:
o	Command style
o	Task style
o	Reciprocal style
o	Small group styles of teaching
o	Self-check style
o	Guided-discovery style
•	Investigate management procedures which effectively increase activity time and decrease instructional and organisation time.
•	Develop appropriate types and rate of feedback plus correct ratios of positive to negative feedback.
•	Preparation for teaching practice.

Participation

ATTENDANCE
This is a professional unit.  If an absence is unavoidable, you have a responsibility to let your lecturer and your peers know, in advance, so that your absence can be accommodated.  If you have missed so much work as to be inadequately prepared for teaching practice, you will not be allocated a school for Professional Practice units.  Unsatisfactory attendance can result in exclusion from further study under University and Faculty Regulations.
EXPECTED BEHAVIOUR OF HPE PROFESSIONALS
•	Punctuality
•	Appropriate dress: clean, sun-smart etc.
•	Asking considered questions – facilitating class discussion.
•	Generating discussion and asking questions that confirm you are thinking beyond your own experiences and looking through the eyes of others.
•	Trying to answer questions that are generated in classes.
•	Negotiated and pro-active communication of personal needs – i.e. can’t participate in an activity lesson (ill/injured) and need to observe.
•	Maintaining an email vigil
•	Consideration of others – offering staff/student assistance.
•	Leadership role in Dip-Ed activities.
•	Making an appointment with staff to catch up on missed work
What behaviours DO NOT represent professional courtesies?
•	Non-attendance – without first confirming with staff a need to do so.
•	Consistently arriving late to class.
•	Needing to be asked to come in and sit down
•	Not recognising the need to stop talking – so the lecture can start.
•	Phone not silenced in class
•	Phone being used in class
•	Being distracted or distracting others.
•	Leaving early – without first confirming with staff a need to do so.
•	Not meeting assessment deadline – without first confirming with staff a need to do so.
•	Criticising others.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
