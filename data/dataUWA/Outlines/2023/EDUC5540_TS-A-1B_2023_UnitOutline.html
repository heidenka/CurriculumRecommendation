Academic Information

Content This unit develops students' expertise in the planning and evaluation of a targeted intervention in primary English education with a focus on early reading (F-2). Students plan a focused intervention project for individual school students or groups of students. The project plan represents a response to a particular reading need identified at individual or group level. The unit contains a strong research informed dimension, which involves the selection and use of appropriate diagnostic, formative and summative assessment tools.

Assignment 1 (65%)
Intervention plan and justification 
This assignment requires you to plan an evidence-based series of 10 targeted intervention lessons for an individual student or small group of students with similar needs. The lessons can be delivered over a period of two to three weeks. You will be provided with several descriptions of children and sets of assessment data (student profiles) to select from. To complete this assignment you will need to:
1) Select one of the children from the student profiles available and analyse their strengths and needs. Complete the profile summary sheet provided. This should be uploaded as part of your assignment but will not count in the word count.
2) Plan a series of 10 lessons (20-30 minutes each) that you could implement over a two-to-three-week period within classroom time. You can assume that there are other children in the class with similar needs so the lessons can be planned for small groups (although some may be one-on-one, if practicable). Your lessons will need to be based on research about ‘what works’ and targeted to address the specific learning need/s you have identified through the analysis of the data. You will also need to state how you will measure and record student learning and engagement during your intervention. You do not need to write complete lesson plans but each lesson should be clearly outlined in the template provided. This part of the assignment is not included in the word count.
3) Write a report of no more than 3500 words (excluding end references) describing your intervention plan and providing a justification based on the assessment data and the research literature. The research literature cited in your justification should be discussed critically and should be current research (or seminal literature, where appropriate). In describing and justifying your plan, you will also need to consider assessment strategies, which may include the collection of observational and interview data, test results, informal assessment results and the analysis of children’s work. As part of the report, include a section on how you would communicate and report on the intervention to parents and carers and how you would meet professional ethics and conduct during the process. Finally, describe how you would evaluate the effectiveness of your intervention plan overall.
Please refer to the rubric.
Assignment 2 (35%)
Professional learning plan and essay
This assessment will help you meet the APST 6.4 graduate standard in giving you the opportunity to demonstrate an understanding of the rationale for continued professional learning and the implications for improved student learning. For this essay, you are required to:
a) Discuss the importance of effective continuing professional learning (CPL) for teachers, with an emphasis on CPL in English/literacy. Your discussion needs to be supported by the current literature and should include a section on the link between teacher CPL and student learning (750-1000 words).
b) Identify an area relating to early reading where you would like to improve your own knowledge and practice. Create a personal professional learning plan that outlines: i) What you need to learn about and how you have identified this need – also describe some ways of identifying your professional learning needs throughout your teaching career;
ii) What resources (including formal professional learning events and programs) you could draw upon and what actions you could take to improve your knowledge and practice in this area. In describing the resources you could access, state how they meet the criteria of effective PL and how they link to the APSTs (500-700 words). The assignment should be no more than 1500 words overall, excluding end references.
Please refer to the rubric.

RESUBMISSION RULE:
Failure to pass an assessment item within a unit (MTeach)
15. Students must pass all components of a Master of Teaching unit to pass the unit overall. Students who fail an assessment item in a unit may resubmit it if:
(a) this is their first request for a resubmission in the unit;
(b) they contact the unit coordinator by email within 5 University working days of the release of the result and formally request a resubmission.
(c) A resubmitted assignment that is deemed to be a ‘fail’ by the unit coordinator, will receive the original failing mark for the component.
(d) Where resubmission is approved, the reassessed mark is capped at the assessment pass mark, unless an application for mitigation is approved in accordance with the University Policy on Assessment: Special Consideration (UP11/23).
(e) A resubmission is normally due one week aft er being approved by the unit coordinator.
(f) Assignments that are failed on the grounds of lateness or academic misconduct will not normally be considered for resubmission.


Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
