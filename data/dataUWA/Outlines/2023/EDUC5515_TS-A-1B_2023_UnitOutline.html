Unit Information

This unit develops students' expertise in the use of research as a fundamental part of teaching practice. Students will explore how research can be used to support practice, critically examine how interventions are an important part of teaching and how research can be practically applied. Students will prepare a proposal to undertake research based upon a practical intervention.

ASSESSMENT TASKS FOR THE UNIT
Task One: Professional learning and teaching through research - 30 marks
This task is comprised of two sections:
Part A:  What role does research play in teacher professional learning and practice? 
(10 marks - 500 – 700 words) 
In answering this question consider the following:
•	How research may inform teaching practice. 
•	What understandings teachers should have about research. 
•	Who are key educational researchers who have influenced classroom practice?
•	How may AITSL standards be used to guide research for professional learning?
•	Is the capacity to research a skill set needed by the 21st Century teacher? 
Check the rubric for specific information.
Part B: Evaluation of a published school-based research intervention
(20 marks – 1,200 – 1,400 words)  
Select a published school-based research intervention from the list on LMS you may find your own paper to review. If you select your own paper, please check with Jennifer and/or Jan that it is suitable before you start work on the assignment. 
In evaluating a school-based research intervention you may consider the following:
•	What is the basis of the intervention and what did it aim to achieve? How well considered in the intervention? 
•	Is there a clear Research Question? Is the purpose clear? What is the paper about?
•	Is the paper well organised and focused? Is it ‘easy’ to read?
•	Is there any inherent bias?
•	How robust is the research? For example, consider the research design, what and how data are collected.
•	Are appropriate conclusions drawn?
•	How relevant is the research intervention to your teaching practice? For example, think about subjects, location, discipline-specific nature. How might the intervention and research be practically applied in the classroom? What can you do with it? 
Task Two: Research proposal/report – 70 marks.
You will write a research proposal that is in the range of 3,500 - 4,000 words. This proposal will be for an intervention-based research project which would have a practical teaching application within a school.
To write a research proposal you will have to develop an appropriate research question that you could (potentially) research in the classroom environment. This may be something that you noticed while on practicum or it may be something that you are interested in. We will spend time in the tutorials working on the development of the Research Question.
NB: You will NOT be conducting this research in a school setting during this unit.
The research proposal should be structured as follows:
•	Introduction to the topic and why it is important (relevant background information)
•	Literature review (What does the literature say?) – some (only some) of the literature may be drawn from Task One Part B. 
•	Aim and research question
•	Research methods (how will the research question be investigated? Participants, data collection process.) 
•	Practical research instruments to be created and explained – documentation to be created. 
•	An intervention plan for 10 days to be created and explained – what will the intervention look like in the classroom. A good idea – can be scaffolded in assessment one.
•	Ethical considerations – a fully completed ethics application.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
