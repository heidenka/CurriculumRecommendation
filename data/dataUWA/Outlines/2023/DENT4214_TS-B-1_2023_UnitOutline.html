Academic Information

Content This unit builds on the knowledge gained in the DENT3007 Fundamentals of Clinical Dentistry 1 unit, and it will be taught in conjunction with the DENT4212 and DENT4213 Fundamentals of Clinical Dentistry 2 Parts 1 and 2 units. In this Unit, students will continue their learning in the clinical discipline of Restorative (Operative) Dentistry with a focus on the diagnosis of teeth requiring direct restorations, the principles and biological rationale for such restorations, and the materials used for direct restorations of teeth. Students will continue to develop their manual skills in the pre-clinical simulation environment in order to perform direct restorations in teeth. Personal and professional development will continue throughout the Semester.

Unit Information

This unit introduces to the students direct restorations including the operative procedures [G.V. Blacks Classification Class 1,2,3,4,5,6]. The topics covered include operative dentistry, biomaterials used for direct coronal restorations, occlusion in permanent teeth, elements required for a comprehensive oral clinical examination, and complex restorations (Amalgam + Composite).
Students are able to (1) Describe the biomaterials (Composite + GIC) used for direct coronal restorations; (2) Execute tooth restorations [ Simple (1-2 surfaces) to Complex (4-5 surfaces) ] (Amalgam + Composite + GIC); (3) develop and demonstrate basic competence in the clinical skills needed to treat periodontal conditions in a simulation + clinical environment;

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit has been designed to accommodate all the students as students have foundational knowledge in clinical dentistry. This unit builds on the skills obtained in DMD1 Units. 
This unit lays the skills required for a general dental practitioner to understand and appreciate the importance of operative dentistry and periodontal instrumentation. Students are encouraged to meet each other throughout the semester to consolidate what they have learned. This will enable students to give each other peer feedback along the way. In addition, students have the opportunity to discuss any concerns with the tutors as well as the lecturers on any issues they may have during the unit.
DENT4214 aims to help students develop the overarching inherent qualities required as a graduate dental practitioner
- Develop the ability and desire to develop disciplinary and interdisciplinary knowledge and skills
- Develop and obtain competency in basic clinical skills
- Undertake self-evaluation
- Develop critical thinking and reflection

Unit Structure

The main goal of DENT4214 is to allow DMD students to get a intermediate knowledge of general operative dentistry required for clinical dentistry. It will also complement DENT4215 to allow students to execute restorative procedures on patients. Our philosophy is to allow students to apply what they have learnt. The combination of theoretical and practical aspects of this unit is to help you get the basic skills required to approach more advanced clinical skills required in the later years. We hope that this unit will provide foundational skills for a graduate dentist. This unit will be taught by combining lectures, practical exercises, and self-directed learning.
Three foundational domains are being taught in DENT4214:
1) Didactic knowledge in biomaterials applied in clinical dentistry [50% of DENT4214] - This domain will teach the students the importance of critical analysis and academic writing 
2) Simple to Complex restorative procedures in clinical dentistry [50% of DENT4214] - This component will teach the students advanced restorative techniques
3) Clinics [P/F]

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
