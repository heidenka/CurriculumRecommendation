Teaching contingency plan (In response to Covid19 restrictions)

As this is a practicum unit, in the event of COVID-19 restrictions we will likely move to delivery of services via telehealth. Pausing some placement activities for a period of time may be required. As this unit operates under a non-standard teaching period there is flexibility in the commencement and end dates for activities within the placement unit. Contingency planning will need to occur on a case-by-case basis via consultation between the unit coordinator, clinic director and student. Weekly clinic meetings and case presentations/topic discussions can be flexible and will be rescheduled or moved online as needed.

Unit Information

The School of Psychological Science at The University of Western Australia acknowledges that it is situated on Noongar land, and that Noongar people remain the spiritual and cultural custodians of their land, and continue to practise their values, languages, beliefs and knowledge. We pay our respects to their Elders both past and present. 
This unit is an extension of the first internal practicum unit for postgraduate clinical psychology students. The aim is to provide a continued introduction to the practice of psychotherapy, and to offer students supervised training and experience in interviewing, assessment, as well as individual and group therapeutic interventions. The unit continues students development of the key aspects of professional practice and includes consideration of the ethical guidelines and legal responsibilities that relate to clinical practice. The unit also furthers students skills in assessment, basic counselling skills, interviewing techniques, therapeutic interventions, note taking, report writing and the clinical supervision process. Students engage in supervised clinical client contact, clinic meetings and a series of case presentations.
As well as working with individual psychotherapy clients, trainees will be provided with the opportunity to work within a range of groups/modules. This may include group therapy (e.g. emotion regulation/management, insomnia, etc) and the Clinical Neuropsychology Clinic. Please refer to the Placement Guide for additional details.
Students are completing their professional training in clinical psychology and are required to pass placement evaluation reports and submit logbooks of their activities to meet placement competencies as per APAC requirements. As this is an UP/UF unit, the weighting of the assessments is irrelevant.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Students are provided the opportunity to be supervised in their first exposure to clinical psychology practice at an internal placement within the UWA, School of Psychological Science, Robin Winkler Clinic. This involves face to face experiential learning in the clinic (inclusive of telehealth as required). This internal placement provides students with the opportunity to accrue APAC required direct client and client-related hours, and demonstrate core APAC clinical competencies, both of which are required as part of this accredited program.

Unit Structure

PSYC5679 operates on a non-standard teaching period commencing at the start of semester 2 and continuing throughout the year.  This unit follows on from PSYC5678 in semester 1.
ATTENDANCE:
Clinic Meeting (11-1pm each Wednesday). Attendance at a weekly one hour Clinic Meeting is mandatory. Formal student case presentations (or discussion topics) will follow the Clinic Meeting. Students are also required to attend weekly individual and group supervision sessions with their nominated Supervisor(s). Students must notify the Unit Coordinator and their Supervisor in advance if they are unable to attend Clinic Meeting or supervision sessions.
CONTACT HOURS: 
Minimum 200 hrs Sem 2 (including observation, supervision, supervised clinical work and case presentations). A record of hours and activities must be kept using the electronic SONIA logbook system.
ASSESSMENT:
Students will be expected to present one clinical case presentation during the semester, as well as submit at least two deidentified intake and termination reports at the completion of internal placement. Formal placement evaluation reports will also be conducted by Supervisors at the completion of each of two individual psychotherapy rotations, and at the completion of each module (approx 4-5) that students have participated in. It is also expected that students have developed competencies administering the WAIS-IV, WMS-IV and WISC-V during the internal placement.
To pass this unit, it is expected that students receive an overall pass on the Supervisor Placement Report for every practicum activity (i.e., each individual therapy rotation and each group/module) and meet required placement hours (i.e., 160 direct client contact hours and 400 total placement hours by the end of internal placement as indicated in the electronic SONIA internal placement logbook). If a student is not on track to pass a practicum activity this will be raised by their supervisor during mid-review, so they have the opportunity to rectify the identified difficulties. Should a student not pass any one practicum activity/module on internal placement, an appropriate course of action for managing the situation will be determined by the Internal Placement Coordinator in consultation with the Robin Winkler Clinic Director and Program Director. Failing one practicum activity/module may be open to remediation depending on circumstance, however a student cannot fail two or more practicum activities/modules and pass the internal placement unit. See the Clinical Psychology & Clinical Neuropsychology Practicum Guide for more information.

Participation

It is compulsory that students be punctual and attend all coursework and other specified training activities unless extenuating circumstances (such as illness or significant personal crisis) prevent them from doing so. Where students cannot attend (or have not attended) the full required activities or classes, they are expected to notify the unit coordinator to provide an adequate explanation and documentation (such as a medical certificate in the event of illness) explaining their absence or lateness. A failure to attend without adequate explanation and documented evidence of illness or other impeding factors beyond one's personal control will result in failing the unit in which the class/activity was missed. Note that even where an adequate explanation and documented evidence are supplied, a student will still fail the unit if he/she has not attended a sufficient proportion of the unit. A "sufficient proportion of the unit" is defined as at least 80%.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.
