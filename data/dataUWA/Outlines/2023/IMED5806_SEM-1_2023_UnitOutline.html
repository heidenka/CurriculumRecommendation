Learning And Teaching Strategy

The unit uses interactive learning strategies in either a face-to-face or online flexible mode (with a shared LMS unit). Students enrolled in the face-to-face mode will be able to attend a two-day workshop. All other unit content (including a folder of learning materials covering the workshop topics) will be available online via the LMS for asynchronous delivery, with no requirement for students to participate online at specific times (other than to meet assessment submission dates). 
We believe you will learn best through reflection on, and application of, your own teaching and learning experiences. We value you as a member of the unit and will encourage your learning through providing: 
•	a supportive learning environment that centres on student needs;
•	transparent learning outcomes as the foundation for learning materials and assessment items;
•	learning materials that are recent, evidence based and recommended by experts in the field (including suggested readings, presentations developed specifically for IMED5806 and high quality videos curated from external sources);
•	facilitated discussions (via face-to-face workshop and online forums) to share information and generate ideas;
•	clear and authentic assessment items to simulate core HPE processes;
•	opportunities for students to receive feedback through peer review and formative assessment; and 
•	mechanisms for students to provide feedback on the unit and using these evaluation findings to revise the unit to ensure it meets current and future HPE needs. 

Unit Structure

This unit is divided into four modules: 
•	Module 1: Simulation in Healthcare – Introduction and Theory (Learning Outcomes 1 and 2) 
•	Module 2: Interprofessional Learning – Introduction and Practice (Learning Outcomes 2)
•	Module 3: Designing and Delivering Simulation – Six Phases (Learning Outcomes 3 and 5)
•	Module 4: Facilitating Interprofessional Learning in Simulation (Learning Outcomes 3, 4 and 5)
The two-day workshop will provide students with an overview of all four modules. In addition, learning materials will be available in folders for each module on the Learning Management System (LMS). 
It is recommended that modules are completed sequentially. Each module is designed to span two to four weeks of a standard semester if completing in an online flexible mode (or approximately half this time for students who attended the workshops). A suggested schedule has been outlined below for face-to-face and online flexible modes to ensure that relevant modules are completed prior to assessment submission dates. However, modules can be completed at a flexible pace with learning materials remaining accessible throughout the semester.

Participation

A range of learning activities have been included for each module to comprehensively address the learning outcomes, whilst allowing for individual learning preferences and flexibility requirements. Where applicable, suggestions have been included to tailor learning activities to the student's academic or workplace context. Whilst completion of learning activities is encouraged and may be monitored for unit evaluation purposes, the only compulsory learning activities are the assessment items. However, students are encouraged to participate actively in the interactive tasks with their peers (e.g. via the Workshop and Discussion Forums) through sharing information / resources and providing peer feedback to optimize both their own, and their peers', learning.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Academic conduct

Academic Integrity is defined in the University Policy on Academic Conduct as "honest, ethical and responsible scholarship". UWA expects the highest degree of academic conduct from all students.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.

Students should familiarise themselves with the information and resources available regarding academic conduct and ethical scholarship at UWA.

If your study has been adversely affected by illness or other significant circumstances outside of your control, you can apply for special consideration. Significant circumstances may include but are not limited to:
