Academic Information

Content This is an introductory unit in marketing research. It provides an overview of the marketing research process and familiarises students with fundamental concepts of marketing research and frequently encountered research topics and techniques used in marketing practice. More specifically, the unit covers formulating marketing research problem statements and objectives and writing a research report. Various research designs are examined, as well as associated data collection forms and data analysis techniques.

Unit Information

Welcome to MKTG2305 Marketing Research
To be an effective marketer, you must be able to collect information and understand the market. One critical way of doing that is by conducting market research. You will be able to do this by learning about the marketing research process, the fundamental concepts of marketing research, and frequently encountered quantitative and qualitative techniques. This introductory unit in marketing research will empower you to gain basic marketing research skills and practice these skills in a natural setting. 
Unit Content
This course covers the fundamental concepts and stages of marketing research within an overall structural framework, including how to integrate stages of research, carry out research in a scientific manner, evaluate survey instruments, communicate research outcomes, and apply research skills. The key topics are: problem identification, defining project scope, developing a research approach, conducting fieldwork, and analysing and reporting research data.
This course involves statistical analysis to address marketing problems. It is assumed that students enrolled in this course have a basic understanding of business statistics, marketing terminology, and concepts. Specifically, it will be helpful if students have completed STAT1520 Economic and Business Statistics or equivalent. To accommodate students from different backgrounds, the teaching team will assist with marketing-related statistical analysis and make trade-offs if necessary. But students with minimal statistics knowledge need to be prepared to learn some remedial statistics independently. 
Unit Goal
Marketing Research students will solve real-world problems to become confident, competent professionals. Students will gain practical skills in obtaining, interpreting, and applying data to make informed effective business decisions.
This course aims to provide students with a background in research methods and will cover issues related to conducting marketing research, data analysis, and marketing evaluation methods. Knowledge of these topics will enable students to implement and evaluate marketing research during their professional careers. 
Students will learn about marketing research by actually doing research. Via carefully designed formative and summative assessments, you will develop skills to communicate your thoughts clearly and succinctly. The knowledge and skills you acquire from this unit will help you become a marketer who can critique, design, and implement marketing research.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

Students will have a hybrid (i.e., blended) lecture each week. In other words, both face-to-face (F2F) and online students will attend the class simultaneously. Lectures will be where the theory and methods of market research are presented. Your attendance is critical to ensure your learning quality and learning experience. 
Lecture slides for each session will be posted on LMS prior to the class. Students are strongly advised to review the slides, read the prescribed textbook chapter(s), and, if applicable, other required materials prior to the lecture. Class time will be spent developing and expanding upon the material presented in the required text.
The lectures are recorded for electronic distribution. However, purely watching the recordings for the whole semester is not the third authorised option (apart from attending F2F and online) for taking this course. Recordings are available on the Lecture Capture System (LCS) for you to catch up under exceptional circumstances or review the course content when necessary. 
This unit includes five tutorial and five laboratory (SPSS) sessions. These are critical opportunities to apply the knowledge and skills learned in the lectures, and develop an understanding and practical experience with the SPSS statistical package. The tutorial and laboratory sessions are available F2F or online. Your tutor and lecturer will also help you with this unit during consultations. 
Please note that you will need to be accessible during the lecture (10 out of 12 weeks) and tutorial/laboratory hours since you will need to gain your class participation marks at those times.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
