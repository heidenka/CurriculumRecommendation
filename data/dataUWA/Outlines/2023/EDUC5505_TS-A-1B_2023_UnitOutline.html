Academic Information

Content This unit models and promotes the active teaching and learning of mathematics in middle and upper primary school. Students explore current evidence-based practices for promoting understanding, fluency, problem solving and reasoning in teaching and learning, set within the content strands of number and algebra, measurement and geometry, and statistics and probability. Students continue to develop and share a portfolio of teaching and learning strategies that actively engage students in working mathematically, and that develop the general capabilities and address the cross-curriculum priorities of the Australian Curriculum. Students apply their knowledge and skills to prepare a unit of work/program for teaching a mathematics topic or theme in the middle and upper primary years.

Unit Information

Mathematics in Middle and Upper Primary provides an introduction to the knowledge, skills, and competencies required by teachers of primary school mathematics (Years 3 to 6). It is focused on equipping pre-service teachers to help children achieve learning outcomes associated with the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics. The unit is delivered as a series of interactive workshops, each focused on a specific pedagogical aspect of mathematics teaching that is well grounded in theories of teaching and learning. Through participation in these workshops, it is expected that preservice teachers will learn to recognise, appreciate and model eﬀective teaching strategies and construct their own philosophical and practical understanding of the teaching and learning process. As well as addressing pedagogical issues, each workshop is designed to help pre-service teachers to become familiar with the mathematical content encountered during middle and upper primary school, and with a variety of teaching resources suitable to teach this content.

ADDITIONAL DETAILED ASSESSMENT INFORMATION
Assignment 1 Lesson planning (50%)
1a. Micro-teaching (20%)
Create an activity and/or manipulative that is suitable for explaining a mathematical concept.
Justify the place and the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity.
Simulate teaching using this activity and/or manipulative. 
Respond to feedback to indicate the effectiveness of the activity and/or manipulative, or how you modified it.
The assignment will be assessed on the following criteria for marking: 
Creation of a learning activity or manipulative, and its suitability for explaining a mathematical concept; 
Justification for the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity, including documentation and APA style referencing; 
Simulation of the use of this activity and/or manipulative in a learning and teaching activity; 
Reflection on the learning and teaching simulation, and response to feedback. 
1b. Sequence of lesson plans (30%)
Using a suitable lesson plan framework, you are required to plan four consecutive 45 minute lessons for a class of 24 mixed ability students in Years 3, 4, 5 or 6. 
You may select outcomes from any topic (or topics) from the Australian Curriculum – Mathematics, Years 3 to 16. 
Justify key components of your lesson planning.
The assignment will be assessed on the following criteria for marking: 
Overall lesson structure, teaching approach, rationale, educational outcomes, lesson aim or key questions, identification of prior knowledge, and lesson timing;
Lesson introduction, key learning activities, lesson conclusion, and assessment;
Applied knowledge of the curriculum in the learning activities, resources and manipulatives used, and areas of differentiation identified;
Incorporation of the proficiency strands, embedding of the general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities, and inclusion of remediation, enrichment and extension;
And a student chosen criterion.
Assignment 2 Programming (50%) 
2a. Analysis of a selection of learning and teaching resources (20%)
Collate four (4) learning and teaching resources suitable for a learning and teaching activity to develop a mathematical concept, and comprising one resource for numeracy, and one resource from each strand (number and algebra; measurement and geometry; and statistics and probability).
For each of these four items, critically analyse why it is a suitable learning and teaching resource. 
Indicate how the resource could be adapted and improved to meet the learning needs of students in an inclusive classroom.
The assignment will be assessed on the following criteria for marking: 
Description of each resource and how it is implemented;
Critical analysis of the suitability of each resource for a learning and teaching activity to develop a mathematical concept;
Indication of how each resource may be adapted and improved to meet the learning needs of students in an inclusive classroom.
2b. Program (30%)
Create or choose a particular manipulative resource, and develop a set of 5 x A4 activity cards for independent or group use of the resource.
Justify key components of your program.
The assignment will be assessed on the following criteria for marking: 
Audience suitability, and curriculum documentation;
Justification for the choice of the manipulative resource;
Discussion of the cultural background, history, development and use of the resource, and possible enrichment; 
The extent to which the activity cards promote and support student learning;
And a student chosen criterion.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.

Penalties for breach of academic conduct vary according to seriousness of the case, and may include the requirement to do further work or repeat work; deduction of marks; the award of zero marks for the assessment; failure of one or more units; suspension from a course of study; exclusion from the University; non-conferral of a degree, diploma or other award to which the student would otherwise have been entitled.
