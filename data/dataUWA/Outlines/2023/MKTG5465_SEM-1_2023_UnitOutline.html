Academic Information

Content This unit introduces students to analytical techniques and research methods (qualitative and quantitative) to analyse consumer data applicable to daily business operations. All topics follow data-driven decision-making framework to demonstrate managerial relevance of data analytics for strategy development/improvement. Univariate, bivariate and key multivariate techniques are covered.

Unit Information

Welcome to MKTG5465 Applied Marketing Research!
To be an effective marketer, you must be able to use, analyse and interpret information about markets and consumers. This information can come from many sources, including marketing research. How do you evaluate the usefulness or accuracy of such information? This unit will empower you to tell good research from bad. 
You will learn about marketing research by doing research yourselves. You will also learn to work in  research-teams, where your individual effort will contribute to accomplishing your team's research objectives. The knowledge and skills you obtain from this unit will help you become a marketer who can design and implement marketing research in-house. You should be able to apply these skills if you/your company commissions marketing research outsourcing it to research agencies.
This course is intended for students that have not previously studied Marketing Research at an undergraduate level. The first part of this unit focuses on qualitative research techniques, while the second part of this course involves statistical analysis to address marketing problems. 
Unit Content: 
This course covers fundamental concepts and stages of marketing research, such as:
1) Marketing research process
2) Problem definition 
3) Qualitative research (sampling, data collection, and data analysis)
4) Quantitative research (sampling, survey/questionnaire design, data collection, and data analysis (descriptive statistics, testing of differences, regressions, and charting)
5) Communicating your findings 
Unit Goals
Current business practices require fast, accurate and data-driven decision making. The aim of this course is to provide students with a solid background in marketing research, contemporary issues in marketing research, data analytics, and interpretation of marketing research insights to assist in strategic and data-driven decision making. Knowledge of these topics will enable students to both implement and evaluate marketing research in workplace if you commission research from an external research agency or need to perform marketing research in-house.
In this unit, you will develop the ability to:
- Demonstrate your skills and knowledge by critically evaluating and devising solutions for business problems.
- Develop more effective communication skills through class participation (practical in-class activities), research reports and presentations.
- Develop competencies to work more effectively in teams.
- Demonstrate self-management, critical thinking and independent learning skills through the completion of the prescribed weekly tasks.

The teaching mode will be as follows this semester:

2) Students enrolled in an online mode:

        • Need to attend 1.5 hours Ms Teams (live online) workshop from 7:40 pm:40 pm to 9:10 pm on Mondays (please join the workshop via MS Teams; see LMS)
Workshops are held each week. Workshops are highly interactive covering both the theory of marketing research and its application through in-class exercises, laboratory exercises (weeks 7-11), and a semester-long group project.
Group project
The group project offers you an opportunity to carry out a mini marketing research project and work on a real business problem. You will work on research objectives specified in the Group Assignment Brief and will develop formal research reports with your conclusions and recommendations as part of your group project. In each workshop, we will be spending time working on your group projects and you will see how theory turns into practice. Make use of my experience in marketing research to develop and/or enhance your research skills. This unit is designed to provide an interactive and exciting team-based and problem-based learning environment.
Live workshops
Live workshops encourage team work approximated to industry marketing research conditions where several consultants work together on the same project. Due to highly interactive and practical nature of the unit, students are encouraged to actively participate in class discussions. Slides for each  live workshop will be posted on LMS (Learning Management System). You are expected to prepare for live workshops as per instructions on LMS for each Week's folder. The concepts covered during the live workshop, your group projects, and pre-recorded lectures will help you prepare for the final examination.

You may be asked to complete two evaluations during this unit: The Student Perception of Teaching (SPOT) and the Students Unit Reflective Feedback (SURF). The SPOT is optional and is an evaluation of the lecturer and the unit. The SURF is completed online and is a university wide survey and deals only with the unit. You will receive an email inviting you to complete the SURF when it is activated. We encourage you to complete the forms as your feedback is extremely important and can be used to make changes to the unit or lecturing style when appropriate.
In case of group project workload grievances, each student will be required to submit a one-page diary documenting her/his contributions to the group project (see "Assessments" folder on LMS).

Referencing Requirements

It is important that the referencing of any sources used in your written work is done properly, if only to substantiate the points you are making in your assignment or project. The APA style is the preferred style to be used. There are some notes for guidance which have been prepared by the library staff: 'APA Referencing Style' (http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/apa). The EndNote software package is a really good system for building up a database of references. Not everyone will want to invest the time in using this system but you should consider it if you intend to build up resource materials or plan to undertake extensive research in a particular area. The library staff have also developed a tutoring package: ‘A Quick Guide to Using EndNote’ which provides the basics for using EndNote with an essay http://guides.library.uwa.edu.au/endnote

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
