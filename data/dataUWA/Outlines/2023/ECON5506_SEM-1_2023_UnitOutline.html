Academic Information

Content This unit encourages participants to explore the behavioural links between financial markets, economic policy and economic performance at an advanced level. The unit focuses on the economics of financial market behaviour and the role of financial markets in macroeconomic performance. Topics include an overview of financial market structure and behaviour, risk and the pricing of financial instruments, financial market efficiency and financial market regulation.

Unit Information

This unit provides an exploration of a few key financial markets including the bond market, international money market, foreign exchange market, and derivatives. Also, the unit looks at the importance of the financial system for the real economy and how financial crises can cause severe economic downturns such as the Asian Financial Crisis and Global Financial Crisis. The IS-LM model is used to characterise the joint movements of output, the interest rate, and the exchange rate in an open economy. International parity relationships which form the basis of the open economy IS-LM model are also discussed in the unit.

Learning And Teaching Strategy

This unit has 2 hours of lecture and 1 hour of workshop every week except Week 7 when the mid-semester exam is scheduled in that week. Lectures and workshops are conducted via MS TEAMS and face-to-face teaching. Students attending the online class must have both their audio and video operative during the class.     

Unit Structure

•	10 online quizzes (3% x 10 = 30%): Students are required to complete an online quiz every week except Week 1 and 7. The weekly quizzes consist of multiple-choice and/or short-answer questions and have a time limit of 15-20 minutes each. An online quiz will become available on Monday at noon and will cease to appear on Friday at midnight in the same week. The deadline for submitting the online quizzes is Friday midnight. No reminders will be sent out for the online quizzes, and students who miss an online quiz will receive zero for that quiz.
•	Mid-semester exam (30%): The MSE will be held online on Wednesday, 19 April 2023 at 9-10 AM. No classes will be held in Week 7 other than the MSE.
•	Final exam (40%): Final exam will be held during the UWA final exam period. It will be an online 2-hour long exam. More information about the final exam will be released in Week 12.

Late submission of Assignments

A penalty of 5 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment item is deducted per day for the first 7 days (including weekends and public holidays) after which the assignment is not accepted. Each 24-hour block is recorded from the time the assignment is due.

For example, if an assignment is late by three days and was given 45 out of a possible mark of 50, you would receive a mark of 37.5 out of 50 (a mark of 2.5 is deducted per day).

If there are a number of tasks within an assessment item, the late penalty may be applied to the whole assessment item after all tasks have been completed.

If an assignment is graded Pass/Fail, failure to submit the assignment in time may result in the student not being permitted to take the final exam at the end of the semester or a teaching period and being unable to progress to the next level of the course or to graduate in the case where it is the final unit of the course;

Penalty for exceeding word limit

Where a submitted assignment exceeds the word limit, a penalty of 1 per cent of the total mark allocated for the assessment task applies for each 1 per cent in excess of the word limit, or the marking ceases once the word limit is reached.
